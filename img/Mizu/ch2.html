Chapter 2: Things I Dislike: Cheating, Thieving Cats

It’s July, and the heat is becoming more intense. Messages from my ex, which used to carry some meaning, have now turned into complaints about the temperature, like “It’s so hot” and “Its really hot~.”

Even with the windows closed at school, the sound of cicadas can be heard, making it feel instantly like summer.

As summer vacation approaches, I see students discussing their summer plans.

“But before that, we have final exams.”

“Yeah.”

Hearing such conversations motivates me too. This will be my first time dealing with final exams.

Lately, I’ve been spending my days after class with Kurei-san, working on creating questions while making sure they are confirmed.

Finding the line between too hard and too easy is surprisingly difficult.

Creating questions that all students can understand without misunderstanding the examiner’s intention proved to be unexpectedly challenging.

I draft all the questions first, and Kurei-san checks them.

Now, it’s the fourth draft, and she’s reviewing it in the staff room after school.

“How does it look?”

Since there was no immediate reaction, I cautiously asked.

Lifting her gaze, Kurei-san smiled softly.

“Yes, I think it’s good. You’ve worked hard.”

At that moment, I let out a sigh…

Not of joy, but of relief.

“Oh dear. Are you okay?”

“…Yes. I’m relieved.”

“You really put a lot of effort into it.”

Kurei-san smiled happily too.

“Hajima-sensei is so earnest. I wonder if you felt a lot of pressure because of that?”

“Well, it’s my first time dealing with final exams.”

“During the midterms, I was the one making them, and you were just watching.”

“Exactly.”

Perhaps because we’ve been talking more day by day, the distance between Kurei-san and me seems to have slightly narrowed.

“I’m sorry for all the trouble, and thank you very much.”

“No, don’t mention it. For a first attempt, this is excellent. Normally, to create exam questions, you’d have to solve several textbooks for the subject matter. The fact that you managed without having to do that shows you’ve been studying hard up until now. You did struggle with the scoring and balancing the quantity and quality of questions, but really, it’s very well done.”

While being praised, the printed drafts were handed back to me. Now, all that’s left is to print enough copies for the students before the exam.

I had started working on it early, but in the end, it was a rush against the deadline.

“You’ve worked hard, so ideally, you’d rest this weekend… But…”

“…I know. I have various other tasks piling up…”

Before the final exams, there’s the grading of the practical tests to be done. The notebooks submitted by the students (mostly loose leaf, though) are almost untouched.

Of course, there are also things that need to be done by Monday.

“It’s already later than usual. Maybe take it home?”

“Yes, I’ll do that.”

“Then, at least take it easy tonight. I’ll stay a bit longer and finish up here.”

After exchanging goodbyes, I left the staff room.

Stepping out of the school and getting on the bus, I tapped on my smartphone.

There were messages from Kirihara and my ex. My ex sent a message with a picture, asking, “I’m going to the pool with my boyfriend tomorrow, which swimsuit looks better?” I replied casually and then focused on Kirihara’s message.

“What about dinner today? Should I order something? Or can you come over to my place?”

The message was sent about an hour ago.

“Sorry, just finished. I’d rather not cook, if that’s okay.”

I sent the message, then sighed, looking up at the bus ceiling.

A reply came back immediately.

“Got it. Then, how about pizza? Is one L-size enough?”

Pizza. I haven’t had it in a while, so I’m quite welcome to the idea.

“That should be enough.”

“Okay. Sorry to trouble you, but could you buy some soda? Pizza and soda are a must!”

“Agreed!”

“Yayyyyy!”

The last message included a sticker of the mascot from the online game we play together, dancing wildly.

I couldn’t help but smile, despite being tired.

Once again, I thought about something I’ve thought about many times before.

If only Kirihara wasn’t a student, there wouldn’t be any problem at all.

She might have been the ideal girlfriend.

After all, since I started visiting her on weekends regularly, I hardly have any unpleasant memories.

When I arrived home, there was a freshly delivered pizza on the dining table.

Glasses filled with ice were also prepared. Kirihara, who came to greet me, sat on a chair, swinging her legs lightly like a little girl.

“Pizza pizza, pizzza♪”

After singing cheerfully, she suddenly switched to a serious expression.

“Now, let us proceed with the opening ceremony. Prepare yourself.”

With words like those from a game’s opening, she opened the pizza box for us.

A classic piece topped generously with cheese, tomato, and salami.

We clapped our hands in unison.

Pouring the soda I bought into glasses, we put our hands together.

“Let’s eat!”

We devoured the first few slices as if we were ravenous.

In the latter half, we took our time, enjoying each bite while sipping our juice.

“The blatant violence of calories… Yet, so delicious. Why is pizza so good?”

“Because it’s bad for you.”

“That’s a healthy statement from a teacher.”

“…Am I being praised?”

“Of course. You’re the man who taught me the taste of home cooking.”

It wasn’t supposed to be anything grand.

“Junk food like this tastes good precisely because it’s occasional.”

With some small talk in between, we neatly split the L-size pizza in half and put it in our stomachs.

Rubbing her belly, Kirihara smiled contentedly.

“Shall we play a game after a food break?”

“Ah, sorry… Actually, I brought some work home.”

“That’s right, you did bring your bag.”

“Sorry.”

“Don’t apologize. It’s inevitable. What kind of work is it?”

“Related to grading.”

“I can’t see that. I’ll have to clear this table soon, then.”

Kirihara stood up and disposed of the pizza trash.

Then, she moved to the room where we usually play games and spread out a kotatsu that had been folded and placed in the corner.

“Are you going to eat something else there?”

“No, I thought I’d study a bit too. Finals are coming up. If I play games near you, I’ll just distract you from your work.”

Despite everything, Kirihara knows when to be serious and reads the atmosphere well.

I brought my bag over and spread out textbooks and notebooks.

Though her posture looked serious, she was wearing her usual shorts and a camisole…

…And because she was facing me, I could see her cleavage.

No matter the situation, she’s nothing but a temptation. Absolutely outrageous.

“Hmm…? What?”

She’s one of those with sharp instincts.

“I just realized it’s the first time you’re studying in front of me.”

It was just a random topic I brought up to divert the conversation, but Kirihara nodded without suspicion.

“During the midterms, the scope wasn’t very wide, so yeah. For the finals, I figured I should at least do a bit.”

It’s so like an honor student to get by with just a little study.

Silently focusing on my work for a while, I occasionally found myself glancing at Kirihara.

She had a cushion spread out on the floor, but her posture changed every time I looked over, perhaps due to discomfort from maintaining the same position. She’d either collapse her legs, stretch them out, or lean forward.

Kirihara’s legs were toned but also soft at the same time. She really had beautiful legs.

…Come to think of it, I used to study like this with my ex-girlfriend during college. We were in the same faculty, so we’d exchange notes and when bored, tease each other and maybe share a kiss.

Such nostalgic memories.

“What? You keep peeking, are you frustrated?”

“No. Just reminiscing about the past.”

“About your ex?”

“…How did you know?”

“You told me a bit about her when we talked on the phone, remember? From your college days.”

“Did I?”

“Yup. You were drinking then. You always forget things when you drink.”

“Only when I drink too much. I don’t get drunk off one or two drinks.”

“You’re weak then.”

Laughing, Kirihara continued to fervently copy her notes onto a blank loose leaf. It seems she’s the type to learn by writing.

I shifted my gaze back to my work and resumed.

“Sensei, when you said you were making the final exam questions, did you do that alone?”

“Of course not. It’d be impossible for a newbie to do it alone. Kurei-san has been helping me.”

“Kurei-sensei, huh? She’s super popular with the guys. They say she looks like she’d be really sexy…”

“Don’t just make things up…”

“Is your assessment about her different?”

“She’s a colleague at work. I’ve never looked at her that way. Though I guess she would stand out if she dressed up.”

“Or so you say, but aren’t you always nervous around her? Just the two of you in the staff room… Ah, no, Kouhai-kun! Like that?”

“Not at all…”

“Really? Not even a little bit of hope? Not even once? Never thought about what it would be like to be lovers with such a senior?”

“Not at all…”

…Well, to say not at all would be a lie.

“How boring.”

It wasn’t said teasingly, but rather with genuine disappointment.

“Kirihara, you’re quiet at school but surprisingly romantic, huh? You like love stories.”

Occasionally, I’d see signs of her watching love dramas or reading romance manga.

“Because, when it comes down to it, conversations between a man and a woman often lead to that, right? Whether it’s at school or work, that doesn’t change.”

“I wonder.”

At the company I quit, there were seniors who ended up marrying after an office romance. Now that I think about it, my ex’s current boyfriend was also a colleague.

“In college, when guys and girls got together, they often ended up in such relationships.”

“It’s the prime time for wanting to hook up, right? Did you have your share?”

“Well, somewhat.”

“Gross.”

“You’re the one who brought it up.”

Even as we joked, the work progressed.

It seems we both concentrate better on our tasks when exchanging casual conversations.

“You might not make moves on me, but you have your own history, huh, Sensei?”

“What’s that supposed to mean? Are you jealous?”

“No, not really?”

Suddenly feeling her gaze, I looked towards Kirihara.

She smiled satisfyingly, almost wickedly.

“Because right now, I’m almost entirely yours.”

…

I turned my attention back to my work, trying to focus.

“Sensei, your face is a bit red.”

I was laughed at but ignored it.

…She really is troublesome.

“Creating them is tough, but once the exams start, having no classes makes things a bit easier,” Kurei-san said, and I found that to be true.

At first, I was nervous about whether there were any mistakes in the questions… but as the exams proceeded without any questions or complaints from the students, I felt relieved.

There were no comments about the exams being too difficult or too easy.

It seems everyone thought Kurei-san made the questions for this exam. Kirihara was the only student who knew I had made them.

…I secretly asked for her feedback.

“It was just normal? The questions were usual.”

Before I finished typing my response, Kirihara sent several messages in a row.

“It means Sensei did a great job. Well done.”

I couldn’t help but feel a little happy.

By the way, Kirihara’s score, despite supposedly not studying much, was near perfect. Just like during the midterms.

As expected the honor student, I thought.

Not just in studies, but Kirihara is smart overall.

She’s even better at games than I am, and whenever there’s new content or an update, she quickly finds the correct approach. She solves game levels even before strategies begin circulating online.

Not only is she good at studies, but she’s also favored by other teachers. Kirihara, unlike me, will likely live a future without making mistakes.

…So why is she so fixated on someone like me?

“Are you okay? Lost in thought again?”

“Did you hear what I said?”

“That’s good. After all, Hajima-sensei, you’re the guest of honor.”

I heard that this drinking party would also serve as my welcome party. I couldn’t miss it.

“I like drinking, but I’m quite a lightweight… but I don’t get drunk off one or two drinks.”

“Oh, that’s a bit disappointing. You like it but can’t handle much.”

“What about you, Kurei-sensei?”

“You’ll see on the day. It’ll be fun.”

…This means she probably drinks quite a bit.

Seeing her smirk confidently, I had that gut feeling.

“By the way, they’re collecting fees for the day in advance, right?”

“You don’t have to pay, Hajima-sensei.”

“Really? Is that okay?”

“Of course, you’re the guest of honor.”

“…Is that really okay?”

“It’s fine. You’ve worked hard this semester, so please enjoy without worrying.”

“I wonder if I really did well…”

It feels like I mostly just troubled Kurei-san for help.

“Neither the principal nor I praise someone who hasn’t worked hard, you know?”

The mention of the principal’s name probably comes from the meeting involving Kurei-san and me the other day.

──You’ve worked hard this semester.

──We’re grateful you’ve been here without taking any days off. You did well.

──Your responsibilities will gradually increase, but let’s keep working hard.

That was the gist of the conversation.

Kurei-san also mentioned, “You managed to create the exam questions properly, and there were no issues with how you conducted your classes.”

“Being humble is important, but being too humble can be troublesome for those around you.”

“…I’ll be careful.”

“Hehe. Whether praised or not, the next challenges will come soon anyway. In fact, it feels like you’re already facing them.”

“…You mean managing the class, right?”

Before the final exams started, a little problem had arisen in my class.

“The plan for the school presentation day isn’t decided yet, right?”

“That’s right…”

At Mori Kawara Academy, right after the summer vacation starts, there’s a presentation day for middle school students considering our school and their guardians.

The students also plan mock shops and events to liven up the presentation day. It’s somewhat like a cultural festival.

Each class receives a budget from the parents’ association and uses it to do something. What they do is up to the students to decide.

“You still can’t get the opinions to come together?”

“…I really want to decide during tomorrow’s homeroom.”

“Right. Considering the preparations, tomorrow is the deadline.”

“Preferably not. If things get too messy, you might have to step in—but the students who didn’t have their opinions favored will be unhappy.”

…Hmm, I scratched my head.

“Make sure to decide within time so you can come to the drinking party feeling good.”

“And if we can’t decide?”

“You have to decide.”

“…Yes.”

I’ve always been bad at resolving conflicts.

…It would be best if the students decided on their own.

All of them are relatively easy profit-makers as long as the cost of ingredients is managed.

Classes without their unique ideas almost always apply for mock shops.

And the profit from the summer presentation can add to the budget for the autumn cultural festival.

In essence, it’s possible to save up in the summer and go all out for the festival.
