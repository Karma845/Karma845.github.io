“…You’re not angry at me?”

“Getting angry won’t help. I can somewhat guess why Kirihara-san acted the way she did.”

Kurei-san bit her lip as if in regret.

“I heard about the time she had a fever and her parents didn’t come to pick her up. Wanting to be spoiled by someone isn’t something I can’t understand.”

“…”

“Tell Kirihara-san that if she has any troubles, she can consult with me. And if she comes to you for advice, let me be present.”

“…Understood.”

In the end, neither Kirihara nor Kurei-san blamed me.

After returning home, I sent a message to Kirihara.

“Was this the right thing to do?”

Her reply came immediately.

“There was no other way. If we told the truth, it would be bad for both of us, right?”

I didn’t know how to respond.

Thank you? Sorry?

…Either response felt like it would disappoint Kirihara.

While I was hesitating, Kirihara sent a video.

It showed her laptop screen.

She was on the network drive management screen, operating the mouse icon to delete some data.

She was deleting a file named “rokuon data” (recording data).

…Probably the data from that day.

The data Kirihara used to keep me tied to her.

“You’re free now. Let’s go back to normal from now on.”

Her messages kept coming.

“Oh, but is it still okay to play online games? haha… That’s still a secret relationship.”

…After a little thought, I typed a reply.

“If that’s what Kirihara wants.”

“Okay then. See you in the game. Thanks for your hard work!”

And so, the complicit relationship between Kirihara and me was over.

It ended with me being protected unilaterally.

Only a sense of shame and emptiness filled my heart.
