Chapter 2: Patchwork Obsession

I loved my dad.

He knew everything I didn't know and was always there to protect me. Someone I truly admire.

I felt at ease under his big hands that stroked me, and I was happy to have been born with hair the same color as his.

My first memories as a child are the sensation of my body being rocked by the carriage, the clear wind, and the endless plains of withered grass. I remember my eyes often glancing up at the dust and sand smoke that rose up in the air.

Spending my days traveling with my parents, who were both adventurers. That was my life when I was a child.

My parents were always on good terms with each other and never had a single quarrel.

They were both well-educated and skilled in the martial arts, and they fought off many powerful demonic beasts with their well-matched combination of skills.

My parents were always requested by various people by name, and at the same time, they were highly appreciated.

I was proud of them, and I did not doubt that one day I would learn from them and become a first-rate adventurer. But those days didn't last forever.

My father ...... passed away.

How did he die, what was the cause? I still don't know.

But I do remember that shortly before he died, my mother argued with him.

No, it was more like a one-sided confrontation.

We were on our way from town to town when the trees and grass had fallen asleep. As I drifted off to sleep in our camp tent, I was awakened by my mother's strong tone of voice.

"What the heck are you thinking!?"

I peeked out through a gap in the tent and saw my mother there, who was unusually loud and impatient.

Dad answered all of Mom's questions, then apologetically hugged her and whispered something in her ear.

Just what did he say, I wonder? My mother's eyes widened and she burst into tears.

Then she bit her lip sadly and hung her head, not pressing my father any further.

Mom, who was always smiling and cheerful and never showed any signs of weakness, cried. I felt a tightening sensation in my chest upon seeing it, and I involuntarily walked out of the tent.

"Lisa ....... Were you awake?"

Their eyes widened when they noticed me coming out.

"Hey, I got an urgent job to do. Dad has to go."

"E...... a, uh......."

Wait, what happened? Why is Mom crying?

My head was quickly filled with one question after another, my bad premonition grew even worse, and my heart was beating fast with shivers as if my whole body was frozen. My mouth kept flailing in impatience to say something, and as if I had forgotten what to say, I only uttered meaningless noises.

"Don't worry, I'll be right back."

After gently patting me, who was anxious and dismayed, Dad went away.

A month later, he came back stone cold.

How? Why? My young mind was crushed by a reality I did not want to believe. And even though I tried to find out the cause, I was never able to figure it out.

And my uncontrollable anger and anxiety were directed toward my mother, who let my father go alone.

Why didn't Mom go with Dad like she usually did?

But that anger eventually subsided. A new life was being born in Mom's belly.

A few months later, Mom gave birth to my sister and we returned to her homeland.

The Principality of Handora. A neighbor of the great Kingdom of Forsina and a member of the Union of Small Nations.

We moved to Oire village in this country.

I didn't know the cause of my father's death. Mom won't say anything either. All that remained was my longing for my father. My dream of becoming an adventurer. And then I met him, who supported my dream.

Nozomu Bountis. My first friend in Oire village. He was the only son of a farmer in this village, perhaps because of his curiosity, and he seemed to enjoy listening to my stories about my travels.

Since then, we spent several years in this village.

Oire village is a small village in the mountains, where all the villagers usually work together to do the farm work. So, we became friends very quickly.

But since when did I become conscious of him?

"......!?"

A bolt of lightning flashed across my vision, and at the same time, I saw a scene I had never imagined before.

In a forest shrouded in twilight. Most likely Spasim Forest.

As I hide in the thick vegetation, I stifle my breath and stare at the scene in front of me.

Two childhood friends. One is someone I once loved.

Nozomu Bountis. The bastard who betrayed me and then threw my feelings for him away.

The other is Ken Notice. The one who supported me when I was in the depths of depression.

These two were facing each other and having a talk in such a deserted place. What was the reason ...... I wonder?

"Just what are they doing ......?"

Feeling anxious, I leaned forward to eavesdrop on their conversation.

*Shock*. Then, with another bolt of lightning, the scene in front of me changed.

A terrifying monster attacked. A pitch-black monster with red eyes. It raised its sword in a slow arc and swung it down at my loved one.

"............!"

With a cry, I intercepted the sword flash and caught it with the saber I had drawn.

For some reason, the loud voice that should have come out of my own mouth did not reach my ears.

The monster's gaze turned to me as I interrupted it. Its red eyes, stained with hatred, were filled with disappointment and disdain.

[......!]

The monster's voice sounded muffled, and I could not understand what it was saying. No, all the sounds around me were distant, and all I could hear was the ringing in my ears.

But I know what its gaze was saying.

Why are you protecting the one who betrayed you? He said.

What is this monster saying?

You are the one who betrayed me......

(Huh? Why am I saying that this monster betrayed me......)

An enemy that attacked us. A monster that can do nothing but kill people. Why would I feel inexplicable feelings of "betrayal" toward such an existence?

Doubts suddenly popped up in my mind. As if perceiving this, the monster quickly brought its face close to mine.

The monster's face in front of me changed into the shape of Nozomu.

[You actually knew everything, didn't you? When I gave you that letter?]

Hearing these words, my eyebrows twitched. My heart jumped and I gulped.

In the back of my mind, I remember the scene of Nozomu who came to give me the letter in the joint class before the Special Comprehensive Training.

I'm sure that Nozomu at that time had the same straightforward eyes as the man I've known for a long time......

(~, What nonsense are you spouting!? You monster!)

I put all my strength into my saber to cut down the traitorous monster.

I'm sure it was all a mistake. Because I saw him throwing away his promise!

[Was that really the case?]

The monster once again questions me. For some reason, his voice has changed to that of a woman.

[Sure, you saw him with another woman and immediately confronted him. But was that really him?]

Yes, of course it was! I saw something unbelievable! That was definitely Nozomu! And he denied the fact that I had seen him, out of his mouth!

I was so emotional that I spat out those words. As if mocking me, the monster shook his shoulders.

[Then why didn't you reciprocate Ken Notice's feelings?]

Because after being betrayed like that, I-.......

[Lie. Well, it's not really a lie, but you had other feelings.]

You couldn't possibly know that......

[Of course, I do. Because I am ...... you.]

The monster's appearance changed again. Red hair spread out from behind the black-tinted shadow, and a new appearance was revealed from within the shadow. The face was ...... exactly the same as mine.

[You were just scared. To be betrayed again. And to know the truth......]

A sense of disgust welled up in me. At the same time, I was struck by a feeling of realization.

I glared at the monster in front of me and put strength into my arms, trying to deny my own feelings.

However, against my will, the saber did not even budge, and the monster only smiled contemptuously at my resistance.

[I'll ask you again. Was it really him who betrayed you?]

Of course it was, didn't I already say so earlier!?

[You didn't doubt it even once? Did you really look straight into his eyes when you questioned him?]

Her words brought the scene of when I questioned Nozomu flooding back to my mind.

He muttered apologetically. He was the one who discarded the dream we had held in our hearts for so long. In the depths of his eyes, a dark light of amusement shone brightly......

(Huh......?)

I felt a strong sense of déjà vu, and something inside of me shook violently.

I've seen those eyes somewhere. Just recently, yes, in that forest .......

[Look behind you......]

Prompted by the whisper that slipped in, I was about to turn my gaze away from the monster.

I must not turn around...... Even though I thought so, my eyes were looking back before I knew it.

[Is that person really the one you're supposed to be protecting?]

There was a black monster wearing Ken's mask.

In front of my stunned eyes, the black monster jerked and attacked me.

Before I had time to let out a scream, I was swallowed from behind by the shadow monster.

A feeling of disgust and rejection, incomparably more intense than when I had just witnessed the monster's transformation, assaulted my entire body. At the same time, my entire body began to be invaded by the shadow.

(......~, ~!!)

I desperately tried to escape by moving my hands, but the shadow's invasion did not stop.

Perhaps amused even by my resistance, the shadow that had swallowed me trembled with ecstasy as it devoured my body, little by little.

Suddenly, something touches the side of my face. I looked to the side and saw Ken's mask, which had approached me as if to creep up on me. His face was distorted, as though he were a little child who had acquired his favorite toy.

[What a weak woman. Only focused on the flimsy facts you could see, and refused to see what was behind it, even though you were aware of it. Filthy coward. Such a woman-......]

I spat at such a sight of me. Then, I raised the saber in my hand and-.......

[-should have just died......]

I cut myself down along with the shadow of my crime.

Rising up as if bouncing.

It was still dark outside the window, and the sun was just beginning to shine on the roofs of the houses that lined the street. Lisa gasped for air and pressed her chest against the choking sensation.

At the same time, she felt an intense headache. The pain felt as if she had been hit over and over again with a hammer from behind her head, and she involuntarily slumped over on the bed. Her long hair hung down, and her eyelids were tightly closed.

A flash of light shone in the back of her eyes. As she remained slumped for a while, Camilla, who was sleeping on the bed next to her, woke up and called out to her.

"Lisa, what's wrong?"

"...... Camilla. No, it's nothing. I'm sorry I woke you."

Pushing back the pain running through her head, she looked up and smiled. Camilla and Lisa have been roommates since freshman year, and have become close friends through that relationship. And Camilla is one among those who has always supported her. So Lisa didn't want to make her worry.

"It's fine, it's time to get up anyway. Another nightmare?"

After a few seconds of silence, Lisa gave a small nod, defeated by Camilla's gaze directed at her.

"......Un."

She started having nightmares. They were supposed to be dreams that would eventually fade away, and yet they stuck to her mind and devoured her over and over again. Not only while she was sleeping, but also sometimes during small gaps in his daily life. Even last night, she woke up five times. Completely sleep-deprived. She probably looks terrible right now.

The dream depicted a scene of Nozomu, who had summoned her to the forest, arguing with Ken. Eventually, they began slashing at each other, and a light blue dwarfish creature appeared beside Ken, who was cornered, and restrained Nozomu.

As he fell to the ground, Ken looked down at him with the most twisted smile one could ever see. At that moment, an incredible amount of power overflowed from Nozomu's body.......

"......!"

Having thought that far, she shook her head to rid herself of her imagination.

Things never happened that way in the first place. Even though he summoned me with a letter, that man never showed up after all.

(But ....... Why do I remember my dreams so clearly after seeing them so many times......?)

Scenes seen in dreams are usually forgotten by the end of the day. Even if they are remembered, they soon become full of holes like insect bites, and within a few days, they are completely erased from memory, never to be seen again.

But her nightmares were bizarrely vivid and did not disappear no matter how much time passed.

On the contrary, they became more and more bloated and irritating.

"What shall we do, Lisa? Should we take the day off?"

Camilla, who had somehow gotten off the bed and come to her side, looked at her with a worried expression.

"I-I'm fine.

I'm just a little tired, that's all. Now, let's get ready and we'll go to the school."

Her words were surprisingly empty and helpless.

Ignoring the lingering fatigue, she went to the wardrobe and began to get dressed.

She wanted to escape Camilla's gaze on her back. She wanted to forget the nightmare she had just had.

But that wish was soon betrayed. Because [that guy] was waiting for her when she stepped out of the dormitory.

A room in the boys' dormitory.

Nozomu, the owner of this room, was getting ready for school in a brisk manner.

The town was still enveloped in dim darkness. It was clearly too early. He dressed in his school uniform and was about to put his katana at his waist......

"......!"

The numbness that suddenly ran up his arm made him wince. He frowned at the numbness that suddenly ran up his arm. He put strength into his arm, which was about to drop the katana.

He managed to put it strapped to his waist without dropping his master's memento.

"That was-...... as expected."

The numbness quickly subsided and the sensation returned. Nozomu could guess what was causing this numbness. Last night, too, he had dreamed of the Dragon King.

"Phew....... alright."

He took a deep breath. Although the anxiety lingered, it quickly calmed down.

After checking his appearance and steeling himself, Nozomu left his room with a bag containing his breakfast and study materials that he had prepared in advance.

Upon leaving the dormitory, Nozomu headed north. The border between the civil and administrative districts. There, he found the building he was heading for.

The Solminati Academy girls' dormitory. Nozomu waited for the person in question to come out of the main gate across the street.

Female students came out of the entrance of the dormitory one after another. When they saw Nozomu in front of the girls' dormitory, they all tilted their heads in unison. Waiting in a place like this would naturally attract attention.

However, Nozomu paid no heed to their stares and kept his eyes fixed on the front gate of the girls' dormitory.

Then, the expected person appeared.

"Lisa, are you all right?"

"Like I said, I'm fine......"

There stood two girls, Lisa with a somewhat gloomy expression on her face, and Camilla, who was standing by her side showing concern for her.

The face of Lisa was pale and she had dark circles under her eyes. She was obviously sleep-deprived. Despite his concern about her condition, Nozomu shifted his mind and walked over to them.

"You......!?"

Camilla wore a surprised expression, then stepped in between Lisa and Nozomu as if to protect her.

But her eyes wavered erratically and lacked composure.

She, too, was one of the people who had framed Nozomu, but at the same time, she was carrying some special circumstances. Nozomu took a deep breath to relax himself, then slowly opened his mouth to Lisa and Camilla.

"Lisa, it's been a while. I believe it's been about two weeks?"

The voice that came out of his mouth as he faced them once again was calmer than Nozomu himself had expected.

"So do you, Camilla. Though I lost myself in anger in the forest, sorry about that."

"How......?"

(......Yep, my mind is calm.)

Facing them like this, Nozomu came to realize something.

His anger did not well up anymore.

Camilla probably did not expect to be spoken to in such a calm tone. She was surprised by Nozomu's quiet and calm voice. Her eyes widened.

"A-A-......."

"Lisa?"

However, on the other hand, Lisa's behavior was strange. Her complexion had gone pale. Her eyes were unfocused and wide open.

Her expression did not seem to indicate that she was okay.

Another important person also appeared here.

"W-Why are you here ......!?"

"Ken, huh......."

Ken was so surprised to see Nozomu here that his face contorted into an ugly smile for a moment, but then he immediately smiled as usual. After that, he ignored Nozomu and walked toward Lisa.

"Good morning Lisa. Sorry, I'm late. It must be a disaster to get tangled up with this guy so early in the morning. Come on, let's go ......"

Ken tried to take Lisa's hand with a deft gesture, as if he was accustomed to doing so.

"~, -no......!"

"Huh?"

But the next moment, slap! With a dry smack, Lisa brushed Ken's hand away.

Ken's face twitched in pain, followed by a look of disbelief.

Lisa, on the other hand, also stiffened, her eyes widening at her own actions. Silence fell, as if time had stood still.

"L-Lisa ......?"

A trembling voice escaped from Ken's mouth. Lisa, also realizing what she had done, her face grew pale with fear.

"~......!"

Then she ran away.

While Ken was taken aback by the abruptness of the event, Nozomu hurried after her.

Lisa went from in front of the main gate of the girls' dormitory to the main street of the civic district. She was physically talented by nature. By the time Nozomu reached the main street, Lisa was already on her way into intricate alleyways.

"Lisa, wait!"

Nozomu called out to her, but she did not stop, instead, her steps became faster and faster.

If one looked closely, one could see the residue of magic power covering her body. She was unconsciously boosting her physical capabilities with her magic power.

Strengthening only by magic power without any magic spells would not have much effect. But even so, an ordinary person would easily be shaken off.

At this rate, he would lose sight of her. Nozomu, who had made this judgment, released his Qi, strengthened his physical abilities, and pursued after her.

But as he rounded a few alleys, his eyes widened at the sight of an object blocking his path.

"Is this ...... a wall of water?"

A three-meter-high wall of water towered over the alleyway.

Nozomu immediately sprang up, putting all his Qi into his legs, and leaped. He leaped over the water wall with his hand on the eaves of the roof of a house.

When the water wall failed to block Nozomu, it crumbled down with a splashing sound.

But by the time he landed, it had already reconstructed itself in the direction he was going.

"The work of Ken's contracted spirit, huh?"

Nozomu, aware of the entity controlling the water wall, applied his Qi to the katana at his waist and drew it. With a single stroke, the water wall was cut in half and collapsed. This time, he broke through the water wall without slowing down.

However, the other party must have decided that a simple wall would not be enough to stop Nozomu. This time, they took a different approach.

"Mist......!?"

Suddenly, a dense fog formed around Nozomu. His vision was so white that he could not see a single meter ahead of him. Naturally, Lisa's back was completely obscured. The next moment, a huge stone wall rushed in front of Nozomu, who was running.

"Damn it......!!"

Just before he slammed into the wall, Nozomu reflexively put his foot on the wall and changed the direction of forward momentum to upward. He ran up the wall of the house and onto the roof.

By the time Nozomu was on the roof, the fog had dissipated and the gentle spring sunlight was illuminating the rows of houses.

Lisa was already out of sight, not a shadow remained.

"I lost her......"

He bit his lip and clenched my fists. But there was one thing he knew.

He had to do something about that spirit if he wanted to talk to Lisa again. That means he can't do it on his own.

"I'll have to start over......"

Nozomu looked once again in the direction in which she would have run away. What came to mind was the promise he made to support her when he was a little boy.

(A promise that I clung to in order to escape, a promise that no longer has any meaning. But still......)

After closing his eyes for a moment, Nozomu turned on his heel and jumped down from the roof.

Once again, he held the once-forgotten determination in his heart.

Its appearance is reminiscent of a light blue child. Called Mefi, it was a lesser water spirit who had contracted with Ken.

A light blue source element flowed from the spirit's hand as it raised toward Lisa, enveloping her entire body. This power erased the events that had just taken place from her memory.

"What do you mean? Why is she rejecting me!?"

Ken's voice was hoarse, as if he couldn't stand it any longer. Mefi had created a soundproof barrier around them so that no one could hear what they were saying, but the expression on his face was clearly that of someone who was being cornered.

Nozomu sent a letter saying he wanted to talk to her, and Ken tried to get rid of him. Having seen their exchange in the forest, Lisa half realized that Ken had tricked her and Nozomu.

Not wanting to let Lisa go, Ken took measures to seal her memories, but the reversal of the facts she had believed had devastated her more than Ken had anticipated.

As a result, she had been seeing her sealed memories repeatedly in the form of nightmares, and even his spirit's suggestion was becoming ineffective.

[Like I said, when you were in the first year, I told you that you should have just killed him. But you prolonged it so badly just because you wanted to see him fall, that's why this happened~~]

Ken gritted his teeth at Mefi's words.

The reason Ken decided to remove Nozomu from Lisa's side was because of the manifestation of Ability Suppression.

A flashback of his old days ...... back in his hometown of Oire Village came back to his mind.

Unlike Nozomu, who was an only child, Ken had many brothers and sisters. There were eight of them.

Including his parents, he had a large family of ten. Among them, he was the sixth child. He was not as young as the younger ones, but not as dependable as the older ones. Moreover, his younger siblings were born in rapid succession, and as soon as he could remember, his parents and older siblings had already stopped paying attention to him.

He had to take care of his young siblings, and above all, farm work required manpower. Inevitably, Ken was expected to do some work ahead of being pampered.

Ken felt isolated amid a large family. This cast a shadow over Ken's childhood.

It was not that he had any ill will toward his family. He just happened to be a little out of the family circle.

He became friends with Nozomu and Lisa, who were the same age as him.

The first friend he made.

The first time he was not lonely. How comforting that must have been to him.

So it was inevitable that Ken was attracted to Lisa.

And he knew that Nozomu and Lisa would be attracted to each other. They were two of his beloved people.

He was happy for them. But at the same time, he also felt a stale, comatose feeling.

Why it wasn't ime? Why did she always look at Nozomu first?

I want her to look at me. Such small jealousy had been smoldering in his heart for a long time.

It was still good in the past. Ken was rather quiet at that time, and Nozomu was the one who pulled Ken along.

So he was able to express his blessing. He was able to rejoice. Even if there was a small lump in his heart.

It couldn't be helped. Somewhere in the back of his mind, he was able to think that way and give up.

But when they came to Solminati Academy, their positions were reversed.

Ken grew up to be one of the best students in his grade, while Nozomu fell to the bottom of the class.

And the jealousy that had been building up since he was a child began to grow. Before he knew it, the sight of Nozomu crawling around in the practical class made his heart ache, and his stomach began to churn every time Lisa stood by him as he was unable to grow, no matter how hard he tried.

Then, his growing jealousy exploded. He took advantage of Camilla, who was close to Lisa at the time, by holding on to her secret and using it to blackmail her and bring down Nozomu.

Camilla rushed toward Lisa, brushing aside Mefi's complaints.

Camilla frowned and bit her lip at the sight of Lisa standing there unconscious as if she were a doll.
