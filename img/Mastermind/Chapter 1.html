It was Friday, on the middle of May.

That morning, right after homeroom, the classroom buzzed with more excitement than usual. Today marked the first seat rearrangement of our second year.

Yesterday, our homeroom teacher announced that there would be a raffle for the seat rearrangement, so I prayed at the shrine on the way home from school. It didn’t matter if it was by the window, in the middle, near the hallway, or at the front or back. I just wanted to be next to the girl I liked—Takase Narumi.

And here came the fateful moment. I nervously drew the lottery—

“Nice to meet you, Fujisaki-kun”

And there it was, I successfully secured the seat next to Takase. Thank you, God!

Just for the record, my seat was in the last row of the second column by the window. Takase was on my left. Sunlight streamed in through the window, making her chestnut hair appeared translucent. Is this what they mean by a girl with an ethereal beauty?

“Nice to meet you. By the way, how do you know my name?”

“I remember. We were in the same class last year even though we didn’t interact much. Do you remember my name, Fujisaki-kun?”

“Takase Narumi, right?”

“Whoa, you remembered my full name!”

Takase is so cute!

For a tall and rugged guy like me, girls who genuinely smile at me were extremely rare. They treated me normally once we got to know each other, but at first encounter, they invariably look scared.

However, right after school started, Takase looked up at me with a smile and said, “Wow, you’re tall. So cool!”

In that moment, I fell for her.

A cute girl, even if it was just flattery, calling me ‘cool’ with a smile. What guy wouldn’t fall for that? None, I could confidently say there was none.

It had been a year and one month since I harbored a crush on her. I wasn’t lucky to get a seat next to her last year, but finally, I had secured the ideal seat. I’ll make the most of this chance!

“By the way, Takase, do you know my full name?”

“Well, um… just a moment, I’ll try to guess!”

“Do you need a hint?”

“If you’re offering, sure!”

“Alright, here’s a hint. It’s a name that like the sun.”

“The sun, the sun…” Takase murmured while touching her temple with her fingertip, striking a pose like she was Ikkyu-san.1 The sight of her was so cute that I’d want it as my wallpaper.

“Hey, Naru-chan, wanna go buy some juice?”

As I admired the cute figure before me, a girl who was sitting in front of Takase interrupted.

She had a blonde hair with azure eyes. Her name was Momoi. Her question made Takase dropped her cute Ikkyu pose.

What’s with this, Momoi? Do you have any idea how long I’ve been yearning for more interaction with Takase? It’s been a year and a month for crying out loud! Can’t you just go get your own juice? Heck, I’ll even treat you later if you just let me have this!

“Nothing.”

“….”

And seriously, no need for the glare. She really disliked guys, huh?

“We are playing a guessing game with Fujisaki-kun’s name now. Do you wanna join, Maho-chi?”

“It’s Taro, right?”

“It’s Haruto.”

“That’s the most useless information of this year.”

Momoi said with a genuine disinterest before leaving the classroom with Takase.

“Man, Fujisaki, you’re lucky to talk to Momoi,” said Yamada as he watched Momoi’s back with a dreamy expression.

“Just having Momoi talk to you is amazing. How much karma did your accumulate in your past life for this?!”

“If this is the result of karma, I don’t want to remember my past life.”

“You really aren’t interested in Momoi, huh? What’s happening to your strike zone?”

“It’s not about strike zone. I’m just not interested.”

Well, I could understand why guys like Yamada were head over heels for Momoi.

After all, she was a half-Japanese blonde with well-endowed figure. She lived in a luxury condo and had excellent athletic skills. However, she wasn’t a flawless superwoman—she was a bit weak in studies which only added to her cuteness. Furthermore, she was caring and actively reached out to girls who seemed troubled.

However, her kindness was exclusively reserved for girls. If she were friendly with both genders, she’d attract a swarm of guys but she was notorious for detesting them.

Those who knew Momoi were well aware of her aversion to guys, which was probably why there were no rumors about anyone confessing to her. Or at least, unheard to my ears—there might be someone crying in secret somewhere.

I turned my gaze to the right to disguise my feelings for Takase.

Next to me, a petite girl was resting her head on her desk. She had long black hair tied into twin braids. She was my twin sister, Fujisaki Kotomi.

Yamada grinned as if understanding the situation.

“Ah, you want to be close to your sister.”

It was understandable why Yamada would say that. Kotomi didn’t speak at school.

She was introverted, shy, a loner, and spent every breaks pretending to be asleep. Perhaps that’s why even the friendly Momoi ignored her.

She could converse normally with family, and if she behaved with the same energy as at home, she could easily make friends. However, for the past few days, she had been unusually down at home and school which worried me.

I doubted she’d suddenly being shy around the family, so there must be something on her mind. Maybe she was anxious about her plans to go buy anime goods on Saturday. A few days ago, she worriedly mentioned, “What if there’s a typhoon on Saturday?”

Even though the forecast for Saturday showed a 0% chance of precipitation, her anxiety probably wouldn’t disappear since she was bit of a worrywart.

“Each school probably has different rules for class assignments.”

I said that to protect Kotomi’s reputation, but the likely reason was that the teachers were concerned.

Kotomi was extremely shy, and as far as I knew, she never had any friends since elementary school. During the first year, she was so isolated that the homeroom teacher, who was worried about her, assigned her to the same class as me.

I felt bad for the considerate teacher, and seeing my sister’s lonely figure pained me. But at school, I tried my best not to interfere with her school life.

I wasn’t being mean. Kotomi asked me to keep my distance. Even though we were siblings, it would attract attention if we were seen together. That’s why she wanted me to leave her alone.

Despite being twins born on the same day, I was still her older brother.

Moreover, I knew Kotomi desired friends. Therefore, I wanted to be there for my sister, specifically by helping her make friends. If she had them, she could enjoy a happy school life.

Ideally, I’d like her to become friends with Takase. If I could date Takase and manage to connect her with Kotomi, we could all have a happy school life together.

It’s just… Takase was an outdoorsy sports girl while Kotomi was an indoor otaku, so whether they’d hit it off was questionable.

Also—

“Move, Tanaka. You’re in the way.”

“Uh, I’m Yamada.”

“I don’t care what your name is.”

Being scolded by Momoi upon her returning, Yamada happily retreated. The guys envied him as he walked away.

Even if the friendship plan went well, the issue was Momoi.

Takase and Momoi were close. If the friend plan succeeded, Kotomi would likely interact with Momoi too. Having more friends was a good thing. Although I didn’t like her, if Momoi could get along with my sister, she was more than welcome.

However, Kotomi could only talk about anime, manga, and games. Even though Momoi was friendly with girls, I doubted she’d enjoy chatting with Kotomi. If the conversation would quickly shift to fashion or celebrity topics, Kotomi would become a statue and the awkward situation would make her leave the group.

If only Momoi were an otaku, those worries would disappear. But that’s not the case.

At least, that’s what I thought at that moment.

But today, I managed to get the seat next to her. We might end up exchanging answers during the upcoming midterm exams at the end of this month. If I could flaunt a perfect score, it should leave the impression of being smart!

She wasn’t being shy around me; at home, we were just normal siblings despite not having interacted much at school. She stumbled on her words simply because it was hard to bring up the topic. For example—

You earned money by harvesting crops and selling livestock products, which allowed you to buy new clothes, furnish your house, or even move to a larger farm.

And because it was an online game, you could interact with other farm owners, the players. You could make friends, chat with them, and there was even a marriage feature in it.

Kotomi, despite being shy, didn’t dislike socializing. The reason she started playing online games in the first place was to make friends. The somewhat hefty monthly fee of 800 yen, which was a significant expense for a middle schooler back then, was covered by our parents mainly because she wanted to have friends.

That’s pretty much all I knew about [Life of Farmer].

“So, what about that game?”

“You see, I actually have a wife in LoF.”

“Not a husband?”

“No, it’s a wife. I use a cool male character.”

Even though Kotomi was shy in real life, she managed to get married in an online game. And to another female character, no less. It was surprising, but then again, it wasn’t like they met face-to-face. If it didn’t involve direct interaction, she could display enough social skills even to the extent of getting married.

“Did you come here just to announce your in-game marriage?”

“No, we’ve been married for two years now.”

“So, you’re not asking for congratulations?”

“Getting married in a game doesn’t warrant congratulations. Games are games, and real life is real life. But…” she let out a gloomy sigh, “She didn’t quite see it that way.”

“She invites me for an offline meeting.”

“Ah… I see.”

I understood the situation.

She was afraid of meeting her online game wife in real life. While chatting could be enjoyable, face-to-face meeting posed a different challenge. Despite being in the second year of their in-game marriage, in reality, it was like meeting for the first time, and her shyness would likely come to the forefront.

However, they got along well in the online game. If Kotomi could overcome the barrier of meeting in person, she might make friends in the real world.

But here’s the thing—Every now and then, I saw news reports about people getting into trouble with someone they met online, and as her brother, I was worried about letting Kotomi go alone. Just like she was pretending to be a man, her partner might be doing the same.

If they found out Kotomi was a high school girl, their attitude could change unexpectedly.

That’s what I assumed, but it seemed I was wrong. Kotomi shook her head.

She’s your wife. Go meet her yourself.

I was told that you could get through producer-related conversations by saying, ‘She’s too good.’ So, I tossed that in here.

Darn it. “Too good” wouldn’t cut it. In that case—
