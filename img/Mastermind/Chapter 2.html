Chapter 2: Confession from My Younger Sister’s Online Wife

On that night, after breezing through my homework, I delved into my usual routine of studying.

The midterms were just two days away, and my efforts from this point would determine whether I’d secure a spot in the top ten of our grade.

My goal was to achieve a perfect score in my strong subject, Japanese history. If I could present a perfect answer sheet, it would solidify in Takase’s mind that “Fujisaki Haruto is indeed smart.“

It might seem dubious to seek study help from someone of the opposite sex whose contact information they didn’t even possess, but well, Takase was friendly with guys too. There was a decent chance she would reach out to me regardless of gender.

At school, Takase was practically glued to the Momoi Group, leaving me with little chance to converse with her. In order to bridge that gap quickly, acing the test was the only way to go.

“Let’s give it my all!”

I sipped on the apple vinegar I had been using as a study companion lately and continued working through the problem sets.

“Haru-nii, are you awake?”

It was around 10pm when I heard Kotomi’s voice along with a knock.

“Yeah, I’m up.” I replied through the door, and she cautiously opened it. 

Clad in an anime shirt and sweatpants, Kotomi seemed on the verge of tears for some reasons.

“What is it?”

“Um, well… I need your help.”

“Is the homework difficult?”

“It is, but no, not about that…”

“Want me to accompany you to the convenience store?”

“I’m a bit peckish, but no, that’s not it.”

“Is there a bug in your room?”

“No bug either…”

Kotomi awkwardly averted her eyes and stumbled over her words. Although we rarely spoke at school, we chatted a lot at home. Even tonight, during dinner, we had a conversation like, “What anime is this BGM from?” and “They use this a lot in variety shows”.

However, Kotomi now wore a distressed expression while sending an uneasy gaze in my direction.

To be honest, I had a bad feeling about this. We had a similar exchange just last week. 

It shouldn’t be the case, but—

“This isn’t related to Mahorin, is it?”

“…It’s related to Mahorin.”

“This is not another meetup, is it?”

“…It’s about that.”

Again? Does the word ‘reflection’ not exist in your vocabulary?

“Why the heck did you agree?”

“W-well, if I refuse, I might hurt Mahorin’s feelings…”

“And what about my mental well-being?”

It was like sending Kotomi to a group full of party animals. This thing shaves away your sanity.

“B-but I don’t want Mahorin to hate me…”

Mahorin was her only friend. I wanted to keep a good impression, but…

“Do you even care if I end up hating you?”

“…Haru-nii, you wouldn’t hate me, right?”

Kotomi looked like she was about to cry.

“Ugh, don’t be such a crybaby. I won’t hate you, okay?”

“…Really?”

With her eyes downcast, Kotomi looked at me to seek assurance. I nodded.

I’m not a siscon, but Kotomi is my precious sister. They said the more trouble a child was, the cuter they were. Kotomi fit that description for me.

Back in elementary school, she used to cling to me all the time. But as we approached the end of our first year in middle school, those who get close with the opposite sex started to stand out, and to avoid attention, Kotomi began avoiding me at school. 

However, that didn’t mean her reliance on me disappeared. When exams were near, she’d come for study sessions, and if a shiny black bug ever appeared in her room, she’d dash straight to my room.

For me, it was only natural to be relied upon by Kotomi, and helping her was just a matter of course. Even if the offline meetup was a hassle, that didn’t mean I’d suddenly start hating her. I really thought my sister was adorable.

“So, is this offline meetup another collaboration cafe?”

“No, this time it’s karaoke.”

“Ah, karaoke.”

In that case, just singing should be fine. If Momoi invited her to karaoke, that meant she wanted to sing, not to talk about otaku stuff. While I was worried about keeping up with conversation during the collaboration cafe because of my lack of knowledge, with karaoke, the risk of being exposed would be greatly reduced.

Perhaps sensing that my expression had softened, Kotomi looked up.

“Do you like karaoke, Haru-nii?”

“Well, it’s alright. I used to go with friends quite often in middle school. Though it’s been a while, I still go once every two or three months with them.”

“Which karaoke place do you go to? ManekiNeko? BigVoice?”

“I don’t really have a preference… Why do you ask?”

“I kind of mentioned I prefer ManekiNeko to Mahorin. It’d look weird if you didn’t have a membership card.”

“Don’t worry about that. I’ve got a ManekiNeko member card.”

Kotomi sighed in relief, “Phew. I’m glad Haru-nii likes karaoke.”

“I’m glad too. I won’t have to prepare like last time with karaoke.”

“Yeah. Oh, but, Haru-nii, it’s not a big deal, but I told Mahorin that Jet Black likes obscure songs. Could you avoid famous ones?”

“Piece of cake. If anything, I’m more familiar with obscure songs.”

“Great! Also, they should only be anime songs.”

Hold up! Don’t casually drop such a bomb!

“I only know a few anime songs, like from Driste!”

“Driste is too mainstream, you can’t sing that.”

“I just lost my only weapon!”

“Don’t worry! I’ll list up anime songs that perfectly fits your voice!”

“When do I need to memorize these by?”

You wouldn’t say tomorrow after school, would you?

“Until Saturday at 1 PM.”

“Saturday, huh? I guess I can manage somehow…”

Today was Wednesday. Just like last time, it was a last-minute job, but having a whole extra two days was a relief. With that much time, I should be able to memorize the songs. It could get confusing, but I could pull through if I just said I was tone-deaf.

“Also, I might’ve bragged to Mahorin about Jet Black being a great singer, so make sure to sing well, okay?”

“Why are you raising the bar like that?”

“Well, I thought if she knows I’m good at singing, she’d hesitate to go with me… Sorry, Haru-nii.”

“It’s fine. What’s said is said. In that case, I’ll focus on memorizing two or three songs and let Mahorin sing the rest.”

“I told Mahorin, ‘I don’t want to let go of the mic.’“

She’s blocking every escape routes!

“If you get invited again, just switch to me in the middle of the chat, alright?”

“Ugh, sorry for causing you trouble…”

“It’s okay now. If you understand, that’s enough.”

Crying or protesting wouldn’t change the inevitable event. No amount of lecturing would change the situation.

Besides, I had already accepted being the stand-in once. Momoi recognized ‘Jet Black Yasha = Fujisaki Haruto.’  Either way, I had to go.

“Time is precious. Hurry up and list down the songs for me.”

“Wait. I’ll prepare them right away!”

Kotomi left the room and returned after about thirty minutes. In her hands were a music player and a tablet—

“What’s the tablet for?”

“You use it to watch anime.”

“…Why anime?”

“Because you can only sing anime songs. After singing, you will probably discuss the anime, so it’s good to have some knowledge. I created an account for you and added them to your playlist! They’re mostly unknown ones, but I guarantee they’re interesting!”

“…How many episodes in total?”

“Fifty episodes.”

Looks like I’d be drowned in anime until the day of the offline meeting.

◇◆◇◆

And so, Saturday arrived. After I finished watching anime in the morning, I arrived at Kinjo Station five minutes before our meeting time.

The meetup spot, the “ManekiNeko,” was on the third floor of the same building we entered last time. I headed to the multipurpose building and took the elevator to the third floor. As the doors swung open, there was a blonde, blue-eyed girl in front of the karaoke place.

Momoi, in her off-shoulder knit dress, seemed to be having an argument with a college student. She was raising her voice with a face full of disgust.

“I’ve told you, I’m meeting someone.”

“But you’re free, right? I could take you somewhere more fun than karaoke.”

It seemed like she was being picked up persistently. Seeing a high school girl with blonde hair and blue eyes was rare, so for a confident pick-up artist, she must be an irresistible target.

“I’m waiting for someone, I can’t go.”

“Then let’s talk.”

“Geez, I can’t stand people like you! If you don’t stop—”

At that moment, Momoi noticed me. 

I thought about hiding in the elevator since interfering might lead to trouble, but once spotted, I couldn’t just stay silent.

“Do you know him, Momoi?”

“No, not at all. He’s just bothering me. Say something to him―”

“S-s-sorry! Excuse me! My apologies!”

He ran off before I could say anything. The elevator doors closed in front of me, and he hurriedly descended the stairs.

“…”

I was well aware that I looked intimidating, but seriously, he’s way too scared. I could get hurt too. 

That’s precisely why the kindness of Takase, who not only didn’t fear me but complimented me with a smile, stood out.

“Thanks for driving him away. I appreciate it.”

“I didn’t do anything.”

“You did more than enough… Are you feeling down?”

Momoi peered into my face, and I turned away with a dismissive gesture.

“I’m not.”

“Lie. You’re definitely— Oh, I get it. You’re shocked because he was scared of you, right?”

“Don’t tease me.”

“I’m not teasing you. It’s good to have a tough face. Not my type, but there are definitely someone who like that kind of face.”

The ‘not my type’ was unnecessary, but since I didn’t want Momoi to like me, I’d take that as a compliment.

“Anyway, your outfit is amazing.”

“Oh, you noticed?”

Since we wouldn’t go to a collab café today, there wasn’t any specific dress code. I came here in casual attire.

The other day, I overheard Takase gushed about her animal fortune-telling, “It says I’m compatible with a tiger type!” That’s why I bought a tiger-patterned shirt and pants with tiger embroidery on the same day. I hoped Momoi would bring up “I saw Fujisaki in a tiger outfit” as a topic when she chatted with Takase. 

“Nope. I didn’t notice. Whose cosplay is that supposed to be?”

“Huh, cosplay?”

“Yeah. Otherwise, it’s just too tacky.”

Too tacky, she says…? No way, isn’t it pretty cool? Who would say tigers look uncool? If tigers were considered cool, then a tiger-patterned shirt and pants should also be considered cool.

Ah, I get it.

“This isn’t your cup of tea, huh?”

“It’s a general statement. The combination is just too tacky. If you had a cute face, the contrast might work, but on you, it just looks like you’re on the job.”

It was frustrating, but she’s got a point. Certainly, some people found tigers scary, and if that’s the case, tiger-patterned shirts and pants would seem intimidating.

Okay, so I look like I’m on the job, huh? That’s probably why the guy fled earlier.

It’s good to know that it wasn’t because of my face, but if Momoi started a conversation like, “I saw Fujisaki dressed so ugly,” it would be the worst. Let’s bluff my way out.

“As you said, it’s cosplay. From a certain anime!”

“What’s the title? I’ll look it up.”

“It’s better not to! It’s kind of, you know, pretty gruesome! Anyway, let’s check-in quickly!”

I pushed my way in and passed through the automatic door. The female clerk at the entrance grimaced upon seeing me enter like I was barging in. I should really stop wearing this outfit.

“W-welcome. A room for two?”

“Yes, two people.”

“How long would you like to stay?”

“Hmm, what do you think?”

“How about two hours?”

“Just two hours?”

I thought we’d be here for a good chunk of time and enjoyed ourselves freely. Did she have other plans today? Well, for me, a shorter time was more convenient. I only memorized seven songs after all.

“I thought we should finish early because of the upcoming exams. If you feel like belting out songs, I’ll accompany you for as many hours as you want.”

“Nah, two hours is fine. These things tend to drag on in the end if they’re too long.”

“I know, right? You end up cutting your original songs midway and adding unfamiliar ones. Eventually, it turns into just watching live performances towards the end.”

Judging by her tone, it seemed like she had experience in karaoke unlike Kotomi. I wonder if she went with her usual group. I’d love to know what kind of songs Takase liked.

“Two hours, please.”

After specifying the duration, we received our microphones and glasses, poured some cola from the drink bar, and then headed to our room. As we sat facing each other on the couch, Momoi handed me the touchpanel.

“You can sing as much as you like.”

Now, what to do?

I remembered four opening songs, two ending songs, and one insert song, making a total of seven songs. Even if we talked about the anime in between, it wouldn’t last more than an hour.

I planned to extend the time by repeating the same songs, but because Kotomi filled in three precious slots with songs that needed you to scream, my throat would die If I repeated them.

I came prepared for that because I thought there would be free time. If it was only two hours, I could keep the flow going while maintaining the image of an ‘otaku who loves obscure anime songs.’ However—

“Let’s sing together, Mahorin.”

Ideally, I wanted Momoi to join as well. That way, I could make it through in one hour without repeating any songs.

“Didn’t you say you don’t want to let go of the mic?”

“That’s true, but it’s my first time at karaoke with someone who loves anime songs. I want to enjoy the excitement together. That seems way more fun, right?”

“I get it. Honestly, I wanted to sing too. It’s my first time singing anime songs with someone else! Finally, I can get excited about anime songs…”

Momoi looked so happy that she couldn’t hide her emotions. If she wanted to sing, she should have just said so. Was Momoi also trying not to be disliked by her online gaming partner just like Kotomi?

“Do you not sing anime songs when you go with friends?”

“I only sing trendy songs then. I keep my otaku side a secret.”

She was pretty thorough. Even if someone had sung an anime song in front of me, I wouldn’t have noticed it. And even if I noticed, I wouldn’t think much of it. 

Maybe it took courage to be open about it, something I, who is not an otaku, couldn’t fully understand.

Anyway,

“Let’s sing all the anime songs we want. And if possible, sing MioMio’s character song too.” 

Momoi had said that she loved all the characters. That’s why her singing MioMio’s song might lead to her wanting to sing everyone’s character songs. I should be able to keep up with the anime talk if it was about Driste.

“I want to sing! Oh, speaking of MioMio-chan, thanks a bunch for the acrylic stand! It looks like this!”

Momoi showed me a photo on her phone. Driste’s acrylics stands lined up in a case with lights, resembling a live concert venue.

“That’s some serious dedication.”

“Right? As you can see, the top row replicates the final episode’s live scene! The bottom row showcases the glamorous daily lives of the idols! And this photo here is my figure shelf! I arranged it to create a dream live venue with music-themed works! This shelf is exclusively for Miku-chan! And then ta-da! A collection of hot-blooded warrior princesses… oh, wait, that’s not it.”

This time it’s a grand assembly! Momoi, with her spirits high, swiped away the ‘that’s not it’ photo. Nooo, show me more!

It was brief, but wasn’t that Takase? She was proudly posing in casual clothes at a café with a coffee in hand.

“Your eyes are sparkling. Was it that impressive?”

“It’s fantastic! Thanks for showing me such cool stuff!”

“Sure, sure! If anything, figures are meant to be seen, right? Show me your figure collection too, Jet Black-kun!”

“Oh, um, I didn’t take any pictures.”

“Really?”

“Yeah. Even when I travel, I don’t take photos; I just store memories in my heart. Anyway, let’s sing already!”

“True,” Momoi said with excited eyes.

While feeling a bit anxious about meeting her expectations, I operated the touch panel and grabbed the microphone.

The title of the song was displayed along with the intro. Momoi looked surprised. 

“What song is this?”

“Cyber ​​Ninja War. Haven’t heard of it?”

“It’s my first time hearing it.”

“Well, it’s pretty niche.”

After saying that, I started singing. 

The beginning was reasonably easy, but the rap inserted in the middle was tricky. Why did Kotomi use a precious slot for rap? I wished she prioritized ease of singing over vocal quality.

When the song ended, I set the microphone down. 

“How was it?”

“Even though I heard it for the first time, it’s a great song. And that random rap in the middle is such an anime thing, right? Well, it’s my turn next! Let’s see, whose character song should I start with?”

Anime discussion? None! The six hours I spent on “Cyber Ninja Wars” evaporated in an instant.

It was the most interesting of all Kotomi’s choices, and there were many scenes I could talk about… I could initiate anime talk myself, but Momoi was already itching to sing.

“I’ve decided! Following your request, I’ll start with MioMio-chan!”

“Yeaaah! MioMio!”

“Don’t mind me and go all out with your otagei dance!”

“Otagei dance!?”

“You said you’re the type to bust out otagei dance when you listen to anime songs, Jet Black-kun.”

That’s news to me!?

“I’ve never seen otagei in real life, so this will be my first time ~” Momoi looked at me with eager anticipation.

I did know what otaku dance was from watching Driste, but I had never danced it myself. Nevertheless, according to Momoi, I was capable. Since I had been doing a flawless job as a stand-in so far, I didn’t want to stumble here.

When the intro played, Momoi gripped the microphone. The moment she started singing, I vigorously shook my hips and spun my arms at high speed.

“Pfft!”

Seeing me like this, Momoi burst into laughter. While coughing lightly, she grinned and began singing the character song. When the song ended, she couldn’t contain her laughter any longer and started laughing out loud while clutching her stomach.

“Wait, Jet Black-kun, you’re too hilarious!”

“D-Don’t laugh so much!”

“Your moves are unexpectedly sharp… the contrast between your outfit and your dancing… hahaha.”

Apparently, it hit the spot. The memory seemed to be etched in her mind as she couldn’t stop laughing.

Wiping away tears from her blue eyes, she spoke again, “Ah, it’s so funny. You’re truly the best. Can I record a video? I won’t show it to anyone.”

“No way, it’s embarrassing.”

“Aww, too bad. Okay then, Jet Black-kun, your turn next.”

Receiving the touch panel, I selected a song and grabbed the microphone. The intro began, likely from a lesser-known anime since Momoi seemed unfamiliar with it. 

Even after singing, the conversation didn’t steer towards anime. She eagerly picked a song, her face brimming with anticipation, and then I flawlessly performed my otaku dance. She almost burst into another laughter while singing the character song.

The call signal rang when both otaku dance and shouts had drained my energy. Momoi, who was sensing my fatigue, responded in my stead. 

“We have about ten more minutes. What do you want to do? Shall we extend?” 

It must have been incredibly fun for her to suggest an extension. If it were Kotomi, she might accept out of the fear of being disliked. However, I was confident I had entertained Momoi enough already. After all, it was Momoi who initially proposed the two-hour limit, so she wouldn’t mind wrapping it up here.

“Let’s call it a day.”

“Are you sure?”

“If you feel like you haven’t sung enough, we can extend it by an hour or so…”

“No need.”

After informing the staff, Momoi sat on the sofa and directed a serious gaze at me.

“Forgive me for the odd question, but… do you have a girlfriend?”

“Why ask all of a sudden?”

“Just answer.”

“I don’t…”

She sighed, seemingly relieved.

“Then, how about going out with me?”

She dropped a bomb that left me bewildered.

Huh? Go out? Me with Momoi?  

Does she like me? It’s nice to hear, but I couldn’t date her. I like Takase. 

But still… nonchalant as she might sound, confessing took a lot of courage. In the end, I couldn’t date her, but dismissing it with a blunt “no” would be too harsh. I needed to choose my words carefully to avoid hurting her feelings.

“Oh… this is a bit hard to say, but… uh—”

“Ah, it’s fine. I can tell without you saying anything,” Momoi interrupted me with a composed expression.

“You’re surprisingly cool about it…”

“That’s because I never intended to date you in the first place.”

“…Wait, are you messing with me?”

“I wouldn’t do something mean like that. I just wanted to confirm Fujisaki’s feelings.”

“My feelings?”

“I wanted to know if you had a crush on me. I thought you might hesitate a bit, but I never expected you to be so uninterested. It’s surprising.”

“…Should I be apologetic or proud?”

“You can be proud. Thanks for not falling for me.”

“You’re welcome.”

“Now, to the main point. Can you pretend to be my boyfriend?”

Don’t confuse me any further!

“Why the heck would I do that? It makes no sense.”

“Sorry for confusing you. Let me explain. The reason I proposed the offline meetup was partly to hang out with you and also to ask if you’d pretend to be my boyfriend. Just to be clear, it’s strictly ‘pretending,’ I planned to drop the idea if you showed any romantic interest “

“Why didn’t you suggest it last time, then?”

Just like this time, I didn’t show any romantic interest to Momoi at the collaboration café. If anything, I wanted to escape as quickly as possible.

“I wanted to observe a bit more. You weren’t overly friendly at school, declined extending the meetup, and even rejected my confession. Though the fact that we’re classmates is a bit of an issue, there’s no better candidate than you. So please, I’m begging you, pretend to be my boyfriend!”

Pretending to be in a relationship with the popular Momoi, huh?

Honestly, I wasn’t thrilled about it, but if I rejected this, she might look for another guy for the role and find a different in-game husband. I didn’t like Momoi in that way, but I didn’t dislike her either. After all, we had played together even if I was just a stand-in. 

If she met a new in-game husband at an offline meetup, and if that guy harbored impure thoughts towards Momoi—and if it’d somehow become a scandal—I would feel genuinely bad.

There was also Kotomi to consider. 

If things went well with a new in-game husband, Momoi probably sever her connection with Kotomi. Momoi was Kotomi’s only friend. I wanted to avoid the risk of losing that.

Though my answer has already been decided, I’d like to hear more details.

“Why pretend to be a couple?”

Did she want to avoid an arranged marriage? Or perhaps she wanted to participate in an event exclusively for couples? Or was this her strategy to ward off guys that tried to hit on her?

“I want to introduce you to my friends.”

…I had a bad feeling about this. I hoped it was a misunderstanding, but lately, my bad feelings tend to be accurate.

“Are these friends… Kotobuki, Aoki, and Takase?”

“You catch on fast.”

Damn it, they’re a close-knit group! Kotobuki and Aoki were one thing, but pretending to be a couple in front of Takase? I had been crushing on her for a year and a month! I didn’t want Takase to think I was already taken! 

But for Kotomi’s sake, I couldn’t refuse.

“Please. I promise it won’t cause any trouble for you, Fujisaki! Ran-chan, Aoi-chan, and Naru-chan are tight-lipped, so they won’t spread rumors at school. I just need you to act like my boyfriend for that one day when I introduce you to them!”

If anything, they’d expect that. Otherwise, there’s no point in introducing me.

Momoi was Takase’s best friend. If she found out we broke up due to a fight, she might disliked me. Momoi was popular, so if we broke up because of differences in values, it should be understandable.

“I’m the only one without a boyfriend, I was worried about feeling left out, so I bluffed that I have a boyfriend.”

Cute! Definitely cute! The kind of cuteness that naturally comes with having a boyfriend!

But Takase didn’t, right? She obviously didn’t have one! Did you know how much I had observed her? I had never seen her acting lovey-dovey with a guy at school!

With fashionable Momoi by my side, I was confident she could transform me into a cool guy. I hoped to be reborn as someone even Takase couldn’t resist falling for!

Carrying these expectations in my heart, we left the karaoke place.
