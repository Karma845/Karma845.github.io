February 15th - February 28th: The School Trip Is Starting

１

School was noisy on Monday after Valentine's Day. This is because some had managed to give chocolates privately on Sunday, while many others did not and shifted to today.

As they passed through the school's entrance, Sandai immediately saw girls confessing to boys, their eyes closed, along with chocolates in hand.

─ S-Senpai! I love you!

─ Me?! Eh... Am I in the middle of my popularity phase!?

─ Wait, that voice is different... Oh no, I made a mistake! I'm sorry! Please give the chocolate back!

─ Ugh...

─ What's with that face? Fine, I'll give you one!

─ Thank you... Do you like me?

─ I think I'll take it back after all.

How Valentine's Day was observed varied from person to person. Some couples used it as an opportunity to deepen their relationship, while others found their feelings reciprocated or their distances closed. On the other hand, some experienced nothing or faced heartbreak.

However, Valentine's Day was an event that everyone, for better or worse, cared about. Inside the classroom, the topic of conversation revolved around Valentine's Day. As soon as they entered, Shino was bombarded with questions about Valentine's Day from Takasago, her friends, and the other gals.

─ Yuizaki-san!

─ Mahiro-chan. Did you manage to give your chocolates?

─ Y-Yes! Yesterday, I sent Shihouin-kun a chat saying, “There's something I want to talk to you about... If you don't come, I'll...” and he came rushing to me in seconds, so I gave it to him then! He said, ‘Don't be hasty!’ I was a little confused, but... I just wanted to say that I would be sad if he didn't came, that's all.

─ I-I see.

─ Good morning, Shino.

─ Good morning.

─ Hey, hey, Shino, you know what? I promised my kohai some chocolates when I was making them, right? Turns out he had club stuff on Sundays, so I went to school in regular clothes yesterday and gave them to him. He was so happy, almost got teary-eyed. Teaching me how to make these chocolates was a great help. Thanks a bunch!

─ I'm so happy for you.

─ It's great, you know, for those who have someone special like that. Especially those of us who don't, we just shared chocolates among ourselves and called it a day. But, you know, making them was fun, so that sense of accomplishment was priceless.

Sitting behind Sandai, Shino and her friends chatted away, and no matter what, Sandai couldn't help overhearing their conversation. It seemed like everyone who participated in the chocolate-making had enjoyed their respective Valentine's Days.

Still, Sandai felt a little sorry for the Class rep, who was being pushed around as a result, even though Takasago probably had no intention of doing so. The Class rep had already taken a seat at his desk and was diligently studying with his reference books, but there was a strange sense of melancholy in the back of her head.

Most likely, the Class rep was the type who was easily pushed around. Unlike Sandai, who had surprisingly good balance, the president was awkward and inevitably destined to be in that situation, whether he liked it or not.

Anyway, it was time for the morning homeroom. Nakaoka, yawning, came to the front of the classroom and looked over the students from the pondium.

"Everyone's here, huh? Let's start the morning homeroom...... Well, there's basically nothing to tell you. The first thing you need to do is to get ready for the school trip at the beginning of the month."

With Valentine's Day now behind them, the next event on the horizon was the class trip. While Sandai and Shino weren't particularly looking forward to it, many of their classmates were excited about the event and discussions about the trip started to fill the air.

Some were already looking up tourist spots in Hakodate, and conversations about that could be heard. By the way, regarding the class grouping, Sandai and Shino didn't really mind being in any group as long as they were together, and Shino had said the same.

Their classmates had been bewildered by this decision, but they didn't dare complain, fearing Shino's potential wrath if they were separated. It seemed their classmates had a

slight fear of Shino going off if they forcefully separated her and Sandai, so they kept quiet.

However……..

Even if one receives the consideration they desired, it's not always guaranteed to lead to a satisfying outcome, as is often the case in life.

Sandai and Shino's flirting was so embarrassing to watch that everyone in the class said they did not want to be in the same group with them.

In the end, the Class rep reluctantly joined their group, saying, "I guess there's no other choice."

Shino, who openly admitted her dislike for the Class rep, looked very uncomfortable when she found out she would be in the same group with him.

Shino and the Class rep didn't get along, and their personalities clashed. Sandai couldn't help but feel that it was going to be quite exhausting.

If there was any silver lining, it might have been when the Class rep raised their hand, and Takasago followed suit.

Takasago didn't have any issues with Shino and was fond of the Class rep, making her a suitable mediator despite her somewhat airheaded nature.

Regardless of the circumstances, there was no choice but to accept the group assignments that had been decided.

While it was possible for Shino to raise objections, Sandai had firmly decided to stay silent about the matter. Objecting would almost certainly lead to Nakaoka intervening, which would only heat things up with Shino and make it unmanageable. Avoiding conflicts as much as possible and smoothly managing the current situation to stabilize her emotional state was Sandai's duty.

"Being in the same group as the class rep...."

"Don't worry, Takasago will be there too. The class rep probably won't bother us much. And if things get tense, Takasago might get emotional and quiet things down. Even the class rep wouldn't want Takasago complaining about the trip being ruined. He seems like the type to be mindful of that."

"Ah, I see..."

"Remember when Takasago asked for your help with the class rep's birthday present? How did things change when Mahiro-chan started crying?"

"Yeah, when Mahiro-chan cries, suddenly the class rep gets nicer?"

"Huh?"

"I see..."

"Well, I'll just hang back and offer support quietly. I don't really have much to talk about with the class rep anyway."

Sandai remembered that the reason Shino disliked the Class rep was her jealousy, which she displayed regardless of gender. That's why he made sure to offer reassuring words at key moments, and lately, it had started coming naturally. Incidentally, Shino also understood that these were words of consideration, giving her a sense of being "number one" for Sandai.

With a smirk, Shino playfully poked Sandai in the back with a pencil. "Ouch!"

"There you go~"

"Ow..."

２

After parting ways with Shino, Sandai headed to his part-time job. On the way there something unexpected happened. A junior female student who had confessed to him before suddenly appeared out of nowhere, spreading her arms wide and blocking his path with a "halt" gesture. Caught off guard by this sudden intrusion, Sandai instinctively tensed up. "Um, do you need something?"

In response to Sandai' question, the junior student nervously gulped. "I'm a day late, but... here's some chocolate!" She held out a cute, wrapped chocolate as she inched closer to him. Sandai took a step back. "No, I'm not really into this kind of..."

"No need to be scared to accept it since Yuizaki-senpai isn't around! I timed it so she wouldn't see me giving it to you!"

"Regardless of what Shino thinks, having someone suddenly appear and give me chocolate is still kind of scary..."

"Boys are simple creatures who get happy and grin when they receive chocolate from any girl, you know. So don't be shy! It's handmade with love!"

"I wouldn't be happy to receive it from anyone except my girlfriend... Well then, see you later."

"N-No! You can't go!"

As Sandai tried to walk past her, the junior student immediately circled in front of him and maintained her "halt" stance. "...Could you please move aside?"

"I'll move once you accept the chocolate."

"I can't accept it."

"Then I won't move!"

"I have a part-time job too. I don't have time to hang out. If you want to give chocolate to someone, find someone else."

"It has to be Fujiwara-senpai!"

"Shino will get mad, you know."

"T-That's why I made sure Yuizaki-senpai wasn't here and came out like this."

"If I tell Shino, she will also find out."

The girl swallowed hard and her eyes welled up slightly. It was evident that the memory of being intimidated by Shino had left its mark.

"This is the moment," Sandai thought, feeling it was a decisive moment.

"I prioritize Shino above all. If you don't back down, I'll tell her about today... There's no doubt there will be a storm."

With his resolute attitude, Sandai successfully put a brake on the girl's persistence.

The girl bit her lip, turned away from Sandai, and mumbled something under her breath as she walked away.

"...What's wrong with me? I'm quite cute, and I've always been able to win over guys, even those with girlfriends. I'm confident in myself..."

Sandai didn't possess the special skill of being able to hear what people muttered to themselves, so he couldn't quite make out what the girl was saying.

In any case, he was just relieved that the storm had passed. All he could do now was hope that this girl wouldn't approach him in a strange way anymore. It was over, done with, so it would be best to forget about it.

Sandai collected himself and headed to his part-time job at the aquarium. However, as soon as he arrived, Assistant Manager Komaki approached him with a somewhat apologetic expression, informing him that Hajime had to take an urgent leave due to a family matter.

"It seems there was a sudden situation at the Saeki residence..."

"I understand."

As he punched in on the time card, Sandai nodded.

"If you ever find it too tough doing this on your own, just let me know. I'll come to help... I'm sorry."

Komaki bowed with both hands clasped together. While it was understandable for someone to take time off due to family circumstances, it wasn't anyone's fault, and Komaki didn't need to bow like this. However, as Assistant Manager, it was probably part of his job.

"You don't need to bow like that... I might have urgent matters to attend to someday too, so I'd appreciate your understanding when that time comes. So, don't worry about it."

"That's a relief to hear!"

"Alright, I'll go to work then."

"Have a good day!"

With Komaki seeing him off, Sandai began preparing for work alone. Handling the responsibilities of two people for the first time was challenging, and he ended up being a bit hasty due to time constraints. Nonetheless, with effort, he managed to get things done. It was just tiring.

During his break, Sandai let out a sigh unintentionally.

While sipping canned coffee from a vending machine, he started checking for new light novel releases for next month on his smartphone to make the most of his break. That's when a notification chimed in.

He initially thought it might be a message from Shino, but it turned out to be a chat message from Hajime.

— It's about time for a break... right?

— Yeah, I just started my break.

— That's a relief! Um, sorry for taking the day off today….

— It's okay, it couldn't be helped with some urgent matter. No big deal.

— You're so kind... You're not trying to make me fall for you, are you?

— We don't need jokes like that.

— I see. Well, um, I'm really sorry. I'll make it up to you sometime! See you!

Even though Sandai is the kind of person who doesn't mind even if nothing is said, Hajime seemed to have decided that he should say something, at least a few words.

Hajime ended the chat with a stamp of a mysterious creature poking its thumb up, which Sandai interpreted as a sign that the conversation was over. He then continued checking for new light novel releases.

As he compiled a list of titles he found interesting, his break time came to an end. Sandai decided it was time to return to work.

"Now then….."

The absence of the usually noisy Hajime made the atmosphere strangely peaceful. However, it also allowed for moments of increased concentration, and Sandai thought that occasionally having days like this might not be a bad thing.

３

As the days passed, the school trip was approaching next week. By this time, students were starting to divide into two groups: those who had already finished their preparations and those who hadn't. Sandai and Shino belonged to the latter.

They hadn't been planning to put too much effort into the preparations, but they also didn't intend to go completely unprepared. So, they decided it was time to start getting ready.

Sandai went to pick up Shino after his part-time job, and then the two of them went shopping together. They were buying things like hats and travel bags.

"How about this cap? Or the fluffy one over here?" Shino compared a cap with a fluffy hat and asked Sandai for his opinion. Sandai wasn't particularly knowledgeable in this area, but since she asked him for his opinion, he couldn't avoid it. He thought for a moment and gave his own perspective.

"...If you want to stay warm, the fluffy one seems better."

"The fluffy one does look warm."

The hats they were calling "fluffy" had an official name written on their tags, but neither of them bothered to read it. They stuck with the visual impression of "fluffy."

"Functionally, I think the fluffy one is the best, but design-wise, it has a certain cuteness to it. It gives off a somewhat childish vibe."

"It's childlike, huh... Then I'll go with the cap," Shino decided, showing that she didn't want something that looked childish. So, instead of the fluffy hat, she chose a cap.

After paying, Shino immediately put on the cap and gave Sandai a quick glance, her lips curling into a small smile. She was silently asking for his opinion.

"You give off the aura of an energetic girl," he remarked.

"An energetic vibe... Wait, does that mean I seemed kinda childish too?"

"Not childish, but more like cheerful, lively, or unisex. I didn't mean 'childish' at all."

"Oh, I see! It’s all good then."

Within the realm of gyaru fashion, there were different styles, including those who went all-in on a cute and youthful look. However, Shino didn't lean towards that type; she preferred a more mature appearance. Of course, her mannerisms and behaviors might have a hint of cuteness, but that was separate from her fashion sense. Humans are complex and multifaceted beings, so it's natural to reconcile seemingly contradictory aspects.

Shino was no exception in this regard. Afterward, they continued to discuss and buy new bags and various small items, preparing for the upcoming school trip. Now, all they had to do was wait for the day of the trip.

４

The day of the school trip had arrived. They had been told beforehand to "gather directly at the airport," so Sandai decided to go to the airport with Shino.

First, he went to pick up Shino, and then they took a train that connected to the airport station. It was around 9 a.m., a time when the rush hour had already passed, so the train was relatively empty.

"It's been a while since I've been on an airplane," Sandai murmured with a sense of nostalgia. The last time he had been on one was during his childhood when he returned to Japan from overseas. Since then, he hadn't had the opportunity, and it felt like a distant memory.

"Have you ever been on an airplane?" he asked Shino.

"You know we're not well-off, and my family has never taken me on a trip or anything, so I've never been on an airplane," she replied.

"I see."

"Like, where have you been on an airplane?" Shino asked.

"Well, it's not exactly a trip; it's more like coming back to Japan," he clarified.

"Huh? What do you mean?"

"I mean, I returned to Japan."

Shino was startled by this revelation, and Sandai, puzzled by her reaction, tilted his head but quickly realized what was going on. He hadn't talked much about his childhood, even though he hadn't hidden the fact that his parents lived overseas.

"I used to live in England when I was little."

"Oh, that's news to me..."

"I never really had the chance to bring it up."

Sandai could be classified as a "returnee," but he didn't really have a strong awareness of it. In total, he had spent much more time in Japan, forgotten most of his English, and, most importantly, he hadn't had any particularly interesting or humorous experiences to share. When his parents' acquaintances tried to take care of him, he had often turned away, and he vaguely remembered playing with kids of similar age during some local childcare activities, but as usual, he hadn't really fit in and mostly played alone. He had never had those kinds of experiences where he could proudly tell anyone that he had expanded his worldview through unknown experiences.

"What's England like?" Shino asked.

"No matter how you ask what it's like... well, conversations are in English," he replied.

"Can you speak English?"

"I wonder... I've forgotten a lot. Well, I think I can manage simple communication, but it's true that my way of speaking is still childlike. The other person would feel pretty uncomfortable. I can't confidently say that I can do it."

"If you can communicate even just a bit, that's something to be proud of. You should own what you can do." Shino encouraged.

"Well, if I freeze up when unfamiliar words or phrases come up, it'll be extremely embarrassing. It's better to say I can't do it, so I don't raise false expectations, and it's easier on me too."

"Mmm..."

"Or rather, whether I can speak English or not, it's not a big deal. I live in Japan. Well, if there's ever an opportunity to go on an overseas trip or something, just consider that I'll be there to help to some extent."

The term "overseas trip" caught Shino's attention.

"An overseas trip..."

"What's wrong?"

"Well, I was thinking that even for something like a honeymoon, you'd find a way somehow, right?"

"Well, to a certain extent."

"I know you have, like, a pretty vague definition of 'to a certain extent,' huh?"

It seemed that Shino accurately understood the implications of Sandai' statement. He couldn't help but smile at how well she knew him.

５

The first person they spotted when they arrived at the airport was Nakaoka. She had dark circles under her eyes, and a complexion that showed her determination not to make a second mistake as a teacher. When she noticed them, she waved them over.

"Hey, Fujiwara! Yuizaki! Over here, come on!" Following her call, they approached, and they could see a few of their classmates had already gathered. They were all excited for the school trip, and cheerful conversations could be heard all around.

However, amidst the lively atmosphere, one person stood out with an unusual air of tension, the class rep

"I've already messed up once before. I can't afford a second mistake. But staying up for Sandai straight is starting to show on my face. Plus, if I don't mutter to myself like this, I feel like I'll lose my focus. It's no good. I can't sleep yet. At least not until we're on the plane..."

People kept their distance from the Class rep, who seemed on the verge of losing it as he was muttering and chanting like reciting a Budhist prayer. Clearly, he looked like a dangerous person. But there was one kind-hearted soul who approached him.

It was Takasago.

Takasago nodded in agreement with the Class rep's ramblings.

I guess it's the weakness of falling in love, they thought. When you fall for someone, you embrace their quirks and flaws along with their virtues.

"So, you'll get some rest once we're on the plane, Shihouin-kun?"

"That's right. It's just a nap, though….."

"Please, take your time to relax!"

"I shouldn't have made you say that. I'm sorry, Takasago."

As the departure time drew near, the rest of their classmates gradually arrived, and soon everyone was assembled. Guided by Nakaoka, who seemed a bit unsteady on her feet, they proceeded with various check-in procedures, like getting their boarding passes and checking in their luggage.

It was at this point that Sandai and Shino realized they weren't seated next to each other.

"Oh... seems like I'm not gonna be sitting next to Sandai."

"It seems that way... It seems like changing our seats now might be impossible."

"Um... Who's gonna be sitting next to me then?"

"I... am"

Takasago timidly raised her hand, confirming that she would be sitting next to Shino. But who would be sitting next to Sandai?

As Sandai glanced around anxiously, one of Shino's friends, a gal, called out to him.

"Hey, I'm sitting next to you, Fujiwara."

"Is that so?"

"Is that a problem?"

"Not exactly a problem, but I was hoping to sit next to Shino."

"You know, you're pretty straightforward, huh? Well, if we switch seats, I'd end up sitting next to Takasago, right? We did get a bit closer during that chocolate-making event, but even so, Takasago is still pretty wary of anyone other than Shino, right?"

The gal's words were not entirely unfounded. While Takasago had grown accustomed to Shino, her friendship with Shino's gal friends was more along the lines of casual conversation, not quite reaching the level of being close friends.

Sandai stole a quick glance at Takasago, who responded with a somewhat awkward smile. Shino, who had been observing the situation, eventually gave up on sitting next to Sandai.

"I'll bear with it 'cause I gotta, but... just don't pull any weird stuff on Sandai, alright?"

"I won't do anything weird. Shino would totally flip if I did, right?"

"Yeah, I’ll totally get mad"

Shino whispered to Sandai to let her know if anything happened, and Sandai nodded in agreement.

Now, the gal who had confidently assured Shino that she wouldn't do anything weird  to Sandai, found herself unable to endure the boredom once they boarded the plane. She quickly broke her promise with Shino and started striking up a conversation with Sandai.

"Hey, I heard from Shino that you guys have already, you know, done it. Is that true?"

"Huh?"

"I mean, it got me curious, you know? Wondering if it's real or not."

"Do I really need to answer that?"

"Not really, but if you don't, I might start thinking Shino's lying. She's a bit of a fibber."

"Shino doesn't lie."

"Okay then, did you do it?"

Sandai knew he was being teased, but even so, being called a liar when it came to Shino was something he couldn't tolerate. So, he calmly and expressionlessly replied,

"We did."

Sandai's resolute response startled Shino's gal friend, who then cautiously poked his hand.

"Oh... So, with this hand, you touched and, you know, fondled and, uh, got all close with Shino, right?"

"Yeah, I guess so. Do you have any complaints?"

"No, I don't, but when you put it out there so casually... I can't help but think, 'Man, Fujiwara's got some nerve, huh?'"

"I don't know what you mean, but I'm just telling you the truth"

"I'm just impressed by your honesty. Trying to hide stuff can sometimes seem, like, shady from Shino's point of view. By the way, your hands... Oh, I get it... they're kinda rough and unexpectedly manly...."

The gal suddenly froze as she was about to playfully poke Sandai's hand. Slowly, she peeked into the aisle and looked toward the back.

Her gaze landed on Shino, who was seated a little farther away. Shino squinted her eyes, and her expression was chillingly cold.

Although it should be impossible for Shino to discern their subtle movements at this distance, it seemed like she had sensed something and was exerting her presence.

The gal quietly turned her attention forward and started humming as if nothing had happened. It seemed like her plan was to let things cool down a bit.

As a result of this sequence of events, the atmosphere inside the plane became indescribably heavy.

— "Hey, what's with this suffocating atmosphere? It was a fun and enjoyable school trip until a few minutes ago."

— "It's Yuizaki-san, right? Look, her friend seem to be teasing Fujiwara-kun."

— "Don't tease Fujiwara, seriously."

— "That’s funny."

— "Feels like a funeral."

— "In times like these, it's usually Nakaoka-sensei who'd be useful….. wait, she's fast asleep!"

— "ZzzZzz..…"

— "Next in line to be useful would be the class rep, who's in the same group as Fujiwara-kun and Yuizaki-san, but he is also sleeping with a grim look on his face."

Normally, Sandai would just ignore situations like this, but given that they were causing a disturbance in this enclosed space, he couldn't continue to ignore it as he usually would.

Sandai rose from his seat, casting a brief glance at Shino before making his way to the restroom. Shino, realizing the intent behind his actions, hurriedly followed him into the restroom.

"....You had a scary look on your face, didn’t you?" Sandai asked within the confines of the small restroom, and Shino pouted in response.

"Because that girl keeps teasing you and it's, like, getting on my nerves.."

"It probably feels like she's not good at keeping quiet. I don't think there's any weird meaning to it like Shino thinks."

"I get it, but just seeing you chat with another girl makes me feel kinda down, you know?"

"Just bear with me for a little while."

"Yeah. But in exchange for holding back... Okay?"

As Shino closed her eyes, Sandai gently pressed his lips against hers. As a result, Shino's demeanor gradually softened, and she began to relax.

— "Both of them just went into the restroom."

— "What are they doing...?"

— "I bet they're doing boyfriend-girlfriend stuff, like, totally. Probably why they're acting all shady on a plane, you know..."

— "Do you think these two, who are not afraid to make out in public even in the classroom, would not do anything?"

— "....."

— "Someone should go check on them."

— "I don't want to. I'm pretty sure I'd collapse in shock if they were actually doing something naughty."

— "Are there any brave souls on board?"

— "The hero is dead."

— "Saying it like Nietzsche's 'God is dead' won't solve anything, will it?"

Exiting the restroom, Sandai returned to his seat. While walking down the aisle, he couldn't help but notice that all his classmates were looking down, but dwelling on it wouldn't change anything. In any case, Shino's imposing presence had dissipated. Sandai thought that if everyone noticed this, things would return to normal, and indeed, after 10 minutes had passed, they did.

The heavy atmosphere from earlier had disappeared, and the plane was now filled with the liveliness of high school students genuinely enjoying their school trip.

６

The school trip was one of the school's events, and at its core was, of course, the pursuit of knowledge. That's why the first place they headed to upon arrival was Goryokaku.

The first day was dedicated to history learning. They grouped up as prearranged, forming lines, and boarded the private bus provided by the school one by one. Unlike the plane, the bus seats were allocated based on the assigned groups, which allowed Sandai and Shino to sit together. The class rep and Takasago were seated in the front.

"Shihouin-kun, you must be tired..."

"....."

The class rep also woke up briefly when he got off the plane, but as soon as he took his seat on the bus, he was asleep again. Similarly, Nakaoka, who was seated in the front row, was snoring away.

Sandai and Shino didn't do much; they just continued as usual. they were passing the time with idle conversation, saying things like, "I knew there was snow," or "The hot springs we both traveled to had more snow on them than the one in Hakodate.”

Being able to sit next to each other instead of being separated, like on the plane, made Shino much calmer, and she didn't exude an intimidating aura.

Before they knew it, they had arrived at Goryokaku. At that point, even the class president and Nakaoka, seemingly recovered from sleepiness and fatigue, had regained their energy.

Now, at Goryokaku, they were greeted by a historian that the school had comissioned, and the explanations about the structure and history began right away.

— "It's hard to tell from ground level, but Goryokaku is a castle shaped like a star. Now, as for Goryokaku, I think it's especially well-known within the scope of Japanese history that you all probably learned at school around the Meiji Restoration. Do you remember what happened?"

— "Ah! The Boshin War!"

— "Correct. More accurately, it's one of the battles in the Boshin War. It's also called the Hakodate War, or the Battle of Goryokaku. During this conflict, a famous person died. Does anyone know who he was?"

— "You haven't learned that much, have you?"

— "Well, we did study it, but..."

— "Here's a hint: the Shinsengumi."

— ".....Toshizo Hijikata?"

— "That's correct. He's one of the famous Shinsengumi members whose names are often used in games, anime, manga, and so on which are familiar to young people."

For high school students, their exposure to history often comes more from creative works than from classroom lessons. Sandai was no exception. While he certainly had studied historical periods like the Bakumatsu-Meiji Era in textbooks and reference materials, he encountered them more frequently in light novels and similar works. He had a particular fondness for a series called "Bakumatsu Tensei: My Unrivaled Journey as a Shinsengumi Foot Soldier," which concluded last spring.

Of course, not everyone delves into creative works. Shino was one such person. She wasn't the type to get into creative works, and given her lack of enthusiasm for studying, she seemed completely unaware of Goryokaku itself.

"I had no clue that Goryokaku was, like, some fancy historical place or whatever…."

"Shino... just to clarify, what did you think Goryokaku was?"

"Um, the name of a Chinese restaurant or something?"

"A Chinese restaurant? Did you perhaps think it was one of those 'Dragon Tower' places?"

"Yeah."

Sandai couldn't help but think that Shino would probably ask him to help her study for the upcoming final exams. However, he knew that wouldn't happen immediately.

Sandai and Shino's high school had three long vacations during the year: spring, summer, and winter. However, the school followed a two-semester system, and the final exams that significantly affected promotions and such were held only twice, in summer and winter. So, even if Shino started to make a fuss, it wouldn't be until around that time.

Still, Sandai believed it might be a good idea to start preparing gradually around April to ensure that Shino wouldn't panic at the last minute. He knew from experience that Shino's inability to study was quite severe.

The last time, they had resorted to a Spartan-style cramming session just before the exams, and while it had worked, Sandai didn't want to go through that again. Shino had been on the verge of tears, and Sandai had been wracked with guilt. It would be much better to build up their knowledge gradually when there was time.

“Shino, by the way, let's change the subject a little.”

“Eh?”

“What would you do if I told you to start studying a little bit in April ......? I'm thinking that maybe it wouldn't be a bad idea to do a little bit of studying for the final exam in the summer.”

Shino stared at Sandai' face for a moment, then turned her gaze away without saying a word. Her attitude clearly screaming ‘I don’t want to study’.

However, if Sandai gave in now, it would only mean that Shino would start panicking again right before the exams. So, he continued to stare at her.

At first, Shino pretended not to notice Sandai's gaze, but eventually, she couldn't withstand the silent pressure. She pursed her lips and furrowed her brow but nodded.

"Well... I think it should be totally cool to kick off the studying around June or July, but... yeah, better not risk any last-minute rush."
