Second day of the school trip.

The schedule for this day became clear as they boarded the bus and overheard conversations around them. It was apparently a full day of skiing, a hands-on learning experience, as Sandai had initially thought. Their school, located in a city area on mainland Japan with relatively little snowfall, offered skiing as an educational opportunity. Since most students didn't have the chance to experience skiing in their daily lives, it was considered a valuable learning experience.

It was worth noting that all the skiing equipment would be provided, eliminating the need for students to bring their own. This was mentioned by Nakaoka, their teacher, on the bus, who explained that including the equipment in the initial trip cost helped reduce the burden on families.

"Let me remind you as your homeroom teacher, this is primarily an 'educational experience.' Tomorrow, the last day with a full day of free activities, is meant to maintain your motivation for the final year before graduation, so you can have fun, but don't forget the learning aspect," Nakaoka emphasized.

While Nakaoka stressed the educational significance, most students saw the day as an opportunity for fun. To them, the plan for today and tomorrow essentially meant two days of play.

However, as their classmates' excitement contrasted with Sandai and Shino's more reserved expressions, they couldn't help but feel apprehensive.

"Hey, Shino, have you ever skiied before?"

While it might have sounded like they were renouncing the educational aspect, if they viewed it as a situation where they recognized the potential danger beforehand and took measures to avoid it, they could consider it a kind of "learning"  too. At least, that's how Sandai decided to interpret it.

However, despite their personal resolve, circumstances sometimes don't allow for it. Except for the completely free last day, they were primarily moving in groups, meaning that even Sandai and Shino would be with others. They were, in fact, with the class representative and Takasago.

Sandai could overhear the conversation between Takasago and the class representative, who sat in front of them.

For some reason, Takasago and the class representative had a very nice atmosphere about them. However, it made Sandai and Shino sensitive to the situation. If they were to resist skiing here and now, it might disrupt the warm atmosphere between Takasago and the class representative.

While Sandai and Shino weren’t concerned about the class representative, both of them had a favorable view of Takasago. It was clear that Takasago liked the class representative and was trying to get closer to him.

While they were willing to cooperate, neither Sandai nor Shino had any intention of interfering. They figured they could maintain their own friendly relationships while allowing Takasago and the Student Council President to do the same.

It was an entirely unexpected turn of events, but working as a group was the best option. There was no other choice. Sandai let out a sigh, and Shino shrugged her shoulders.

The part about there being another child was especially puzzling. Sandai's perception was that during his time in Japan and when he was abroad, he didn't have friends. That's why even getting along with Hajime was a significant struggle for him.

However, as he continued to wrack his brain to bring back memories, he began to have a faint recollection of a brief period of time when he happened to play with another child.

It was such a brief and incidental interaction that it didn't qualify as a friendship. Sandai couldn't recall their conversations or even the face of the other child.

"Well, you were quite young at the time. It's only natural not to remember. Even if someone asked me to recall myself as a kindergartener, I wouldn't remember a thing. But it makes me feel like I'm only human sometimes, thinking back to those days. I have a feeling I've been a little more lenient with you."

"I've never felt that you've been lenient with me..."

"I also gave you a push regarding your situation with Yuizaki. If it wasn't you, who was involved with Yuizaki, I'm not sure if I could have done the same. Anyway, as a teacher, my intention is to help students grab a better future."

Nakaoka shrugged as she said this. Her gestures were casual, but it was clear that she wasn't evading the question and was speaking from her heart.

When Nakaoka had tried to set him up with Shino, she had been somewhat overly meddlesome, no doubt about it. At the time, Nakaoka had provided a clever-sounding excuse, but even if that excuse were true, there was no need for her to go to such lengths. However, Sandai figured it might have something to do with the sentiment that had grown out of their past encounter.

Upon reflection, Sandai realized that Nakaoka treated him differently from other students, often engaging in actions she wouldn't take with others. Nakaoka had even accompanied him for time-passing activities, which she probably wouldn't have done for most other students. Also, there were times when she seemed to sternly warn Shino, which was unusual.

While It could be considered simply her actions as a teacher, but on the other hand, it felt like she was warning Shino ahead of time so that the burden on Sandai wouldn’t grow. There was a sense of looking after him more than what a typical teacher would do.

Sandai didn't want to acknowledge this, and he pursed his lips. Suddenly, Nakaoka grabbed his ear and pulled him closer.

"Ow, ow, ow!"

"Don't dwell on it so much. I just wanted to have a little chat about the past. That's all. You know, when I was younger, I found it strange how people would dote on their nieces and nephews, but now, I can kind of understand that feeling. They're adorable... Anyway."

Nakaoka released his ear, stood up, and took a step away.

"If Yuizaki ever happens to see you in a situation like this, I don't know what she might do. That's all I’m going to say. Hang in there, young one."

Sandai remained silent. Nakaoka left but turned back for some reason.

"As for how far you've gone with Yuizaki, I can somewhat sense the atmosphere, but do make sure to use protection. Haha!"

Nakaoka was still Nakaoka, making an unnecessary comment, a parting line that was sure to spoil everything. Sandai watched Nakaoka walk away, wearing a bewildered expression. Then, he decided to step outside for a bit to feel the night breeze.

It was still early spring in Hakodate in March. Sandai gazed up at the night sky. Winter hadn't completely let go yet, and the cold wind still blew through the city. However, the clear night sky, void of the heavy clouds that had covered it during the day, revealed the sparkling stars. The night sky stretched out forever and ever.

Sandai remembered Shino's concern for Class rep and Takasago. Nakaoka's information about the two was still fresh in his mind, and he decided to inform Shino about it through chat messages. He knew Shino was most likely asleep at this hour, but he thought she could read it in the morning. Sandai briefly organized the text and sent the chat.

Shino's response came quickly, and it appeared that his message had woken her up, as there were several typos due to sleepiness. In summary, she said, "I'm glad that they're okay."

Sandai apologized for waking her up and assured her that both the Class rep and Takasago were fine. Shino replied with a simple "I see."
