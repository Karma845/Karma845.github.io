Digression ①: Mr. Bacteria, You're Slimy, Aren't You?

Although Sandai's part-time job is cleaning the aquarium, mainly responsible for general cleaning around the premises. He didn't usually deal with much regarding the tanks themselves, but occasionally, he would be asked for assistance.

Today was one of those days when he was asked for help. He was tasked with a special cleaning involving draining the water from a large aquarium tank. This timing was chosen because it was after the New Year's holiday and winter break for students, resulting in fewer visitors. The plan had been set in motion around last week, but due to unexpected personal matters, one of the originally planned staff members had to take sudden leave. So, they turned to Sandai to fill in the gap.

Considering the current low number of visitors, both Sandai and Hajime weren't particularly busy. Therefore, without much reluctance, they agreed to help out.

"Alright then, let's start draining the water. Once it reaches a suitable level, we'll stop the drainage temporarily. At that point, you can go in and use a net to catch the fish"

"Got it."

"Fujiwara-kun works diligently without a single complaint. He gets things done incredibly quickly. As for Saeki-kun... well, he's a bit... yeah..."

As Sandai continued to silently carry out his instructions like a machine, the male employee who had been giving cleaning instructions saw a figure in the corner.

There was Hajime, sitting girlishly and half-crying. He was not very athletic and had been slipping and falling repeatedly, likely due to his lack of coordination.

"Ugh..."

"Are you okay? Did you get hurt?" Sandai asked.

"Yeah..."

Holding out his hand, Sandai helped Hajime slowly stand up. However, he almost slipped again right away, prompting Sandai to hurriedly support his body.

"Thanks~! Eww~ It's so slimy~"

"That sliminess is probably biofilm."

"Bio... film?"

"Bacteria."

"Eek~ I don't like bacteria~"

"Bacteria aren't all bad. Anyway, be more careful."

"Yeah... I'll go change for now."

Hajime let out a cute sneeze, "Heh-chu," then shuffled awkwardly towards the changing room. As Sandai watched Hajime's back, an indescribable expression crossed his face.

Hajime's work clothes were clinging to his skin, outlining his body's contours, giving off a distinctly feminine impression.

"... Saeki-kun really does look like a girl. By the way, whenever Saeki-kun is in the changing room, everyone inexplicably feels awkward and ends up going outside before he finishes changing," a male employee muttered.

The discomfort regarding Hajime wasn't only felt by Sandai; it seemed everyone around was experiencing the same sentiment. Sandai understood their feelings quite well, as he had his own suspicions, so he nodded in agreement.

"Speaking of which, Fujiwara-kun is really close friends with Saeki-kun, right?"

"Well, we're friends, yeah."

"I have to ask, though, is Saeki-kun really a boy?"

"He says so himself, and I think he is, but..."

Hajime wanted to believe in Hajime's words, but there was a more pressing reason for needing it to be true. Their current friendship was grounded in the fact that they were of the same gender. If, for some reason, Saeki-kun wasn't a boy... various problems and complications would arise. Thus, Sandai preferred not to delve too deeply. As long as he didn't observe it, it would remain nonexistent, much like Schrödinger's cat.

"If even Fujiwara-kun doesn't know, I doubt anyone else will."

"Yeah, you're probably right."

Sandai gave a nonchalant response, then continued scrubbing with the deck brush. A few minutes later, Hajime returned after changing his clothes.

"I'm back~"

"Welcome back. Now, let's get back to cleaning."

Sandai made sure to pay extra attention to Hajime's balance to prevent him from falling again as they continued working. The cleaning inside the aquarium went smoothly, transitioning back to the regular maintenance of the facility.

As usual, the scene resembled any typical part-time job—except conversations tended to stray more into personal territory due to the lack of visitors.

"By the way, how's your relationship with Yuizaki-san?"

"It's going well."

"It's pretty impressive that you're able to date someone as intense and jealous as Yuizaki-san without any issues."

"Well, I haven't had any prior experiences, so it might be due to that. Also, I don't mind adjusting for the other person. She's my first girlfriend, and I want to treat her well."

"I envy Yuizaki-san."

"...You envy her?"

"Yeah. I also prefer for somebody else to match my pace rather than the other way around. Isn't it enviable to have somebody who's willing to take in and accept everything as their partner?"

It seemed Hajime was aware of his somewhat self-centered nature.

And it was, indeed, true.

Sandai remembered various things about Hajime . He tended to act on his own whims, reasoning that he wanted to do something. Hajime seemed to want someone who would accept him for that... perhaps.

Well, even if he attempted to predict everything from mundane conversation to underlying emotions, the accuracy of such guesses was dubious.

In any case, they returned to their routine work.

Their conversation flowed as a casual exchange, and they continued with their assigned tasks.

The sky was clear—no signs of storms—and the wind was gentle.

Today was such a day.
