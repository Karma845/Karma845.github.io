Digression ②: Who Would Have Thought We'd Ecounter Choco Croissants In The Middle Of Town?

On that day, Sandai waited for Shino near the bronze animal statue close to the station. It was their rendezvous for a date. However, he had mistakenly arrived twenty minutes early. If he had arrived an hour early, he could have killed time somewhere, but with just twenty minutes to spare, he decided to sit quietly on the edge of the stairs in front of the statue.

And then it happened by chance. He ran into Mei.

"Oh, it's Choco Croissant..."

"Eek..."

"What's with the 'eek'? Don't react like you've encountered a monster."

"Is Shino-pi here nearby?"

"Don't just ignore me... Well, whatever. Shino's not here. We're meeting up, but I got here too early."

"Wow, so it's like, 'I'm so excited for our date! I got here way too early!' kind of thing?"

"Yeah, something like that."

"You're not mad at me for making fun of you?"

"Well, it's true that I'm looking forward to the date with Shino, so there's nothing to get mad about."

"Wow!"

"Could you please stop with those exaggerated reactions?"

"But even if you say that, Mei won't stop. That's just how Mei is."

Mei stuck her tongue out and sat down next to Sandai.

"Why did you sit next to me?"

"Mei also has a meeting here, you know, in front of the statue."

Upon closer inspection, Mei's outfit had a bit more flair to it, resembling the kind of look she'd put together for hanging out with someone. She wore a hoodie and a lace-trimmed skirt. Her shoes were laced-up boots, and her hair was meticulously styled, making her look like an exquisite Choco Croissant.

"A meeting... with a boyfriend?"

"If it were a date with a boyfriend, I'd choose a completely different outfit. I do want a boyfriend, but I don't have any candidates."

"You want a boyfriend, huh? I remember you had a thing for flashy guys, right?"

"Yes, that's right. Can you introduce me?"

"I only have one friend, and he's not exactly flashy. He's a cute kind of guy. So, I can't make that introduction."

"Having only one friend is way too lonely, you know?"

Mei propped her chin on her hand and looked at Sandai with a pitiful expression.

"Don't look at me like that."

"Well, even if you say that, having just one friend is nothing but pitiable, you know. But, well... Shino-pi can be possessive, and maybe having only a few friends like you, he-pi, is just right," Mei remarked.

Mei's words were somewhat on the mark. Shino was the type to continuously check if there were any opposite-sex connections, so it was probably better for Sandai to have few friends. Because he had hardly any friends, he didn't give Shino unnecessary worries.

"Well, I do think sometimes that having few friends is for the best. It spares me from making Shino jealous or anything like that."

"Shino-pi can be really scary when she's angry."

"Shino tends to direct her anger toward those around her rather than me when she gets upset. To avoid involving others, I'm pretty careful."

"It's not just Shino-pi, many girls get angry at those around them rather than their boyfriends. Not that it applies to everyone, but Mei probably does the same. After all, if

you complain to your boyfriend, he might not like you anymore... But when you're in a relationship because you fell in love with him, you don't want to be seen as a narrow-minded person. So, it's better to blame it on others..."

Girls directing their anger, more like fangs under the guise of anger, at those around them instead of their significant others seemed to be a result of various complex emotions, and it appeared to be quite common.

"Shino-pi, she really doesn't want to be disliked by He-pi, does she?" Mei looked at Sandai with a mischievous glint in her eyes.

"What's with that expression..."

"I'm just wondering what's so appealing about this plain boy."

"...There's no particular reason. It feels like I just fell in love out of nowhere. But whatever the reason, since Shino chose me, I intend to live up to her expectations."

As Sandai affirmed this, Mei lifted one eyebrow and made an expression of interest. However, she immediately snorted with a “hmph.”

"Not really... Well, I just wish you happiness. Anyway, I have a friend coming over, so see you later."

Mei stood up and headed toward her friends who were gesturing to her.

─ It looked like she was talking to that boy over there. Do you know him?

─ Just a bit.

─ Wow, he's quite handsome.

─ That plain guy has a girlfriend, after all. Shinopi, the part-timer.

─ Huh? Isn't Shino Yuizaki at the same part-time job as Mei? She's really popular with the boys at our school, too. She had a boyfriend.

─ It seems they started dating around last fall or so.

─ Mei is trying to steal Yuizaki's boyfriend...?

─ We just happened to meet, and that's not my thing, you know?

─ If it were your thing, would you consider taking him?

─ It depends on the time and situation. If I really wanted him, I might.

─ She's trying to make me feel like a bad woman, but Mei, you have a weak mental, so you'd probably run away if you felt intimidated.

─ ...I can't deny that.

What Mei was talking about with her friends was unknown to Sandai, but he didn't need to know. There was no need for him to be concerned about Mei and her surroundings.

As they continued talking, Shino arrived. She was dressed in a white blouse and denim overalls, giving off a somewhat plain vibe. Shino's fashion had actually been changing little by little since she started dating Sandai. Initially, she seemed to be more conscious of current trends, but now she placed a stronger emphasis on cleanliness and seasonal appropriateness.

For better or worse, her style seemed to blend in naturally when she was with Sandai. It didn't feel forced, and it appeared that she had adapted to match Sandai's style over time.

While she still retained some of her gal-like qualities, it was a balanced mix compared to before.

"You’re here."

"I’m ready."

"Shall we go?"

"Yeah."

"I haven't decided what I'm going to do, so it's going to be random, but as long as it's not too tiring."

"Okay."

"Okemaru Suisan."

"Okemaru ...... Susan?"

Shino, who was not well-versed in otaku-related internet memes, had no idea about the origin of Sandai's statement and was simply bewildered.

By the way, Sandai knew this would happen and used it deliberately. He found it amusing when Shino was confused because she was cute, and sometimes, he liked to see her like that.

Using memes that the other person doesn't understand was a mutual thing. Sandai, too, often didn't understand slang that gals frequently used, and when Shino questioned it with a "?", he  found it amusing.

"Speaking of which, while I was casually looking for something interesting on my phone, I found this place."

"Hmm? What kind of place?"

"This one."

"Oh, that place... Have you been there?"

"Yeah. It wasn't that great."

"I see... Then how about this one?"

As they leaned on each other, sharing a scarf and strolling at a leisurely pace, they chatted. It was just a glimpse of their everyday life.
