Epilogue – If You Could Say It In Words, There Would Be No Reason To Paint.

“Even though you didn’t get the prize, personally, I really like how this painting turned out. It’s like you have shed something, retaining your style yet somehow daring. There will be a nationwide contest again at the end of August, let’s challenge.”

Originally, the art preparation room forbade food and drinks. However, Hiiragi-sensei had made it clear that this was his lair. He nonchalantly passed me a cup of coffee, while downing his own./p>

“May I ask you something.” He set the cup down. “Is this a painting you drew because Kashiwazaki joined the competition?”

“Yes, it is. Just as I had told you, I couldn’t draw at all during the time before that. The one who put me back on track was Yuri, and you said my art has changed– has grown… I think that’s her handiwork too.”

Because she participated, I regained the frenzy I needed. Jealousy and admiration, because I could express all the feelings in my chest onto the canvas, I could move one step closer to my dream.

To sum it up, it was all thanks to her who stayed as my goal, as my rival, from the day we met until now. There was no better way to say it.

Hiiragi-sensei just stared at me with an unreadable expression.

“Is there anything?” I couldn’t stand the stare any longer.

“Nothing… Geniuses are difficult to handle. Even if you shelter and take good care of them, they could take it as a pressure. I have seen too many lost their way because of it.” His voice was unusually bitter. “And even more crushed by the publicity and media of the star-hungry industry. Looking like this, the talent you pine for, is it really worth it?” 

His philosophical ramblings too, were the same as Yuri’s gaze, things belonged to the “Geniuses”. Or he was just warning me of the future ahead of us as a veteran who wanted to see younger generations suffer no longer.

He was probably concerned about his students future and gave proper advice. He even took my point of view into consideration and sympathized with my feelings. Hiiragi-sensei was a good teacher.

“Yuri is different. I think you’re underestimating that girl.” 

But despite his concerns, I just couldn’t think so. Yuri wasn’t the type to be easily swallowed by the rough waves he spoke of. She would probably be enjoying herself as she surfed through them.

“Your absolute confidence in her would weigh her the most…” he mumbled, “Nah, enough with worrying. Your existence will certainly support Kashiwazaki, just… just don’t crush her yourself.” 

I wonder if he would see me as another ignorant kid. But despite that, I believed that the day would come when the world would roar the name of the genius Kashiwazaki Yuri.

◇◇◇

Out the preparation room and down the hallway. In habit, I veered to the second year classroom and spun back. I was already a third year student, I reminded myself as I righted my course. And with the word “third year”, I was reminded of Sugawara-senpai.

Late March, the night before she left for Tokyo, she had called me to a nearby cafe. Remembering the favor I owed and the troubles I had caused, I hastily grabbed some overpriced, high-end sweets from the station.

Everyone gave her at least a glance as she made her way to our seat. A beauty, truly unfitting for Maimori. After she finished stirring the sugar in her coffee, she began.

“The initial objective I had set, ‘Getting Yuri to draw’ was a success. With this, I think I no longer have any unfinished business here.”

“Yes. I had already known that she would win a prize the moment she gave her word that she would participate. Although I set my sights on getting a better prize, guess that didn’t really come out well. But the honorable mentions and above will be exhibited at Kouji Art Museum in Tokyo from September 1 to 13. Please let my painting say my thanks again at that time.”

I owed her more than I could thank her, and I caused her more trouble than I could ever apologize for. That was why I ran my mouth about getting one of the prizes as an act of gratitude, that was before I knew the results.

Her eyes locked on mine. Had I been improvident because I thought I knew how she thinks? Looking at her perfectly chiseled face and seeing myself in her big eyes, I wondered. What had I done to be in such a position? 

Then,

“I like you. No matter what, I won’t give up on you.”

The conversation flipped a perfect 180 degrees. My head went blank.

When she told me over the phone that she liked me, I had clearly turned her down. Besides, after I weaseled my way to paint Yuri instead, I didn’t think she would still hold such affection towards me. There was no other reaction than to be flustered or dumbfounded.

But I still knew what I should say.

“Um… See, I’m sorry, but I can’t. I still…” I desperately searched for the next word.

“Yup, I’m good with that for now. But from now, I’m definitely going to make you look at me.” 

She removed the bracelet she always wore from her left wrist and placed it on my hand.

“Senpai… This is…”

It was as if her sky blue eyes were drawing me in. Utilizing her long lashes, she blinked gracefully. 

She straightened and proclaimed. “I think you wouldn’t prefer a girl who put love before her dreams, so I have to go…” Lonesome yet determined, she continued on, “I’ll make you fall for me. Wait for me when I polished my charms, and make sure you’re ready for it.”

Someone with as much charm as her polishing it even more? Would I even survive that? That was going to be a big test for my love for Utako and my self control.

“Uh, um… Please be gentle with me?”

The processing of my brain had reached its limits and I had already said a reply so embarrassing I wanted to roll around on the ground with my head in my hands.

To cover up the reply that would be one of the pages in my dark history, I hastily offered her the sweets I brought. 

“Um, Senpai. I want to repay you, but I’m not sure what I should do. At least, please have these sweets. If there’s anything I can do to be of assistance, please don’t hesitate to ask me.”

“Ahaha. Just as I thought. Your sense of duty never ceases to amaze me. Well~”

Was it because it was unexpected? Or was it because of her charms?

I could follow her motion with my eyes, yet petrified, unable to move as she pecked me on the cheek. 

“So you did it, Senpai…”

“I can’t believe I did something so classic like this…damn. Aah!” She squirmed sheepishly. “But… Yup…I’m happy I did that!”

Even though she was the one who had done such a bold action, she was smiling bashfully. The gap between her demeanor was too cute. No, I turned my eyes away.

“By the way, I’m saving my lips for when you decide to do it, ‘kay?” This is an overkill…

But she peeked at me, her plump lips forced their way into my view. This time around, I can’t resist blushing. As if she enjoyed looking at my face, she flashed me a grin brighter than in any magazine cover she was in.

A short time after the results for the Kouji Exhibit was posted, a call from Senpai came.

–“Tell Yuri congratulations on winning the grand prize. And then, when Yuri comes to Tokyo, you’ll come too, right? Tell me as soon as you can decide a schedule.” Was what she said.

I wanted to grow into a man who could be calm enough when she teases. And so I started fostering a new goal. 

My life as the most senior student around, however, wasn’t going smoothly. And the reason for that being, in Maiei High, students stay in the same class even when moving up, or in short, I was stuck with Kashiwazaki Yuri until graduation.

And when I entered the classroom, the usual racket between Yuri and Seino greeted me.

“Aah! stop that talk! Stop that before it’s too late, people will get irritated, trust me.”

“Can’t I? He’s my first boyfriend! Let me enjoy myself!”

“Hmm… No, no one would mind, no one cares about you anyway. By the way, your boyfriend isn’t even famous or anything, what’s the point of bragging?”

“Really, would you just shut up?! Talking with you spoils the mood!”

A sigh escaped me as I listened to their conversation. I approached her seat, the seat on the last row by the window, and gave her a chop on the head.

“You’re noisy since morning. What happened?”

“Here, look at this. I can’t hold it in–” She bursted into laughter.

Her phone showed Seino’s SNS account, the latest post being two cups and some gushy hashtags. 

“Seino…I don’t usually take Yuri’s side, but…” I thought Seino would know better…

“Komiya’s cruel too! But… I don’t care what Yuri says, but if you say so, then maybe I’ll consider changing.”

Her shoulder slumped as she returned her concentration to operating her phone. From their posture, I couldn’t feel the slightest vibe of someone preparing for entrance exams. I smiled wryly at myself as I tried to imagine what would happen in one year’s time.

I had started studying for entrance exams and also started attending an art prep school in Sapporo. Still, I was drinking in my last days of highschool in my own way, being silly with friends and painting like a fool. 

And about Yuri…

“Yuri, come to club activities today. Nah–… I thought you would finally take painting a little more seriously…”

 After the Kouji Exhibit, her days without painting resumed. 

“Me? Painting seriously? What is that? Which parallel universe are you talking about?”

“Just a while ago you were painting Utako for the Kouji Exhibit! Eh? What’s wrong with what I said? Did I reincarnate in another world?”

“I had never associated telling lame jokes with you.”

“You were the one who started talking about parallel universes!”

If I were to tell some stranger that this silly girl was a genius pianist, no one would have believed.

“Both Hiiragi-sensei and Utako are waiting for your new painting, you know? And I’m participating in the next countrywide competition this August, you too, Yuri.”

But she just rejected me flatly. “Nahh, the one before was because of you two. I don’t have such motivation now. I don’t feel the fire of passion burning inside me. Go find some heart pounding goal, if not then I won’t, that’s it.”

My dream is to surpass the genius in front of me. I knew that it seemed impossible, but I wouldn’t have this burning drive if it was anyone else. That made her lack of goal irritating to me.  But still, was a goal something that could be defined by others? Was that something I had the right to say?

As I was mulling it over fervently, something knocked my forehead.

“Ouch?!” I cringed back, hands pressing where I had been knocked. Seeing my reaction, Yuri smiled triumphantly. I realized that she had flicked me in the forehead.

“Don’t get too happy, Sousuke. You’re a hundred light years away from getting hold of me. Listen, all you have to do is look at me,” she commanded.

She had said that line once before. But this time, there was no clamor from the classmates. They probably knew that this was not a romantic confession, but her domineering possessiveness.

Sugawara-senpai had been suspicious about Yuri’s feelings towards me, but there was no way that was the case.

Besides, she said she was motivated when painting for me and Utako. 

The day when we could fulfill our wish as promised might not be too far away now. I mean, we cared about each other this much. 

“Sousuke.”

My head jerked her way in response. She gave me her usual devil’s smile.

“Don’t look at just those boring visible stars. There are so many more invisible to us, Utako said that, didn’t she?”

Unable to grasp the meaning of her words, Seino and I tilted our heads in unison.

Before entering the art club room, my phone buzzed.

[Yuu-chan’s painting was really exhibited at the Kouji Art Museum! You’ll come to Tokyo this Fall, right? Can we meet?  If you have the time… I want to see you again.]

Even a short message like this, she must have blush bright red while sending this. After an unintentional chuckle, I tightened my expression  and tied my apron. 

I slid the phone back into my pocket, the reply was for later. Today too, I, an untalented, ordinary Joe, couldn’t beat the genius.

I am aware of this fact, so I couldn’t neglect daily efforts.

To fulfill the promise the three of us made on that day.

For my own dream.

Today, too, diligently, carefully, and passionately, I wielded my paintbrush.
