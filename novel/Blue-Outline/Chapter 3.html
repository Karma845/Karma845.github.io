Chapter 3: Seeing What Shouldn’t Be Seen

『Wow~, The hydrangea festival~』

『We used to go there a lot as a family. They have a lot of delicious stuff at the stalls.』

My two sisters say cheerfully, sharing memories across the tablet screen.

The advancement of technology is a blessing because I can easily converse face-to-face with my sisters who are overseas.

(I wonder how people used to communicate it in the past?)

『But who would’ve thought Amane Onee-chan would casually go on a date to the Raihara family’s favorite outing spot with a guy.』

“It’s not a date.”

I say, denying Kanon’s words with a wry smile.

Despite being a high school senior, I still don’t understand romantic feelings between men and women as well as my sister Kanon does.

She’s only in her first year of middle school but is way ahead of me emotionally… What does that say about me?

“Chifuji-san and I are not in that kind of relationship. He’s just accompanying me as I work on overcoming my weaknesses.”

『He seems like a good person. Very mature, too.』

Says Shion, the more laid-back of the twins.

While Kanon is lively, Shion is calm and collected.

Their faces may look alike, but their personalities are polar opposites.

“Yes, Chifuji-san is a very nice person, and he’s so calm and collected that it’s hard to believe we’re in the same grade.”

He’s been working part-time for a long time, so maybe that’s why he’s so mature.

He seems like he can handle anything that comes his way.

“I wonder if all boys my age are like that.”

I hear stories about boyfriends from my classmates, and most of them seem a bit more mischievous. ‘They act so childishly,’ some even complain like that.

『No, no, most boys of the same age are more childish than girls!』

Kanon retorts a bit heatedly. Maybe she has her own experience with it.

『For him to accept Onee-chan’s foolish and bizarre antics this time, that is stalking in your ‘Jirai-kei’ (Landmine-type) attire, and by the way, someone who has such a suspicious smile, is really something unusual. Normally, that would be impossible.』

“I know…”

I can’t deny it.

I’ve been told my smile can be rather mysterious.

I’m not really aware of it, but apparently, when I’m annoyed, my face turns pretty evil.

『So, Onee-chan had his help but didn’t make any progress?』

『But you know, Kanon, that’s just how Amane Onee-chan is. Instead of saying she’s ‘tired,’ she’d rather take care of someone else to regain her energy』

『That’s true, but, Shion, we know very well, so it ends with ‘Well, that is simply the way she is.’, but to others, she might seem like a weird creature. How does that even energize her?』

『I know right~』

“Ugh…”

Once again, I can’t even argue.

『Considering Onee-chan has spoiled us for so long, it might be strange for us to say this, but maybe you should try changing that part of Onee-chan’s personality.』

Kanon suggests hesitantly.

“Yes, I will change. Onee-chan has decided to change”

I declare to Shion. Hearing that, Kanon speaks, averting her eyes.

『…Well, we’ll support you, at least.』

Kanon, being my sister, knows.

She probably doesn’t think I can do it… but who’s to blame for giving her that impression?

I can’t even complain.

『But you know, that Chifuji-san? He says some amazing things, doesn’t he?』

“What do you mean by ‘amazing’?”

When I ask her, Shion replies in her usual relaxed tone.

『Eh~? Saying ‘I’m tired’ when you’re tired is being spoiled, isn’t that quite something~?』

『Huh? Shion, were you listening to what Onee-chan said? You know, Onee-chan can’t handle it unless it’s brought down that low to that level of being spoiled~ It’s because Onee-chan who’s no good…Wait, what were we talking about?』

“It’s about me being at fault…”

『I see~.』

Kanon nods in agreement. Sorry for being such a failure of an Onee-chan…

『Eh~? No, that’s not it. We were talking about how saying ‘I’m tired’ is being spoiled, and that’s amazing… what are you talking about, Kanon〜. Regardless of whether our Onee-chan is no good or anything like that, it’s a word that doesn’t come out unless you really feel that ‘being tired is spoiled’, so that’s pretty amazing〜.』

……That being said, it might be so.

I was thinking just like what Kanon said, but there seems to be a way to perceive it like Shion.

I still don’t fully understand what kind of person Chifuji-san is.

…Because of the guilt of stalking, I have been very afraid to say anything that would make him think I was probing into his life.

Also, Chifuji-san didn’t seem to be the type to actively reveal various things about himself.

The only new information I got today was that it was his first time having a bento in a tiered box.

I do not know him very well.

***

“Umm~….Hmm~~~~~”

“Take your time, it’s alright.”

A week from the day we went out to the Hydrangea Festival, another Sunday has arrived.

“….What…What I want to see, right?”

“That’s right. It’s what you, Raihara-san, think of as ‘I want to see’ by considering only yourself.”

The place we came to is a cinema in the city center.

As she had failed in both ‘dragging me along for shopping’ and saying ‘I’m tired’ when tired, this time, we chose something that could be decided immediately on the spot.

Going to the cinema together and choosing the movie she wants to watch, that’s the plan.

When we were shopping, Raihara-san said, ‘I can’t think of any place I want to go for myself.’

That’s why, this task might not be easy either.

Back then she also said, ‘Especially when I think about ‘dragging someone to a place I want to go to,’ it makes it even worse!’

Compared to that, she should feel less selfish this time.

Also, unlike the challenge of saying ‘I’m tired’, the physical condition is not related, so it’s easy to try.

While pondering such things, next to me, Raihara-san continues to worry in front of the cinema’s wall, where today’s screening list is posted.

“….What I want to see, what I want to see, what I want to see, saying what I want to see…”

I’ve heard she likes movies, so it’s not that she’s not interested.

However, asserting her own liking in such situations seems to be her weakness.

It might be better to wait patiently rather than speak rashly.

I aimlessly observe the situation inside the cinema to pass the time.

A cinema, huh?

In fact, I have hardly ever been here.

Maybe since my uncle took me here a long time ago.

There are faces that are looking forward to the fun that is about to start and faces immersed in the lingering impact of the movie they watched.

I see, so this is the kind of atmosphere a cinema has.

It must be a good place to enjoy holidays for the majority of families.

…No, I got lost in my thoughts.

Anyway, today we’ll see whether or not ‘the third time’s the charm’ is true——

◇◇◇

“… Chifuji-san!”

“Yes?”

That moment came surprisingly easily.

“…I want to watch this!”

“Alright, let’s watch it.”

Raihara-san points to a movie title, and I nod.

It would be tactless to explicitly commend or praise her for this.

Naturally, I should accept her suggestion as if it was nothing.

“…But, it might not be to Chifuji-san’s taste… it might bore you…”

“Not at all. I also want to see what Raihara-san wanted to watch. …Let’s see.”

Confirming the title Raihara-san was pointing to, it was unexpectedly a hardcore action film.

It’s a blockbuster from Hollywood that’s just been released.

“…Well, do you dislike such things?”

“No, no way. Rather…”

The title of this movie is so masculine that it’s easy to think that she picked it because of me.

Maybe perceiving such thoughts of mine, Raihara-san shakes her head vigorously.

“I want to watch it, really-… actually, I’ve watched all the works of this director, even the old ones… I also have them on Blu-ray.”

“Oh? I see.”

“Yes! I usually go to watch them with my sisters, but my mother and friends don’t like these, and watching alone feels lonely…”

“Got it, let’s watch this movie.”

What becomes clear is, the time spent just now was not about her being undecided about what movie she wanted to see.

If the newly released film is by her favorite director, then for Raihara-san, the decision on what to watch is already made.

The struggle was whether to say it or not.

“…I’m glad. …this feels surreal… is this really okay…?”

“It’s okay. Now, let’s go buy the tickets. The time is… is it displayed on that monitor? …Hmm, what’s that display?”

The upcoming show has a note saying [4DX]… What’s that?

“Ah, it’s 4DX. The seat moves according to the content of the movie, there are winds, splashes of water, and even mist with fragrance, and so on.”

“Oh, that sounds interesting.”

So, there is such a thing…

No, come to think of it, I remember hearing about something like that from a friend.

“I tried it with my sisters before, it’s like an attraction…!”

“Sounds good, let’s go with that one.”

As the next show was about to start soon, we bought tickets and drinks and entered the theater.

***

I think I realize I’m at the movie theater not when I enter the building but when I’m watching commercials before the main feature in the dim theater.

It’s a thrilling time, but there is a different thrill in my chest today.

——I said it! I said what seemed like my selfish desire!

It was an amazing feeling.

I can sense the machinery made of many gears within me making a grinding noise in surprise, as if something strange has been caught in it.

I feel nervous, and my heart is racing… No.

To be perfectly honest… it doesn’t sit well with me…

For now, I think I shouldn’t ignore this feeling. I must face it and change.

I have to seal and bury the me who only thinks about doing favors for others and doesn’t act selfishly.

I must do it, I surely can. Surely.

As I think these thoughts, the pre-show commercials changed to a new one, and sweet words from the commercial softly melted into the air in the theater

It seems like a youth romance movie.

…as expected, even for me…

Being in a dim, classic dating spot with a boy of the same age doesn’t mean I feel nothing.

The thrill before the movie screening, the thrill of having done something seemingly selfish, and the thrill of this situation.

All are different kinds of excitement.

‘Amane will eventually come here on a date with someone she likes’——that’s what my mother said on the way home from the movie theater before.

I wondered about it back then.

“…What’s wrong?”

Chifuji-san asks in a low voice.

It’s because I was intently staring at his profile.

“N-no, it’s nothing.”

While also speaking softly, I shake my head.

… A date, huh?

A date refers to an outing between lovers; it shouldn’t refer to just a man and a woman going out.

So, this is different, right?

No-no, even thinking such a thing is very rude to Chifuji-san.

He’s accompanying me to help me overcome my weaknesses, and here I am, frivolously thinking it’s a date.

…I think Chifuji-san is a wonderful person. Rather, I think he might be too kind.

I was stalking him, but he still listened to my story and cooperated with me.

Thinking he is a nice person, and feeling very grateful. These two things are certain.

Then…as a man?

Since yesterday, I have talked a bit about such things with my sisters, and even if it’s rude and unacceptable, I inadvertently still think about it.

But honestly, I don’t know.

To begin with, I don’t know if it’s something that I can understand or recognize.

…Well, even if I were to like him, whether Chifuji-san would consider me… including being a stalker, I’ve exposed so much disgrace…

“Look, we barely made it!”

“But…”

“No buts!”

…Oh my.

I was so lost in my thoughts that I did not even know that the commercials were over.

I was brought back to reality by voices from beside me.

After a quick glance and to my left (Chifuji-san is on my right), a parent and child had arrived.

A mother and a boy who seemed to be in lower elementary school.

As they hurriedly took their seats, the main feature of the movie started right in time.

The opening scene was a flashy action sequence.

A flood of sound unique to movie theaters rolls in.

The air vibrates intensely, and the seats shake back and forth.

Yes, this is the impact of 4DX.

Peeking to my right, Chifuji-san also seemed impressed.

It seems like the corners of his mouth are slightly raised.

Noticing my gaze, he told me, ‘This is amazing,’ with only the shape of his mouth without making a sound.

I’m glad, he seems to be enjoying the movie.

◇◇◇

Suddenly,

“Ah…”

“Oh, come on! What are you doing…!”

Oh my…

The boy in the seat to my left had spilled a bit of the juice he was holding.

Perhaps it’s because of the shaking of the seat.

I try to take out a handkerchief to wipe it, but then I realize that I should not be doing such a thing.

…Right, his mother is here after all.

An outsider shouldn’t just abruptly barge in.

Besides, my current fashion is Jirai-kei (Landmine type).

I really like this outfit, but it might be a bit intimidating.

Speaking to them out of the blue might surprise them.

While I’m thinking about these things and trying to suppress my impulse, the mother had already wiped up the spilled juice properly.

…Hmm, I’m glad I didn’t do anything.

I shouldn’t snatch away her opportunity to take care of people.

That kind of happiness…how nice~~ No, wait! I must suppress these feelings!

But even after that, the boy spilled his juice and snacks several times and said he wanted to go to the bathroom.

Patience…patience, patience, patience!

I somehow suppress my overwhelming desire to care, stimulated by the boy’s clamor.

No-no, form is emptiness and emptiness is form…

“I told you, why are you—excuse me, miss, I’m so sorry for making a lot of noise and bothering me since a while ago…”

“Oh no, no problem.”

I’m perfectly fine, it’s not a trouble at all and if you don’t mind could I help with looking after him?

Oh look, there’s something on his mouth. Let me get that for you… such words seem like they would slip out of my mouth if I let my guard down for a moment!

Somehow enduring everything, I shake my head with a small smile.

The mother was apologizing to the person sitting on the other side too.

It seems tough.

I wish she could just relax during the movie and I could be the one that—no-no, it’s not allowed to do that.

Then, the boy spills juice again because of the shaking of the seat.

The boy’s behavior shakes my resolve again.

The movie screening time impeccably turned into a place for my spiritual training.

“I-it was fun, wasn’t it!”

“Yes. …By the way, are you okay? You seem to be very exhausted…”

“No, no, I’m not tired at all.”

I endured. I really endured a lot…

…I may not have absorbed much of the movie content, but I realize my own immaturity.

Talking about such things, Chifuji-san and I exit the cinema and return to the movie hall entrance.

The lights are brighter, and I realize, oh, the movie time has ended.

“Was 4DX okay for you, Chifuji-san… oh? You look a bit pale…”

“…Do I?”

Is it just my imagination? But, he seems a bit pale…

“Mom told you, right? Be careful because the seat shakes!”

“…I’m sorry.”

“That’s enough! We are never coming to the movies again!”

“Ah…”

When I turned around at the sound of the yelling, it was the parent and child who were next to us earlier.

Hmm, it seemed tough for the mother too, but it’s inevitable for a child of that age to make little mistakes.

“Why are you always so whiny!”

“I’m sorry…!”

Oh my…

“Wow, they are here, the typical ‘exploding’ mom and child.”

“It’s totally a common thing at spots where people bring their kids. Seriously, I used to get told stuff like that all the time too.”

I hear such a conversation from a couple nearby.

Certainly, it’s a common sight in shopping malls and supermarkets.

…Hmm, going to say, ‘It wasn’t a bother, it’s okay. So, you don’t need to worry about that…’ would be inappropriate.

In the end, with an ‘Enough! Let’s go home!’ the mother headed towards the exit with big strides, and the boy trudged along after her.

It’s difficult, isn’t it… I was about to say.

“…Raihara-san, excuse me. I will go to the restroom for a bit.”

“Ah, okay. I’ll be waiting.”

Saying ‘Excuse me’ to me again, Chifuji-san headed to the restroom.

…There was a lack of energy in his voice, and his awkward movements were a bit concerning.

Then, shortly after, a message to my smartphone.

『It seems I feel sick from the seat’s shaking. Sorry, but could you wait a bit?』

“Ah…!”

I wonder if he’s okay!? So that’s why his complexion was off.

I reply, telling him to take his time and to contact me immediately if he feels any pain.

Then a stamp of a polite dog saying thank you came back.

I worry while waiting for a while.

“…I’m sorry, and thank you for waiting. I don’t know, it’s shameful.”

Chifuji-san who returned, is indeed somewhat pale.

“No-no! I’m the one who should be sorry; I should have chosen a regular screening…”

“I mean, I also said I really wanted to see it. Haha, honestly, it was really fun, but it seems my body didn’t agree.”

“Let’s rest a bit. The coffee at that shop is delicious. …Ah, maybe not as much as the shop where Chifuji-san works.”

“That’s good. It would be hopeless if they surpassed us in taste when they are already winning in location.”

Chifuji-san says, jokingly. His tone seems to lack its usual vigor.

Let’s take a break slowly.

We enter a shop attached to the movie theater and sit down with our drinks ‘I’ll buy them, so please rest at the seat,’ I said, but ‘It’s against today’s purpose,’ he insisted.

By the way, I wonder what should I do?

I was worried that I don’t remember much of the movie because I was constantly distracted by the parent and child next to us…

But Chifuji-san, with a laugh, said he couldn’t concentrate much either due to the shaking of the seat.

“No, it’s embarrassing…but I have always been prone to getting sick. When I was little, I used to get fevers all the time.”

“Is that so…”

“I feel I have become much more stronger now, but sometimes I still get unwell during the changing of the seasons.”

“I see…It’s exactly this time, isn’t it? Please be careful with colds; it seems it will get much colder at night from today.”

“Ah, I didn’t know that. It has been quite warm recently. Thank you, I will be careful.”

Because the image of a mature, reliable man is stronger than that of a boy of the same age, I arbitrarily thought that he must be physically strong too.

But if that’s not the case, I am worried.

That means he’s a hard worker and likely to overexert himself, and that’s…

“…It might be odd for me to say this, but please rely on those around you if something happens…”

“I will.”

Chifuji-san nods and smiles gently.

◇◇◇

“…Chifuji, sorry, is it still hurt…?”

On Tuesday, during lunch break in the classroom, one of my friends who was eating with me said this, putting his hands together.

“…Ah? No…”

“But you’ve been so quiet, and you didn’t answer when pointed out during the class… sorry, my ball hit you quite hard…”

Our first class in the morning was physical education, and the subject was softball.

This friend of mine threw a ball that apparently slipped while playing catch and hit me in the side of the head as I was practicing nearby.

“It’s alright, it doesn’t hurt or anything now.”

“Really? But I caught a glimpse when you were changing, didn’t it look like a bruise?”

“….Is that so. No, it probably didn’t. You saw it wrong.”

“Really?”

There’s nothing wrong where the ball hit and there’s no pain.

So, this is not a lie. Then, another friend speaks up.

“But, isn’t Chifuji acting strange today? The reason you got hit by the ball during PE is because you seemed spaced out and didn’t notice it.”

“Is that so… was I spaced out? I was feeling a bit sluggish and couldn’t move quickly…”

That’s why I couldn’t avoid it.

Come to think of it, even now, my body feels strangely tired.

Did physical education class do this to me…?

“…Hey, sorry, I’ve been wondering, isn’t Chifuji-kun’s face red?”

“Kusakabe-san?”

This time, Kusakabe-san (a girl who’s on the gardening committee with me), who was spreading out her lunch with a group of girls nearby, turns around to join the conversation.

“Really? I can’t tell.”

“Girls notice people’s complexion really well. …Hmm? Now that she mentions it, maybe?”

My two male friends, taking Kusakabe-san’s words, intently peer into my face.

“It’s red! Excuse me a bit!”

Kusakabe-san, having stood up from her seat, comes over and puts her hand on my forehead.

“..It’s hot! You have a fever!”

It’s true I feel a little light-headed, and just as Kusakabe-san said, it was indeed above normal when measured in the infirmary.

However, it wasn’t too high, and since there was no one to pick me up, I decided to leave early for now and go home by myself.

That would have been fine, but…

“…This is bad; my fever got worse.”

I didn’t feel noticeably ill when I left the house this morning.

Even when everyone at school pointed it out, I only thought, ‘Maybe I was a bit feverish?’ But, it’s getting worse while I am on my way home alone.

Even so, I have to do something about it myself.

With shaky steps, I stop by a drugstore on my way home.

It’s especially large around here and has a good selection of products… well, is there any reason why it has to be a good-selection store?

I don’t know, I just feel like a bigger store is better.

I should buy antipyretics and, also, uh, sports drinks and jelly drinks…and what else?

…Ah, I can tell my thoughts are becoming duller.

I shake my head two-three times and let out a sigh.

Despite this, the foggy feeling doesn’t clear.

“…Chifuji-san?”

At first, I thought I was hearing things.

“What a coincidence. Are you shopping?”

“…Raihara, san?”

“Yes. This place is great, it’s big and has a selection of cleaning supplies… Oh?”

In her basket, there are products for mold cleaning.

I guess it’s that time of year, the rainy season.

“Chifuji-san, your complexion…and also, why at this time on a weekday?”

“Mold cleaning…I need to do it soon too… I even have the tools Raihara-san recommended…”

“Chifuji-san?”

“Yes? Ah, sorry….”

What was I saying now?

I feel like whatever came to my mind just came out of my mouth.

What was I asked by Raihara-san? This is not good, not good.

“Sorry, I am a bit spaced out…”

“…Excuse me.”

Swiftly, Raihara-san places her hand on my forehead; then her face shows concern.

“You have a fever… I see; you left early, didn’t you?”

“…Embarrassingly, I am feeling a bit sick.”

Come to think of it, I feel like I had such a conversation with her the day before yesterday.

She even told me to be careful as it gets cold at night, but I completely failed to heed the warning.

“So, I need to buy antipyretics and sports drinks…Oh, why is Raihara-san here on a weekday…?”

“Today is the foundation anniversary. (TN: Maybe referring to school.) No, more importantly, how about you, Chifuji-san? …how about your family?”

“Yes? Ah…no.”

I hastily close my overly relaxed mouth before it can say anything unnecessary due to the fever.

“…I’m alone today.”

“That…”

Raihara-san’s face shows concern.

Her expression seems to be vividly saying she can’t help but worry.

I could already imagine what she was going to say to me.

“With the state you are in, it might be dangerous to go home alone… Moreover, when you are sick, you need to eat nutritious food…Chifuji-san, I will accompany you to your home!”

“I appreciate your concern, but you shouldn’t…”

“Bringing a woman who seemed like a stalker into your home must be very uncomfortable, I know! But I won’t do anything strange, I swear!”

“No, ever since Raihara-san managed to speak your mind last time, I’m not worried about that at all… anyway, having you take care of me here would be meaningless… To make Raihara-san take care of me…”

My thoughts are becoming foggy again, but I muster up everything and manage to squeeze out a few words.

“You know, it’s like ruining the beginning of a successful diet by eating a cake…”

Is this the right expression?

But I think she understands what I am trying to say.

“Mmm… When you say it like that… but…”

Seemingly understanding, Raihara-san thinks hard.

I don’t want to undermine her efforts and success… I think it’s better to leave now.

“…thank you for your concern. In any case, I’ll be fine… It would be bad if I infected you with my cold… So, then…”

Saying that, I turn my body and leave the spot.

I’m happy with Raihara-san’s feelings, but I don’t want to trouble her.

Some fever reducers and several jelly drinks will do…and some sports drinks. That should be enough.

Holding the basket containing those, I go to the cashier, pay, and leave the store——

“…Cheat Day!”

At that moment, a voice calls out to me, and my arm is grasped firmly.

“Raihara-san?”

“It’s a cheat day!”

…Cheat day?

What’s that? I think I’ve heard that before. 

◇◇◇

…Cheat day?

What’s that? I think I’ve heard that before. 

“Cheat day is; when you restrict your diet, continuously restraining it can lead to a drop in basic metabolism, making it harder to lose weight, so you designate a day where you can eat whatever you want!”

Ah, right, something like that.

Raihara-san, who has been taking care of athletic younger sisters, must be knowledgeable about this.

“And It’s the same thing! How about considering today a cheat day? Today, you can be spoiled to your heart’s content!”

“No, but that’s…”

I thought it was a bit of a forceful analogy, but I guess I was the one who brought up the diet analogy first.

“…However, to be spoiled would be…uh…”

This is bad; I feel like my fever is genuinely starting to rise.

I feel dizzy, and my body sways.

“…No arguments! I won’t leave you alone!”

“Ra-Raihara-san…”

“Let’s go… uh, I, um, know where Chifuji-san’s house is…”

Ah right, she probably found out where I live during her period of observing me.

It’s embarrassing, really.

And unavoidably, I become conscious of her, because her body is warm and soft——

Her support provides an amazing sense of stability, and with this body feeling dizzy from the fever, it seems impossible to shake her off.

(Of course, I won’t do that)

She doesn’t engage in sports or anything, and yet her physical talent is apparent.

“It’s good to have talent…”

“Huh?”

“Ah, it’s nothing…”

It’s bad; I really can’t control myself due to the fever.

I’m blurting out whatever I’m thinking.

Having talent is good, I’m envious… No, that’s not it.

I hope I don’t say any more strange things…

And although it’s late, I guess it’s a given that she’s going to take care of me.

“… I’m sorry for the trouble.”

“Oh, Chifuji-san should know very well that I don’t find this sort of thing troublesome.”

When she says that, indeed, I have nothing to say.

An old public housing complex on the fifth floor, that’s where I live.

There are several occupancy conditions, but the rent is unbelievably cheap for a 2LDK layout.

“Have you had lunch?”

“Now that you mention it, not yet…”

With my vision getting blurry, I’m now on the bed.

My body feels so heavy doing anything, and while talking to Raihara-san, even just keeping my upper body raised is reaching its limits, so I completely lie down flat.

My head was hot, and I was shivering… then Raihara-san put a blanket over me.

I dimly watch her gently patting the blanket.

I dug around in my head for a memory of someone doing this to me, but I couldn’t find it.

“Before you take your medicine, it’s better to have something in your stomach. Can I use the kitchen and the ingredients?”

“Sorry… I will definitely thank you later…”

“I have already received a lot from you, Chifuji-san.”

With a voice that seems to embody the very concept of gentleness, Raihara-san says so and leaves my bedroom.

Someone is cooking for me while I’m lying down; I can’t remember the last time I was in such a situation.

“Ah…”

Pull yourself together. Don’t slack off just because you have a fever.

I must be proper…get it together.

I, who lack any talent, must at least be proper and reliable at any given time, or else I can’t survive.
