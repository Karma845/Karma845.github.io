Chapter 2: The Summer When Something Changes

Summer has finally begun. The infamous, sweltering summer of Kyoto.

When the rainy season cleared in July, I felt like the students started to get excited all of a sudden. After the Friday seminar, I could hear the fulfilled ones in the lab, talking about going to summer festivals or to the beach. I have no connection to such things.

Nanase seemed to be discreetly reapplying her lipstick a little away from the circle. I could see her smiling at the small hand mirror.

Even though it’s not a smile directed at me, I couldn’t help but feel a thump in my chest.

“Hey! Nanase, won’t you come to the beach with us?”

At that moment, Kinami shouted in a voice that echoed through the lab. Nanase, who was suddenly addressed, flinched and responded in a high-pitched voice,

“W-what!? Uh, the beach!?”

“We’re talking about going to the beach during the summer break. Let’s go, Nanase!”

Nanase looked around with a panicked expression and then shook her head vigorously.

“Sorry… I, the beach is a bit…”

“Oh, then how about the pool?”

“I, I… um, that’s right, I can’t swim!”

As Nanase stuttered, Kinami burst into laughter.

“No, but even if we go to the beach or pool, we don’t have to swim, right?”

Nanase tilted her head, “Is that so?” wondering what exactly they do there.

She probably doesn’t know how the fulfilled ones enjoy the beach or pool. I don’t know either, for that matter.

“Anyway, it’s fine if you can’t swim! I’ll teach you step by step…”

“Yusuke, you’re persistent. You just want to see Haruko in a swimsuit, right?”

Sudo said that and lightly hit the back of Kinami’s head. Kinami didn’t seem to be offended, and just laughed, “Was it that obvious?”

… I thought as much.

However, going to the beach with seminar friends would be the very picture of the rosy summer Nanase aspires to. Why did she turn it down?

Nanase glanced at me, perhaps noticing my gaze.

She waved her hand slightly, and I gestured to her, “Don’t look over here.”

On my way to the bicycle parking area after the fourth period, I spotted Nanase from behind.

Her chestnut-colored long hair was tied up high, and large earrings dangled from her ears. Her outfit was somehow refreshing and summery. I was impressed by how she dresses stylishly in the summer.

My summer attire is basically just T-shirts.

I had been keeping my distance but suddenly, Nanase turned her head and looked around.

“Ah, it is Sagara-kun after all!”

“Whoa. What do you want?”

“I just had a feeling you were there.”

I pride myself on having a low presence, but how does Nanase always notice me?

Since Nanase stopped, I had no choice but to stand next to her. Then I asked her.

“…Nanase. You were invited to go to the beach today. You should have gone.”

“No, absolutely not! No way!”

Nanase’s voice was almost a scream. Surprised by her extreme rejection, I wondered what was so bad about it.

“Is it because you don’t want to be seen in a swimsuit?”

Well, I also think she shouldn’t show her swimsuit to someone like Kinami. Nanase mumbled, “That too,” and then said,

“…The beach, the pool… if I think about my makeup coming off at such places… I’d rather die than show everyone my bare face.”

…Ah. So that’s the reason.

It seems Nanase is excessively afraid of her bare face being exposed to those around her.

I think her bare face isn’t that bad. There are probably quite a few guys who prefer it that way.

Not that I’m one of them or anything.

“By the way, Nanase, do you have any plans for the summer break? You’re always with Sudo and the rest… It doesn’t seem like you’ve made any progress.”

Perhaps I hit the nail on the head, as Nanase pressed her chest and slumped down dejectedly.

“Plans for the summer break… I don’t have any. Maybe I’ll just go home for Obon.”

“…Huh.”

“But, you know. I do have something I want to do this summer! There’s a café near my apartment that has a tropical parfait for the summer, and I’m going to eat it!”

The goal she declared so proudly turned out to be surprisingly modest. I couldn’t help but feel deflated.

“Just go and have it now…”

“But, I don’t have the courage to order a parfait by myself.”

Nanase stopped mid-sentence, then lit up with an idea and exclaimed, “That’s right!” I had a bad feeling about this.

“Sagara-kun! Let’s go and have a parfait together right now!”

…I knew she’d say that.

“Why does it come to this…”

“Please! Eating a tropical parfait is my first step towards a rosy life!”

Nanase put her hands together in front of her face and pleaded desperately.

Somehow, I felt like I was being persuaded by some strange logic, but when she said, “You said you’d help, right?” I found it impossible to refuse.

“…I’ve got a part-time job tonight… I’m going home right after this.”

Nanase cheered “Yay!” and, humming a clumsy tune, straddled her bicycle.

With her ponytail fluttering, she rode off on her red bike, looking dashing, and I reluctantly followed her.

We arrived at a cafe with a calm atmosphere. It wasn’t as noisy as the chain coffee shops, and it seemed like a place students could easily frequent.

When we opened the solid wooden door, a bell chimed with a “clang-clang.” The aromatic scent of coffee tantalized my nostrils.

“Welcome! How many in your party?”

We were greeted by a young woman staff member, who looked slightly older than us.

Nanase, with a slightly tense expression, answered “Two.” We sat down across from each other at a window-side table.

The sofa was fluffy and comfortable. I could live here, it’s that cozy.

“See? The person just now, she was beautiful, wasn’t she?”

Nanase said, seemingly entranced. Apparently, she was referring to the female staff member we had just encountered.

Glancing back to confirm, indeed, she was a beauty. The tear mole by her eyes was distinctive, and she had a certain sensuality about her.

She seemed strong-willed, and I felt she had an aura similar to Sudo’s. I think Nanase is more beautiful, but I guess it’s a matter of preference.

“I sometimes come to this cafe to study. It’s quiet and stylish, and I really like it here.”

Nanase handed me the menu, and as I unfolded it, I saw that a single cup of coffee cost six hundred yen.

To someone like me, who usually drinks cheap, carton-packed coffee, that price was enough to make my head spin.

“Look, this is it! The summer limited tropical parfait!”

When Nanase pointed to the price of the parfait, an involuntary “Wow” escaped my lips.

“That’s expensive. Can’t believe paying so much for just a parfait. I’ll stick with coffee.”

Seeing Nanase’s face fall at my words, I immediately regretted it. It seems I had inadvertently poured cold water on her pure excitement about the parfait.

“…Then, I’ll have the parfait. Excuse me, could I place my order?”

Nanase smiled forlornly and called over the server. Having missed the chance to apologize, I silently sipped my iced water.

After a while, my coffee and Nanase’s tropical parfait arrived. The parfait was topped with a lavish amount of colorful fruits.

“Wow, it’s cute!”

Nanase exclaimed with joy. It looked delicious, but I can’t quite understand the feeling of calling food “cute.” Girls seem to be creatures that incessantly call everything “cute.”

After taking a photo, Nanase carefully inserted her spoon into the parfait that seemed difficult to eat.

“Mmm, it’s really delicious!”

Nanase said, smiling happily. Having spent quite some time with her, I’ve realized that Nanase is incredibly expressive with her emotions.

Happiness and joy; her positive feelings are openly displayed. That’s a part of her that makes watching her never dull, a perfect slice of life in action.

“I’ve always been curious about this parfait but ordering one alone seemed to require a bit of courage. Ahh, I’m so glad I finally got to eat it.”

For her, this parfait must hold a value beyond 1,500 yen.

Since what people value varies from one person to another, I shouldn’t have said anything unnecessary.

I took a sip of my coffee without adding sugar or milk.

It had a deep aroma that was different from instant coffee.

Since it cost six hundred yen, I might as well savor it to the fullest.

Despite it being a weekday, the cafe was crowded, and the staff were bustling around hectically.

Nanase paused and looked at a “Staff Wanted” sign on the wall, murmuring to herself.

“They’re looking for part-timers here…”

I remember Nanase mentioning that she didn’t have any part-time jobs.

To me, part-time work is just a means to earn money, but for many students, it isn’t just that. Expanding one’s social circle beyond university could be a good thing for Nanase, who is aiming for a rosy university life.

“Why not give it a try? You might as well start a part-time job.”

At my words, Nanase’s eyes widened in surprise.

Then, she looked down unconfidently and poked at her parfait’s ice cream with her spoon.

“But… everyone there is so sparkly, and it might not suit me…”

“That’s the kind of sparkly crowd you’re aiming to get along with, isn’t it?”

“Plus, I’ve never worked part-time before… I wonder if I can handle customer service.”

“Customer service can be surprisingly manageable. The important thing is not to let your emotions get the best of you”

I’ve been working at a convenience store for two months now, and I think I’ve been doing pretty well, despite my lack of friendliness. I initially had a strong aversion to it, but it’s not so bad once you can handle your emotion. There aren’t many customers who expect excessive communication from convenience store clerks. Well, the quality of customer service at a cafe might be different from a convenience store.

“Maybe I’ll try practicing a bit.”

After clearing her throat a few times, Nanase straightened her back. She looked straight at me and smiled.

“Welcome!”

When I remained silent, Nanase looked embarrassed and urged me to say something with her lips pursed.

Snapping back to reality, I took a sip of my coffee to disguise my reaction.

“It’s okay, isn’t it?”

“Really? Do you think I can do it?”

“You’re suited for it… I think. Nanase, you’re hardworking, have a good memory, and you’re friendly. You’re the type who could probably do well in any workplace.”

After all, there would be plenty of customers who would pay six hundred yen for coffee if they could be served by a clerk like her.

What people value varies from person to person.

Nanase had been looking down in thought, but after a while, she seemed to make up her mind and looked up.

“Sagara-kun. I’ll try applying for the job! Thank you. I’ll give you this parfait as a thank you for pushing me forward. It’s delicious.”

Saying this, Nanase handed me a spoon. I took it impulsively and then wondered if this was what they call an indirect kiss. Am I the only one who cares about this because I’m a loner?

…Well, it doesn’t matter. She probably isn’t thinking about anything like that.

With a sense of resignation, I scooped up some peach, cream, and ice cream with the spoon and took a bite. It’s probably delicious, but I couldn’t really taste it.

“It’s delicious, isn’t it?”

Smiling, I handed the spoon back to Nanase. She took it with an unperturbed face and continued eating her parfait.

I took another sip of my coffee, trying to hide my agitation. Somehow, my body temperature had risen, and I regretted not ordering iced coffee instead.

And then, a few days later, Nanase, having finished her job interview, came to my room and gave a peace sign, saying, “I got the job!” If this helps her world expand and brings her one step closer to a rosy university life, then paying six hundred yen for that coffee was worth it.

Gion Matsuri is a summer tradition in Kyoto.

Once July arrives, floats called yamaboko line up along Shijo Street and Kawaramachi Street in the heart of Kyoto, and the sounds of Gion Bayashi echo through the air.

On July 17, there is an event called Yamaboko Junko, where the yamaboko parade through the streets of Kyoto. From the 14th to the 16th at night, the evenings are called Yoiyama, with many stalls lining the main streets. When students in Kyoto say they’re going to Gion Matsuri, they usually mean this Yoiyama.

…That’s what I heard from Tsugumi-chan, a Kyoto native girl.

Wearing a yukata and going to Gion Matsuri with a boyfriend seems to be a status symbol for Kyoto girls.

Perhaps because of this, hastily made couples seem to increase before Gion Matsuri, or so they say.

“Well, it’s super crowded, so there’s a high chance they’ll just end up fighting and breaking up.”

While Tsugumi-chan explained, I sipped my Frappuccino and nodded with a “hmm.” There’s no way for me to verify how accurate her story is. It might even be slightly prejudiced.

The first Sunday of July. The rainy season hasn’t officially ended, but it’s a clear, sunny day.

Tsugumi-chan had invited me out shopping. There are youth-oriented fashion buildings in Shijo Kawaramachi, Kyoto’s downtown area, and the pre-summer sales had already begun.

I fell in love with a blouse but gave up on buying it due to its not-so-cute price.

Shopping is definitely fun. It would be even more enjoyable with money.

After shopping, we entered a coffee shop near Sanjo Bridge. The limited-time peach Frappuccino had a refreshing aftertaste and was delicious. But, it was quite pricey. I’m sure if I told Sagara-kun the price, he’d make a face and say, “You could order a set meal at Kaifuutei and still get change.” I smiled a little at the thought.

But no matter what Sagara-kun thinks, a limited-time Frappuccino shared with a friend is priceless to me.

The Kamogawa River outside the shop reflects the relentless sunlight.

“Couples sitting at equal intervals along the riverside” is a typical scene at Kamogawa, but in this scorching heat, I think it looks hot before feeling any longing for it. Still, some couples sit closely together, showing impressive perseverance.

“Haru-chan, are you going to Gion Matsuri?”

“Uh… I don’t really have any plans…”

When I answered, Tsugumi-chan said, “I see,” and sipped her Frappuccino through a straw. To tell the truth, I’d like to go to Gion Matsuri.

I had never participated in local festivals before. I listened to my classmates planning to go to festivals with a face that said I wasn’t interested. I really wanted to wear a cute yukata with friends, take pictures, eat candied apples, and enjoy an extraordinary atmosphere.

If it were Sacchan, she’d casually suggest, “Let’s go together,” in situations like this.

Even three months after entering university, I still find it hard to invite friends out. The fear of being rejected crosses my mind, and I hesitate.

Even today’s shopping trip came about because Tsugumi-chan invited me.

But if I really want to pursue a rosy university life, perhaps I should be the one to proactively reach out. Sagara-kun would probably tell me to invite them myself if he were here.

Gathering my courage, I opened my mouth to speak.

“Hey, Tsugumi-chan…”

“Oh yeah. I wonder if Sacchan is going to Gion Matsuri with Hiroki-kun. I heard Hiroki-kun was planning to ask her out.”

My mustered courage was crushed, and I swallowed the words I was about to say. I sipped the almost-melted Frappuccino and asked,

“Why, Houjo-kun?”

“It seems like they’re on the countdown. They’re likely to start dating soon.”

I covered my mouth with one hand in surprise. Houjo-kun and Sacchan get along well, but they said it wasn’t like that. Was I the only one who wasn’t informed?

Seeing my shocked reaction, Tsugumi-chan hurriedly tried to reassure me.

“No, I didn’t hear it directly from Sacchan. It just seems like they’re getting along well.”

“Oh, I see…”

Sacchan and Houjo-kun dating. They do make a good match and would probably be a wonderful couple. But it’s a little shocking. I want to be happy for them, but it feels lonely.

If Sacchan is going to Gion Matsuri with Houjo-kun, maybe it would be a bother if I invited her. Tsugumi-chan and Nami-chan have boyfriends too, so maybe it’s better if I don’t say anything unnecessary…

“Hey, hey Haru-chan. Can we go back to that store later? I can’t forget the dress I didn’t end up buying.”

“Yeah, sure! I want to take another look at that blouse too!”

After going back to the fashion building with Tsugumi-chan, I ended up buying the cute blouse with the not-so-cute price.

For a while, I’ll follow Sagara-kun’s lead and live frugally, deciding to increase my shifts at my part-time job.

There is a chain convenience store where I work part-time near Shijo Shinmachi, the center of the Gion Festival.

Normally, I’m stationed at a store a bit further away, but today I was called in for backup.

“You can enjoy the atmosphere of the Gion Matsuri; it’ll be fun. Do your best, Sagara-kun!”

That’s what the manager said as she sent me off, but honestly, I’m not enjoying it at all.

The Yoiyama of Gion Matsuri was three times more crowded than I had imagined.

Between the floats and food stalls, a sea of people writhed and squirmed. It was so packed that walking properly seemed impossible.

Just looking at it was suffocating. Amidst the sounds of the Gion Bayashi, the voice of a girl rings out, “How about some chimaki?”

Normally bustling with cars, Karasuma Street has now become a pedestrian paradise.

Drenched in sweat, I stood in front of a stall right outside the convenience store. Fried chicken and bottled drinks were set at prices significantly higher than usual.

It’s a cheeky strategy to take full advantage of the Gion Matsuri.

It’s so hot. Less than an hour has gone by, and I’m already yearning for the cool, air-conditioned inside of the store.

I even endure the heat in my own room without turning on the AC, and yet, here I am suffering the heat at my part-time job too.

“Excuse me. I’d like to buy this.”

At that moment, a woman wearing a red yukata approached to buy a drink.

I took the thousand-yen bill, handed over the change and the bottled drink. Mustered the bare minimum of friendliness, I bowed my head and said, “Thank you very much.”

The woman smiled and returned to the person who seemed to be her friend. As I watched her nape peeking out from her yukata collar, I thought it wasn’t too bad.

I told Nanase otherwise, but the truth is, I quite like yukatas.

They have this indescribable sexiness despite the minimal exposure. Women in yukatas appear thirty percent more beautiful, I believe.

I have no intention of diving into the crowd, nor do I plan to spend money on ridiculously overpriced night stalls, and I was getting a bit tired of the ever-present Gion Bayashi, but that doesn’t mean I dislike the Gion Matsuri itself. Being able to see many women in yukatas is quite a pleasure.

It’s now half-past nine. I wonder if Nanase is walking around somewhere in her yukata at this moment.

──I’m looking forward to Gion Matsuri. Houjo-kun and Kinami-kun are coming too.

That’s what Nanase said when we met at university yesterday.

By the way, it seemed like Kinami took a liking to her. I bet he’s overjoyed seeing Nanase in a yukata. A feeling of irritation mixed with envy wells up inside me.

Just then, my smartphone in my pocket vibrated. Sneaking a peek at the display, I saw a LINE message notification. It’s from Houjo.

[“Good work.”]

[“I’ll share a photo of Nanase in her yukata with you!”]

Then came a notification: [“A photo has been sent.”].

What is he thinking? Sending me that photo, does he expect me to be happy? If so, that’s a huge misunderstanding.

But as if driven by something, I opened the LINE app. It’s not that I’m interested, but now that it’s been sent, I have to check it.

I opened the chat room with Houjo, and below the message, there should have been a photo of Nanase in her yukata──but there wasn’t.

No matter how long I waited, it just kept loading.

…Come on, my smartphone, work harder!

Apparently, due to the dense crowd, the signal was very poor. The budget carrier I’ve contracted with is known for slower data speeds, and my phone model is a few generations old. Damn, maybe it’s time for an upgrade…

Feeling let down, I slumped my shoulders. That’s when someone patted me on the back.

“Sagara-kun, good work.”

“Ah, good work to you too.”

It was my senior at work, Kazuha Itogawa.

She’s a third-year student in the Faculty of Economics at the same university as me. A beautiful woman with an active vibe and short bob cut, she’s kind and takes good care of others.

Normally, she works at the same store as me, but it seems she was also called in for reinforcement today.

“You can take a one-hour break, okay? Make sure you hydrate since it’s hot.”

Today’s shift is a long eight-hour haul, with a one-hour break in between. Relieved that I could finally escape the heat, I let out a sigh of relief.

“By the way, Sagara-kun, you’re not from Kyoto, right? Don’t you want to enjoy the Gion Matsuri?”

“…No, I’m good.”

Just looking at this crowd is enough to make me feel full. I was about to head back inside to the store’s backyard to rest when a woman in a yukata caught my eye.

I couldn’t see her face, but I thought she looked beautiful. More than anything, she had good posture. Her yukata was dark blue with white morning glory patterns, and her red obi was neatly tied around her waist. Her chestnut hair was tied up, with some strands loosely waved. The nape of her neck peeking out from her collar was delicate and shockingly white.

As I absentmindedly stared, the woman slowly turned towards me.

Our eyes met, and she lifted her red lips into a smile.

Standing there was an exceptionally beautiful woman, not betraying any expectations.

“Ah, Sagara-kun!”

Startled by my name being called, I came to my senses. She was waving at me with a beaming smile.

“I was surprised. What are you doing in a place like this?”

The yukata beauty was none other than Nanase. Clack-clacking her geta, she hurried over to me. Tilting her head slightly, the floral ornament in her hair swayed.

“…Ah… I’m working.”

I managed to reply at last. Noticing my voice was hoarse, I swallowed. My throat had become parched without me realizing it.

“…What about you? What are you doing here alone?”

I couldn’t see Sudo or Houjo around her. Nanase smiled with her eyebrows lowered.

“That’s the thing, I got separated. I just got in touch with them, so I’m about to meet up. I think they said they’re near the Naginataboko float.”

“Oh, I see…”

“What’s this? She’s super pretty. Is she, by any chance, your girlfriend, Sagara-kun?”

At that moment, Itogawa-san teased me, nudging me with her elbow.

I hastily denied, “No, she’s not.”

“We’re in the same seminar at university.”

Nanase responded with a smile, not seeming to take any offense. Itogawa-san’s interpretation of our relationship was ambiguous, with a teasing grin on her face.

“Sagara-kun. You’re about to take a break, so why don’t you go enjoy the festival with her for a bit?”

“Really? If you don’t mind, let’s go together for a little while!”

Nanase said cheerfully, clasping her hands in front of her chest.

That’s out of the question. Walking around with Nanase in her yukata would definitely lead to misunderstandings if someone saw us.

I was about to refuse when Itogawa-san chimed in.

“Besides, it’s dangerous for such a cute girl to walk alone; she might get picked up by some random guy. Don’t take Gion Matsuri lightly.”

“…Uh.”

That might indeed be a possibility. In fact, there was a pair of men not too far away, sneakily glancing at Nanase.

…It seems I won’t be able to rest in a cool place just yet.

“…My break is just an hour. I’ll go with you to where Sudo and the others are and then come back.”

Nanase’s face lit up, and she nodded with a “Yeah!” I steeled myself and dove into the crowd with her.

360° degrees, everywhere I look it’s people, people, and people.

The walls of people make it difficult to walk straight. It’s already sweltering, and the heat makes it hard to breathe.

From Shinmachi Street, where the convenience store is located, to where Sudo and the others are, it would normally take less than ten minutes.

But with this crowd, it’s hard to move forward as I want to.

I could feel Nanase, walking half a step behind me, grabbing the hem of my T-shirt tightly. For a moment, my heart skipped a beat. When I turned around, she looked at me with an apologetic face.

“Sorry. I don’t want to get lost again…”

“…Well, it’s okay.”

Nanase’s forehead was slightly sweaty, and her cheeks were redder than usual, probably because of the heat. A sigh escaped from her slightly parted lips. I felt strangely affected.

Noticing my gaze, Nanase started fiddling with her bangs uncomfortably.

“Is, is my face okay? Is my makeup smeared? Are my eyelashes still on?”

“…You look the same as always.”

As if reassuring myself, I responded. Nanase murmured with relief, “That’s good.”

“Anyway, I never thought it would be this crowded… Ah! I wanted to try a candied apple! Sagara-kun, can I buy one?”

Nanase raised her voice excitedly, pulling on the hem of my shirt. Reluctantly, I followed her to the candied apple stall.

“One, please.”

“Here ya go, thank you!”

The candied apple stuck on a chopstick cost five hundred yen.

It was a rip-off price, I thought, but seeing Nanase’s eyes sparkle as she received the candied apple, I held back any unnecessary comments.

A giant float could be seen in the distance, illuminated by the festival lights, shrouded in an extraordinary atmosphere.

The girl walking next to me still held onto the hem of my shirt with one hand.

Just that alone made me feel so restless – maybe because she was dressed differently than usual.

“I’ve never had a candied apple before. I always thought it looked beautiful… so this is what it tastes like.”

Nanase muttered to herself. It was a small voice, almost drowned out by the noise of the festival.

“I never went to a local summer festival in my hometown. I didn’t have friends.”

“…Huh.”

“I’m glad I got to wear a yukata and come to the festival with friends. I guess it was good that I tried hard. Thank you, Sagara-kun.”

I said nothing. Surely Nanase wasn’t expecting a reply.

By the time Nanase finished her candied apple, we had arrived at the Naginataboko float.

A woman in a bright light blue yukata was waving wildly; it must be Sudo. Next to her was Houjo. It was good that they managed to meet up safely. With this, my role was completed.

“I’m going back to work now. See ya.”

“Ah, Sagara-kun… wait, wait a minute.”

“What is it?”

“Um, well, you see. I, my…”

Just as Nanase started to speak,

“Nanase! Finally found you!”

A male voice rang out from not too far away. Parting the crowd, Kinami rushed over. Nanase’s face tightened slightly, and she gripped my shirt harder.

“…What’s wrong?”

She mumbled a vague “Uh, um…” before I could press her.

“Do you not like Kinami by any chance?”

Nanase gave a wry smile and nodded slightly.

Well, I had somewhat guessed it. It’s unfortunate for Kinami who seemed to have feelings for her, but he was uncooperative even in group work, so it’s his own doing.

“Kyah!”

Before Kinami could reach us, a tall man bumped into Nanase.

Before I could ask if she was okay──Nanase suddenly flung herself into my chest. The pure white nape of her neck was right before my eyes, and my heart felt like it was flipping over.

“Nanase.”

The girl with her face buried in my chest smelled unbelievably good despite being sweaty. My hands, with nowhere to go, roamed aimlessly in the air.

“What happened?”

“My eyelashes.”

“Huh?”

“My false eyelashes fell off…”

Nanase said in a fading voice. It seemed that the fake eyelashes glued to her eyelids had come off when she bumped into the tall man. I let out an exasperated sigh.

“Who cares about some eyelashes…”

“No, I can’t! It makes a huge difference with or without them! What should I do? I absolutely can’t let anyone see me like this.”

Nanase’s shoulders were shaking. I couldn’t understand it, but the presence or absence of lashes on her eyelids seemed to be a very serious and important matter for her.

While I was wondering what to do, Kinami had arrived right in front of us. He saw Nanase, who seemed to be hugging me (from his perspective), and furrowed his brows suspiciously.

“What’s going on? I thought who’s that with Nanase…”

“…We just happened to meet here.”

“Hmm. What’s up with Nanase?”

“…She seems to be feeling unwell.”

I said, trying to sidestep the issue, and Kinami expressed concern with, “Really?”

“Nanase, are you okay? Should we go somewhere to rest?”

Kinami reached out to Nanase, who still had her face buried in my chest. Nanase’s shoulders jolted, and the hand gripping my shirt trembled.

Before Kinami’s intrusive hand could touch her, I forcefully knocked it away.

“Huh? What are you doing?”

Kinami was obviously annoyed by my sudden action. He glared at me with dissatisfaction, but for Nanase’s sake, I couldn’t back down.

“…I’ll take her. That’s okay, right, Nanase?”

Nanase nodded silently, over and over. As we walked away, I could hear a sullen “What’s with him” from behind. I might have made an unnecessary enemy, but it couldn’t be helped.

Sagara-kun took me to the toilet at Shijo Subway Station. I fixed my false eyelashes firmly, touched up my foundation, and reapplied my blush and lipstick.

Finally, with a decent face, I let out a sigh of relief.
