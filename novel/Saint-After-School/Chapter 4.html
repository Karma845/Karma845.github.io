Seira pointed to a ramen restaurant that claimed to specialize in tonkotsu ramen.

“Should be fine. There are no customers lined up.”

“It’s decided then.”

The two passed through the store’s curtain with great anticipation.


“—Here, enjoy!”

In contrast to the bustle of the city, the interior of the restaurant was deserted, and the ramen was delivered in the blink of an eye after they placed their order.

Yamato involuntarily gulped down the thick aroma of tonkotsu ramen with shiny thin noodles, topped with green onions, wood ear mushroom, boiled egg, and roasted pork.

“”Itadakimasu.””

After that, they quickly take a sip of the soup.

“”───!””

The two looked at each other and nodded excitedly.

As soon as they took a mouthful of the soup, the aroma and richness of the thick tonkotsu filled their mouths, and they felt the delicious taste running through their bodies.

Next, he took a bite of the noodles.

“”Mm-hm!””

The two nodded to each other again.

This was what ramen tasted like after a day’s work… For the first time in their lives, both Yamato and Seira felt as if all of today’s fatigue had been swept away by the exquisite sensation they had just experienced.

After that, they couldn’t stop.

Chewing on the roasted pork with the firm, thin noodles, sometimes with wood ear mushrooms, and sometimes with boiled egg, they ate and savored the taste, refreshed their body with water, and went back to the ramen again.

Yamato thought the taste was almost addictive. Combined with a feeling of hunger and fatigue, he had the strange sensation that all of this had been converted into a sense of accomplishment at the end of the workday.

While Yamato was eating, Seira’s hand spontaneously went to the jar of garlic.

However, she must have noticed Yamato’s gaze. She stopped her hand and pulled it away. Then, as if to cover up her uncontrollable urge, she took a sip of her soup.

After all, Seira was probably still concerned about the peculiar smell of garlic. It was an unusual problem for Seira, but Yamato could not bear to see her put up with it.

Hence, he said to her,

“It’s okay now, you don’t have to put up with it.”

When hearing that, Seira was troubled and responded with a “gnu.”

Yamato thought that he should press her further and continued in a calm tone.

“Eating what you like, as you like, shouldn’t be a bad thing. Isn’t it?”

Then, Seira, who seemed to have given up on the idea, turned to Yamato.

“Then, Yamato put garlic in it too. That way, even if it smells a little, I think it can be covered.”

“O-Oh.”

Her unexpectedly cute request made Yamato tensed up, but he quickly complied.

…Well, he was not certain if that could be covered or not.

The garlic in this restaurant also seemed to be of the self crushing type, and in turn, the garlic was crushed with a special tool to mush it down before being thrown into the ramen.

Then, Seira took a bite of the newly garlic flavored ramen.

“Nnn~~!”

Seira was speechless and fainted with happiness.

Yamato followed her lead and took a big gulp of his ramen.

“Oooh…”

It was good. And it was thick. It was intense. The combination of the tonkotsu ramen and garlic was so good together that it could even be called devilish.

The Saint who was fascinated by the devil ate at such a rapid pace that she lifted up the whole bowl and drank the soup in one sitting. Even the waiter was taken aback by this.

“—Phew. Thanks for the food.”

She finished the meal. It was a magnificent meal.

Yamato was fascinated by Seira’s straightforwardness, but then, realizing that she was ahead of him again, he hurriedly shoveled the rest of the soup into his mouth.

As expected, Yamato was reluctant to drink all the soup, but his male pride would not allow him to not do so.

Yamato soon finished his meal too, and the waiter called out, “Thank you for finishing!”

Thus, the “Seira eating ramen with garlic” moment that Yamato had been waiting for so long returned.

As soon as he left the restaurant, Seira immediately covered her mouth with both hands.

It seemed that she was still bothered by it. She was looking somewhat downcast, and there was no sign of her earlier vigorous appearance.

“…The ramen here is delicious.”

“Yes.”

“For all that, there weren’t many customers. I wonder why.”

“How would I know?”

“You see, there’s a long line of people waiting for ramen over there. Maybe it’s a location thing.”

“…”

Finally, she didn’t even answer me.

Since Yamato felt a little lonely at this point, he decided to speak his true feelings.

“I was happy. Shirase hasn’t put garlic in your food for a long time.”

“…”

Even without a reply from Seira, Yamato continued.

“It’s cool to see Shirase eat so rapidly. And I simply admire the way you eat what you want to eat, as if by instinct. Of course, I mean that in a good way!”

“…”

There was still no response, but Yamato nevertheless spoke his mind.

“I know I like it, the way Shirase eats ramen with garlic.”

“…”

After all, there was still no response from Seira, but she stopped walking.

“Shirase?”

Yamato, curious, stopped and turned around.

Then Seira looked up, red to the ears, and glared at him.

“…Dummy.”

She muttered to Yamato’s face.


During the train ride home, they were completely silent.

This was because Yamato felt strangely embarrassed by their earlier exchange, and since Seira did not seem to be interested in talking at all, they fell into silence.

Moreover, because Seira, who was concerned about the smell of garlic, sat far from Yamato, the situation remained difficult for them to talk.

Having given up on trying to resolve the situation for the time being, Yamato decided to check out the reviews of the ramen restaurant he had just entered.

Yamato thought that the restaurant would be highly rated, but his prediction turned out to be wrong.

The reviews were all over the place, with some describing it as “ordinary,” “mediocre taste,” “just another ramen shop,” and still others calling it “average.” It was a ramen store that could be found anywhere.

So why did Yamato find it so delicious?

There are several possible reasons, but the biggest one was that he ate it after his part-time job when he was tired and hungry.

However, even though he understood the theory, Yamato was not satisfied with his feelings, so he registered on a review site and wrote a five-star rating, saying, “It was the most delicious ramen I’ve had. I will probably never forget the taste. I recommend it with garlic.”

“Phew.”

Immediately after that, Seira, who was sitting opposite Yamato, let out a laugh.

Perhaps she was looking at something interesting on her phone, Yamato thought. He hoped she would be in a better mood when they got off the train.


However, there was no conversation after getting off the train.

As soon as they passed through the ticket gate, Seira began to walk ahead of Yamato.

(Maybe I stepped on a terrible land mine…?)

While Yamato was worried, they came to the usual fork in the road.

“Hey, Shirase.”

When he called out to Seira, who was walking ahead of him, she stopped dead in her tracks.

“I’m sorry if I offended you. So, would you stop being mad?”

“…I’m not mad at you.”

“B—”

“But I’m kind of embarrassed.”

Seira looked back at him, her cheeks flushed red.

Yamato opened his mouth while admiring the sight.

“I-I don’t really care about the smell.”

“Yeah, okay. I know. So, sorry about today.”

When she apologized, Yamato was at a loss as to how to react.

Seeing Yamato fidgeting, Seira nodded to herself.

“I’ll make the next outing by phone or message. It’s already late today.”

“R-Right! I agree!”

Yamato’s uneasiness caused him to swallow his words, but he calmed himself down and continued.

“I’m really grateful to you for going along with my part-time job today. It was really reassuring and fun. ─ Well, good night then.”

Yamato then turned his back to her.

“Hey.”

“I don’t think I’ll ever forget the taste of today’s ramen too. ─ Then, good night.”

After a small wave with her hand, Seira left with a satisfied look on her face.

As she left, Yamato thought about the word “too” Seira had used.

In other words, she had read the review that Yamato had just posted.

“…Oh, God, that’s so embarrassing.”

Understanding this, Yamato blushed and muttered.

Looking off Seira’s back, who was now skipping along in a good mood, Yamato smiled with a sigh.
