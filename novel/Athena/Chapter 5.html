Chapter 5: What Red Means to You

I think it was over ten years ago.

“Suika, do you know what the cutest creature in this world is?”

After the end of practice in our home dojo, my mother suddenly asked me this question.

“…A cat?”

“Well, cats are cute, indeed. But that’s incorrect.”

My mother shook her head at my answer and said with an incredibly serene smile,

“The cutest creature in the world is a man.”

“…Why?”

“Oh, don’t you think so, Suika?”

“I don’t think so… Because their bodies are big, and they’re strong…”

Adult men have an imposing presence. Even boys my age, with their loud voices and high spirits, were intimidating to me, who was just about to enter or just entered elementary school.

“You see, Suika, men have big bodies and strong strength for a reason. Unlike us women, they don’t have demons inside their hearts.”

“…?”

“Since they don’t have demons inside, they need to be big and strong… otherwise, they couldn’t survive. It’s probably a handicap given by God… That’s why they are adorable.”

At the time, I couldn’t understand what my mother was saying at all. I couldn’t even think if that was the case.

“…Do I also have a demon inside me?”

The scary men don’t have demons, but I, a weak woman, do. I couldn’t quite digest that.

“Yes, you do. Because you’re a woman.”

My mother and I were sitting face-to-face on the dojo floor, sitting properly in seiza. My mother, looking straight at me, said,

“Suika, you’ll come to understand one day. That you have a demon inside you. A greedy demon that wants to protect something precious as well as consume it.”

My mother, with her back straight and in a beautiful posture as she spoke, suggested that even inside such a beautiful woman like her, there was a demon.

“The demons inside the women of the Ado family are particularly terrible… When you find something more precious than your own life, I wonder what you will do.”

◆◇◆

It was all a mystery.

For some reason, the meeting place was a park downtown, and to begin with, the fact that person called me was incomprehensible.

But… it was something I couldn’t just ignore.

“There’s something I need to talk to you about regarding Miyashiro-kun.”

Given that statement, I had no choice but to go.

“…Okay, this is it.”

I arrived at the specified park. For the record, I ran here from my house, which took about fifteen minutes.

Entering the park and looking around, I saw several children playing energetically on the lawn under the blazing sun and…

A familiar person was sitting alone on a bench in the shade of a tree.

As she noticed me, she came out of the shade and approached.

“Hello, Ado-san.”

“Sorry to keep you waiting, Kujo-senpai. May I ask what you need from me straight away?”

As I asked with a smile, she—Kujo-senpai also smiled thinly. Her beautiful posture sent shivers down my spine. She didn’t seem human.

“Sure, quick to the point. Do you recognize this?”

“Eh, …that”

Seeing what was in her hand, my eyes widened.

It was Kuuya’s smartphone. The case on it was one we had picked out together. There was no mistaking it.

“It was lying near the roadside… Look, where the vending machine is. Around that area.”

“…I see.”

Ah, so that’s why she called me. To have me deliver it to Kuuya’s house.

─I didn’t say that, though. Because there were two things I wanted to ask first.

“How did you know my phone number, Senpai?”

“That must be the same answer to the next question you’ll ask me.”

Her response was odd, but I went along with it and asked another question.

“Did you just happen to find Kuuya’s smartphone?”

“I’m a bit of an oddball, you see.”

Straightforwardly, she said. As if stating something obvious.

“There’s a bit of a risk for me in telling this story. So, let me confirm something on my end first.”

“…What is it?”

“Do you know what Miyashiro-kun was doing today? Did he have any plans to go out somewhere that you heard about? Is there any way to contact him now?”

She fired off a series of focused questions. Somehow, I thought, ah, she seems like a science person.

Of course, most of my mind wasn’t occupied with such trivial thoughts.

“…I didn’t hear about any plans for today. Nor about any plans to go out… If”

As I spoke the following words, I felt disgusted with myself.

“If he had plans to go far, he almost always tells me.”

“…Hmm, I see. So at least we can rule out a planned outing.”

“If Kuuya is not reachable now… I’ll try calling his home.”

Without his smartphone, the next option was the home phone.

The call rang several times but eventually switched to the answering machine. Kuuya usually answers the phone if he’s at home.

“He’s not answering, …Hmm… So, it seems…”

“Um, could we have a more detailed conversation, please?”

From my perspective, Kuuya might have just dropped his smartphone while out on a short errand nearby—the expected situation.

“Do you have any idea? …Though I hate to think about it, something serious might be happening.”

“Yes. …Well, yes, that’s right.”

This was the best course of action.

Kujo-senpai muttered quietly, then said,

“I’m a stalker of Miyashiro-kun.”

“…What?”

“Oh, but I’m not the type who physically follows him around. The goal of a stalker is to gather information, right? So, I track him electronically, not physically.”

“…What? I mean, wait, um”

I couldn’t catch up. …What?

“I’ll disclose the detailed methods, but I’ve been extracting various information from his smartphone and smartwatch daily. GPS data from his smartphone is one of those. That’s how I knew his smartphone was dropped here.”

“…What…”

“Unfortunately, his smartwatch isn’t a cellular model that’s always connected to the net, so now that he’s dropped his smartphone, I can’t extract GPS or other data… Ah, right, and the data I got from his smartwatch around noon today is crucial.”

“…”

Though my brain still hadn’t caught up with the situation, I understood this was something I needed to hear.

“The heart rate data from the smartwatch can give us some idea about the state of the autonomic nervous system. Roughly speaking, it can tell us the “Stress Value.”

“The value was incredibly high around noon. And then, according to the location data, he left his house and walked into the city… probably on foot, based on the speed… and likely dropped his smartphone along the way. That’s all I can tell from the data I have.”

“…”

If.

If I were to take everything she said at face value, it could indeed indicate that something might have happened.

“…So”

“Yes, that’s why I called you. I thought you might know something. …Ah, you understand why I knew your phone number without me having to explain, right?”

If she’s extracting data from Kuuya’s smartphone, getting my number, which would be in his contact list, would be trivial.

“I believe something not so peaceful might be happening.”

“…If something not so peaceful is happening, it’s already happening right in front of me. Because there’s a woman here claiming to be Kuuya’s stalker.”

“That’s true too.”

…What should I do?

Kuuya comes first. Thinking of him, what should I do now─

“Ah, but you know, Ado-san. If there’s really nothing you can do right now, that’s fine too.”

“…?”

“Sorry for suddenly bringing this up. It’s inevitable, isn’t it? Not everyone can immediately provide useful information in situations like this.”

Kujo-senpai said with full confidence.

“Or rather, there’s no one who can provide information I don’t already know.”

I don’t have eyes like Kuuya’s. But something so obvious, there’s no way I could mistake it.

“If it’s about him…”

Kujo-senpai clearly stated, with the face of a woman talking about her own belonging.

“I’m the one who knows the most about him in the world.”

“How long was the smartphone lying on the roadside?”

“About two and a half hours ago, I suppose.”

“A person’s walking speed is about four kilometers per hour; Kuuya is a bit slower. Even if he started running at some point, considering his stamina, he couldn’t have kept it up for long. Subtracting that, and assuming he didn’t use any form of transportation, Kuuya should be within a ten-kilometer radius from here.”

“I agree. Here, take a look.”

Kujo-senpai took a tablet out of her bag and showed it to me. A map was displayed on it, with a circle drawn around our current location.

It was a ten-kilometer radius circle. This person had made the same estimate as I had.

“The problem is whether he took some form of transportation… There are no stations within this range, so it must be a bus or taxi. If Miyashiro-kun took one of those,”

“Taking a taxi is 100% impossible.”

“…Why?”

Asked in return, the answer was simple.

“Because I wouldn’t know about it.”

“If Kuuya had taken a taxi, especially if something about his condition seemed odd, there wouldn’t be a single driver in this town who wouldn’t contact me.”

“…What are you saying?”

“Regarding the buses, the stops within this range are these and these and these… Within the last two and a half hours?”

Considering the harsh temperatures and sunlight of this season, especially around the hot midday, many people would opt for buses to avoid walking.

If Kuuya was among them…

“Hmm…”

My phone rang. The caller was a familiar woman who runs a bakery with her husband in the shopping district.

“Hello, Suika speaking!”

“Suika-chan, long time no see~! Thanks for coming to the store the other day~”

“No, no, your new creation was delicious!”

“Right~! It’s my masterpiece, after all~! But sorry, let’s put that aside.”

The change in topic made me instantly grasp why the woman had contacted me. Because it was as I expected.

The timing of this call was too perfect to be mere coincidence.

“You see, just before noon today… around eleven, I guess, I took the bus, from Fujimi to Toka Mori. Then, I saw Kuuya-kun on the bus. It’s rare for him to use this route, so when I happened to catch his face, he looked really pale…”

“Whaaat…!”

“I wondered what happened… I thought maybe he was going to the hospital, but he got off at Rinkouzura, and there aren’t any hospitals around there… So I was wondering what happened. That’s why I thought I should at least let you know.”

“Thank you very much, I’ve been unable to contact Kuuya right now… It seems like Kuuya left his smartphone at home…”

Considering the situation was not yet confirmed, it wouldn’t be right to unnecessarily escalate things. Kuuya might end up troubled later.

Even though I knew it was wrong, I made a seemingly plausible conjecture.

“Ah! I understand, maybe he’s panicking, thinking he dropped his smartphone, and is searching for it.”

“Ah~, I see! So that’s it! But then he might still be looking for it now… It’s so hot outside, I’m worried.”

“I’ll go to the bus stop where Kuuya got off and hand it to him! Thank you very much!”

“No problem, see you~. Come to the store with him again~”

“Yes!” I replied as I hung up the phone and touched Kujo-senpai’s tablet.

“About an hour ago, Kuuya got off at a bus stop called Rinkouzura. That area doesn’t have stations or other bus stops nearby, so Kuuya’s current location is expected to be within a four-kilometer radius from there.”

“…Hey, your character was like how you were on the phone just now, right? But now you are… No, never mind that. Ado-san, you are,”

“In this town, the Ado family is somewhat well-known.”

I interupted before Kujo-senpai could continue.

“I’ve been making friends with many people since I was young, using that to my advantage. Not just in the town, but at school too. I made sure people remembered my face, made friends with them… and let them know what’s important to me.”

Conveniently, though I certainly wouldn’t say it’s a good thing, the fact that he was physically weak also worked positively in this situation.

No, saying it’s a coincidence would be wrong.

I utilized the fact that there are people who think, “He’s physically weak, so I’m worried about him.”

“I won’t say it’s as much as a mountain, but I receive a considerable number of contacts every day. Mixed in with various small talks, naturally, there are messages like, ‘By the way, I saw Kuuya-kun around this place at this time.'”

This is the first time I’m revealing this to someone else. But now, it’s necessary.

“It’s not just sightings. Rumors and various stories also reach me.”

I stated that clearly.

“As long as Kuuya is in a place where people can see him, he’s under my surveillance.”

By the hands of the townspeople, none of whom think they’re actually keeping watch.

“…You”

“I’ve intentionally created this situation, of course… To keep track of as much about Kuuya as possible, for that reason.”

A battle of oddballs, I wonder who comes out on top.

Kujo-senpai didn’t respond to my words but instead asked a question.

“…I want to ask. Hey, you once brought up a discussion during the sports festival committee meeting about changing the mixed-gender setup to separate genders, right? What about that?”

“Putting aside how you, who weren’t supposed to be there, know about its outcome… I heard from several sources that there were girls who liked Kuuya.”

“When you entered the library while I was studying there with Miyashiro-kun, what was that about?”

“It couldn’t possibly be a coincidence, could it?”

I had been checking on this person since around April. Despite being rumored to dislike people, she seemed to talk only with Kuuya, who sat next to her.

“A library committee member contacted me saying, ‘She’s unusually in the library… and she’s with a pretty senpai.'”

The people who contact me don’t necessarily think I’m dating Kuuya, but at the very least, they recognize that I have special feelings for him.

Kujo-senpai sighed deeply.

“I regret this. It’s highly regrettable… Women’s intuition is right, isn’t it?”

“What kind of guess?”

“That you and I are similar.”

Kujo-senpai muttered and sighed again. To her, I said,

“What you’re doing is very wrong. It’s a crime, and having someone like you sitting next to Kuuya is truly the worst.”

“You’re blunt.”

“Yes. But I’m equally, if not more, terrible. I’m not decent, not pretty. I’m scum, using people’s kindness for surveillance and manipulation behind the scenes.”

I extended my right hand to the person in front of me.

“Scum should work together for now. Let’s use each other effectively.”

“I intended that from the start. I’m the one who called you out, after all.”

“Ah, but please correct one thing.”

Holding her hand, I looked straight into her eyes, smiling as I said,

“When it comes to knowing the most about Kuuya in the world, that would be me.”

“Ah?”

“What is it?”

A gaze filled with hostility and malice flew towards me, and I immediately returned the same.

Under the bright sunshine, in a peaceful park, two good-for-nothing women were glaring at each other.

…The children who had been playing looked at us with frightened eyes. I’m sorry, we’ll move soon…

For now, the two of us headed to a nearby bus stop. The bus from here stops at the place where Kuuya got off.

“There’s something very important I haven’t asked you yet.”

Kujo-senpai posed that question to me.

“In the end, you do love Miyashiro-kun, don’t you?”

I think.

Ah, right. Except for my family, this is the first time I’m clearly putting it into words.

“I love him, more than anyone else.”

The summer’s heat was pouring down from above. Along with the piercing heat and pain, the sensation of voicing those words felt etched into me.

“…So. Well, I’m still superior.”

“What? In terms of years and seasons, we’re different.”

“All those years, and you’re still just friends in the end? Is that something to be proud of?”

“Coming from someone who can’t even be friends, even just sitting next to him, I can’t say much.”

“…”

“…”

While waiting for the bus, our conversation was somewhere between assessing the situation and provoking each other.

“…By the way, Miyashiro-kun can predict how other feels, right? Considering you’re always together, he must be completely aware of how you feel, isn’t he?”

“No, that’s impossible. Kuuya sees me only as a very caring childhood friend… Otherwise, we couldn’t have stayed so close, for so long.”

“…What do you mean?”

“…”

“Anyway, what you’re doing is strange, isn’t it? Despite being so obsessed with Miyashiro-kun, you never try to move beyond the childhood friend position… Honestly, at first, I thought you two were dating.”

With a calm tone, never looking directly at me, Kujo-senpai continued.

“You seemed perfect for each other, from any perspective. It was clear you understood each other. That’s why I don’t understand your risk management. If you have such a relationship, it seems to me the realistic risk to be wary of is not being rejected, but being stolen by another girl while you’re dawdling.”

I hesitated whether I should explain the situation to this person. Kuuya’s past isn’t something I should discuss with others based on my own judgment.

But the current situation might indeed be connected to that story… The slightly stiff expression on Kuuya’s face yesterday flickered in my mind, igniting a sense of urgency within me.

…This person next to me took the risk of her crimes being exposed to act in this manner. In that regard, I think she should be trusted, and perhaps I should disclose the information that needs to be disclosed.

─I’m sorry for doing this on my own.

While apologizing to Kuuya in my heart, I began to speak.

“It’s going to be a bit of a long story.”

“The bus will take a while to come anyway.”

“Right, then──”

As I spoke to her, I dove deep.

Sinking my consciousness into my own past.

I was still young, about a second-grader in elementary school.

“Thank you very much.”

Whenever I left the dojo after giving thanks to my mother, I was always staggering.

The Ado family’s training was rigorous. Striking, throwing, finishing moves, swords, spears, bows—mastering them all was the mark of a Ado woman. The training was harsh, to say the least.

However, frankly speaking, I didn’t like fighting or anything like that at all. There was no joy in becoming stronger for me.

Why do I have to do this?

That’s what I thought all the time back then, but still, I felt a sense of duty because “I am a child of this house.” If I was born into the Ado family, then it was something I should do, and I wanted to meet my mother and grandmother’s expectations.

My heart was caught in a vice of emotions, and my body was worn out from fatigue.

There was a special place of solace for me.

Near our house, on a small hill, there was a single large tree. Only when I was there, I could forget about things I had to do or didn’t want to do.

That tree was incredibly certain in its existence. It felt like it would surely always be there, a tree that existed long before I was born and would continue to exist long after I was gone.

A certain something that never wavered. In hindsight, I must have found comfort there because I had nothing like that in my life at the time.

I often literally leaned on it and fell asleep. As a child, that’s how I endured the daily training.

A fluttering, unstable spirit without something to anchor on. Easily swayed by even the slightest thing, losing sight of where to go, a timid and fragile existence. That was me at the time.

“Suika~!”

“…Kuuya-kun.”

He often came to call for me, a boy one year older living nearby.

“It’s dinner time! They say it’s some really good meat!”

He was incredibly bright, unlike me. His large smile could be recognized from a hundred meters away, his trademark.

Our fathers had been friends since the old days, and our families were close, often dining together.

“Let’s go back!”

“…Yeah.”

He was someone who, without fail, helped and pulled me along, shy and poor at expressing my own thoughts and feelings.

Always the center of attention with his sunny disposition, and perhaps because of his keen observation skills, he quickly sensed what I wanted to say and took care of everything.

I was very grateful, admiring, and thought he was amazing.

There might have been a vague sense of affection, but it wasn’t to the extent that I was consciously aware of it as a romantic feeling.

“Did you draw today too, Kuuya-kun?”

“Yeah!”

“I see…”

In addition, at that time, I harbored another feeling towards him.

That was “envy.”

Spending time calmly drawing pictures inside the house or in the garden. While he was doing that, I was enduring harsh training in the dojo, which inevitably made me think, “I wish I could do that too.”

It seemed free from pain and suffering.

…That was the world surrounding me, and a significant upheaval happened in the summer of that year.

“Ah…”

“Sorry, Suika. It just has to be done.”

I clearly remember my grandfather, now passed away, apologizing to me at the time.

Due to some land readjustment or something, I didn’t understand the details as a child. All I knew was that the tree had to be cut down, no matter what.

“…It’s okay if it can’t be helped.”

“…Is that so?”

“Sorry, sorry,” my grandfather repeatedly stroked me. I strongly believed it wasn’t his fault. There are things in this world that just can’t be helped.

That tree was on the Ado family’s land. If the Ado family, with its strong influence, had insisted, it might have been possible to save the tree.

But the Ado was not such a family.

The family worked for the development of the surrounding area and the community, serving as a model. I am proud of my grandfather’s decision at that time.

As planned, the tree was cut down (it seems it was difficult to replant it somewhere else), and the hill was leveled. I couldn’t bring myself to watch it happen at all.

I had to keep going strong even without that tree, so I devoted myself to training. But it was just a façade, and even my usually strict mother and grandmother seemed concerned during that time.

And then, about a month after the tree was cut down.

“Suikaaa, Suikaaa!”

I heard a voice calling me from the entrance. It was a familiar boy’s voice.

At the entrance of the Ado house, Kuuya and his parents were there. My parents and grandfather were also there.

But my eyes didn’t go to them. They were drawn to a single point, inexplicably there, the tree that should have already been gone.

I don’t know how many breaths I took or how many times I blinked. After some time passed, I finally realized it was a painting of that tree.

It was a strange sensation. Different from a photograph. The reality of the tree’s presence was cut out in the form of a canvas, right here, now.

Along with that incomparable sense of security.

“…This?”

Kuuya-kun had.

When I asked, I finally noticed his complexion was terribly pale.

“Will you accept it?”

“Y-Yes…”

The framed painting was heavy. Not just the weight of the object, but something more.

“Kuuya-kun,”

“I’m glad I could give it to you… Sorry, I’m a bit… haha, sleepy…”

“Kuuya-kun!?”

After handing me the painting, he collapsed. By the time his father and mother supported his body, he had already lost consciousness.

“We’ve caused a commotion, sorry, Daisuke. We’ll take Kuuya home now.”

“It’s not a commotion, but… is Kuuya-kun okay…?”

“He’s my son, after all,” Kuuya’s father said with a smile to my father. There seemed to be some resolve in his expression.

Kuuya’s father was also a painter. His paintings were like delicate glasswork that seemed to shatter if touched carelessly by rough humans, incredibly ephemeral, delicate, and stunningly beautiful.

And perhaps, just as his paintings suggested, he wasn’t very physically strong.

“Sorry, Suika-chan, for the suddenness. If you like it, this child would be happy too.”

Those were the words of Kuuya’s mother, also a painter. A beautiful woman with distinct features, her voice and expressions always carried a strange vibrancy.

Her artworks were mostly themed around love, characterized by flamboyant, vividly bright reds. Like her, they were brimming with vitality, irresistibly capturing people’s attention with an undeniable attraction. They were said to contain the most vivid reds in modern Japan.

She often said, both in public and private, “My paintings, my red, are all my feelings for my husband.” My parents were quite close, but Kuuya’s parents were equally matched in their affection for each other.

Even after Kuuya and his family left, I remained frozen in the entranceway.

Facing the painting in my hands, unable to move.

I felt I shouldn’t take my eyes off it. Because this painting, this painting contained…

“Suika.”

“Grandfather…”

“Grandpa isn’t well-versed in art. Honestly, I don’t understand much about paintings. But, living a long life means you meet all sorts of people.”

My grandfather squatted down to meet my eye level, gazing at the painting with me.

“Among them, very rarely, you’ll encounter someone who’s fighting a battle with everything on the line. Even in an era without arrows or guns flying. Someone who risks their life, and somehow, achieves something by shaving off their own life. You could say they exchange their life for something else.”

“…”

I had no choice but to nod. Because I understood.

With parents like his, Kuuya was bound to be talented. But the painting before me wasn’t just about talent.

The painting exuded the scent of life. Not just paint and oil, but something else layered and poured into it, reaching my nostrils with a dense intensity.

That’s why it was so beautiful, certain, raw, and dear to me.

Something miraculous, now in my hands. Drawn by him, given to me.

Risking his life.

Something stirred within me.

“…Daisuke-san, Kuuya-kun is…”

“Just as Masataka said. No doubt, he’s his son. The same, capable of the same things.”

My father’s voice in response to my mother was somewhat stiff.

As if to reveal the reason for that stiffness, ──then, Kuuya didn’t wake up for three days.

“Kuuya-kun!!”

“…Suika? …Ah.”

On the third day of my daily hospital visits, just when everyone else had left, he woke up.

The weather outside was beautiful, casting deep shadows in the hospital room due to the contrast.

“Where is this…?”

He coughed several times after speaking abruptly, and when I handed him a cup of water, he drank a little.

“Thanks… a hospital, huh. Why am I here…?”

“For me! You drew…! And then…!”

“…Ah~! …Right, sorry, I was just tired. That’s why!”

He laughed off lightly.

“Just tired… Kuuya-kun, you’ve been unconscious for three days…”

“Was I asleep that long? Seriously? …Hey.”

Then, Kuuya looked extremely anxious. Perhaps the fear of having been unconscious for so long hit him─

“How was the painting?”

My expectations were completely off the mark.

“I was wondering if I managed to paint a good one… I haven’t heard what Suika thought about it.”

“…”

“Suika, you’ve seemed so lonely all the time. I was thinking if there was anything I could do… How was it? Did it help at all? …Did it cheer you up?”

“Kuuya-kun, weren’t you… in trouble?”

“Me? Well, I was… I knew going all out would end up bad. My dad even said, ‘If you keep it up, it might be irreversible.’ …Waking up in three days, I guess I’m pretty strong, huh?”

“…”

“But, I wanted to paint that. Well, no, it’s like this.”

Shyly, awkwardly, he said,

“I just hoped it would make Suika smile. I love it when Suika smiles.”

“That’s… all?”

“Yeah, yeah, pretty much.”

“Ah, saying that makes me sound stupid!” Kuuya said, breaking into his usual broad, hundred-meter recognizable smile.

“…”

I couldn’t breathe.

After painting something like that, in such a manner.

Why, why is he…

Why is he, oh, so…

“I…”

“Yeah?”

“I’m not scared anymore. Because Kuuya-kun gave me the greatest thing in the world.”

“…Really?!”

In front of his delighted face, I felt deeply ashamed.

I had envied him for painting, thinking he didn’t have to endure pain or suffering.

In the dojo, both my mother and grandmother were strict, but it was still just training. I never felt like my life was being shaved away, because it wasn’t a real battlefield.

But the person in front of me was different.

He fought, possibly shaving away some, or all, of his life in the process. He fought for me.

“That’s great, then! I’m glad!”

On the hospital bed, his smile was pale. Pure white.

There was another thing I was ashamed of.

That was not giving my all to the training until now. I looked at my hands.

With these frail fists, can I protect someone precious? …No.

“…Uh, …Uh.”

“…Suika?”

“Uh, …!”

“Suika!? What’s wrong, does it hurt somewhere!?”

“Uuuuu!”

Shaking my head vigorously, tears spilled from my eyes. Unstoppable.

Embarrassed, frustrated, pathetic. What have I been doing all this time?

“Ku-Kuuya-kun… Kuuya!”

No more hiding behind a younger girl.

“…? Suika…?”

“I, I, I will!”

“Alright, what…? I’m listening…!”

“Get stronger! So strong that it’ll surprise Kuuya!”

I squeezed his hand tightly, vowing.

Something unshakable, certain, was born within me at that moment. Something I had always wanted, giving me a profoundly happy feeling.

“I will! Become that!”

While speaking in front of Kuuya, I made an additional vow in my heart.

Someday, to become someone who can protect you from everything in the world, no matter what.

──And then.

In the end, I didn’t make that vow in time.

It really happened so suddenly that I think no one could fully grasp the reality of it.

Two years had passed since Kuuya gave me that painting. I was in the fourth grade of elementary school, and Kuuya was in the fifth.

It was a hot summer that made you wonder if something had gone wrong, the kind that made you tilt your head in confusion.

Since that day two years ago, I had devoted my entire being to training, feeling myself grow day by day. Therefore, I believed it wouldn’t be long before I could truly protect Kuuya.
