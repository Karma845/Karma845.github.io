Chapter 15: The First Solo Clear

1

"Details!? About Amyotrophic Magic Sclerosis?! A-And a treatment for it?!"

I approached the pop-out in surprise and read the words aloud. It contained details about Amyotrophic Magic Sclerosis and stated that there was no access to a treatment for it.

"Details... If I focus on what I want to know in my status, will the details appear?"

It seemed that by focusing on what one wanted to know in their status, more detailed information would appear. However, not everything would be displayed, only specific things. Detailed information about one's job or skills would also appear, but sometimes access would be denied.

"Access denied... If I level up this strange skill, maybe... I'll be able to find a cure for Amyotrophic Magic Sclerosis?!"

I had the skills ‘Eye of God’ and ‘Access Permission’, which I obtained from clearing that golden cube, but my Access Permission was only at Level 1.

"I don't know how to level up my skills, but it's probably in the dungeon."

In this world, skills were recognized, but people used them without knowing their proper names or effects. For example, the well-known skill "Provocation" could gather agro from dungeon monsters.

However, no one knew if its official name was actually "Provocation".

It was just a name that people started calling it.

Since I could see my status, I would probably be able to see the skill's name, or maybe the name that people had given it would be displayed.

"I've heard that clearing dungeons and getting stronger skills is possible. So, maybe!"

I held onto hope.

The cure for Amyotrophic Magic Sclerosis, an incurable disease that millions of people suffer from around the world and that no one had been able to understand, might be found.

If so, Nagi would be able to wake up.

I will help Nagi, who is trapped in darkness in her own body.

I held Nagi's hand tightly.

It's still cold as ever.

But still, she's alive, so there remains a possibility that she'll smile again.

"I'll help you, Nagi. Just wait a little longer. I'll definitely save you. And when I do..."

I made a vow to Nagi, who couldn't respond.

"Smile for me like you always do."

...

"Well then, Ijuin-sensei and Yamaguchi-san. Thank you in advance."

"Yes, leave it to us. Though there's a limit to what I can do."

I said goodbye to Ijuin-sensei and Yamaguchi-san from the home care service and left the hospital. It was already past noon, but it didn't feel like much time had passed since I woke up at home this morning.

"I want to dive into the dungeon, completely clear it to gain rewards, and become stronger. If I do that, maybe I can go to higher level dungeons and increase my skills."

I decided to dive into the dungeon.

With this 24 million yen sword, I should be able to solo even an E-class dungeon.

But there's a catch.

"The dungeon points are the problem..."

Dungeon points are the ironclad rule set by the Dungeon Association.

When applying to dive into a dungeon, they are allocated based on the strength of the members.

To dive into a dungeon, the total point count must be 10 points.

For example, if you want to dive into an E-class dungeon, you will be given 2 points for each E-class clearer. In other words, you just need five E-class clearers.

From there, for every higher-ranked clearer that participates, an additional 2 points are added.

So if a D-class clearer participates in an E-class dungeon, it becomes 4 points, and if there are two D-class and one E-class clearer, the total points become 10.

For a C-class dungeon, it's 6 points, so you can apply with one D-class or two E-class clearers.

What about unranked clearers? Zero points, of course.

They are treated the same whether they are present or not.

These are the rules for applying to dive into a dungeon.

There are some finer points, but this is the basic rule.

You can't just dive into a dungeon which could put your life at risk.

It's the ironclad rule set by the association that has caused numerous casualties in the dungeon. And if you dive into the dungeon without applying to the association, it's the same as poaching.

It's like stealing resources from the dungeon.

If you're caught, your clearer’s license may be revoked. In the worst case, you could receive a prison sentence as a criminal.

"...Well, what should I do? But then again, there's the reward for complete clear..."

I pondered. This is a crime.

But, looking at the status of various Cubes today, I realized that for most Cubes, to completely clear them, being solo is a prerequisite.

I, who have spent my life protecting rules, for the first time am going to break them.

I had quite a bit of resistance towards it, similar to the feeling of shoplifting.

Nonetheless, I've made up my mind.

"I prioritize Nagi over the rules! If I get caught... I'll apologize and rely on Tanaka-san!"

I decided to just go for it.

Above all else, Nagi is the most important thing to me.

If that means breaking the rules and causing trouble for Tanaka-san, then so be it.

And as for a higher level dungeon, that's another story, but for an E-ranked one, I shouldn't get in too much trouble.

Because there isn't that much money involved.

To put it in perspective, it's like shoplifting versus bank robbery.

With higher level cubes, you can make billions with just one clear, but with E-ranked dungeons, it's only a few thousand yen.

That's why I-

"I'll do my best!"

decided to aim for a solo clear.

That being said, I don't want to get caught, so I've decided to aim for a dungeon in the sparsely populated countryside.

I know I'm being cowardly, but it's better to not get caught.

I returned to the guild and used a computer to look up a list of cubes in Japan.

"Let's see... there are quite a few. There are over 200 E-ranked cubes in Japan alone..."

There were over 200 E-ranked cubes throughout Japan.

I'd mainly been active in Tokyo, so I didn't know much about the countryside or how they were clearing dungeons.

Even though it's called the countryside, it's only a little ways away from Tokyo.

Looking at the map of Japan, most of it could be considered countryside, just forests all around this country.

That's when I fixed my gaze on one location on the map.

Countryside or rather mountain? I set my sights on a Cube in a close by town and headed from Tokyo towards the direction of Mount Fuji.

"The location is Shimamura? They probably sell Western-style clothes there."

I decided to secretly complete the cube in that village. It seemed like it would take about three hours to get there by train and bus.

Without further ado, I prepared myself and set off.

Honestly, there was also the slightly violent reason that I wanted to test this sword out.

“Well, I’m a guy so I can’t help but get excited.”

I kind of understood the feeling of a killer, but of course not enough to direct it towards people. I embarked on the three-hour journey.

It felt like a solo trip without any real plans, but that was exciting in itself.

As for money, I had savings that I could use.

Luckily, Tanaka-san covered my medical expenses, so I had some spare cash.

Two hours being shaken around on the train and one hour being shaken around on the bus.

After using the regular public transport system, I arrived in Shimamura.

Looking around, there were so many rice fields and small houses that it made me question whether I was still in the same world.

"Hmm, the air here is so fresh! This is the countryside! It's like I've traveled back in time."

The peaceful countryside is so tranquil that you can almost hear the murmuring of the river. Despite the loud chirping of the cicadas, the air carries the delightful scent of the countryside. Natural sounds are the only thing audible, enough to make you forget about the hustle and bustle of the city.

Although I ended up wandering aimlessly, it's already 4 pm in the evening.

“Alright, let's do our best before it gets dark!”

I ran from there to the cube.

As an unranked person, I had to take breaks like an ordinary human, but in the end, I reached my destination.

In the middle of a remote mountain, the Cube stood quietly there.

The area around it was cleared and turned into a square-like plaza in the midst of the forest. Clearers probably take measures regularly to prevent a dungeon collapse.

"Oh, so this is the cube here. Blue... alright, it's an E rank."

I gazed at the cube that shone suspiciously like sapphire.

Then, just like with the Tokyo cube, the status was displayed.

Remaining Magic Power：43/50（+1/24hr）

Clear Difficulty：E Rank

◆Reward

First Clear Reward（Complete）：Magic Power +10

・Condition(s) 1　While the dungeon hasn’t been cleared before, defeat the boss.

Complete Clear Reward：Current access level is unable to display this.

・Condition 1　Clear while solo.

・Condition 2　Defeat a 100 or more Blue Bears.

・Condition 3　Defeat the boss within one minute。

"...Blue Bear. If that's the weakest monster, I should be able to handle it."

They were a species called Bear type, resembling bears but were considered a type of animal. They were referred to as beast types which included many different types such as wolves, bears, and lions.

They appeared in the same color as the Cubes, which was their basic feature.

Of course, their appearance was far from that of a bear and was more filled with killing intent. But if asked what they resembled, it would be a bear.

And the Blue Bear was the weakest of these bears with a blue appearance.

Each Cube’s individual species, also known as ‘Color type’, had the same appearance except for their color, but their strength varied greatly depending on the color.

Even so, this E-class Cube with the same blue color was known as the weakest color, so I should be able to handle it.

"Speaking of which, if this remaining magic power reaches its maximum value of 50, does it cause a dungeon collapse? If so, defeating monsters or bosses should reduce the remaining magic power, extending the deadline. If that seems to be the case, it makes sense."

I would have to defeat dungeon monsters and bosses regularly and enter rest mode.

As a result, I speculated that the remaining magic power would probably disappear.

If I can test it out, I should.

"Now then, let's go. I wonder if it's because of this weapon that I'm not as scared as before? Or is it..."

I approached the cube without any hesitation.

Although it was E-class, I would have been too scared to enter alone before. But now, I didn't feel like I would lose.

Of course, this was probably also due to my equipment being far beyond what was expected of me.

But maybe it was also because I faced true death inside the Golden Cube and was able to stick to my will.

Compared to those immensely powerful monsters, there was no fear in facing the weakest blue type.

My heart raced, my body was tense, and I was nervous.

But I knew this feeling wasn't fear.

"Alright, here I go!!"

I touched the cube, and with a crisp sound, the beautiful walls rippled with blue and gently welcomed me.

On this day, I began my first solo dungeon clear.

There was no fear, only one thing.

This pounding in my chest.

2

"Cave-type, huh... A little dark, but I can just barely see."

I open my eyes in the cramped, suffocating space.

I'm surrounded by a stone-enclosed cavern that emits a true ‘dungeon’ feeling.

There are various types of dungeons: cave-like places, man-made looking temples, snowy mountains that seem like other worlds, and grasslands.

Dungeon might just be a name; it might be better to call them other worlds. Well, originally, they were called the Great Labyrinths of Another World, but dungeon is easier to understand.

"Well then, it's no good to simply clear it. Still, strategy is vital for survival. Let's not do anything reckless."

I proceed through the dungeon alone.

This dungeon has already been explored, and a map has been made.

I downloaded the map onto my smartphone and open it.

If I can follow this route, it should be clearable in two or three hours.

I grip the sword at my waist and proceed cautiously.

Then, a drooling blue bear emerges from the shadows.

Its skin is a dull blue color, and its size is about the same as mine.

A Blue Bear, being blue which is called the weakest color, is slightly smaller than a regular bear.

Still, there's no way a person can handle a regular bear with their bare hands, but I have this sword now.

"By the way, can I see the status of monsters?"

I consciously stare at the Blue Bear, and as expected, I can see its status.

Name: Blue Bear

Magic Power: 10

Attack Power: Reflection rate▶40%=4

Defense Power: Reflection rate▶20%=2

Agility: Reflection rate▶40%=4

Intelligence: Reflection rate▶0%=0

"I see! I can see it! It's stronger than me barehanded, but... it looks quite weaker than I expected. Alright, then... phew... let's go!"

I ready my sword and crouch down.

Judging by its status, it's stronger than me, but with this sword equipped, I seem to be considerably more powerful.

Then, the Blue Bear notices me and charges at me, drooling.

If this had happened before, I might have hesitated or involuntarily closed my eyes.

But, not now.

"It looks slower, no, that's not it. I’m just not looking away."

I can see the Blue Bear's movements clearly.

For a moment, I thought it was because of the Eye of God but that's not it.

I'm just calmly focused on every move it makes.

I simply don't look away.

And with that, the world seems to change.

"Compared to that damned demon angel!!"

Compared to that embodiment of terror, that demonic angel, the Blue Bear is nothing.

"It's just a beast!!"

With a single strike, I slash down the Blue Bear from above.

My attack power, strengthened by this weapon, can kill the weakest E-class magical creature in one hit.

"Phew... I wonder if I've become stronger."

I definitely felt more confident in that battle.

The weapon has indeed made me more powerful, so my attack power has increased, but the process leading up to landing a hit was clearly different for me than from before.

Could it be called high-altitude training?

The Blue Bear really felt weaker, and I didn't feel like I would lose at all.

But I can't be careless.

My defense status hasn't changed at all.

"Ah, another one's coming. As for the magic stones... I'll pass on picking them up. I don't know how to explain this to the guild."

As I stared at the Blue Bear's corpse while reassessing my strength, two more Blue Bears emerged from the shadows.

I took the same stance. Even if it's two against one, I don't feel like I will lose.

I would usually pick up the magic stones, but I decided to give up because I wouldn't be able to explain it when cashing in at the guild.

...

An hour later.

"Phew... this is quite a challenge, but... hmph!"

I had defeated my 60th Blue Bear.

I had already found the boss room, but the condition for a complete clear is to defeat 100 Blue Bears.

So, I search every corner of the dungeon, looking for places where Blue Bears are hiding.

It felt like a quest in a hunting game to search around and hunt a bunch of the same monster.

They're hard to find. Damn it, where are you ‘weakest bear’?

"That's why there aren't many people who have completely cleared it so far. As well as being solo, this dungeon might not even have 100 monsters..."

As I continued searching, I could hardly find any more after I surpassed 90.

I sat down in the dungeon, spreading out the water and rice balls I had bought at a convenience store, expecting it to be a long journey.

It was already past 8 PM.

"Only five more to go, let's hang in there a little longer..."

I was quite tired, but the sheer number of 100 was simply a lot.

"Oh!? They're here!!"

Despite my fatigue, I kept searching the dungeon.

Then, I found a group of five Blue Bears near what seemed to be a cave's water edge. Desperate to suppress a cry of joy, I raised my sword and approached the Blue Bears.

By now, I had searched so much that the joy of finding them was greater than that of winning the battles.

"That makes 100!!"

After defeating the 100th Blue Bear, I felt victorious.

By now, I could easily handle 5 vs. 1 as I had figured out their habits.

『Condition 2 has been achieved.』

"Oh, they're telling me. How kind."

The cold, mechanical voice echoed in my head.

The voice that one could here when having cleared a Cube, called by us as heaven’s voice. It informed me that I had achieved the second condition of completely clearing the dungeon.

"I'm glad. If I had miscounted and found out I was one short after defeating the boss, I would've gone mad."

With a small victory pose, I headed toward the boss's room.

The boss's room was always behind a large door, just like in any other Cube.

I had only seen E-class boss doors firsthand, but I had seen images of other boss doors on the internet.

Grander or rather solemn, they became larger and more ominous as their ranks increased.

I exhaled deeply, replenishing my oxygen.

I wasn't scared, but it was still the boss.

I took a deep breath and switched my mindset from the relaxed one I had just moments ago.

The E-class boss, in other words, an opponent on par with a hobgoblin with a broken left hand.

"Alright!"

I opened the door and entered.

The large square room was dimly lit by torches with blue flames.

In the center was the boss of the Blue Bears.

Its fur was a different shade of blue from the dull-colored Blue Bears - it was sapphire blue, just like the cube itself.

The beautiful color was highly sought after as a material for high-end clothing.

"Sapphire Bear... It's huge. It's even bigger than me."

The sleeping Sapphire Bear slowly rose to its feet as it noticed my arrival.

It drooled as it stared at me.

I had heard its name before, but this was my first time seeing the jewel-like bear that was both beautiful and powerful.

I readied my High Wolf Fang Sword.

As a C-class monster, the High Wolf was far more powerful.

The Sapphire Bear seemed cautious, perhaps recognizing the weapon made from the material of its superior.

Then, the battle began with the howl of a wolf.

I put everything into it from the start because the third condition was to defeat the boss within a minute.

I had no time to scout it out first.

As we both charged at each other, our paths crossed.

The Sapphire Bear tried to bite me, but I passed downwards.

It went just as I had anticipated.

Having hunted 100 Blue Bears, I had grasped the tricks and habits of their combat.

If there was something they could bite in front of them, they would prioritize biting over slashing.

That's why I didn't dodge head-on until the last moment.

As expected, the Sapphire Bear lunged to bite at my throat.

With limited options, it was easy to dodge the anticipated bite trajectory.

I crouched under the bear's jaw and, combining the force of my legs, I thrust my dagger forcefully from below into its throat.

I then put more force into the embedded sword and pierced through the Sapphire Bear's skull.

Without a chance to cry out, the Sapphire Bear, as if entrusting its weight to me, died.

At that moment, I successfully defeated a boss solo for the first time.

"I did it. I really thought it through and defeated it. Me... unranked me!"

I laid the Sapphire Bear down and wiped the bloodied sword.

After finishing my first boss battle, I felt as if I had grown a little stronger.

That was probably just a matter of mindset.

『Conditions 1, 2, and 3 confirmed, complete clear reward will be granted.』

The usual system voice played simultaneously with the boss's defeat.

The voice announced the complete clear reward I had aimed for.

"Alright!!"

I made a small victory pose.

At the same time, particles of light enveloped me.2

"...Oh? I'm back, huh? They won't tell me what the reward is."

When I open my eyes, I find myself inside the usual cube after defeating the boss.

The sparkling walls of the cube slowly collapse and enter a dormant mode.

"So... Did I really get a reward?"

I stare at the wall and display my status.

Since the remaining magic power is at 0, it seems that, as I expected, the remaining magic power represents how close the dungeon is to collapse.

Remaining Magic Power：0/50（+1/24hr）

Clear Difficulty：E Rank

◆Reward

First Clear Reward（Complete）：Magic Power +10

・Condition(s) 1　While the dungeon hasn’t been cleared before, defeat the boss.

Complete Clear Reward（Complete）：Magic Power +30, Class Upgrade Ticket (Beginner)

・Condition 1　Clear while solo.

・Condition 2　Defeat a 100 or more Blue Bears.

・Condition 3　Defeat the boss within one minute。

"I'm glad. It seems I got it... I do feel like I've gotten stronger... right? And a Class Upgrade Ticket? Huh? Is it this?"

The full clear reward is +30 magic power.

Also, it seems I received something called a "Class Upgrade Ticket (Beginner)."

As I look around, I find something resembling a bronze-colored ticket at my feet.

I pick up the ticket and examine it.

Type: Item

Title: Class Upgrade Ticket (Beginner)

Attainment Difficulty: C

Effect: A job’s class upgrade test will begin once ten tickets are collected.

"Class upgrade test? ...Will this 'no occupation' written on my status change? I thought it was just a derogatory term for being unemployed."

I stared at my hand and checked my status.

Name: Kai Amachi

Status: Good

Job: None

Skill(s): The Eye of God, Access Permission Lv1

Magic Power: 35

Attack Power: Reflection rate▶25%=9

Defense Power: Reflection rate▶25%=9

Agility: Reflection rate▶25%=9

Intelligence: Reflection rate▶25%=9

Equipment:

・The Emblem of the Knight

・High Wolf’s fang sword = Attack Power +120

"......Alright......Yes!"

I look at my own status and make a victory pose with both hands.

My magic power had increased from 5 to 35.

Having more than 10 magic power is the condition for E-Rank.

In other words, I've now evolved from the long-despised unranked title to E-Rank.

"If I keep going like this, I..."

Excited, I wanted to head to the next dungeon.

However, it was already past 8 pm, making it difficult for today.

Thinking that there's no point in rushing, I decide to go home for the day.

I spend another three hours getting back to my rundown apartment.

But my footsteps that day were different from the usual dungeon returns.
