In the Dungeon Association, it's possible to measure magic power.

They use the vibrations generated when magic power flows through a magic stone to measure the magic power of the person being tested.

That being said, the frequency of the vibrations is used as the basis for what's called magic power.

And I suppose the magic power value I can see in this status is the same as that value.

"Speaking of which, they said it's integrated with human common sense, but I wonder if it's showing me things in a way that's easy for me to understand?"

E-Rank is for magic power between 10 and 50. D-Rank is for up to 100, and C-Rank is for up to 1,000.

From there, it increases by one digit at a time, so B-Rank is up to 10,000, and anything beyond that is A-class.

Anything higher than that is called S-Rank, and at that point, I don't really understand it anymore.

There's actually an even higher class that exists, but the inflation is so extreme it's surprising.

With the above calculation method, the magic power difference between the upper and lower ranks of C-Rank is a whopping ten times.

I think there should be more divisions, but since each class is further divided into lower, middle, and upper ranks, it might be fine as it is.
