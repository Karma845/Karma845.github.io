Chapter 1

Suddenly, a white light burned into Eisuke’s retinas, blocking his vision.

He felt as if the world had turned upside down. The stinging pain in his eyes gradually eased, and when he opened his eyelids….

“Wait… what the hell is thiiiiiiiiiiis…?!”

What appeared before his eyes was a blue ocean stretching out endlessly. A huge island of floating vegetation. Cities and towns could be seen in many places.

He was thrown head first into the air at an altitude of about 3,000 meters.

“Wait, wait, wait, this has got to be a joke, hey…!”

Eisuke was forced to parachute, but without a parachute. The force of the wind hitting his whole body felt painful, and the intense feeling of floating tried to dull his consciousness. Gritting his teeth against the fierce gravitational acceleration acting on his body, his eyes were riveted on the unfamiliar landscape of the world before him.

The huge continent was not the Japanese archipelago, whichever way you looked at it. The surface of the ocean was getting closer and closer, and the resolution of the view was increasing as if it were a magnified photograph.

Then Eisuke caught his breath and his eyes blinked several times.

“What the hell… is that?”

There was a mysterious girl on the beach with a seven-colored holy light radiating from her entire body. With her hands outstretched like a benevolent goddess, and her clear eyes staring at Eisuke, it was as if she was trying to welcome him into her heart as he crashed headlong from the sky.

The distance between her and Eisuke was getting smaller and smaller. After less than a hundred meters, when her silhouette was clearly visible… softly, the acceleration Eisuke had been experiencing was instantly reduced to zero.

“W-wow…!?”

Eisuke’s body floated as if in weightless space in front of the girl’s eyes, who hugged him tightly.

“Finally we meet…!”

“Eh…?!”

Eisuke couldn’t hide his confusion when he was suddenly passionately embraced by a girl he didn’t know. The girl hugging Eisuke’s chest was looking at him with tears at the corners of her eyes. He could only swallow saliva.

She had long, silky blonde hair that reflected the sunlight like gold dust. Her blue eyes were as clear and beautiful as jewels. Her bare skin was as white as snow, and her body so slender that it looked as if it would break at the slightest touch. Her moist eyes gave off an air of fragile beauty, but the way she smiled softly exuded a touch of elegance. She was a stunningly beautiful girl.

“…Ah! I-I’m sorry! I didn’t mean to be so rude…!”

The girl finally realized her bold action and blushed. She hurriedly pulled away from Eisuke with a huff and then cleared her throat.

“Ah… I really don’t mind, but first I want to ask you something.”

“…?”

Eisuke frowned and asked the puzzled girl.

“Where am I?”

The girl’s face changed to a serious expression at his words.

“…This is a different world from the one you used to live in. You could say it’s another world from your point of view.”

Eisuke swallowed saliva and turned around to take another look at this world.

The horizon stretched on forever. The sky was clear and blue, with bright white clouds. Two suns peeked out from where he stood. To top it off, in the distant sky, a creature that could only be called a dragon was flying with its wings flapping calmly.

No, he didn’t need to check again. Suddenly, he was brought across the sky, had a bird’s eye view of a continent he had never seen before, and… met a beautiful and mysterious girl. With all these elements, there was no room for doubt: This was a different world.

“Is this real?” Eisuke involuntarily mumbled.

“I’m Marie… funyu!?”

In this state of stupefaction, Eisuke pulled the cheeks on both sides of the face of the girl in front of him.

“Hyah, his hihen ha heam.”

“Yes, I see it’s not a dream. So it really is a different world.”

“He-hease, eho hit how!”

The girl’s reaction was funny, as she didn’t try to force Eisuke away, and to which he was satisfied and let her go.

“Whoops, sorry, sorry, I thought I was dreaming.”

“Hey, don’t you usually check it with your own cheeks?”

“I have a habit of wanting to pull anything that looks soft.”

“Is that really a habit? Doing that with people you’ve just met will leave a bad impression on them.”

“I’m trying to be more honest with myself. Don’t get so upset. Your beautiful blonde hair is going to fall out.”

“It’s not going to fall out! And this is not the time to do this!” The girl who had been carried away by Eisuke’s lighthearted tone was quick to shake her head. “Well, if I may be so bold, may I ask your name?”

“Hey, hey, did you just summon a guy whose name you don’t even know?”

The girl giggled at Eisuke’s jaw-dropping comment.

“Hahaha… Actually, I know who you are.”

“…Huh?”

Eisuke’s mood changed with the girl’s answer, and he looked at her with a questioning look. She instead answered him in a more serious tone.

“We need you here. You, who have a strength superior to the common people.”

…She knew that Eisuke was a person with strength beyond humans.

It was not by coincidence or miracle, but Eisuke was summoned with a clear reason that had to do with him.

“Who are you? Let me hear your name first.” Eisuke’s face took on an amused expression and he asked irreverently. However, his eyes were not smiling at all. They were the eyes of a man who was coldly trying to determine whether she was a friend or an enemy.

“Well, you’re the one who interrupted me by pulling my cheeks when I tried to say my name first…” The girl let out a small sigh, saying resentfully. She bowed reverently and looked up again. In contrast to Eisuke’s dark eyes, her blue eyes were full of expectation and a light that expressed willpower. “I am… Marielle. Marielle Lay Arestina. I am the princess of the Arestina Empire, and… I am the reincarnation of a goddess of this world.”

Eisuke was stunned. This was the first girl he had met since he came to this other world. The person who brought him to this world was the princess of a country, and a reincarnated goddess.

“So, I’ll leave the details for later…”

“Wait, wait, wait, are you serious? You’ve got to be kidding me. You can’t ask me not to be curious if you tell me you’re the reincarnation of a goddess.”

Marielle tried to move on, but Eisuke couldn’t help but raise his voice at her words.

“But right now, there’s something more urgent…”

“My-My lady… guh…!”

What emerged from some bushes behind the beach with a rustling sound was a beautiful woman with wounds all over her body.

“Rukino!” Marielle rushed over to her as she saw her collapse on the beach. Eisuke also looked at her in surprise.

She was a beautiful woman with a dignified face, with a mature and intelligent appearance. Her straight, brown, medium-length hair gave off an air of nobility, and she was much more mature than Eisuke. But more than the visual, Eisuke was glued to the fine sword at her waist.

…A knight. That’s what Eisuke thought as soon as he saw her.

“I-I’m sorry…! I’m sorry I lost, to mercenaries…!”

“This is terrible… Wait, I’m going to cast a healing spell on you now…!”

The knight named Rukino had her light medieval noblewoman’s clothes torn, and painful scars like burns peeked out of the holes. Marielle extended her hand towards her, looking as if she was in a hurry.

At that moment, a slight light emanated from Marielle’s small palm, turning Rukino’s wound into beautiful white skin again in a matter of seconds. No trace of the wound remained.

“That’s magic, isn’t it…? Then this really is a different world…?”

“Huh…?” Rukino reacted to Eisuke’s surprised murmur. As soon as she saw his face, her eyes widened, “You’re…? So, you’ve successfully performed the summoning, my lady!”

“Yes! It’s thanks to Rukino and the others who were able to buy me some time!”

Marielle said to Rukino with joy. Eisuke, who didn’t know what was going on, asked the girls with wide-eyed surprise.

“Hey, wait, explain to me first. What’s the situation?”

“M-My name is Rukino…! I’m an imperial knight in the service of my lady…! From now on let’s get along… gah…!

“Rukino, please rest!”

“No, I don’t need such an exaggerated self-introduction at this time! Anyway, let me explain first….”

“There they are!”

The discussion between Eisuke and the others was interrupted by a threatening voice.

Four strong men came out of the forest. With swords, bows and clubs in their hands, they stood in front of Marielle and Rukino, licking their lips and laughing.

“Surrender now, Princess Marielle… or should I say ex-princess?”

“P-please stop it now! Why are you doing this…!?”

“Why? Because of that generous man! Wasn’t it nice of him to hire some thugs like us!”

Marielle complained with a saddened face, but the men only raised vulgar voices.

“But-but, he…!”

“Haha, I see. I see.”

“Huh?”

Marielle raised her voice in confusion. Because in the middle of the exchange between Marielle and the men, words were heard spoken in a serious voice from her side. At the same time the owner of that voice finished saying those words, he jumped from Marielle’s side at high speed.

“Hyaah!?”

Marielle’s blonde hair fluttered in the wild wind. She squinted her eyes for a moment and when she could look…

…One of the men was thrown off by a flying kick.

After the unannounced kick came out of nowhere, Eisuke grabbed two of the three remaining men by the head and smashed them into each other’s, then quickly jumped to the side of the remaining man and hurled him like a cannonball towards the other two from before. He then launched a running kick at the three that were piled up. As if they had been hit by a super-heavy object, they were thrown off with great force, rolled and stopped moving. This happened in just three seconds.

…In a matter of seconds, Eisuke had subdued the four thugs.

He waved his hands in boredom.

“Well, it’s done.”

“Huh… eh…!?”

Marielle was simply surprised. Since it seemed that Eisuke, who still didn’t know anything about this world, was confused, but still threw kicks and flying kicks without hesitation. And then, he spoke as if nothing had happened.

“I know the kind of people you can beat up without mercy or remorse. Like these. They’re a group of four big guys chasing you. Same modus operandi as the yankees. And that’s just what I specialize in.”

“Yan, kees…?”

Marielle tilted her head as the word was unfamiliar to her. She had summoned him without knowing his name. Then of course she wouldn’t know what he did daily. He was fairly well known for being the “Yankee hunter”.

“Well, now that there’s nothing to interrupt us, let’s hear the rest of that beautiful story, shall we?”

“Huh?”

“No ‘huh?’. You didn’t bring me here just to beat up these small fries, did you?”

Marielle was speechless as Eisuke shrugged. He easily called the strong thugs that Rukino, Princess Marielle’s knight, had had difficulty fighting, even though they outnumbered him, small fries. He did not mean it as hyperbole, exaggeration, or a facade, as could be seen by the silence of the men lying on the other side.

“The reason you summoned me. …Who do you want me to help you defeat? Of course, that’s what I want to hear.” Eisuke smiled sarcastically.

Marielle swallowed.

She let out a small sigh and answered in a low tone.

“…I’ll tell you the story. Not long ago, a demon king ruled this world.”

The moment he heard that, Eisuke silently knelt down on the spot and made a pump fist.

“…Um, what are you doing?”

“…As you can see, I’m so excited that I can’t stop shaking…” Eisuke replied to the bewildered Marielle without moving a muscle in his pose.

“…Well, maybe our concepts of Demon King are different. Here, the Demon King is the worst of the worst, and he sent demons to various places to invade this continent and began a regime of tyranny…”

“Yes, that kind of Demon King I imagined.”

“What, really!? Then why are you happy?!”

“Of course I’d be happy! I’m sure he’s the kind of asshole you can punch and no one would complain, in fact, they’d cheer for you! Well, I totally get it! I just have to punch him until I’ve had enough! Otherwise, why would I be brought from another world?!”

Marielle’s face tightened as Eisuke approached her with an excited expression on his face.

“Um, no, that’s not…”

“It’s okay, no problem! I’ll give that damn bastard a good beating, don’t worry! And where is this Demon King? That way? Is it that way!?”

“P-Please! You need to calm down! You’re wrong! You’re jumping to conclusions too fast! The Demon King has already been defeated!”

“Huh?” Eisuke stiffened like a rock.

“…No, no way, what about what you just said? All that stuff you said about the Demon King, what was it for?”

“I thought to explain it in order! The Demon King has been defeated and sealed a long time ago!”

“W-who was it!? Who would do such a thing without me!?”

Eisuke’s words grew louder in frustration and surprise. Marielle lowered her eyes a little and answered in a low voice.

“…It was the Heroes.”

“…Heroes?”

“In this world, there were already people who were summoned from the same world as you to defeat the Demon King, people who have obtained different abilities: Heroes. They joined forces and defeated the Demon King.”

“What was… what you just said…?”

Eisuke was mortified, and this time his knees gave way. It was impossible for anyone not to be surprised to hear that the Demon King had already been defeated by the heroes. Marielle smiled bitterly, half amused, and spoke fluently.

“I-I told you I’m the reincarnation of a goddess, didn’t I? When the coming of the Demon King is foretold, according to tradition, the goddess reincarnates through the blood of Arestina’s royal family. Therefore, I was born with the ability to give the divine power of the goddess to those who are qualified to defeat the Demon King. The general term for those who are chosen by that power is ‘Heroes’.”

“Wait a minute, then what’s the reason you summoned me? Don’t tell me you want me to help those Heroes or clean up their mess?”

Eisuke asked resentfully. Marielle then let her gaze wander.

“…No. I want you to stop them.”

“…What?”

Eisuke couldn’t believe his ears. Marielle exhaled sharply and told him:

“The one to stop is the Heroes who defeated the Demon King and took over our country.”

The young people who were summoned from the same world as Eisuke… the beings called Heroes had taken over this country. Eisuke swallowed unconsciously.

“Drunk by the immense power they had acquired, after defeating the Demon King, they refused to return to their original world and took over every city in the entire Arestine Empire. …Even the imperial capital where we used to live.”  Marielle lowered her eyes and looked at him with a piercing expression. “…Please. We have no choice but to rely on your power that is beyond human. I have already summoned two beings like you, but their abilities were not enough to stand against the immense power of the heroes. But your ability is…”

“…Hmm?” Eisuke then interrupted Marielle. It was because he heard something she said that didn’t help but cause him surprise. He opened his eyes and asked: “Did you just say that there are two people like me…?!”.

“Y-yes. You are the last person I summoned to this world, the third one to be exact.”

“The last…? The third…?”

It was the biggest surprise of the day to Eisuke. It was no wonder. He thought he was the only one.

“People with abilities beyond human limits, like you, are already fighting for us.”

“Like me…?”

A person whose growth rate was abnormal and had no concept of human limits. He was not the only one. They were summoned before him. Eisuke was surprised by this fact.

“Ah, this is the worst! Why are we in such a mess?”

“It’s because we’ve been summoned to another world.”

“I know it! That’s why I’m complaining! I don’t know why you keep repeating it…”

A complaining voice and an indifferent sounding voice became a duet and echoed in the forest.

The forest opened up, and the deep green that blocked the sun’s rays had disappeared, leaving a meadow-like space. Two girls stood there, back-to-back.

They were cornered. Men with weapons like swords, bows and other noisy weapons.

…But to say “cornered” was extreme. In fact, it was the men who seemed to be taking all the damage.

“They’re giving us a hard time…! What the hell is wrong with them…?”

“We’re just high school girls. …Well, the other girls are a little less pretty than us, aren’t they?”

“…It should only take a minute…”

“T-That’s because values vary from person to person, right?!”

“Even if you say ‘right’…”

At first glance, the situation appeared to be hopeless. In such a situation, the appearance of the two, who were having a lighthearted exchange without any sense of tension, was just as they said, they were ordinary high school girls wearing a shabby school uniform.

Yui Koikuzue. A second-year student in high school.

The large hoodie she wore with her school uniform, her short haircut, and the headphones she wore around her neck gave her the appearance of a high school girl with the sweetness of a Yamato Nadeshiko[1]… but her face was as cold and expressionless as that of a mechanical doll. At best, she was calm and serene, but at worst, she was a short girl with a gloomy impression.

And then there was Serika Hayami. Also a second-year student in high school.

She had a cardigan wrapped around her waist, a scrunchie tied to her arm. Her hair, dyed bright brown, was pulled back in a side ponytail, and if she had to describe herself, in one word, you’d say she was a “modern high school girl”. You might even say she was a “gal[2].” With her well-proportioned style, bright looks and personality, she was a girl with a pop star charm that you could easily imagine at the top of the school society caste.

Why were such extremely modern girls summoned to this world?

What Yui held with her eyes narrowed emotionlessly was two pistols. She held a pistol in each hand, with a brown barrel that shone with varnish. Serika, also staring with narrowed eyes, was crouched down, throwing her right leg back and her left leg forward. It was her combat stance.

The answer is that they were both, according to Serika, a bit different from the concept of “normal” high school girls.

“Tsk, deyaaaaaaaah!”

A strong man bellowed and rushed towards Yui. In his hand was a real sword, sharp and serious. However, Yui was undeterred and pointed the muzzle of the gun at him. Then, her cold eyes narrowed even more…

“…!”

Dang!

The sound of the gunshot roared through the forest.

The bullet she fired was aimed right at the hilt of the sword the man held and ricocheted off it.

“Nna…!? Gah…?!”

Another bullet was fired without delay, aimed at the man’s breastplate. The small piece of metal holding the breastplate came loose. With the barrel of the gun pointed at his chest, which was now empty after losing the breastplate, Yui muttered under her breath.

“…The next shot is going to go into your heart, not a piece of your armor.”

Yui joked with a nonchalant tone, while having airs of being a skilled sniper. …It should be noted that she only started shooting after coming to this world. She had never been in a war zone where bullets were flying, nor did she go through bloody shooting training in her previous world. …But even so, the bullets that came out when she pulled the trigger went precisely where she was aiming. It was hard to believe that she could even shoot in the dark.

“Wh-why a little girl like you…?”

“Because I’m not an ordinary little girl.”

As she said this, she turned around quickly.

There was another man who looked like he was going to jump at her from the forest behind. As she looked at the man in front of her, he was planning to attack her by surprise.

“It’s useless.”

However, Yui calmly muttered and stepped back to jump back. Pointing the barrels of her pistols at the man… she pulled the trigger.

The bullets hit the man’s thighs with precision. Fresh blood spurted from the wounds like a bottle of champagne on New Year’s Eve.

“Ughhhh!”

She stared at the man, who was screaming in pain and thrashing around on the floor, and exhaled.

“If you’re going to sneak attack, you should be quieter.”

“Eh…!?”

“The sound of your breathing, your heart beating nervously. …If I concentrate, I can hear it very loudly.”

“Huh…?!”

The man was sweating and his eyes widened in amazement. Needless to say, it was impossible for you to hear the heartbeat of a person who was far away from you. Yet Yui said, as if it was nothing, that she could hear it. She even said it was very loud.

She held the gun and lightly hugged her body.

“…It’s because I’m more sensitive…”

“…The way you say it can lead to misunderstandings…”

“…But it’s not badly put…” Yui furrowed her eyebrows slightly at Serika’s response beside her.

Yui was… a girl whose five senses had developed beyond human level.

She could see things the human eye couldn’t, hear things you couldn’t hear, and feel things you couldn’t feel normally. That was why she was no ordinary girl and could shoot accurately without training or a scope.

“Damn them, they’re freaks!”

Two men stood side by side, bows at the ready. They quickly pulled the arrows from their waist packs and pulled the strings. The arrows reached Serika like the wind. She clicked her tongue.

“Who are you calling a freak?!” Serika said those words and disappeared.

“…Eh?”

The two archers were stunned as the arrows they shot pierced the void. A swift wind blew past them.

“Well, that’s it, isn’t it?”

A voice suddenly rang out; before they knew it, Serika was behind them. In her arms, she held a large number of arrows.

“It can’t be possible…!”

The men rushed to check… there was not a single arrow left in their quivers they carried on their waists. She had taken them away. Serika scattered the arrows on the ground in pieces.

“Without arrows, you can’t fight anymore, can you?”

“Wh-When was it…?

“No, no, it isn’t when. But this very instant. Didn’t you see how fast I went?”

Serika stuck her tongue out at them mischievously and mocked them. The two men shuddered.

Serika, like Yui, was also a girl who had developed abilities beyond those of a human being in the process of growing up. What pushed her beyond her limits was her agility. She could run as fast as the wind, or even faster, being faster even than sound. It was no longer just high-speed movement, but instantaneous. She could not be called “normal” in any way.

Unlike fictional heroes, these girls did not acquire this kind of power due to some special event. Yui, in her daily life, listened and watched carefully. Serika was always sweating and running in the playground and on her way to school every day. Without realizing it, they had somehow exceeded human limits in their respective fields. That’s why they were both high school students who were discovered by Marielle and summoned into this world.

The two exchanged words matter-of-factly as they effortlessly fended off the ruffians who approached them.

“I wonder if they were able to summon the last one correctly.”

“Marielle’s voice is heard from the other side. Perhaps she is talking to the person she summoned.”

“I see, so we didn’t buy time in vain, huh?”

Yui was shooting and disarming the men, and Serika was giving high-speed spinning kicks to the jaw.

“In that case, why don’t we just get it over with and join them?”

“…Yeah, let’s do that.”

…The only one left was a hooded man with a sword.

The two rushed towards him to finish the battle.

“Hee, Yui and Serika, huh? I didn’t think there was anyone in the same situation as me. They have some pretty interesting skills.” Eisuke tapped his knee, amused as he listened to the details of the girls who, like him, had abilities beyond human.

“At the moment, the girls are fighting bandits and mercenaries in the surrounding area, buying time so we can finish your summoning. I think they should join us soon.”

“Hmm… Are they strong?”

“Of course, they are much stronger than most humans. They are strong enough to fight without magic or special weapons.”

“But they are weaker than the Heroes… Aren’t they?”

Eisuke pointed out, striking a chord. Otherwise, there would have been no need to summon Eisuke with such zeal. Besides, although Yui and Serika’s abilities might be tremendous as humans, it was doubtful that they could compete with the Heroes who had acquired great power to fight the Demon King. Marielle made a bitter face.

“It can’t be helped. The Heroes are too strong in comparison. …But I think they are fine for now. On the contrary, as long as they don’t face any Heroes, they shouldn’t lose either.”

Marielle replied with a calm tone. Eisuke then looked around.

“Now that I think about it, those yankees from before were after you, weren’t they? I think they said they were hired by someone or something, but who were they referring to?”

Eisuke asked nonchalantly, and contrary to his nonchalance, Marielle began to speak, her face contorted in anger and pain.

“…The numerous Heroes we summoned are now calling themselves rulers of cities and towns across the country, behaving authoritatively and imposing selfish systems as they please. In short, they are building their own kingdoms everywhere. …And the one who controls this whole area is Sir Ken, one of the Heroes.”

Hero Ken. The Japanese-sounding name was proof that he was from the same country as Eisuke.

“…What’s that guy doing in this place?”

“…Hunting.”

“…Hunting?”

Eisuke repeated, and Marielle bit her lip in disgust.

“This forest and the coast have many natural resources that can only be found here, such as animals and fruits that serve as food, trees and minerals that serve as materials. But Sir Ken has taken the liberty of making this area his territory, and has gathered and hired bandits and thugs from all over the country to live in this area. Then he started attacking people who came to collect resources, calling it ‘hunting’.”

Marielle gripped the hem of her dress tightly. Rukino couldn’t stand it either, a blue vein appearing at her temples.

“He’s a very despicable kind of Hero. He regards this area as the board of his own game, and takes pleasure in watching from the side as those who enter are attacked by the bandits hiding in the area. Truly, he is an irredeemable outcast.”

Eisuke frowned. Probably not just him, but all the Heroes had the feeling that they had become all-powerful gods. In a world they controlled, their pawns moved as they pleased. They were intoxicated by the pleasure of playing dirty in their game.

“That’s a nasty son of a bitch. I don’t think I could make friends with the likes of him.”

“But we’re safe for now.”

“Safe?”

Rukino cleared her throat and said confidently. Marielle also nodded her head confidently.

“Yes. He doesn’t show off as much as he enjoys watching. At high speed, Lady Serika confirmed an approach from the surrounding forest. The Hero was nowhere to be seen.”

“And as long as the Hero does not come out, neither Yui nor Serika will be defeated. While they buy time by defeating the bandits, I had to summon you from the other world, and you’re supposed to join them before the Hero appeared. A perfect strategy.”

Eisuke rubbed his chin silently. They would take advantage of his opponent’s taste for watching and create an opening. He had a strange feeling about the perfect strategy Marielle and Rukino were talking about.

“…If the jerk doesn’t show himself, where is he then?”

“Huh? We don’t know…”

“Eh?”

“Why should I know that? Serika made a high-speed patrol, and Yui was sure she could spot anyone hiding. If you’re here, it means there are no Heroes around.”

“Hidden…” Eisuke had a bad feeling.

Yui and Serika were proud of their strength, and after all, he was like that Hero.

“Hey, where are those Yui and Serika now?”

“Why?”

Eisuke spun on his heels and asked quietly. Marielle and Rukino looked at each other.

“Maybe this way? I might be wrong, though.”

“Yeah, well, if you’re wrong here, they’re likely to die.”

“What!?”

“If we don’t hurry, those girls could be in danger.”

Eisuke told them bluntly. They were both shocked.

“What do you mean!?”

“Exactly what I said. What’s with the silly faces? You’re the ones who had been outclassed from the beginning. Those two will definitely fall prey to Hero Ken.

Marielle and Rukino were stunned at Eisuke’s serious words. Rukino cried out in dismay.

“Are you mad!? We already told you that we checked the Hero’s absence! And even if he hid, Yui’s five senses could detect it!”

“Right. If there was a suspicious guy hiding in the bushes, they’d certainly notice him.”

“What…?”

“‘Hiding’ and ‘lurking’ are not the same thing. His purpose is to have fun, right?”

“Wha-what do you mean…?”

“No matter how much he liked it, no one would settle for watching things from the side.”

Especially if Ken thought what he was doing was a game… he probably wouldn’t be satisfied with just watching. A simple truth that even a child could understand. No one would get satisfaction from watching a game from the side. Everyone would want to participate in the game. This was especially true if it was a more skilled player than the others. What’s more, if the game was a versus game, what was the most fun to do?

“Being smarter and beating your opponent. That’s the most interesting part.” Eisuke said, as if speaking to himself. He pointed at Marielle with his chin. “Hey, Marielle, what would you do if you were a Hero?”

“Huh?”

“How refreshing would it be to beat a bunch of good guys who can’t be beaten by your group of men when they come to your territory?” The smile on Eisuke’s face was that of a con man. Definitely that of an evil person. But that was why he understood what the twisted Hero was thinking. They were of the same ilk. The plans of twisted people could only be read by those who were also twisted. Eisuke raised his index finger. “If I were him, I’d do that. If you’re in the middle of a group of ruffians, or if you’re convinced your opponent is going to win, you catch him off guard and say, ‘Actually, I’m the Hero’.”

…At that moment.

Dogooo!

Suddenly, the landscape became dazzlingly bright and a tremendous torrent of energy burst forth from the forest.

“Kyaah!”

The three of them staggered. Eisuke went to reflexively help Marielle, who was going to fall.
