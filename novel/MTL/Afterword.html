In the beginning, the first thing I did when I wanted to write this work on the ground was to consult all the myths I could find out, trying to understand the gods' lords.

But the more I investigated, the more confused I became.

That book is not the same as the legends written on top of this one!

The intelligence was too scattered! It was all contradictory!

At that time, I was not accustomed to the act of reading books, nor did I have any prior knowledge related to mythology, so I was bleary-eyed.

The content of the different documents handed down varied, seemingly due to the historical background of the many peoples and countries, or to religion. I failed to understand this at the time, and just kept consulting books and the internet until I figured it out, and a few of the gods impressed me who knew nothing about them.

This was especially true of the goddess named Freya.

This god of great beauty was targeted by both enemies and companions, amorous and spirited, and at the same time a terrifying queen who would collect the souls of those who died bravely in battle. I feel that self-esteem is also very high, the heart should be strong to be invincible, even if the recklessness of the other party provokes anger, but also will say "so what? I'm Freya, right? Or is it that her feelings for her husband (Oded) are not love, but love?

Just when I was thinking about such things, the Goddess Lord named Freya took an important place in this story along with the girl named Syr.

Among the myths related to the Goddess Lord, there is a legend that I like very much.

The goddess who met her husband (Oded) again embarked on a long journey home, and on the way there seemed to be a large number of flowers blooming on the earth.

I thought that it must be a very beautiful field of flowers.

I wanted to reach that "field of flowers", so I wrote this story.

Then, I felt that I was able to write a little bit about what it would be like after I reached the field of flowers.

The mythical legends are fragmented and full of contradictions, not telling us which is the correct interpretation. The gods are really unreasonable and impersonal. But that's the best kind of god, isn't it? Sometimes they cry like normal girls, don't they?
