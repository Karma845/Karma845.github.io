“O-okay.”

“Alright.”

“If Teruno gives up halfway and comes back home, we’ll contact you again.”

“Okay.”

“Alright, I’m counting on you.”

Then the call ended. Why is Teruno trying to take me home?

Reflecting on it, Teruno did seem a bit different than usual today. Just before leaving, it felt like Teruno was trying to tell me something.

(What is Teruno thinking?)

The only way to find out is to ask her directly. For now, I decided to return to the clubroom.

“Ah, Daiki-kun, you’re back.”

“Sorry for stepping out so suddenly.”

“You have a phone call, so it’s not a problem at all. By the way, can I ask what the call was about?”

“Well, it seems like my sister is trying to come here for some reason.”

“Huh? What do you mean?”

Ai-san tilts her head.

I guess it would be hard to understand without context.

“I don’t know the details, but it seems she’s trying to take me home…”

“Now I’m even more confused. Your sister is Teruno-chan, right?”

“Yes, that’s right.”

“Is she cuter than Kei?”

“Hey.”

“Well, jokes aside, do you have any idea why Teruno-chan is trying to take you home?”

“Uhh…”

I thought about it but couldn’t come up with a reason.

“…I don’t know.”

“In that case, we’ll just have to ask her when she gets here.”

Just then, the sound of the clubroom door slowly opening caught our attention. We turned to look.

“Teruno!”

There, peeking through the slightly open door, was Teruno.

She looked a bit surprised when she heard my voice, then hesitantly entered the room.

“Cute! Are you Teruno-chan by any chance?”

“Y-Yes…”

“Sorry, did I scare you? I’m Ai Shimizu. Feel free to call me Ai-chan.”

“Yes…”

Teruno was completely on guard.

She becomes cautious when she’s in a place full of people she doesn’t know.

“Teruno, Dad was worried.”

“I left a message saying I was going to Onii-chan’s place.”

“Even so, everyone will worry if you suddenly disappear. Anyway, I’m glad you’re actually here. I’ll call Dad now so he can bring you back home.”

“…No.”

“What?”

“I won’t. Onii-chan should come home with me too.”

It’s not unusual for Teruno to be demanding, but this level of stubbornness feels unusual.

“Don’t say that. I’ll go home after we finish stargazing.”

“No, go home with me right now.”

“Why are you saying this?”

“Because Onii-chan…”

“Hey, Hondō’s sister, don’t make things difficult for Hondō.”

“…Who’s this scary person?”

Teruno’s voice is considerably lower than usual—a clear sign she’s in a bad mood.

“Don’t worry, she’s not scary. This is Shimizu-san.”

“…I understand.”

“Teruno?”

“I understand. This scary woman forcefully invited Onii-chan to go stargazing. Onii-chan is too kind to refuse, so he’s here even though he doesn’t want to be…”

“Teruno!”

I was surprised at how loud my own voice was.

“O-Onii-chan?”

“Apologize to Shimizu-san.”

“W-Why? I haven’t done anything wrong…”

“Apologize.”

“Onii-chan…”

The next moment, Teruno suddenly turned toward the door and ran out.

“Teruno!”

I hastily followed her into the hallway, but Teruno was already gone.

“I’m sorry, I have to go find Teruno.”

“Wait.”

“I’m sorry, Shimizu-san, but I have to go find Teruno quickly…”

“Do you have any idea where your sister might go?”

I was stumped by the question. She’s right, I had no idea where Teruno might have gone.

“Well…no, I don’t.”

“…I’ll go too.”

“What?”

“I’ll go with you to search for her. I mean, this happened partly because I carelessly spoke to your sister.”

“This has nothing to do with you, Shimizu-san…”

“You once came looking for someone’s mother even though you had nothing to do with her. Besides, the more people we have, the faster we’ll find her.”

It’s true that searching with multiple people is far more efficient than doing it alone.

“That’s right, that’s why I’ll help too.”

“I’ll help as well.”

“Of course, count me in.”

“Ai-san, Seto-san, Yousuke-san…”

“At a time like this, you should rely on others. You’re always helping others, after all.”

Is that so? I always feel like I’m the one being helped.

But if Shimizu-san says so, maybe that’s true.

“…Thank you, Shimizu-san. Can I count on your help?”

“Sure, leave it to me.”

And so, we all set out to find Teruno.
