Interlude Ⅲ

We, the members of the Sena Club, also gathered at the hotel where Sumisumi met Kanzaki-sensei’s parents.

The restaurant’s location was shared via LINE beforehand. Sumisumi’s contingency plan was for everyone to rush in as reinforcements if the original plan went south.

Following Asaki-chan’s suggestion, we all wore our school uniforms for easy identification.

And just as Sumisumi predicted, Yoryor’s older sister, sporting sunglasses, was casually waiting on a sofa on the restaurant floor. 

“Hey, everyone. Nice to see you again.”

Upon noticing us, she greeted us warmly.

“Oh, you’re on that side,” Yoryor’s sister said disappointedly while looking at Asaki-chan.

“Why did you even make a plan that would inevitably fail? And it would be you who would suffer the most when it failed, right, Arisaka-san’s sister?”

“That’s harsh. I’m genuinely concerned about Shizuru-chan.”

“Then why did you lash out at me in the cafeteria?”

At that moment, Yoryor’s sister’s expression changed.

“What are you talking about?”

“You deliberately provoked a fight between me and Arisaka-san to deflect criticism towards Kisumi-kun. Aren’t you quite overprotective?”

“Well, I was the one who dragged him into th—”

“Onee-chan, you treat Kisumi differently, don’t you?” Yoryor interjected into the conversation.

“Oh, so yesterday’s enemy is today’s friend?”

“Don’t try to dodge. I’m asking sincerely,” Yoryor stood before her sister with a determined expression.

“We’ll talk about this later. Since Shizuru-chan is working hard now, it’d be best not to interfere. Your intrusion is unwelcome.”

The older sister rose to her feet and blocked our path. 

“Kisumi-kun mentioned this himself, that he’ll fail as a stand-in boyfriend if he’s on his own,” Asaki-chan stood beside Yoryor. 

“He always underestimates himself. If only he had a bit more confidence.”

“I agree. But I think Kisumi-kun’s modesty is part of his charm.”

“…You have the guts to say that in front of Yor-chan, huh?”

Yoryor’s sister assessed us with a glance.

“If anything, it’s impressive how confident you are. Aren’t you planning to swoop in at the crucial moment when Kisumi-kun and Sensei are cornered so you can resolve everything smoothly? You really know how to seize the opportunity.”

Asaki-san provocatively smiled with a refreshing expression.

“…..”

The older sister lowered her sunglasses slightly and shot us a displeased look.

“Hasekura-san, I’ll leave the inside to you. Go ahead,” Yoryor said.

“Are you really handing over the good part?”

“Aren’t you a class rep? Your partner is working hard for our homeroom teacher, so go lend him a hand.”

Yoryor entrusted Asaki-chan with this task. It was a weighty baton that surpassed any challenge or trust.

“—If Kisumi-kun has a change of heart, don’t complain.

“That’s impossible,” Yoryor scoffed.

Asaki-san took the lead towards the restaurant.

“What’s gotten into you, Yor-chan? You’re surprisingly bold.”

“I want to talk to you alone, onee-chan. Let’s have a sister quarrel here.”

“…That sounds interesting.”

Yoryor’s sister stepped aside easily to clear out our path.

“Then, we’ll assist Sena.”

“Indeed. Don’t mind us, Yor-senpai!”

Nanamu and Sayu-chan followed suit.

“Hinaka-chan too, let’s go. She’ll be fine.”

“Yoryor…” 

Just like during the inter-class ball game, Sumisumi had secretly asked me a favor. He was worried that Yoryor might get pressured when talking to her sister. 

However, that was an unnecessary concern. 

The current Arisaka Yorka could handle it.

“Good luck!” I called out to her back then hurried to catch up with everyone.
