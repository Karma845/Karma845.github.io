Chapter 2: Spring Time Machine Disco

Predicting things is the most interesting game in the world.

It has never once gotten old for me.

Like—

—If I put my head down and start studying seriously for a test, I probably won’t fail.

—If I make an effort to socialize with people more this year, I can make friends.

—If I talk to Nito during the next break, I can patch things up with her.

—If I knuckle down and study hard for entrance exams this summer, I might be able to scrape through and get into a university.

Relying on those “predictions”, I fooled myself into thinking I still had some potential and ended up endlessly procrastinating. And by the time I realized they were more like delusions than actual predictions, it was already too late.

It was too late to recover that lost time.

At that point, I couldn’t really change my attitude anymore.

I wasted time pointlessly, not lifting a finger… and now I’m stuck with this disappointing present.

That’s more or less how my high school life ended…

And today, here I am, having made several more predictions.

This time, though, it’s not a delusion. It’s a genuine prediction. Hopefully.

“So, it’s still just a theory, but…”

I showed Makoto the notebook I had hastily written last night, sacrificing my precious sleep in the process. I pointed to the jam-packed page where that theory was written.

“I wanna test it first. To see if my prediction is correct. As in, if it’s really possible to change the past!”

This is probably the most motivated I’ve been since starting high school.

Something unfamiliar and hot was rising up in my chest. Maybe this is what they call “hope.” When I saw that faint glimmer of possibility, I couldn’t help but seize the moment and take action.

But, compared to how wound up I was—

“Hmm.”’

Makoto, sitting across from me, lethargically sipped her iced tea through a straw.

She turned away and stared out the window.

“Change the past, to save Nito-senpai. . is that the TLDR?”

It was the day after the graduation ceremony.

Despite it being midday, the fast-food joint near our school was packed.

There were men in suits who appeared to be working, and young people around our age who seemed to be studying. A group of lively uni students sat at a nearby table. Just the sight of them was enough to drain my mental energy, knowing I was a ronin now.

But now isn’t the time to talk about stuff like that.

The matter at hand was yesterday’s mysterious phenomenon. Based on that, I’d constructed a theory. One that could save Nito by changing the past. And, I was determined to test it out, no matter what it took.

“So, I need your help, Makoto!”

Again, I delivered my heartfelt plea to her.

“Look, I’m a graduate now. I can’t get into the school without your help,

‘cause you’re still a student.”

The trigger for time travel is likely playing the piano.

By playing Nito’s song, I was sent back three years to the day of the entrance ceremony. And then I came back to the present by playing it again.

So, the trigger has to be the piano. In short, if I touched those keys again, if I played Nito’s song in the Astronomy Club Room—I just might be able to travel back three years.

Sure, there were a lot of uncertainties. Like, there’s no guarantee the piano was the real trigger, and even if I could travel to the past, I didn’t know if it’d be the same past as the one three years ago. As long as I didn’t know the rules behind it, anything could happen. That being so, all I needed to do was experiment.

Well, there’s no point just thinking about it, I have to test it out and see the results. For that to happen I needed Makoto’s help.

That’s why I asked Makoto to meet up with me today on such short notice.

But Makoto seemed surprisingly lethargic.

“. .Senpai, you sometimes get like this, don’t you?”

Makoto let out a bored sigh.

“It’s like you’re a science geek or something. You’re just throwing all these technical terms at me.”

“Ah. . yeah, I s’pose you’re right. Sorry.”

As someone who once aspired to be an astronomer, I sometimes get carried away with my explanations. I guess some people might just say I’m an otaku who talks too fast. My grades were too shit to confidently call myself a science major though.

“And…”

Makota finally turned to face me.

“…I think it’s impossible, to be honest.”

“Impossible? What is?”

“Everything, from start to finish.”

Crossing her arms and tilting her head slightly, Makoto continued.

“Time travel? Changing the past? You don’t seriously believe that’s possible, do you?”

“. .Uh.”

“I mean, you can’t just expect me to suddenly believe something like. It’s just too much of a stretch.”

She had a point.

Well, yeah—going back in time was something you only saw in Sci-Fi manga and anime. Even if it’s possible, saving Nito is a huge leap to make.

“But Makoto, didn’t you say your memories changed?”

“Yes, that’s true.”

Makoto gave an immediate nod.

“It’s true my memories have changed from you being a loner to actually having friends.”

“See? That’s proof, isn’t it? Proof that the past was changed.”

“Nah, I think it was just a misunderstanding. Maybe both of us were just confused and temporarily went a bit loopy.”

“You really think so?”

“It’s more likely than time traveling back three years, isn’t it?

“. .Uh, hmm.”

Another fair point. It’s hard for me to argue with that…

“Plus, even if you can change it, what’re you planning to actually do?

How’re you going to save Nito-senpai?”

“Oh! Yes, about that…”

I flipped through the pages of my notebook and moved from the

“Phenomenon Analysis” section to the “Nito Rescue Plan” section. I’d carefully planned that part too, of course.

“There are lots of things to think about, but I figured priority number one should be the Astronomy Club’s survival.”

“The Astronomy Club?”

“Yep. It was disbanded after my first year. We kept using the club room without permission for a while, but it was basically just squatting at that point.”

The Astronomy Club where Nito and I met. When we started high school, it was hanging on by a thread. That year, it fell below the minimum number of members required and was disbanded by the time our second year rolled around. In turn, it was one of the main reasons Nito and I drifted apart.

Anyway, we continued using the club room without permission, and Makoto, who came later, also hung out there. But there’s no doubt that it deepened the rift between Nito and me.

I should add, because of that, Makoto isn’t technically a “junior of the Astronomy Club.” Instead, she’s more like a fellow member of club room squatters. When you put it like that, it kind of has a student protest vibe to it.

“So, first, I’m thinking of recruiting members to stop the club from disappearing.”

“Mm. And how exactly is that gonna save Nito-senpai? Like, if the Astronomy Club exists, Nito-senpai won’t disappear?”

“. .Well, I was thinking about a lot of things yesterday.”

I crossed my arms and looked down at the table. An ad for a part time job was stuck to my tray.

『Would you like to join our staff? Hourly wages starting from 960 yen~.

Students and housewives welcome.』

“Her music career seemed seriously tough, especially after second year. I dunno how to put it—she was really famous but she became gloomier than before. She seemed more like the singer NITO even when she was at school.”

“. .Really? I probably didn’t notice.”

“Yeah, well, I guess I didn’t really notice it back then either. But you know, I was going through old pictures on my phone recently, and I remembered something. Maybe because of the gap between my perception and reality, I always thought, 'Wow, Nito is amazing,' and that she was doing great. But maybe there were signs that it wasn’t really like that.”

They were pictures of Nito from her first and second year, and some playful videos that I’d taken.

While subtle, I thought I caught glimpses of Nito’s changes in them. Her sad look and the lack of energy in her voice. The random negative comments, or the fact that she wasn’t as talkative. Maybe those were her signs.

“Honestly, though, I can’t be sure if that’ll lead to the reason for her disappearance.”

I’d be the first to admit that.

The reality was there’d been no new info from the authorities since. There was rampant speculation online about the cause, but at the end of the day, they were all just rumors.

She might’ve disappeared for reasons completely beyond my imagination.

“You know what? I thought the first step was to be there for her, and be the kind of person she could talk to about her problems. But to be honest, I ended 38

up giving up, and we drifted apart. Now, though, I really wanna step up and support her, in a way that truly matters.”

“. .I see.”

“That’s why I wanna give it a shot. I need your help so I can play the piano in the club room.”

“Hmm. .”

Makoto fiddled with the wrapper of her straw with her fingertips. The damp wrapper gradually crumbled under her nails.

At the very least, it didn’t look like she agreed with my opinion.

“. .Why do you seem so against it?” I asked hesitantly.

“You still don’t believe it?”

“It’s not just that I don’t believe it.”

Makoto leaned her elbow on the table. She stared out the window and let out a sigh tinged with sadness.

“. .Is it really okay to change the past?”

“—Hey, you guys.”

“Wha—?!”

Someone abruptly called out to us from behind.

Startled, I spun around…

“. .Who’re you?”

It was someone I didn’t know.

A random young guy was standing behind us. He had a dignified and well-defined face, a slight wrinkle between his eyebrows, thin lips that exuded confidence, and a well-toned body. He appeared to be in his early twenties, give or take a few years. He had this kind of. . intimidating aura, like a tough older brother, as he looked down on us.

Huh? What’s this? Don’t tell me we’re about to get mugged or something. .?

“Oh, I was having lunch with my friends over there and happened to overhear your conversation.”

“Oh, s-sorry. Were we being too loud!?”

“Nah, not that. Do you guys by any chance. .”

He lowered his voice a bit.

“. .know Nito?”

“. .Errr, um. .”

Ah right, did he hear us mention her name? Oops, I should’ve lowered my voice a bit. But, how should I respond? Is it okay to say we know Nito? She’s a celebrity, so should we keep it a secret? Plus, this guy looks kind of scary.

I glanced at Makoto for help, but she was looking out the window, pretending not to notice.

Well, I can’t blame her. Even as a guy, I’m pretty scared in a situation like this.

“…Yeah. We went to the same school as her.”

After hesitating for a bit, I decided to answer honestly. Besides, it felt like lying to this person would be difficult.

“So, um, we’re pretty worried about her. .”

“Figured as much.”

He let out a short breath.

“Actually, I’m also an alumni from that school. Graduated last year.”

“Oh, I see. So you’re a senpai. .”

“Right.”

He gave a small nod.

Something about his gesture was boyish, making him seem less of a delinquent than I initially thought. At first glance, he definitely seemed like a tough guy—

“. .Wait! Rokuyo-senpai!?”

I couldn’t help yelling as it suddenly clicked.

“You’re Haruki Rokuyo-senpai, aren’t you!?”

That well-defined face and commanding presence. No doubt about it. I remember seeing him numerous times at school.

This was Rokuyo-senpai, who was so famous at our high school that not a single person hadn’t heard of him. He was the heart of the popular crowd, excelling in both sports and academics. But, he wasn’t the flashy type. He was serious and straightforward, and if something didn’t sit right with him, he would speak up to both teachers and seniors. It’s like he stepped out of a badass battle manga or something—that’s who the famous Rokuyo-senpai was.

“Oh, yeah, that’s me. You know my name and everything, huh?”

“Well, anyone who went to our school would remember you. .”

Speaking of which, the Rokuyo family are wealthy landowners in the Kichijoji¹ area, and one of their ancestors is even mentioned in Japanese history textbooks. On the other side of the fence, the rising star of the Sakamoto family is my cousin Kippei-kun, who won the Minister’s Award² in an invention contest when he was still in elementary school.

“Anyway, about Nito—I was shocked when I saw the news too.”

“Oh, that’s why you asked. By the way, Rokuyo-senpai, did you know Nito?”

“. .Sort of.”

Rokuyo-senpai’s face clouded over slightly for some reason.

“We were working together at some events, and we talked a bit. .”

Ah, right. Nito and Rokuyo-senpai were often involved in committees and event organization together. That’s probably where they interacted. But his expression seemed to imply there was more to it than that. It looked like he had some thoughts about Nito…

“. .Did something happen?”

I couldn’t help but ask.

“Did you and Nito have a disagreement or something?”

“No, nothing like that.”

Rokuyo-senpai smiled.

His lips twisted into a pained expression, almost like he was making fun of himself. I didn’t know why.

“I guess you could say we were rivals. . No, we couldn’t even be that.”

What did he mean?

What happened between them for Rokuyo-senpai to make a face like that. .?

Before I could ask, he continued.

“Anyway, I’ve been wondering what happened to her. .”

As if trying to wrap up the conversation, he pushed on.

“Then, I heard you say something about being able to help her, and I just had to ask.”

“I see. . Sorry, but I don’t have much solid information either. .”

“Hmm.”

Rokuyo-senpai reached into his pocket and took out his smartphone.

“Anyway, give me your contact info. If you find out anything about Nito, I wanna know. If there’s anything I can do to help, I’ll do what I can for you guys.”

“Huh, ah, r-really. .?”

It was a bit sudden, but I couldn’t exactly refuse when he put it that way.

I hesitantly took out my own smartphone and exchanged Line IDs. With a simple, “See ya”, Rokuyo-senpai returned to the table where his friends were waiting.

The tension finally seemed to ease. Across the table, Makoto took a deep breath and let out a “Phew…”

“I’m totally exhausted. . That was so scary. .”

“Yeah, I didn’t expect to run into him, here of all places.. ”

He didn’t seem like a bad guy, but the tension was overwhelming. Just being in the presence of his aura was enough to drain my energy. .

“Anway, I don’t have the energy to think about anything anymore. .”

With a tired expression, Makoto slurped the rest of her drink in one go.

Then, as if resigned, she rested her chin on her hand.

“. .For now, should we give going to school a try?”

The way she muttered it made it sound like she was talking to herself.

“I still don’t believe it, but let’s try it at least once.”

“Thanks, you’re a lifesaver.”

“—Honestly, don’t you suck too badly at this?”

In the Astronomy Club Room, Makoto sighed deeply after I’d been struggling at the piano for nearly twenty minutes.

“Why haven’t you been able to play it even once today? How’d you even manage to do it yesterday?

“J-just hold on a sec!”

I played Nito’s song on my smartphone again and wiped my sweaty palms on my pants.

“Well, yesterday I managed somehow ‘cause I had some momentum going. .

But now that I’m calmer, it’s actually pretty tough, haha…”

I can’t play it.

Nito’s melody is just too difficult and I can’t play it.

I wasn’t the type to touch instruments much to begin with, and Nito’s song itself was very complex. It wasn’t just the white keys that needed to be used, but the black keys too. The hurdle was just too high for an amateur.

Rather, wasn’t it a miracle that I was even able to play it so smoothly yesterday?

“Ahhh, what should I do? Should I make some sheet music or something?”

“No, forget about tricks like that. Let’s just keep trying. C’mon, play it, play it.”

“Mmm, well, I guess so.”

I started hitting the keys again.

Still, my fingers didn’t move the way I wanted them to, and I kept having to start over again and again.

“If only there were notes like in rhythm games, this would be wayyy easier to play,” I grumbled to myself as I let my fingers dance on the keys.

“I’m telling you, if there were notes falling from above I’d nail it without a single mistake.”

“Sadly that only exists in the world of games.”

Makoto crossed her arms, acting coldly.

“I don’t have all afternoon. If this is gonna take too long, I’ll just go home.”

“No, no, no, you’ve got plenty of free time, right? I mean, it’s not like you have a part-time job or plans to hang out with friends, do you?”

“So what? There are still plenty of things to do. I wanna play Apex and I haven’t even watched the VOD of my favorite V-tuber from yester— huh?”

Makoto had apparently noticed something as she was speaking.

She looked at my hands on the keys.

“Wait. . Did you just manage to play it?”

“. .Huh?”

I lifted my finger from the last key I just pressed. I looked down at the piano in front of me too.

She might be right. I probably didn’t miss a single note just now.

“Yeah, somehow I actually played it—”

—Lights danced around me.

In the darkness, they began to orbit around my body.

It’s the same as before.

The same phenomenon as yesterday, happening again.

And gradually the lights sped up, my vision turning completely white—

“. .Meguri-kun?”

When my vision cleared—Nito was in front of me.

“What’s wrong? What’re you doing?”

Her glossy black hair, her eyes peering at me with curiosity, her straight posture, and the uniform that suited her slender body so well—

“. .A-ah, uh, that. .”

With a slight thumping in my chest, I looked around.

The sun-bleached curtains, the musty smell, an upright piano, an old radio cassette player, and an old world map with Germany divided into East and West.

There’s no mistaking it, this was the club room. The Astronomy Club Room.

As predicted, I’d returned to the day of the entrance ceremony.

“Well, you see, I was just thinking about a lot of things…”

I took another look at Nito.

“. .Sorry, I was spacing out.”

Despite it being the second time meeting again, despite it being a sight I’d seen yesterday—Standing in front of her, my heart filled with unconditional love. Just the fact that Nito was there, having lost her once, made me uncontrollably happy.

Unaware of my feelings, she said, “Hmm? Hey, wait a sec, you’re acting suspiciously.”

Nito shuffled closer to me.

The faint scent of her shampoo, and her body heat where we touched. .

“Hey, were you staring at me while you were spacing out, Meguri-kun? The fee for looking at me is 600 yen per second.”

“No way, that’s way too expensive! It’d cost me around 40,000 yen just to look for a minute.”

“Obviously! I’m not some cheap girl, y’know.”

That’s right. I always used to joke around and laugh with Nito like this…

“. .Oh yeah. Um, just to be sure, can I ask you something?”

I took a step back and cleared my throat.

“What month and year is it right now?”

“. .It’s April 5th, 20XX.”

I thought she might find my question strange, but to my surprise, Nito answered easily.

“I mean, you could just check the calendar on your smartphone for that…”

“Ah! Right! That’s true, haha. . It really is April 5th.”

The latest model at the time.

Sure enough, the date Nito mentioned was right there on the screen.

“Yep, yep, just as I thought. .”

As I pocketed my smartphone, I came to a few realizations. I felt like some of my questions had been answered.

Firstly, the trigger to go to the past is definitely playing the piano.

So, to return to the present, I probably just have to play Nito’s song again.

It was an uphill battle earlier, so it won’t be easy to go back and forth, but for now, I’m relieved that I won’t be stuck in the past.

Next is figuring out how time works regarding the destination of time travel.

From the looks of it, it seems I’ve picked up from where I left off last time.

Put simply, every time I play the piano, I will alternate between advancing time here—three years in the past—and the present I just came from. It doesn’t seem like time moves forward in one while I’m in the other.

So—

“Hmm, seems easy enough…”

I can easily manipulate the past to suit my needs this way.

If time moved forward in the past while I was back in the present, there was a chance I might miss something important. But if I can spend time in both timelines without that happening, then I shouldn’t miss out on anything.

Although, it’s also a tad scary to think time in the present might advance while I’m here…

“So, I was thinking about what it’d take to revive the Astronomy Club.”

It’s better to strike while the iron is hot. So, it’s probably best to talk to Nito about it today.

That was explained when the Astronomy Club was disbanded in my previous high school life. The club was scrapped because it didn’t have the minimum of four members. Therefore, this time, all I have to do is change that outcome.

“So, I figured I’d try recruiting. It’s still early in the school year, but I think we can find two people. Maybe.”

To be honest, I do feel quite a bit of pressure to recruit those two people…

It’s super intimidating to talk to students I don’t know. But Nito’s future was at stake. Her life was at stake. That being the case, I can’t just cower here—especially since I’ve already been through high school once before. As the “me” who has returned from the future, there’s every chance that recruiting new members will be a piece of cake. Hopefully.

I have to turn these wasted three years into something Nito can be proud of.

Despite appearances, this is my second time around in high school, so rounding up members is going to be a piece of cake! Surely.

Huh? Did I get the wrong end of the stick? Maybe she didn’t want to join the club?

“Sure, I’ve thought about it too! Joining the Astronomy Club, I mean!

I figured as much.

“But, I wanna know more about you first! I wanna know your intentions, Sakamoto!”

“My intentions?”

“Yep. You’re. . working really hard, aren’t you? Like, to keep the club going.”

Ah, yeah, that makes sense. Even from an outsider’s perspective, it was pretty obvious that I was desperately trying to recruit members.

“But I was wondering. . why? Is it to get closer to Chika or something? If that’s the reason, it worries me. She’s a serious girl. I don’t want you to have that kind of ulterior motive.”

“No no no, that’s not my intention!”

I hastily shook my head.

“I seriously do want to become an astronomer! That’s why I wanted to keep the Astronomy Club going. .”

“. .Oh, really?”

Igarashi-san tilted her head, looking surprised.

“‘Cause, from my perspective, I couldn’t help but think maybe you actually liked Chika or something. .”

Hearing her say that made me regret my knee-jerk excuse. Without thinking, I’d shot down the “reason” for getting close that Igarashi-san mentioned.

But. . I wasn’t being honest. The main reason I wanted to keep the Astronomy Club going was so I could stay close to Nito. I definitely have that feeling for her, and I should’ve shared that.

Igarashi-san truly cherishes Nito, of that much I can be certain. Certain because of all the stories she’s shared with me, and what I heard on the day of our graduation. When I found out Nito had disappeared and I heard her heartbreaking cry. That cry was one only someone who truly cares about Nito would make.

“. .Sorry, I lied.”

I came clean.

“You’re spot on. I’m working hard to recruit more members for the sake of being with Nito. I do want to be an astronomer too, but I can’t deny that either.”

“So, that means…”

Igarashi-san peered at my face with an unexpectedly calm expression.

“You like Chika?”

“. .Yeah, I do.”

After a moment’s hesitation, I honestly admitted it.

“I’ve liked her for a long time.”

Crap, I accidentally said something I shouldn’t have.

“For a long time. ” It hasn’t even been a month since Nito and I met in this timeline. That comment was clearly out of place. But, I got carried away in the heat of the moment.

“. .I see.”

Whether it went over her head, or she misunderstood something, Igarashi-san dropped her gaze as if she was satisfied.

“So, you really do like Chika.”

“Well. . yeah.”

Argh. This is really embarrassing. What the heck is this!? Seriously, why am I getting into deep love talk like this when I should be calm!?” To be fair, it’s the kind of situation where you should talk about it, but man, I said “I like her”

with such confidence! I feel like my face is about to burst into flames!

But, as if to wake me from such embarrassing thoughts—

“. .Then, wouldn’t it be better not to get in the way?”

What a heartbreakingly lonely expression.

Igarashi-san’s voice quivered, as if she was on the verge of tears, but she forced a smile—

“If you’re really that serious about Chika and she goes along with it…wouldn’t me joining the club just be a hassle for her?”

Those words from her caught me by surprise. I was under the impression this girl had no intention of reining in her possessiveness towards Nito.

“. .Y’know, even I’ve got some level of self-awareness.”

I guess Igarashi-san saw right through me.

She gave me a wry smile.

“I’ve been thinking about it too. That this kind of dependency isn’t good, I mean. I’m a high schooler now, so I’ve gotta learn to be more independent.”

Another surprise. That she had the ability to look at herself objectively like that.

“But, I also think it’s not right to force ourselves apart, if that makes sense?

At the same time, getting too close and weighing her down is also not what I want. I wonder what the best thing to do is. .”

Again, it resonated with me.

Before the rewrite, I’d never had the chance to experience things like this. I had barely any friends, and I never came into contact with the struggles of someone my age. It doesn’t matter if it’s a smiling classmate, or a flashy girl who seems like they haven’t got a care in the world—everyone has struggles in their life.

Knowing that, I can start to imagine that maybe this girl also had some regrets at the graduation ceremony. Maybe she thought there were things she could’ve done, and things she should’ve done in those three years.

“. .Maybe you should try finding a new type of relationship?”

I was half talking to myself as I gave Igarashi-san that suggestion.

“Not dependence, but a relationship where you can stand by her side. As equals. Why not try looking for it in the Astronomy Club?”

I could almost see Igarashi-san’s eyes pop as they suddenly went wide.

She stared straight at me through her strange colored contact lens.

Igarashi-san’s facial features looked pretty childish when I saw her like this.

Compared to Nito, who looks her age, or Makoto, who looks mature, Igarashi-san’s face could easily be mistaken for a junior high schooler’s, or maybe even an elementary schooler’s.

In those features, I could see her innocence, or maybe, her purity.

She stared at me with a confused look for a moment.

“. .Ugh.”

She broke eye contact and looked down, gripping the chains of the swing tightly, and let out a pained voice.

“Huh? Hey, what’s wrong?”

“. .I wanted you to be a jerk.”

“Say what?”

“I wanted you to be an asshole, Sakamoto.”

“What? Why?”

“Because if this is how it’s gonna be…”

Igarashi-san lifted her head. She looked straight at me, eyes swimming with tears. She looked equal parts regretful and happy as she muttered her next words.

“. .Then there’s no way I can stand in the way of your relationship with her.”
