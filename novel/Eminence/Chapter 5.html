Chapter 5: Makabe Shizuru Is Not Cut Out For Romance

Act 1

『Makabe Shizuru is stalking Takinami Ruika.』

Such rumors were being spread.

More precisely, Makabe Shizuru is following her around, taking advantage of the fact that he’s a member of the library committee, and getting close to Takinami Ruika.

A guy in my class was kind enough to tell me. He also gave me advice, “Stop with the stalking.”

They have no trust if I do say so myself.

***

“But interestingly, there was no damage done.”

During the lunch break, after finishing my lunch, I looked around the classroom and said to Karube Kagemitsu.

According to a classmate of mine, rumors started circulating during yesterday’s lunch break and have spread quite a lot.

But unfortunately, my name doesn’t ring any bells, so it halved the effectiveness of the rumor. After all, when someone hears that rumor, the first thing they would think of is who that guy is. In fact, when I went to the cafeteria to get a cup of coffee a few minutes ago, I didn’t hear anyone whispering anything to each other or blatantly pointing their finger behind my back when they saw my face.

“Rumors are more interesting the more famous the subjects are.”

“That’s how it is.”

Karube stated nonchalantly, and I nodded in return.

That’s why, rather than such half-baked rumors, the fact that Masaru-sensei used to be an aspiring opera singer is much more interesting. Masaru-sensei is a large man with a loud voice, and even when the doors and windows are closed, one can hear him speaking from the classroom next door. When I heard this story, I was deeply convinced.

“Who do you think is the source of the rumors?”

“Naoi.”

Karube responded immediately.

Ahead of his gaze, Naoi’s group was happily making noise. There were less than ten people in the group, including several girls.

“It’s possible.”

I had an argument with him just yesterday morning. That was enough to make him angry and give my name a bad stigma.

“But Naoi would never do such a thing.”

But the timing is just too good. If it really was Naoi’s work, I have to say that it was shortsighted.

“I don’t understand why you regard Naoi so highly.”

“Half of it is just my ideals.”

Ideal that this is how Naoi is supposed to be. I’m just imposing that ideal on him.

“If it’s not Naoi, then…”

Karube then still looked at Naoi’s group.

“Well, who cares who did it? It will disappear soon. Especially since it’s not that interesting.”

Rumor has a short shelf life.

§§§

At the library after school that day.

I was at the check-out counter with my textbooks spread out.

“Oh my, found the delinquent librarian.”

Suddenly, I was called by such a nickname.

I looked up and saw a beautiful senior student there.

“What’s more, a stalker.”

She giggles.

“Did you hear about the rumors?”

“Yes. How did that happen?”

When Takinami-senpai answered in this way, rather than tilting her head at the inexplicable, she seemed to be dismayed by the rumor.

Of course, she has a good idea of where it came from.

“That said, if my stalker is Makabe-kun, I would welcome it with open arms.”

“I don’t think you should say that anywhere but here. You never know if some guy will misunderstand.”

I reminded her, who was saying stuff in a playful manner.

She shrugged her shoulders.

“I guess so. I’ll be careful. By the way, are you studying?”

“Yes, I was having a hard time understanding something in class today.”

So, taking advantage of the lack of students coming to the counter, I was reviewing the textbooks here instead of doing it at home.

“Well, but I’ll do the rest when I get home.”

I close the textbook.

I have come to some understanding. The bigger problem is that my work as a member of the library committee might be neglected. Just like now, I was unaware of Takininami-senpai’s presence. I had assumed that I would be able to tell when a person would approach, but it seems that I was inadvertently absorbed in what I was doing.

“Why? Why not just do it here?”

“Un?”

“I mean, you have a great teacher just in front of you.”

I was looking only at Takinami-senpai, who was strangely cute and good at what she was doing.

“Are you implying that you’re going to teach me?”

“Yes. I’m quite good at academics if I do say so myself. Shall we go to the reading table?”

Takinami-senpai said that and walked toward the reading tables.

While I was wondering what to do, she put her bag on the table by the window. She seemed to be sitting with her back to the counter. This would mean that I would be sitting with my back to the window, looking towards the counter. If someone comes in, they would be in my line of sight. If they are regulars, they might call out directly to me.

I decided to take advantage of her kindness and quickly put a message on the counter, “Currently away.”

We sat across from each other at the table. I spread out the textbook between us.

“Then, please do.”

“Sure.”

Takinami-senpai smiles.

“Ah, wait a second.”

She seemed to have remembered something. Then she took an eyeglass case out of her bag. Naturally, there were glasses inside. They were slender and square-shaped, much like the ones I had seen at the store the other day.

“Did you buy one?”

“Yes. Of course, it’s just for show though. I never thought I would be able to use it so soon.”

Takinami-senpai smiled innocently as she spoke.

It must not have been a cheap purchase, but she bought it for a performance she never knows when will happen. Well, I guess she was going to make this development happen somehow though.

“Then―”

Takinami-senpai unbuttoned the top button of her blouse.

“Sorry for the wait. Here, go ahead.”

“No, wait a minute.”

Takinami-senpai stopped moving while leaning to look over the textbook. I can almost see something beyond her wide-open collar.

“What?”

“What do you mean, what?”

What if someone sees? Thinking so, I checked my surroundings and then realized. I realized that all the remaining students in the library were on the other side of the room. They could only see her backside even if they turned their head this way. I was the only one facing her from the front.

“Oh, this?”

Takinami-senpai looks at her own chest.

“I thought it would motivate you. Being a serious yet alluring teacher.”

“Of course not…”

On the contrary, I’m so concerned about it that what I would be taught is bound to pass from my right ear to my left ear.

“Really? Don’t you expect a reward afterward? Like, ‘Well done, alright, it’s time for the reward you’ve been waiting for.’”

She then smiled meaningfully.

But soon, her face turns serious.

“Come on, get to it. Are you really motivated to do this?”

“That’s my line.”

Even if I were to refuse the reward, I would not be able to focus with her giving a lecture dressed like that.

“Let’s see. I’ve done this once before, so you will get bored. I’ll think of a new situation.”

“……”

That’s not the issue, though.

While I sigh inwardly in disgust, Takinami-senpai asks me while buttoning up her blouse.

“By the way, Shizuru. Where was the ‘Name of the Rose?’”

“I can tell you, but I won’t come to pick you up even if you’re starving.”

According to what I had heard before, Takinami Ruika had maintained excellent grades through hard work and was therefore very good at teaching.

When that was over, it was just time to close the library, so Takinami-senpai also left. I closed the library as usual and returned the key to the staff room before leaving school.

And so it was when I came down to the entrance.

“Takinami-san, maybe you shouldn’t go to the library so often.”

“Oh, why is that?”

The voice came unexpectedly, and I quickly hid myself behind the stairs.

The one who answered was, of course, Takinami-senpai. Even if her name had not been mentioned, I would have recognized her just by hearing her voice. But who was the other person? At least it was not Takajou-senpai’s calm, sweet voice.

“Because, you know, that rumor.”

“Rumor?”

Takinami-senpai asks back.

It was a topic we had discussed just a few minutes before. There was no way she could have not known. But I guess she dared to ask back.

“The librarian is stalking you, Takinami-san.”

“Oh, that thing. It’s alright. He’s not the type of person who would do such a thing.”

She smoothly said.

“But it’s also possible that he’ll misunderstand your intentions.”

“That’s right, that’s right.”

It appears that there are several people advising Takinami-senpai. Her classmates, probably.

I heard that after school, Takinami-senpai passes the time by chatting with her friends in the classroom and guiding them in their studies. I guess she came to the library after doing so today and rejoined the girls on her way home. I don’t know if it was by chance or by appointment.

“Besides, I don’t think it’s Takinami-san’s image to bother with a guy like that…”

“I-Is it…?”

Takinami-senpai responded with a puzzled look on her face.

I think they’re saying the most reasonable things. I’m sure that they’re one of those who hold the ideal image of Takinami Ruika.

“It doesn’t suit her image, huh…”

I mumbled to myself.

I silently turned on my feet.

I’ll tell the teacher I forgot something in the library, borrow the key, spend a little time there, and then leave.

◇◇◇

I got home thirty minutes later than usual.

Fortunately, Hasumi-senpai didn’t get angry at such little difference, nor did she ask me why I was late. Rather, she said she was glad that the timing coincided with Uncle’s return so that they don’t need to eat separately.

And so, after dinner, at night―

“Shizuru.”

While I was in my room reviewing the lessons taught by Takinami-san, I heard a voice from outside.

When I opened the door, Hasumi-senpai was standing there. She was wearing wide pants and a shirt, a very conservative fashion.

“I accidentally made two cups of coffee, so I’ll give you the other one.”

Indeed, she had a cup of coffee in both hands. But what kind of accident is that? Was she playing or something?

“Thank you.”

When I was about to accept the cup, Hasumi-senpai quickly pulled it away. My hand shook.

“…Did something happen?”

She looked at me with a straight face and asked.

It seems like the other cup of coffee she accidentally made was for this. How perceptive.

“Nothing really.”

“Are you telling the truth?”

Hasumi-senpai asked again.

“Really.”

I answered her again, clearly.

Nothing in particular. My decision just happened naturally that way.

“Then, good.”

Hasumi-senpai finally handed me the cup.

“If you have something on your mind, you can tell me.”

“Um, Hasumi-senpai.”

She was about to go back to her room, but I stopped her. Hasumi-senpai turns around and looks at me.

“What?” She asked.

“Why did you tell Naoi that we’re siblings?”

I asked her.

“Why, huh…”

She mumbled as she thought about the meaning of the question in her head and brought her coffee cup to her mouth. She leaned against the hallway wall.

“I’ve known Naoi since junior high school and I know he admires me. I guess I wanted to tell him the truth.”

Just about what I expected. She really cannot tell a lie.

“Hasumi-senpai, you’re pretty sincere, did you know that?”

“Sincere? No way. It’s not that big of a deal.”

Hasumi-senpai laughs, as if I said something outrageous.

“I really don’t want to tell a lie, you see. Once you lie, you either have to apologize afterwards or take it to your grave. Both are difficult for me to do.”

“……”

“I especially don’t want to lie about how I feel. It feels like I’m killing myself.”

This thought Hasumi-senpai has must have been her true intention.

That’s why it’s even more heartbreaking.

I should have been like this. I should not have lied to Naoi. I should have believed him, believed myself, who wanted Naoi Kyouhei to be the kind of person I wanted him to be, and told him the truth.

‘It feels like I’m killing myself, huh?’

I wish I had half as much sincerity as she did.

“In your eyes, Hasumi-senpai, I must look like I’m suffocating, right?”

“Hmm…”

Hasumi-senpai thought for a moment, and then,

“Before, sure. Now, it has reduced.”

“Now?”

“I wonder who was talking about somebody else’s b*****s, saying he wants to see me in my swimsuit. Is that what you call faithful, or rather, loyal to your desires? Haah, what a perverted little brother I have.”

“Uh…”

I was at a loss for words at Hasumi-senpai’s taunting words.

“I’m sorry…”

“It’s fine. I’ll get angry if you go too far, but so far you haven’t. Though, I’ll really get angry if you go too far…”

She said it twice, as it was important. And the second time, she looked at me with stern eyes. I’ll be careful.

“You’re saying whatever you want. Then, how about you saying something to Takinami-san too?”

“Eh…?”

“You like her, right? I don’t know what’s holding you back, but maybe you should say something to her and see how she reacts. Then, think about what you’ll do after seeing it.”

“…I could.”

That’s right, the problem and solution were already there.

But things were changing.

“Back to the topic―”

And I cut to the chase.

“I think it’s better if you don’t mention that we are siblings anymore.”

“Of course, I’m not planning on going around saying it to people. I would need to mention Dad if that happens after all.”

Hasumi-senpai chuckles.

“That said, I don’t think we need to have a lie ready in case that’s where the conversation is heading.”

“……”

Is she implicitly calling me out? Me, who tried to dodge the topic by saying we live nearby? No, that’s wrong. She doesn’t talk that way.

“What, Shizuru? Do you think it’s lame for me to be your older sister?”

“No, that’s not what…”

Rather, it was the opposite. Takinami-senpai told me to have confidence in myself. However, only a few would think that not just anyone can do the job of a library committee. For many students who don’t think so, Makabe Shizuru is not cut out to be Hasumi Shion’s sibling. Nevertheless, if she reveals that I’m her younger brother, Hasumi-senpai’s image will worsen.

What’s more, people were beginning to think that I’m a stalker.

“I’m sorry if it looks like I’m making you lie, Hasumi-senpai. But please keep the fact that I’m your brother a secret as much as possible. Thank you very much for the coffee…”

“Eh? Ah, wait, Shizuru!”

I one-sidedly ended the conversation and closed the door without hearing Hasumi’s reply.

That’s it for Hasumi-senpai. Takinami-senpai has already been decided. ―Then, all that’s left is Naoi.

First, let’s apologize to him.

Naoi has replaced his own jealousy with anger at being lied to by me. There is no doubt about that. But it’s also true that I lied to him. First of all, I will apologize for that.

I had calculated that by doing so, I might be able to cut through his stubborn attitude, but more than that, I wanted to put my own mistakes to rest.

Act 2

The next day.

When I got on the train to go to school, I found Takinami Ruika there.

She’s standing with one hand holding onto a strap above and her other hand holding a textbook. It must be a reference book since it’s a different size from school textbooks.

It looks like she’s completely focused on reading, so she hasn’t noticed me. Let’s just get away from here while she’s still not looking. Just when I was thinking that, she looked up as if something was calling to her. Naturally, she recognizes me.

“Oh, good morning, Makabe-kun.”

“Good morning.”

I missed the timing to leave.

“If you were there, you should have called out to me.”

“I didn’t want to disturb you.”

“Geez, this is embarrassing…”

Takinami-senpai murmurs.

We faced each other near the door.

“Do you always enter through this door?”

“No, I try to ride any openings, so it’s not fixed.”

Apparently, some people keep things fixed, not only time for waking up and walking to the station, but even a specific door to go through. My classmate, Heshikiri Sakura, has the same idea. It seems like she’s anxious when doing anything different.

“Is that so? Since you told me before that this is your usual time. I tried to delay my departure a little… I guess it’s fate, huh?”

“If you say it like that, a guy would misunderstand you. It’s just a coincidence.”

Although, in this case, she’s deliberately saying it like that, knowing I wouldn’t misunderstand her.

And then, the train shook.

“Ah.”

Unless she was standing in a very unstable position, she would have been able to stand on her feet. Nevertheless, Takinami-senpai lost her balance and with a small scream, she fell onto my chest.

“?!”

I suddenly felt a very soft sensation. Not to put it strangely, but it’s as if Takinami Ruika was made of something soft. And her elegant scent tickled my nose, which I could smell due to the distance between us.

“…Are you okay?”

“I-I’m sorry. What am I doing…”

How blatant.

Moreover, instead of moving away, she leaned even further.

“Takinami-senpai.”

“Come on. Last time, you were getting hugged by Takajou-san.”

When I whispered her name to warn her, she looked up and spoke in close range.

I let out a small sigh.

“Makabe-kun?”

As expected, she couldn’t stay attached to me forever, so Takinami-senpai let go of my body. Then, perhaps sensing something suspicious about my behavior, she tilted her head.

I fell silent.

The train arrived at the Kobe Sports Park station and the doors behind opened.

The number of passengers at this station is small. No one got on or off at this door either.

An announcement is made that the door will soon be closed.

Then, just before the doors closed―I got off the train.

“Shi…”

Takinami-senpai’s voice.

I looked back and saw her face behind the closed door, her eyes were wide open. The train started to move, and soon I couldn’t see it.

“That should do it.”

I deliberately voice out my words.

There are many trains in the morning. The shortest interval is about three minutes and the longest is six minutes. As usual, I’m not running late, so if I catch the next train, I will be on time for school. I might be able to make up for the delay just by walking faster than usual.

The problem was Takinami-senpai waiting at the station.

Naturally, the probability that she would want to hear the reason why I did such a thing is high. The thought of it made me dejected and hate myself.

Suddenly, my phone got a notification. It was a message from a chat app.

I opened it up.

『I’m sorry. I took my joke too far.』

While I was reading and staring at that careful text, two more messages arrived.

『I’ll go on ahead.』

『I will apologize again this afternoon.』

She felt bad about it. She must have thought that I got angry because of her joke. However, I was simply imposing my ideals onto her. And I will continue doing so.

Because of the guilt I felt, I couldn’t reply.

§§§

After school.

After class, I looked around the classroom as I began putting away my stuff.

Naoi’s group has already gathered. But they will now separate from there.

First, Naoi and his self-proclaimed partner, Murodo, are off to their club. The Handball club. There is another core member, Tatsuno, but he’s not a member of a club, so he will join up with a friend who was in the same class as him in the first year after this. And Izumikawa will be leaving school alone.

Naoi’s group is united when the leader is present, but the ties of the others are thin.

They exchanged some words and then dispersed. I followed Izumikawa out of the classroom.

“Izumikawa.”

When I called out to him when he was alone, he jumped and turned around.

“Wh-What is it? You want something from me…?”

Why is he so nervous? Is it because his friends aren’t around, or is it because there’s something he knows?

“I need to ask you something.”

“……..”

Izumikawa was silent, perhaps wary of my appearance when I suddenly talked to him.

“It was you who spread that rumor, right?”

“H-Huh?! W-What proof do you have to say that?”

Izumikawa probably knew that I was going to ask him about this topic. Even though he was prepared for it, he panicked because I hit the bull’s eye. In a sense, this reaction could be said to be proof.

At his voice, the gaze of other students suddenly gathered around him, he backed down with an annoyed look on his face.

“Izumikawa, you’re mistaken.”

“Mistaken?”

“That’s right. I’m not a cop. Even if I don’t have any proof, I will still insinuate that it’s you. You’ve been spreading rumors with no proof, so we’re even, right? All I have to do is ask Naoi to do something about you, Izumikawa.”

“S-Stop it!”

Izumikawa screams.

There’s no need to tell a teacher since we’re high schoolers. If that’s the case, the only thing left to do is to tell the leader of the group to which Izumikawa belongs, namely Naoi. He is a man of integrity, so he would not leave anything unjust and would confirm the facts with Izumikawa. And Izumikawa is more afraid of Naoi’s disapproval than receiving guidance from a teacher.

“I-If it’s proof, I have it. I saw it. I saw you and Takinami-san at Harbor Land last Sunday.”

“……”

It seems like he didn’t start a rumor out of nothing. Izumikawa saw us. On Sunday, he saw me and Takinami-senpai meeting outside. Hasumi-senpai was there that day, but she had already left.

In fact, that alone does not mean that I’m stalking Takinami-senpai, but Izumikawa dared to bend the facts there deliberately.

“What’s with you? You both get along with Hasumi-san and Takinami-san. I feel sorry for Kyouhei-kun. After all, Kyouhei-kun is…”

Perhaps afraid to speak out, Izumikawa was vague about the last part.

“So that’s how it is…”

I was mumbling to myself.

Izumikawa stuck to Naoi not because he just wanted to have a fun high school life. He also purely admired Naoi. That’s why he noticed his faint infatuation with Hasumi-senpai and sensed the recent change in him.

As Karube had deduced, Izumikawa was afraid that Naoi’s liking for me might cause a relative decline in his own reputation, and that he might be dropped from the group. But more than that, he was thinking about Naoi and trying to get rid of me. That’s why he spread bad rumors about me.

I may have slightly misjudged Izumikawa Satoshi a little.

“What?”

“Nothing really. I just thought that you really care for your friends.”

“H-Huh?!”

He cares for his friends. That’s why I have to say this.

“But Izumikawa, Naoi is a man of integrity. He won’t be pleased about what you did.”

Immediately after that, Izumikawa stopped dead in his tracks.

I stopped as well and faced him.

“S-Shut up. Besides, if only you weren’t around―”

“I know. That’s why, leave the rest to me. I’ll take care of Naoi.”

Like Izumikawa, I too want to do something about Naoi. The problem is that I have yet to come up with a concrete plan.

I found myself near the stairs. Izumikawa will go down from here to go home. I didn’t go downstairs, but went to the library.

“Later, Izumikawa. See you tomorrow.”

“H-Hey…”

I tapped Izumikawa twice on the shoulder and left him.

At the moment, only the members of his group are aware that something is wrong with Naoi. However, Naoi is in the limelight. If this continues, sooner or later, it is bound to spread throughout the class.

I would like to do something before that happens.

◆◆◆

At the library, after school.

Takinami Ruika showed up a little earlier than usual.

“Good afternoon, Makabe-kun.”

“Good afternoon.”

We greeted each other as usual.

However, our conversation didn’t continue from there. Takinami-senpai was standing in front of the counter, looking like she wanted to say something, but couldn’t seem to proceed.

“Um, about this morning―”

Eventually, she made up her mind and opened her mouth.

“I took it too far. I’m sorry I got carried away. I got on that train since I thought I would be able to meet you, Makabe-kun.”

She sincerely apologizes.

“No, I’m sorry too. I acted on impulse. I’m very sorry.”

“No. Besides, it’s not your fault that Takajou-san hugged you.”

“Well, that person likes to fool around.”

I chuckled.

“But I don’t think it’s right for someone like you to get carried away when meeting me.”

“I suppose.”

Takinami-senpai then moved her face a little closer. She continued whispering as if she was telling a secret.

“I’ll be careful when people are around.”

“Do it even if no one is watching. It’s surprising how much people notice when you’re slacking off while no one is looking. My late mother used to say that.”

I responded to her mischievous reply. I tried to keep my tone as light as possible so as not to make the atmosphere heavy since I was bringing up my mother who passed away.

“I think it’s a nice saying.”

Takinami-senpai went back to her original position. She had a faintly dubious expression on her face.

“Speaking of which, we’re going to the movies tomorrow.”

And then she changed the subject.

“Come to think of it, there was something like that. We’re only going to watch one movie so it’s a relief.”

“We’ll be dismissed before lunchtime So if you’d like, Makabe-kun, we can have lunch together.”

“Thank you for the invitation, but I’ll refrain. I don’t want to make enemies with every guy in the school.”

I softly declined.

This isn’t me playing hard to get, I’m more or less serious. As Takinami-senpai had said, tomorrow we will watch one movie and seperate by noon. Many students will probably have lunch and play with their good friends. I don’t know where the students of Akanedai High School would be, or rather, they would be everywhere. It would be too scary to have lunch with Takinami Ruika in such a situation.

“Alright. But there’s still time, let me know if you change your mind.”

Takinami-senpai gave a gentle, mature smile.

Soon after, the bell rings. The remaining students began to put away their belongings with a look that said, “Is it that time already?”

Then, right after all the students except Kanata-senpai left the room,

“Say, Shizuru? If Sannomiya is not good, I know a place not too far, how about it?”

As usual, she changed her tone.

“No matter how many times you ask, my answer will be the same. Look, Takinami-senpai, please go home. I’m going to close the library soon.”

“Wait, Shizuru.”

She didn’t hesitate to call me out again.

“I’ve been wondering for a while. What’s with that way of speaking?”

She asked me with a stern look.

“What do you mean, isn’t this how you treat someone older than you?”

“Yes, that’s right. That’s the correct way. But you should treat me differently. You should be blunt and curt, just as you always have, Shizuru.”

Judging from an outside perspective, that sounds like a bad junior.

“I’m trying to change that attitude.”

“Stop. Don’t give me that conventional smile. Don’t talk to me like a polite underclassman.”

Takinami-senpai and I faced each other.

She glared at me and I met her gaze.

“Say, Shizuru? What are you angry about? I already apologized about what happened this morning, didn’t I? Or are you still mad at me?”

“I’m not angry.”

I was never angry in the first place.

“Takinami-senpai, it’s almost time to close the room. Please get ready to leave the room.”

“?!”

When I quietly told her as a member of the library committee, she lightly choked.

Takinami-senpai glared at me once again, picked up her school bag from beside her feet, and left the library. Her back figure looks very firm and resolute.

It makes my heart ache a little.

It’s probably the same guilt as this morning. It’s all right. It will eventually numb me out. Or maybe Takinami-senpai will give up on me first.

“That was an interesting scene.”

As I stared at the doorway where Takinami-senpai had disappeared, I heard a voice behind me. I turned around and saw Kanata-senpai standing with her back resting on the table.

“I don’t think Takinami would give up with just that.”

“…”

Kanata-senpai is probably right. But she needs to, otherwise, it’ll be a problem.

“So you’re not going to ask why.”

“I know what’s on your mind.”

She easily told me that. Well, I think I’m being hasty if I say so myself.

“Are you okay with that?”

Kanata-senpai continues to ask.

“It’s not about me being a good or bad choice. This is the best option. We need to treat all factors equally otherwise we won’t get the right answer.”

“Really? If you ask me, you seem to be turning away from the most important factor. Isn’t that exactly why you should treat all factors equally?”

“What’s that important factor?”

“I’m surprised. Are you that stupid to ask me that?”

“……”

I involuntarily fell silent.

“Besides, you’ve got it all wrong.”

Oddly enough, those were the exact words I had said to Izumikawa during lunch break.

“Why should you give the right answer?”

“Huh?”

A dumb sound escaped from my mouth.

A single phrase is all it takes to kick me down.

“You tried to distance yourself one-sidedly and realized that it was a mistake, now you want to start over? How self-centered, don’t you think so?”

“…I do.”

Of course I think so. No man could be more selfish.

“Then you know the answer, don’t you? Don’t upset my feelings any more. Don’t upset me any more.”

Takinami-senpai coldly said, then turned on her feet and walked back inside the library.

I was left alone in the hallway.

No wonder she was angry. It was too selfish of me to ask her to pretend it had never happened because I apologized. Takinami-senpai must have made her mind long ago, and she came here today to tell me that.

It looks like I was too late.

Even though Takinami-senpai was inside, it would be foolish to return to the same place. I felt like just going home, but I had a role as a library committee member and a responsibility. I had to fulfill my daily tasks.

I returned to the library and took a seat at the counter. And just then, as if waiting for that moment, Kanata-senpai arrived.

"Shizuru, the magazine I'm looking for is not here."

She brought up the matter suddenly. I didn't have time to think about unnecessary things, so I was somewhat relieved.

"Just to be sure, let me search here as well. What magazine is it?"

"It's 'Sadō' (茶道, tea ceremony)."

I typed as instructed and performed the search. If I narrowed down the results to magazines, there was only one that included that string of characters. It must be this one.

I displayed the bibliographic information and checked the details. Even though they had stopped accepting new issues before I entered the school, they still had the back issues. The placement should be on the magazine's back issue shelf, and there were no changes to the magazine title. Confirming each detail one by one, I finally understood after double-checking the title.

"Kanata-senpai, where did you look?"

"On the back issue shelf, under the 'Sa' section."

"If you look closely, the title is pronounced as 'Chadō' (ちゃどう). I think you'll find it if you search under the 'Ta' section."

I had a feeling I had heard about it on TV. The head of the tea ceremony pronounced it as 'Chadō.' It seemed that this specialized magazine for tea ceremony was also pronounced as 'Chadō.'

"I'll give it a try."

Without even saying thanks, she headed towards the bookshelf where the back issues were stored. It was unusual for Kanata-senpai, who specialized in history, to overlook something like this. Perhaps when I mentioned it, she suddenly remembered.

Before long, Kanata-senpai returned. In her hand was a single magazine. She came all the way to show it to me.

"I found it."

"That's great."

Feeling a small joy in having been helpful, I nodded.

"It seems Takinami is not here."

"Huh?"

Prompted by Kanata-senpai's words, I looked towards the reading area, and indeed, Takinami-senpai was not there. The texts were left as they were, so maybe he was searching for a book that could be helpful somewhere, or perhaps he had stepped out of the library for some reason.

"You went out with Takinami earlier. What did you talk about?"

Looking intently at the empty seat, Kanata-senpai asked me.

"Nothing much. It wasn't an important conversation."

"I can guess the general idea."

Probably. If so, why did she ask?

"'Insanity is doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results.'"

Kanata-senpai suddenly uttered those words.

"Wasn't that Einstein?"

"People tend to think so, but it has been spreading that he didn't actually say it."

So it seems it wasn't Einstein.

"And what's the meaning behind it?"

"Even if you repeat useless things countless times, it remains useless. It's better to realize it sooner."

Kanata-senpai said it as if praising me.

"In my interpretation, it means if you can't achieve the desired results by repeating an experiment, it's due to the lack of reproducibility. It suggests considering a different approach. However, that's assuming it's the quote from physicist Einstein."

"In physics, there is only one conclusion that can be derived from a single event. No matter how many times you repeat an experiment with incorrect assumptions, you cannot obtain the desired result.

"I don't know who said those words, but they sound like a denial of the idea of continuing to challenge without giving up."

"And then?"

Souta-senpai asks.

"What do you want, from me?"

It was clear from the beginning, without even asking such a question.

"I'm sorry, Souta-senpai. Can you watch the counter for me? I'll step away for a bit."

Saying that, I leave the counter.

I never had any intention of giving up. If that's the case, it's better to act quickly.

"You've got quite the courage."

"I don't feel comfortable leaving it unattended multiple times. Just sitting here is enough. Besides, no one will come anyway."

To begin with, there are few users today. And if they realize that Souta-senpai is at the counter, they will surely turn back.

"By the way, please take responsibility for instigating this."

I leave the counter to Souta-senpai and head towards the back of the bookshelf.

I enter the second-to-last row from the very back. It's the section for English literature, and there's even the original version of 'Harry Potter'.

There, I open my mouth calmly.

"There's something I want to ask you."

"Huh!?"

There is a faint sign of someone holding their breath from the backmost row behind the bookshelf.

That's right. Takanami Reika is on the other side.

She said before that the farthest back of the bookshelf is the best place for thinking and being alone. That's why she came here.

"There is something fatally missing in me."

"Missing?"

I hear Takanami-senpai's voice.

We continue the conversation without seeing each other.

"I know how to behave and what expression to show depending on the situation at that time. But because I'm too good at it, I can't help but doubt if my own feelings are artificial."

This is the first time I'm telling Takanami-senpai about this.

"In other words, I can't have confidence in my own feelings."

"Is it like what Shizuru often said, that you're not suited for love?"

"Yeah. Don't you find it funny? Even though I can objectively see the situation, including myself, I have no idea about my own feelings at the center."

I unintentionally self-deride.

"I like Takanami-senpai. There are times when I doubt if my feelings are genuine. But no matter how much I think about it, those feelings are certainly within me, and they haven't disappeared. With these feelings, I want to face Takanami-senpai."

This is my utmost sincerity at the moment.

However, it may not be convincing to Takanami-senpai. After all, it's like saying, "I like you," without knowing for sure if I truly feel that way. There couldn't be a more insincere statement. Moreover, just a little over ten minutes ago, she rejected me. Will it be so easily overturned?

But her response was not an answer.

"I also have it to some extent."

Takanami-senpai speaks quietly.

"I told you, right? That there are times when I feel like I might be 'empty.'"

"Yeah."

She keenly senses what others expect from her and has become the present "Takanami Reika" by meeting those expectations. However, because of that, she feels lacking in agency and "empty."

On the day we first met, Takanami-senpai posed a question to me.

"Do you have a proper sense of 'self'?"

And...

We are similar.

We observe ourselves and our surroundings from a bird's-eye view, and based on that, determine the right way to act. Takanami-senpai takes a long-term perspective, while I take a short-term one. That's the only difference.

That's why Takanami-senpai calls me a "kindred spirit."

"But I have Shizuru. With Shizuru, who has the same eyes and sees the same things, I don't have to meet the expectations of those around me. I don't feel 'empty.' I can believe that I have a proper 'self.'"

That's right. That's why Takanami-senpai is her true self in front of me. While I wonder if it's okay for me, at the same time, I feel a slight sense of superiority about it.

"Hey, Shizuru?"

The voice calling my name didn't come from behind the bookshelf but from the side. I turn towards that direction.

And there stood Takanami Reika.

"Just as I have Shizuru, don't you think Shizuru has me? That's what I believe."

"Huh..."

The words don't smoothly register in my mind, and I ask in return.

"Because when we're alone together, Shizuru, you don't use honorifics and you're not friendly, right? But I think that's the real Shizuru, and there are no lies there."

Indeed, I adopt such an attitude only in front of her.

Takanami-senpai saw through that I adapt my expressions and choose my words according to the situation. I thought that if I continued doing such things in front of her, who had seen through it, it would expose my vulnerable self.

"...Maybe."

Takanami-senpai and I are similar. To borrow her words, we are "kindred spirits." If that's the case, just as Takanami-senpai's "self" is by my side, perhaps my "self" that doesn't doubt my own emotions is also by Takanami-senpai's side.

"That's why, I think you should find out for yourself. Just keep being together as usual."

Takanami-senpai suggests with a somewhat hesitant tone.

"Well, maybe that's for the best."

"R-Really...?"

For some reason, her eyes blinked.

"What's wrong? You were the one who brought it up."

"W-Well, just a moment ago, I... about Shizuru..."

"Yeah."

Finally, I understood what Takanami-senpai was concerned about. She had once rejected me, yet she was trying to act as if it never happened. She was conflicted about it herself.

"It's fine. I'm doing the same thing."

After all, I was the one who initially tried to do something so unrealistic. If she says that, it's like a dream come true.

"I'm glad. Things can go back to how they were."

Takanami-senpai smiled like a flower.

Somehow, her smile was dazzling, and I averted my gaze. Perhaps I wanted to escape from this situation.

"I'll go back first."

"Wait, Shizuru."

Takanami-senpai called out to me as I tried to pass by her and return to the counter.

"Um, Shizuru..."

For some reason, her pronunciation suddenly became unclear.

"Do you really... like me?"

...

In response to her hesitant question, blushing, I unintentionally fell silent.

I did say it. Maybe I let my guard down because I wasn't looking at her face while speaking. That's why I wanted to quickly leave in order to avoid being touched by her.

I boasted that I have feelings for her. However, it's embarrassing to have it confirmed by someone else. It's even more so when it's from the person herself.

I had asked Kanata-senpai to cover for me. I need to return soon.
