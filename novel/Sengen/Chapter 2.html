Chapter 2 - The Past.

If I return this and say I’m not going, I’m going to get in trouble.

A girl? No way…

How the hell did this happen…

That’s how I ended up going to school for the first time in a week.

“W-What’s the matter, Eri-san? C-Could it be another incident?”

It’s as if there’s a completely different person there from the usual Nanase…

“Kiritani-kun, you forgot your line again.”

I was so engrossed in Nanase’s performance that I hurriedly said the next line when she pointed it out.

Then Nanase and I said our lines in turn.

In the midst of it all, I realized something.

Nanase was somehow very much alive when she was acting.

I felt that her usual dignity had been further enhanced, or rather, I felt that she was in an atmosphere of “Please look at me.”

She was the complete opposite of me, who usually spent my time slacking off and not attending school seriously, having nothing in particular I wanted to do and no dreams, she looked so sparkling to me.

“I should probably pause more for the line I just said, and say the line before that more forcefully.”

After the acting practice was over, Nanase held a script in her hand, writing something in it.

“What are you doing with that?”

“I’m writing down what kind of acting I should do for each line. I have another performance of ‘The Maid’s Amazing Deduction’ this coming weekend.”

The expression on Nanase’s face as she looked at the script while replying was serious.

So this is what it’s like to be someone who’s really trying to achieve their dreams…

When I thought about it, I had a weird feeling around my chest area.

“Kiritani-kun, what’s wrong?”

Nanase looked into my face and asked me as she thought I was acting strange.

At the same time, the distance between her beautiful face and me decreased dramatically.

“No, it’s nothing.”

“Are you sure? I hope so then.”

When I turned my head away and replied, Nanase looked a little worried, but returned her attention to the script.

“Well, my next line is…”

Nanase was writing more and more in the script. She was very quiet, and it was a little strange to see her, who was usually so noisy, become that way.

Nanase was absorbed in her work, not paying attention to me.

At this time, Nanase is definitely heading straight for her dream. I wonder if this is what she was talking about before, that having a dream always allows you to be who you are.

And I kept looking at her like that—

“…How nice.”

I was surprised at the words that escaped my mouth.

Apparently, Nanase hadn’t heard me as she was still concentrating on writing the script.

I wondered if what I had just said had been influenced by the atmosphere of the place. Or was it—

I thought about it for a while, but in the end I couldn’t come to a conclusion at that time, and my lunch break came to an end.

“Hold on! Kiritani-kun!”

I was starting to have a feeling there’s going to be a catfight…

If she really isn’t not that bad of a person… no, I still don’t think so.

Why does Ayase keep pestering Nanase like this? Isn’t she a bit too persistent to be just an antagonist? …Maybe I’m thinking too much.

Which one of us won’t give up? You’re trying to force me to come watch you practice.
