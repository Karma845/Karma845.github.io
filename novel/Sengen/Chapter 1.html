Chapter 1 - The Encounter.

Mid-April. The cherry blossom season was coming to an end.

It has only been a week since the new school year started.

The first-year students who have just entered the school are gradually getting used to high school life, and the second and third-year students who have advanced and changed classes are probably deciding on their position in the class and what group they belong to.

On the other hand, I—Kiritani Kakeru, would be a third-year in high school this year.

It was my last year in high school, so I should normally be busy with a lot of things… But in reality, I haven’t been to school once this semester.

So what do I do when I don’t go to school—

“Alright, one more kill and I’ll get my wish of 20 kills.”

It was past seven in the morning and I was playing a shooting battle royale game that is currently very popular in Japan.

I started playing around spring break, and for the past week I’ve been holed up in my room . That was why I haven’t been able to go to school.

“Hey, Onii-chan! Last night you were playing games until midnight… eh, and now you’re playing games again!?”

The one who suddenly opened the door and walked in was my sister—Kiritani Momoka.

Unlike me, who has a very average face, Momoka, who was now in the second year of junior high school, was cute and popular at school. It’s a good thing she doesn’t look like her big brother.

“Good morning, Momoka. Your brother is a little busy right now, so can we talk later?”

“What are you so busy with? You’re just playing a game.”

“There are busy times in games too. If you don’t usually play games, you wouldn’t understand.”

I was now at a very critical juncture.

I’ve been playing this battle royale game for about a month now.

I’m just one step away from my goal of 20 kills.

So I’m sorry Momoka, but now is not the time to be listening to my sister—

“Hehhh.”

The TV screen went dark with a click sound.

…Huh? What the heck just happened?

“Goodness. You were playing games until midnight last night, and you’re still playing games in the morning, you idiot.”

Momoka was holding the plug of a gaming console in her hand as she spoke.

“Momoka-chan! Your brother told you that he was busy playing games, right!?”

“So now you’re not busy.”

“No, that’s true, but…”

Ahh, my 20 kills…

“Anyway, Onii-chan, you should go to school today.”

“Don’t worry, imouto-chan. Your brother doesn’t have to go to school yet, so it’s fine.”

When I answered with a smile, Momoka looked at me with a frown.

“…Onii-chan, are you planning to only go to classes until the very end of your academic year again?”

“Of course I intend to.”

Ever since I entered high school, I’ve only taken enough classes to make sure I pass each year.

There are many reasons for this, but the short answer was that it was too much trouble to go to school.

There was a general opinion that it was better to go to school, but I don’t think so at all.

In the first place, going to school and taking classes will almost never be useful in the future.

Do you think you will ever use calculus or ancient texts in society?

To be honest, I don’t think most people would use these unless they were a scholar.

In other words, 90% of the time you spend in school is wasted.

Well, I understand that if you are in a club and you go to school because you enjoy the club activities.

But unfortunately, I’m not in any club, and if anything, I don’t have many friends, so I don’t even go to school to talk to them.

What was the point of me going to school? In fact, I could even say that not going to school was the right thing to do.

That was why I only go to school as much as I need to, and on the days I don’t go to school, I spend my time playing games, reading manga, and being lazy, like now.

“You know what, Onii-chan? This is your last year in high school. Why don’t you go to school properly for the last time?”

“What are you talking about? Because it’s my last, I’m going to work hard enough to stay in school this year. Have you ever heard of the term ‘original commitment’?”

Momoka sighed in exasperation as she said this.

“…Haa. Why is my Onii-chan so uncool?”

“I got our father’s face, so you can tell our dad.”

“No one’s talking about your face. Yes, your face is unattractive, but…”

“You’re my sister and yet you’re relentless.”

Hey, Dad, it seems that we are both uncool.

“Anyway, I think you should go to school today.”

“Good grief, I told you it’s okay if I don’t go yet.”

“If you don’t, mom said she’d throw away all of Onii-chan’s games and mangas.”

“…Seriously?”

When I asked her fearfully, Momoka nodded her head.

“Well, you don’t have to go to school if you want to have all of your games and comics thrown out. I’ve already told you that.”

After saying that, Momoka walked out of my room.

I looked around my room. Dozens of video games and dozens of manga.

If I don’t go to school today, this will all be thrown away…

“…Then, l should get ready.”

Then I put my arms through my uniform for the first time in about a month.

It was the seventh day of the new school year.

I decided to go to school for the first time.

　　◇◇◇

“Mom, you were really going to throw away my games and comics.”

When I went to the living room to get ready for school, I found that my mother, who usually worked as an office worker at some company, was not working today and had prepared a lot of garbage bags.

She thought I wouldn’t go to school anyway, so she stood by beforehand.

Mom, please have a little faith in your own son…

“Is the cherry blossom already in bloom…”

The high school I go to,Seiran High School, had many cherry trees planted all along the road leading to the school gate.

However, all the cherry blossoms had already fallen. If I had come to school on time, I might have been able to see the cherry blossoms in full bloom.

…But I personally don’t feel like going to school for that reason, so I don’t feel the slightest regret. It was more fun to play games and read manga at home than to watch the cherry blossoms.

“Yo! Kakeru-chan!”

Someone tapped me on the shoulder from behind.

When I turned around, there was one handsome, fresh-looking boy there.

He had an atmosphere that resembled somethingresembled something out of a shoujo manga.

“What is it, Shuuichi?”

“What’s with that reaction?”

A handsome boy laughed amusingly at me as I looked at him boringly.

His name is Amahisa Shuuichi.

He is in the same grade as me and is also from the same junior high school.

And he is the only friend I have.

“It’s your first day at school this year. Last year you didn’t come to school for two weeks after the second year started, but this time you’re early.”

“…Well, yeah. If I don’t go to school today, my mom’s going to throw away all my games and manga.”

“Seriously? That’s super funny!”

“…It’s not at all funny.”

I calmly replied, but Shuuichi was still laughing. Is this guy making fun of me?

“Well, whatever. I’m glad you’re here at school.”

“That sounds so fake.”

“No, no. Seriously, I’m happy for you.”

Shuuichi smiled and put his hand on my shoulders.

“It’s too hot. Stop it.”

“Come on, don’t be shy.”

“And I’m not shy.”

I removed Shuuichi’s arm from my shoulder and started to walk away.

And soon Shuuichi came alongside me.

“Unfortunately, Kakeru and I aren’t in the same class this year.”

I muttered to myself.

“…I see.”

“Don’t get so lonely.”

“Huh? I’m not lonely at all.”

Shuuichi was smirking even as he said that. This guy is just so annoying.

“Instead, I’d say, there’s that particular girl in your class.”

“That girl? Who’s that girl?”

“You want to know more?”

“No, I’m not that interested.”

“If you want to hear it so badly, Shuuichi-kun here will give you a special lesson.”

“Shuuichi doesn’t have any ears?”

Even though I returned his words in a dumbfounded manner, he didn’t care and continued talking.

I knew he didn’t have ears.

“That girl who’s in your class…”

Shuuichi said in a smug tone, but continued.

“It’s Nanase Rena.”

At the moment I heard that name, I was sure I looked a little annoyed.

Because Nanase Rena was known as the most problematic girl in school. Even I, who only goes to school moderately, know about her.

We’ve never been in the same class, so I only have a vague idea of what she looked like, and I’ve never talked to her in person, so I don’t really know what she’s like.

…But the rumors about her that are frequently circulated are pretty outrageous.

During my freshman year, there were many stories, such as the mysterious guerrilla event called Rena Festival that started without permission, or the campfire that was held on the school grounds at night after the sports festival without permission by gathering all the students in the school.

“You’re lucky, Kakeru, to be in the same class as the most famous person in the school.”

“How? This is not good at all.”

“I don’t know. Maybe you’ll start to enjoy coming to school and you’ll want to go to school every day.”

“Absolutely no way.”

In fact, if I had such a problematic person in my class, I might not want to go to school any more than I do now.

“As a friend from junior high school, I’m hoping you’ll start coming to school properly soon.”

“You’re bringing that up again after all. I told you I don’t like to discuss it.”

I said to Shuuichi, who looked worried.

In the first place, I went to school as little as I could during my freshman and sophomore years, and now I feel like I can’t take school seriously anymore.

“Kakeru, you were going to school normally in junior high school. Why did you become such a mess the moment you entered high school?”

Suddenly Shuuichi threw a question at me.

“I’ve been telling you. I’m just tired of going to school.”

“You always give the same answer, but that’s an absolute lie.”

“You know, Shuuichi, even if you don’t go to school, you can get a high school diploma if you pass the high school equivalency exam, and then you can take the university entrance exam. There are many places where you can get a job even if you are a high school graduate… The adults in the world say that if you don’t go to school, it’s the end of your life, but that’s not true at all.”

“That might be true, but…”

When I gave a long explanation, Shuuichi gave a wry smile.

I’ve said a lot of things, but I know that I should go to school. Some mentalists told me that if I don’t go to school, I won’t be able to learn communication skills.

Well, I still wouldn’t go to school every day, but…

Because it was exhausting in so many ways. Only people who experienced what I have would know what I mean.

“Kakeru, even so, I’m still worried about you.”

“I appreciate the sentiment, but it’s none of your business. I mean, we’re going to be late for school if you keep talking about things that don’t matter.”

“…Heh heh, I get it.”

Shuuichi muttered as if he had given up.

Then he suddenly started talking about other topics, like how a new girl had told him off, or how the principal’s wig had almost come off at the opening ceremony.

It was very helpful that he could sense my mood and stay out of it like this.

To put it mildly, he was too good a friend for me.

I would never tell him this, because I think he would get carried away if I told him.

Then, we walked along the path of the fallen cherry trees, exchanging idle conversation.

I changed my shoes at the gate and headed to my class room alone.

Incidentally, Shuuichi happened to meet his girlfriend, whom he has been dating since their freshman year, in front of the school gate, and they went to their class together first. It seemed Shuuichi and her were in the same class.

Shuuichi asked me to go with him to the halfway point even though we were in different classes, but I refused.

If I were there, I’d obviously be in the way.

“Nanase has arrived!”

Suddenly, I heard such a voice from a male student.

In addition, I heard a name that I just heard from Shuuichi a moment ago.

I turned around, curious, and there was a girl wearing a white parka over her uniform blouse. Her skin was white, and her hair was brownish and long enough to reach her shoulders.

She had a pretty face, but she was also cute, and frankly, quite a beautiful girl.

“Rena-chan good morning~!”

“I heard you were called into the staff room yesterday!”

“As expected of Nanase!”

“Rena-chan, you look so pretty today~!”

“You better do something today!”

A beautiful parka girl was approached one by one by male and female students passing by in the hallway.

“Good morning, everyone! Today I’m going to enjoy school life to the fullest, so I’ll be looking forward to working with you all~!”

She smiled affectionately and waved to each of the students.

The parka girl did not have the air of being difficult to approach that was often associated with beautiful girls, but rather wore an atmosphere of friendliness.

I see… That parka beautiful girl is Nanase Rena.

I feel like I’m seeing her clearly for the first time. I never thought she was such a beautiful girl…

By the way, the parka she was wearing over her blouse was a complete violation of school rules.

But out of all the students in the school, she was the only one who was allowed to wear a parka.

…Of course, when Nanase first came to school wearing a parka, the teachers warned her and confiscated it, but she still wore the same parka all day, every day, and the teachers all gave up on her.

It was a tremendous obsession, wasn’t it? She was obsessed with wearing a parka.

In addition, Nanase Rena has an enthusiastic fan base.

I’m talking about the kind of people who are talking to her right now.

Nanase has had a number of problematic behaviors since her freshman year.

However, it seemed that this had caught the fancy of some students and had created an ardent Nanase fan base.

This was what Shuuichi told me earlier, that she already has a few fans among the new students.

What kind of thing would achieve that in just one week…

“That Nanase is still on a roll, isn’t she?”

“Yeah, crazy.”

“They all are treating her like an idol.”

“I can’t believe she’s treated like an idol when all she does is cause trouble.”

On the other hand, a group of boys and girls were talking badly about Nanase on the side.

If there were enthusiastic fans, it was not surprising that there would also be strong opposition.

Nanase was a student who stood out, for better or worse.

“In my last year of high school, of all times, why am I in the same class as Nanase…”

I muttered to myself with a sigh. I hope nothing troublesome happens…

Then I turned my back on Nanase, who was talking to her fans, and went to the classroom first.

I arrived at the classroom and opened the door to find my classmates chatting in their groups.

It has already been a week since we started our new class, so most of them have probably found a place that suits them.

On the other hand, a guy who came to school for the first time a week late doesn’t have that kind of place at all.

After checking my seat on the seating chart posted on the bulletin board, I move so as not to stand out too much. If I stand out too much, people think, “has he not come to school for a week?” And it would make me feel awkward.

“…Oh goodness.”

I was moving along smoothly, but when I got close to my seat, I despaired.

A boy I didn’t know was sitting in my seat.

He was also chatting happily with the boys behind him, who were probably his friends.

Well, what should I do? It seemed like it would be solved by me saying to the boy sitting in my seat, “That’s my seat,” but it was not that easy in school.

The boys had sporty hairstyles and looked very athletic, and if I insisted on claiming my seat, they might react negatively. That would be very troublesome.

…So, should I go out into the hallway or use the restroom to kill time here and wait for the boys to leave my seat…?

“Sorry, I’m gonna go to the bathroom.”

Then the boy in my seat got up. This was my chance. I had to sit down quickly while I could. So I quickly sat down in my seat before the boy came back.

The boys’ friends were a little surprised, but they didn’t say anything.

The boy who had been sitting in my seat also came back and looked at me with blinking eyes.

However, he now sat down on a nearby empty seat and resumed chatting with his friends.

No, don’t just sit in someone else’s seat and chat. It’s just plain annoying.

I’ve been thinking about it, but I can’t seem to say it out loud…

…Haaa, why do I have to be so careful just to sit in my seat?

With that in mind, I looked around.

I needed to see what kind of students were in my class.

“I can’t do it. I have club activities.”

“Why not? Why not just go to karaoke? You need to take a break once in a while.”

“The tournament is coming up, so I can’t take a day off.”

“I have a song that I really want to sing.”

“Go with the others.”

At the back of the classroom, there was a group of five boys and girls, and the handsome guy was clearly the leader of the group and a beautiful girl was having a conversation with him.

I know them both well. We were in the same class last year.

The handsome man’s name is Akutsu Atsushi.

He was not as brisk as Shuuichi, but a bit more ornate and handsome. He was a member of the basketball club, and has been a regular member since his freshman year, and was now the captain and ace of the team.

That was why I heard that he was often confessed to by girls. I heard that his behavior was not so good, but he somehow was popular with the girls. But I didn’t understand why.

And the beautiful girl’s name is Ayase Saki.

Long black hair and piercing eyes. She looked more like a beautiful woman than a pretty one, and had a slender figure.

However, she has a queen-like atmosphere or a thorny air that is often associated with female leaders, and Ayase was a beautiful girl who was the complete opposite of a particular someone I met this morning.

…I know them, but I’ve never spoken to them or even greeted them properly, so they probably think I’m just a gloomy guy who comes to school once in a while.

If they’re not careful, they might even forget that they were in the same class as me.

“Then I’ll go to karaoke with Saki-chan!”

“Then maybe I’ll go too~!”

Two of the Ayase group’s — Suzuki Tatsuya and Suzuka Takabashi started talking. I was in the same class with them last year as well.

“Tatsuya has club activities, right? Don’t slack off!”

“Atsushi is really serious about his club activities, isn’t he? What about karaoke, Mei?”

“Eh, I-I…”

The girl, called Mei by Ayase, was the last of the group who had never said a word before. Her last name was Tachibana.

“Of course you’re going, right?”

“U-Umm… yeah.”

Tachibana gave a small nod. …She was the one who really didn’t want to go. But Ayase’s words pressured her into nodding her head.

I’ve been in the same class with Tachibana since second year, but she was the weakest link in the Ayase group, so I saw things like this all the time.

But no one can stand against Ayase and Akutsu, so everyone pretends to ignore them. Including me, of course.

In terms of school caste, the Ayase group probably was at the highest.

If I anger Akutsu and Ayase, I’ll be in big trouble, and I have to be careful not to get too close…

“Good morning, everyone!”

Suddenly, a cheerful voice echoed through the classroom.

I looked over and saw Nanase, the beautiful parka girl, at the door.

This was the first time I’ve seen someone greet the whole class like this, other than a character from an anime or manga.

“You’re late, Nanase!”

“Hehe, I actually overslept a bit today…”

“Rena-chan After school, let’s go eat crepes at that new place in front of the station!”

“I like crepes! I don’t have any plans today, so that’s totally fine~!”

Apparently, there are some Nanase fans in this class.

…I mean, this parka beauty was a heck of a communicator. It was amazing.

…Really? Nanase’s seat is next to mine? I don’t like the idea of sitting next to a girl who has both fans and enemies in school because I feel like something might happen.

“Good morning!”

“Eh, g-good morning…”

I was surprised. Would one normally talk to someone they have never spoken to before like this? That was beyond casual

She has clearly said “she’s getting carried away”, loud enough for others to hear.

After all, they had been fighting as soon as they saw each other since shortly after entering high school.

“I know. But I’m a person with a big heart. I don’t take anything that some random person says seriously.”

“Who are you to judge me!? Stop looking down on other people!”

“You’re the one who’s looking down on me. Can you please stop trying to come after me every time?”

The moment everyone thought the slap was going to hit Nanase.

Phew, I guess I managed… When I was feeling relieved in my heart, I felt a glance from next to me.

“Ummm… do you need anything?”

“No, nothing~”

“? Why are you lying to me?”

And yet I’m having lunch in an empty classroom in the old Seiran High School building, which is separate from the main building.

Moreover, as it was an old building, there was a breeze coming in from somewhere, making it slightly cold.

Maybe that’s why basically no one comes here.

“…Haaa, it’s still cold here.”

I blurted out as I continued to eat my egg sandwich.

The reason I’m spending my lunch at the old school building is Akutsu.

Since the day of the volunteer event, he hasn’t done anything to me directly, but he sometimes stares at me when I’m in the classroom, which is pretty scary.

Especially during the lunch break, Akutsu was also having lunch with his friends in the classroom, which made things awkward.

That’s why I’ve taken refuge in this gloomy place.

This will be where I’ll spend all my lunch breaks until graduation…

Well, I’m only coming to school for the minimum amount required, so I could put up with this.

“But this classroom. Why are there so many books in this classroom?”

As I looked around the room, I saw a large number of books lined up or stacked on bookshelves and desks. They were of various genres, including novels and magazines.

“Hmm? This is…”

In the midst of the large number of books, one book suddenly caught my attention.

It was a book about acting. Why a book about acting?

Just as I was thinking this, the door suddenly opened.

W-Who…?

I turned around, startled.

“Huh? Kiritani-kun?”

It was Nanase, to my surprise, who walked into the empty classroom. She was carrying her lunch box, and she was wearing her trademark white parka over her blouse.

“Nanase, why are you here…?”

“That’s my line. Why are you here?”

“I’ve been… going through a lot of things.”

If I said something like I was uncomfortable because Akutsu was in the classroom, Nanase, who started it all, might be bothered, so I muddled my words.

“And why is Nanase here?”

“Me? I’m eating lunch here.”

As she said this, Nanase took a seat next to me and untied the knot in the wrapping of her lunch box.

You just sat next to me without a second thought. Well, I don’t mind…

“Do you always eat lunch here?”

“Yeah. I’ve spent most of my lunch breaks here since first year.”

“Since first year… could it be that everything in this room is Nanase’s personal belongings?”

“That’s right~”

When I asked, Nanase replied in a tone like, “What’s the problem?”

“No one uses this place. I keep it to myself.”

“What makes you think that?”

Even though it is an empty classroom, Nanase is probably the only person in the world who would turn a school classroom into her room without permission. It’s just like her, I guess.

“Ah, by the way, you saw me yesterday, right?”

“Eh? What are you talking about all of a sudden?”

“I’m saying you came to see it yesterday, right? The play I’m in.”

For a moment, I wondered how I should respond to the sudden topic.

If it was something Nanase wanted to hide, I would pretend I didn’t know about it, but since she’s been so open about it, I guess I don’t have to worry about it.

“Well, yeah, but… gah, you noticed me?”

“You were in the front row, you know. I noticed you rather quickly.”

“I-I see…”

I also noticed Nanase on the stage at once.

It should not be at all surprising that she noticed me, too.

“Do you like theater, Kiritani-kun?”

“No, I’m not. My sister just asked me to go with her yesterday and I went along. She likes those kinds of things.”

“Kiritani-kun has a sister! I wish I had a brother or a sister too~”

Nanasese looks at me with envious eyes.

Apparently, she doesn’t have any siblings.

As someone who has a younger sister, I don’t think it’s good to have siblings.

My sister wants me to go to school every day…

“So, do our classmates know that Nanase is an actor?”

“I don’t think they know. It’s not that I’m hiding anything, but I’m pretty sure that only very few people in the school know about it.”

“Eh, really?”

“I joined a theater company just before I entered high school, but it’s only recently that I’ve been able to play a real role on stage, like yesterday.”

“Is that so…”

I was thinking that Nanase must be having a hard time, when she suddenly remembered something and shouted, “Ah!”

“Hey, hey! What did you think of my performance?”

Nanase asked with an expectant look in her eyes.

Does this mean she wants a compliment? Well, her performance was amazing…

“U-Umm… I don’t know much about acting, but I think you did a great job.”

“For real!?”

Nanase brings her face close to mine with a goofy smile.

Whoa, that’s too close.

“F-For real.”

“Well~ I’m happy to hear that!”

Nanase was smiling and in a good mood.

She seemed to be very happy with the compliment.

“But to be honest, I was surprised. I had no idea Nanase was an actor.”

“It’s not that big of a deal, though. I’m just being put on a play for a theater company I’m in.”

“I still think it’s amazing. …Nanase wants to be more famous?”

When I asked her, Nanase looked a little thoughtful.

Then she turned to me and stared.

“Actually, my dream is to be a Hollywood actress.”

She told me so with a serious expression.

I was momentarily at a loss for a response to the sudden and unexpected words that came out of her mouth.

“Hollywood actress, that’s a very lofty goal.”

“Is that so? But I read in some book that the bigger your dreams are, the richer your life will be.”

“I don’t have the faintest idea what kind of book that is…”

Well, even if I knew, I wouldn’t read a hundred percent of it.

“There are a lot of books in this room, does this have anything to do with Nanase’s dream?”

I asked her while looking at the books around me, and she nodded her head.

“During my lunch break or after school when I don’t have rehearsals with the theater company, I come to this classroom and practice acting by myself, or read novels to broaden my senses.”

After listening to what Nanase had to say, I picked up one of the books on the desk nearby. It was a romance novel.

When I skimmed the contents, I was surprised.

The novel had pages and pages of detailed discussions about what kind of acting she should do if she were to actually act out the interactions between the characters.

Nanase had written this down, and maybe similar notes were written in all the books in this room. That was enough for me to know how serious she was about her dream.

“You’re serious about being a Hollywood actress.”

“You didn’t think I was joking, did you? That’s horrible, Kiritani-kun.”

Nanase was pouting and started to get angry.

I didn’t think it was a joke, but I didn’t expect her to be this serious about it.

“Hey, Kiritani-kun, do you have any dreams for the future?”

Suddenly, Nanase threw a question at me.

“Why so sudden?”

“I told you about my dream, so I thought it was Kiritani-kun’s turn to talk about it.”

“I didn’t want to know about that in the first place.”

She usually just goes ahead and does what she thinks is best…

“So, Kiritani-kun, what is your dream?”

“Dreams… You don’t have dreams when you’re in high school.”

I also had dreams when I was in kindergarten and elementary school.

…But I don’t have any dreams now that I’m more aware of things.

“Even if I have a dream, more often than not, it won’t come true.”

After I muttered this, I immediately realized my mistake.

“S-Sorry…”

“You don’t have to apologize. What you’re saying is true, Kiritani-kun.”

Nanase says so, but I made an unbelievable statement in front of someone who is seriously trying to make her dream come true. …Haha, I feel like dying right now.

“It’s true that most dreams don’t come true, but you know what, when you have a dream, you can always be who you are.”

“Who you are…?”

“In short, it means you can be yourself!”

Nanase stated firmly.

If you have a dream, you can be yourself, huh.

Is that why she is always able to act as she pleases, without worrying about others or her own position?

“Ah, by the way, Kiritani-kun. I’ve got a favor to ask you.”

“Something you want me to do? I don’t want to.”

“Hey, don’t turn me down before I say what it is.”

Nanase gave me a vigorous tsukkomi.

Because it doesn’t look like she’s going to ask me for something too difficult…

“Then I guess I could at least hear you out… What do you want me to do?”

I asked fearfully.

“Didn’t I tell you earlier that I practice by myself in this empty classroom?”

“Yeah you did.”

“But you know, when I’m by myself, I can practice, but I feel like I’m progressing slowly or it’s hard to do.”

“…Well, theater isn’t something you do alone.”

“That’s why I’d like Kiritani-kun to accompany me in my practice from now on. Specifically, I’d like you to say lines for characters other than the ones I’m playing. Can you do that for me?”

Nanase clasped her hands together and striked a begging pose.

But I’m going to have to refuse.

“No.”

“I’m sorry. I didn’t hear anything. Can you say it again?”

“Um, I said I’m not—”

“Hmm, I didn’t hear you again. One more time—”

“You definitely heard me right!?”

“Ah, did you just say you’d help me?”

“I never said that! Don’t make things up!”

Ah, god. Is she trying to get me to go along with her practice at any cost?

“…can I go back to class?”

“S-Stop! Hold on! I just want to talk to you one more time!”

As I left my seat, she frantically held me back.

“It may seem like I’m joking, but I’m serious about becoming a Hollywood actress.”

“I never thought you’re joking, though…”

I understood how serious Nanase was about her dream by the amount of books in this classroom and the amount of notes in the books I saw earlier.

“So please! Please come with me to my practice!”

“Why does it have to be me? What about your friends?”

“I have a few fans here and there, but not so many friends…”

That’s where the words broke off.

I guess that meant she didn’t have any close friends that she could casually ask for help.

It was understandable. She was a famous troublemaker in the school.

“All right. I’ll help you if you want.”

“Really! Yay!”

Nanase did a guts pose with joy.

To be honest, I could have said no, but I couldn’t easily say no to someone who was so desperate.

“But only on the days I go to school. I only come to school on days when I can get credit.”

“That’s totally fine! …But I see. You’re not skipping school without a plan, are you, Kiritani-kun?”

“Don’t tell me I’m slacking.”

When I argued back, Nanase chuckled at me. She’s so rude.

As I was thinking this, Nanase unexpectedly grabbed my hand.

This caused my heart rate to go through the roof.

“I’m looking forward to working with you! I’m counting on you, Kiritani-kun!”

“Y-Yeah… I don’t know what you’re expecting from me though.”

After I replied, I quickly moved away from her.

I really want her to stop the abrupt body touching. It’s bad for my heart.

This was how I came to accompany Nanase in her acting practice.

To be honest, normally, I would have said no, but this time I thought it wouldn’t be a bad idea to cooperate with Nanase, who was strangely serious about her dream.

But I didn’t think at the time that this would be the start of a major change in my life.
