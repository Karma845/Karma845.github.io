Next, she made eye contact with me and urged me to hold out my hand.

I sighed and held out my hand.

Nanase then squeezed my hand.

Her hand was soft and a little cold.

“Me and Kiritani-kun are going to work hard from now on! Ooh!”

Nanase suddenly said something like that.

No, what? Don’t suddenly do something I haven’t heard of.

“Come on, Kiritani-kun, say ‘ooh’ too!”

“Eh, o-okay.”

Apparently, I had to say “ooh” too.

Since she’s so supportive of my dream, I’ll do it.

“I’m going to work hard! Ooh!”

“O-Oh!”

…What the heck is this? I don’t know what it means.

I was so confused.

“I hope you can become a teacher, Kiritani-kun.”

Nanase gave me a shout of encouragement and laughed at me.

She always gives me courage in the most important places, doesn’t she?

“You too, Nanase, I’m sure you’ll become a Hollywood actress.”

I also gave Nanase a shout out like that.

From the bottom of my heart, I believe she can become a Hollywood actress.

On the other hand, if she can’t become a Hollywood actress, who can?

And from that day on, I started studying hard to get into the University of Education.

In order to fulfill my dream of becoming a teacher.

“Onii-chan!”

One day in the morning. Momoka’s loud voice could be heard from outside the room.

Footsteps that were just as loud came closer and closer, and the door opened with a bang.

“Onii-chan! It’s time to go to school! How long have you been asleep?

“Good morning, Momoka.”

As I was changing into my uniform and making myself presentable in the room, Momoka was standing near the door, stunned.

“Momoka, what’s wrong?”

“What’s wrong is my line! I didn’t expect my brother to wake up so early. It’s been a week in a row.”

“It’s a good thing, isn’t it?”

“That’s true, but it’s definitely not right. What’s going on?”

Momoka’s question made me think for a moment.

“Well, if I had to guess, I’d say I found my dream.”

“Onii-chan’s starting to go insane. He’s broken.”

“I’m not broken. You’re a terrible sister…”

While I was having this conversation with my sister, I finished making myself presentable.

“Okay, Onii-chan is going. Lock up the house, okay?”

“Eh? Are you going to school already?

“Yes, I have to study in the morning.”

Momoka was incredulous when she heard my words.

You’re so rude, my sister.

After I told Nanase about my dream, I started going to school every day and doubled my study time.

I’m also planning to attend a summer course at a cram school during the summer vacation.

To be honest, studying was hard, but it wasn’t so hard when I thought I was working towards my dream.

I wondered if Nanase felt this way when she was practicing her acting.

Come to think of it, she seems to be working hard at the theater too.

The other day she told me she was given the lead role.

Since Nanase is working so hard, I should work just as hard, if not harder.

We both must make our dreams come true.

◆◆◆

I spent many days working hard to make my dream come true.

I went to school early in the morning to study, and after school I went home and began to study right away.

During long vacations, I spent the entire day studying at a cram school.

As I kept repeating this routine, summer passed in a flash, autumn came, autumn also passed quickly, and then winter came.

On the day of the exam, I was a little nervous, but not so much that it affected the exam.

I believed that if I could show what I had accumulated so far, it would definitely lead to a result.

I finished the exam without any problems, and now all I had to do was wait for the announcement of my acceptance.

My parents and Momoka were freaking out until the announcement was made, but I was strangely calm.

It wasn’t that I thought I would definitely be accepted.

But I had worked so hard that I didn’t have any regrets, so I guess somewhere I knew I would be okay.

Then came the day of the University of Education’s acceptance announcement.

The numbers of those who had passed the entrance exam were posted on the bulletin board amidst a large crowd of examinees.

At that time, I was alone and checked the numbers one by one.

When I saw the number in the middle of the board, I took a deep breath.

—I had been accepted.

The moment I found out, I was relieved rather than impressed.

I thought, “Now I’m much closer to my dream.”

After I passed the exam, I looked for a place to live on my own since the university was located in a place that I couldn’t commute to from home, arranged the furniture I would use, and occasionally relaxed at home.

And so the months went by, and the graduation ceremony came for us third-year students.
