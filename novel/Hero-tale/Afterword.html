Afterword

Hello, this is Sakaishi Yuusaku.

Thank you for picking up this book.

This work is about a protagonist who fights using his connections as a weapon.

The protagonist, being powerless, has learnt the greatness of relying on others. Like in the first volume, in the second volume, the protagonist builds new connections and fights using the connections he has built up so far.

The main character, Ned, again connects with many people, but in reality, due to the Corona disaster, he is not able to connect with many people. I felt this a little while writing this second volume.

This summer in particular has been raining than usual, and I’ve spent more boring days than usual in my room. This year I have not been to the sea, mountains, summer festivals or fireworks displays.

Originally, I am not the type of person who participates in events that much, but even if I didn’t, I seemed to realise that summer was coming when events were taking place in my neighbourhood. For example, I would sometimes go for a run when it was dark outside, and on the way there I would see a summer festival being held at a shrine I was passing and realise, [Oh, so it’s summer now], which was a regular occurrence every year.

It felt surprisingly lonely not to see the traditional festival.

On the other hand, however, I could somehow predict that my schedule would be free this summer, so I was able to devote myself to work with all my energy. This gave me a sense of fulfilment, so I think I had a good summer. It is good to have a year like this once in a while.

I hope that next year I will have a summer where I can see people without hesitation and problems.

I would like to write a story about what the main character in this work, Ned, would do if he had a series of days when he couldn’t see people…..I would like to write such a story one day.
