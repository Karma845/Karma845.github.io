Chapter 3: Before and After Becoming Lovers

“At last, I’ve got it.”

The name Arisaka Yorka lit up on my smartphone screen. I finally secured her contact information after a year.

“I’ve waited so long…” I collapsed onto my bed, on the verge of tears of joy.

This moment of relaxation in my room marked the culmination of accomplishments I’d lost count of just today, and I couldn’t help but grin.

“We’re finally starting to feel like a couple.”

“Now, what should I send?”

I had been typing and deleting messages for about an hour.

I should have just sent a stamp or something impulsively.

The pressure not to ruin the lingering atmosphere of the day with a poorly chosen message made me hesitate, that’s why I was still struggling to send the first text.

Unable to come up with a clever message, I decided to give my overworked brain a break and placed my phone by my pillow.

“Who would’ve thought I’d end up dating Arisaka…”

As I gazed at the ceiling, memories of my long friendship with Arisaka Yorka flooded back.

Arisaka Yorka had already become the talk of the school by the time she entered.

A stunning beauty who got the highest score on the entrance exam and refused to deliver the opening speech as the representative of the new students. Even after admission, she consistently secured the top spot in all tests.

Moreover, this mysterious beauty had one peculiar trait — she hated socializing.

Despite attending classes, Arisaka maintained no personal connections beyond that. Every lunch break, she vanished from the classroom, and no one knew where she went.

──The beautiful, aloof honor student at Eisei High, who made no friends, sought none, and kept everyone at a distance.

She wore silence like armor, never flocking with others, and her face never bore a smile. Those who dared to disturb her serene tranquility faced her unrelenting retribution.

During breaks, regardless of class or grade, someone would confess to her. Arisaka, adept as she was, never rejected anyone outright. If someone initiated a conversation, she wouldn’t make eye contact, pretending not to hear, causing about seventy percent to give up. They’d pretend to talk to themselves and move away.

The persistent twenty percent used relentless gestures and actions to forcibly grab her attention. But her cold gaze and beauty overwhelmed them, making them retreat with words trailing off.

The last ten percent, undeterred, persisted in expressing their feelings unilaterally. To these perpetrators of borderline harassment, Arisaka had only one response:

“You’re a nuisance.”

Her consistent attitude spread throughout the school, and by the end of last Golden Week, those foolish enough to confess had completely disappeared. Arisaka, with her threefold crown of appearance, intellect, and indifference, became unapproachable even to her classmates.

High schoolers weren’t children who took “getting along with everyone” literally. Choosing relationships while seeking an optimal level of mutual comfort was a sign of developed social skills.

In this way, the beauty known as Arisaka Yorka became an untouchable presence that could only be observed. This was a stance Arisaka desired, and as long as one refrained from meddling, no issues arose.

However, the school, responsible for its students, couldn’t just let it be.

Arisaka’s preference for silence and indifference puzzled her homeroom teacher, Kanzaki Shizuru. Concerned about her low level of participation in class, Kanzaki-sensei decided to intervene and find someone to be the bridge between Arisaka and the rest of the class.

The responsibility fell on me, Sena Kisumi. The reason was simple, I was in the same class as Arisaka and served as the class representative.

“Arisaka-san deliberately avoids building connections with others.”

“It seems like she’s really not good at socializing, huh?”

“Rather than not being good, it’s more like she just hates it. So, Sena-san, could you become close to her?”

This beautiful teacher always threw impossible tasks at me out of nowhere.

“──Kanzaki-sensei, this conversation is taking quite a drastic turn.”

“You can do it, Sena-san.”

The beautiful female teacher insisted on my capability.

“No matter how much others tried to talk to her, it was all in vain. Pushing normalcy onto someone special like Arisaka is not the way to go. She always disappears from the classroom during lunch break.” I expressed my honest opinion, trying to evade this impossible mission.

“Arisaka-san is usually in the art prep room. Sena-san, please go.”

For some reason, Kanzaki-sensei knew her whereabouts.

“Wouldn’t it be better if someone of the same gender handles such a delicate issue? As a guy, my presence might raise unnecessary alarms.”

I tried to gently refuse again with what seemed like a reasonable excuse, but refusal wasn’t an option from the start.

“Anyway, just go to the art prep room once. We’ll talk after that.”

Unable to resist Kanzaki-sensei’s insistence, I reluctantly went to the art prep room where Arisaka was supposedly hiding.

“You’re a nuisance. Go back. Just disappear.”

Of course, Arisaka wouldn’t welcome an outsider. She bombarded me with three consecutive rejection words as if launching a preemptive strike.

As I observed cautiously from behind the door, Arisaka embodied wariness. She looked extremely displeased, radiating maximum hostility.

She watched me without letting her guard down, attempting to drive me away with just her sharp gaze. Normally, one would retreat under the pressure of her intense stare.

However, what caught me off guard was Arisaka, who should have taken the initiative silently, actually speaking.

“Sena-kun, how did you find this place?” Arisaka eyed me suspiciously as I stood before the door.

“Oh, you know my name?”

“You’re the class rep. Just answer.” Arisaka’s interrogative tone suggested she had a suspect in mind.

“That teacher! You didn’t tell anyone else, right?”

“I didn’t.”

“Good. Don’t ever tell anyone. Now, goodbye. Don’t come back.” She declared firmly, determined to keep anyone out of her territory.

“If you don’t want to hear abusive language, then disappear from here.”

“At least you’re aware of the abuse.”

“Well, it just occurred to me – we’re talking quite a bit, aren’t we?” As far as I knew, no student had ever conversed with Arisaka for this long. This might be a new record.

“Who cares!” Arisaka forcefully closed the door.

This marked our third encounter.

Although I felt uneasy heading to the art prep room, meeting her turned out to be surprisingly enjoyable. Somehow, she was unexpectedly interesting when you talked to her.

That’s how, without a hint of romantic interest at first, I found myself visiting the art prep room whenever I had some free time, driven merely by idle curiosity.

Frustration toward me must have built up. Almost threateningly, Arisaka forcefully slammed her hand against the shelf holding oil paintings.

The shelves swayed, and I noticed the oil painting stacked on the top one was about to collapse, aiming to land right on her head.

“Arisaka!”

I tossed my lunch aside and rushed into the art prep room, instinctively covering her as the sharp edge of the canvas landed heavily on me.

“Ugh!?”

The wooden frame was solid, and pain shot through my back, shoulders, arms, and the back of my head, reflexively tensing my entire body.

In that moment, I unintentionally ended up embracing her tightly.

“────!”

I heard her gasp near my ear. However, due to enduring the pain, I hadn’t comprehended the situation. We both lost our balance, collapsing to the floor in a tangled heap.

When I opened my eyes, Arisaka’s face was right in front of me.

“Intrusion. Sexual harassment. Assault.”

Tears welled up in her eyes as she laid rigid beneath me.

I quickly moved away from Arisaka while staggering because of the pain. The fall from the oil painting, along with hitting my elbows and knees during the fall, made standing up difficult. My whole body hurt so much I felt like crying.

“Sorry,” I apologized for now.

Glancing at the floor, my lunch—once a sandwich—laid crushed beneath the fallen oil painting. The pain was still excruciating, and any trace of my appetite had vanished.

All that left was an awkward silence.

Arisaka slowly stood up, staring down at me. “It’s fortunate we’re at the far end of the school. Even if I scream, no one will hear.”

“My bad, I got carried away. This is my fault.”

“Geez, we’ll need to clean up this mess.”

“I’ll handle it.”

“In either case, you can’t move for a while, right?”

I remained seated on the floor, leaning against the wall until the pain subsided.

“…Fair point. How about you? Any injuries?”

“My butt hurts. If I make a fuss, maybe I won’t have to see your face again in the school.”

Terrified by the fear of someone else holding the power of life and death over me, I shivered. While threatening to turn my high school life into hell, Arisaka casually rubbed her plump ass through her skirt.

No. If things go badly, will I be kicked out of school? Cut me some slack!

“Why are you flaunting your butt in front of a guy?”

For someone who always presented herself perfectly, wasn’t this a bit careless?

Bang! Out of nowhere, Arisaka’s long legs extended right next to my face, delivering an astonishingly sharp kick against the wall.

I held my breath and froze. “A-Are you trying to kill me?”

“My brain can’t compare to the genius who aced the exams… Now…can you please get your legs off? I’m begging you.”

“Would heaven be getting further away?”

“Are you a pervert or what?! Your panties are on full display!”

“──Uh, w-wait!”

Arisaka, who had realized the current situation, hurriedly retracted her leg and clutched the hem of her skirt. She retreated all the way to the window, her face as red as an apple.

“Y-y-y-you saw?”

“I’ll make an effort to forget.”

“Just say you didn’t see anything!”

“Then, wear something less conspicuous! It’s way too sexy!”

“I wear it because it’s cute! Men have no business criticizing my underwear preferences.”

“Well, that’s true…”

Our eyes met, and both of us fell into an awkward silence. That weird fluttering sensation still coursed through my body. Arisaka was clearly filled with regret about her carelessness.

The air became increasingly tense. I endured the pain and stood up. “Anyway, I’ll clean this up properly.”

“Shouldn’t you go to the nurse’s office first?”

“Then, after school, I’ll take care of it.”

I picked up the scattered paintings and leaned them against the wall. It was still tough to reach the top shelf and put the paintings back. Yet, leaving this place and walking away from this situation didn’t feel right.

“You don’t have to come back.”

When I left the room, Arisaka said those words to my departing back.

“You’re unbelievable. I can’t believe you actually came,” Arisaka said, still at school. The oil paintings remained as I’d left them.

“You’re quite the healthy one, Sena-kun.”

“What’s with the moralistic textbook-like expression?”

She looked exasperated, not even showing a sign to help. She remained seated in her usual chair, unmoving.

“If you were a bit more flexible with your relationships, I wouldn’t have to come here. You know how persistent Kanzaki-sensei is, right?”
