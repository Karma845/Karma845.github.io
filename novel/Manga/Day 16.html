Confinement Day 16

It’s a new day, and today I’m focused on working on a manga gag inspired by the notes I took yesterday. Considering the genre on which the story will be based, short chapters instead of long plots would be appropriate.

Eventually, the girl enters the room with a tray, it was time for lunch. Today’s menu was Japanese food. Rice, miso soup with eggplant, and grilled fish.

Before I knew it, she sat down across from me, and proceeded to eat. It seems that eating together will be a common thing between us from now on.

Watching her taste her food, I was a little surprised to witness her wonderful use of chopsticks, details like this hint that this is not just anyone. I even felt a little embarrassed.

I have to do my best to be able to strike up a conversation with her. But because of my nervousness, I had forgotten the question for the first topic of conversation I had already planned in my head.

I put the chopsticks down on the chopstick rest and with great courage opened my mouth.

"Hmm?"

"Umm, hey, is it okay for you if you tell me your name? It just doesn’t feel right for me not to address you in the right way, it feels weird."

It’s been over half a month since I started living in captivity with her. And yet, I still don’t know anything about her. I should at least know her name.

"Konata. In Kanji the meaning is ‘here’."

My body stiffened as I tried to pull the bones out of the fish. After a while of silence, I spoke again.

"I see, my name is…"

"I know."

"Oh? Really? My real name?"

I was perplexed at such a statement.

"Of course." She replied as she nodded her head vigorously.

"I—I see. After all, you had possession of my tablet, my phone, and even my bank PIN. It was obvious that you might already know what my true identity was."

"Yes."

Konata answered cautiously, and then continued to focus on her food. But at that moment I remembered something.

"Although… On my tablet my real name is nowhere to be found. I only use the pseudonym with which I represent myself on social networks. And my phone uses a pattern lock, not a pin…"

My thoughts filtered through my mouth. I was struggling to find a correlation between one thing and the other. There were still loose ends that I wasn’t entirely convinced about.

"Your wallet. I only had to look at your driver’s license or insurance card to find that information."

Her answer was so obvious that I felt kind of silly for not having thought of it sooner. But…

"And I already knew that before you passed out."

However, what she went on to say was even more surprising. So, does it mean that she already knew perfectly well who I was?

"Oh, right. You knew when you were in line and I dropped my phone without realizing it. I had also said my name out loud when the store clerk didn’t know how to spell my name."

"..."

Konata smiled vaguely and began to pick up the grains of rice left on her plate.

"Still, how could you come up with my birthday? It’s not like I made it public… Or did I?"

But, now that I think about it more calmly, I probably did do it unconsciously. Not directly. But if the girl was smart, she probably found a certain pattern among my posts, or I just missed a photo of a birthday cake.

"You’re wrong, actually… I am a fan." She said with an embarrassed expression.

"Really?"

"Yes, I have every volume of your work."

"Amazing."

"Even with bonus content that you’ve published."

"I see… That makes me very happy."

A big smile formed on my face. And my words of gratitude came from the bottom of my heart.

I have received many comments like that from my fans on social media. But this is the first time I have met one in person.

I’ve been approached on occasion to sign autographs of my works in bookstores, but I’m not the kind of artist who likes that sort of thing.

And in the face of all these unexpected developments. I was missing an important topic to discuss. If this girl is a fan of my work, why is she holding me hostage?

Although I was very intrigued to get out of this doubt, I decided to keep it to myself, as I ran the risk of ruining the good mood I was in.
