Truth and What Lies Ahead


 


The master of Clan Judgment, Chaos's petty crimes were handled in secret.


The crime of intentionally damaging the fence in the Misty Forest, causing adventurers to get hurt. It was also concealed that the one who fell into that trap was the currently renowned S-rank adventurer, Sasha.


As a result, Chaos was demoted to D-rank adventurer status, and the clan was disbanded as punishment.


Sasha was surprised that Judgment, which she had just joined, was disbanded, but she took in all the members and made them part of Clan Sacred.


The official reason for Chaos's demotion was that he had committed a petty crime. Sasha seems to think that her own clumsiness caused her injury, so she doesn’t seem to know much about the crime Chaos committed.


"So... what about him?"


"He left the royal capital. He probably can’t continue his adventurer activities here anymore."


Haise heard the details from Geist in the Guildmaster's room.


Sipping his tea, he listened to the news of Chaos being demoted.


"Sasha doesn’t know anything, huh?"


"There’s no need to worry about such a small fry. Besides, I’ve already dealt with Chaos. During the process, Apollon showed up and started saying things like 'he’s got a nice ass'... well, never mind, I shouldn’t say more."

Apollon.


Haise decided not to dwell on what the cardinal of the church had done.


Geist cleared his throat and changed the subject.


"Anyway, we’ve installed new fences. From now on, we’ll also be doing regular patrols. Let’s consider this matter closed... Haise, you should be careful too. When you become famous, there will be those who envy you."


"I’m a solo adventurer, so it’s not a problem for me. But don’t you think the clan side also has some issues in this case?"


"Hmm..."


This whole mess was something like "a clan that struggled to operate due to members being poached, retaliating against the clan that poached them".


Well, to be precise, it wasn’t poaching. The teams simply switched sides because they found Clan Sacred more appealing than Clan Judgment.


And then, out of jealousy, Chaos harassed Sasha.


"I don’t know much about it, but there are a lot of teams switching to Sasha’s clan, right? Because of that, aren’t there a lot of clans that can’t maintain their operations and end up disbanding?"


"...Well, that’s true."


Just from checking, over ten small clans have disbanded.


Some teams, instead of disbanding, have chosen to operate under Clan Sacred and take on requests from them.


"Speaking of which, Presea mentioned something at the bar. She said that in the Yggdra, there are almost no clans. The Yggdrasil is the main focus, and under it, a few large clans operate as subsidiaries, while solo adventurers take on requests from the Adventurer’s Guild. The roles of solo, teams, and clans are clearly defined there."


"That’s true. It’s not just Ivis; the other four great clans are the same. The fact that the four great clans are recognized as massive entities is why almost all other clans fall under their umbrella."


"In Sasha’s case, she hasn’t gained that level of trust yet, which leads to competition over requests and teams leaving..."


"There’s nothing we can do about that. Which clan a team belongs to is up to the team’s preference. To operate smoothly like the four great clans, you need experience, time, and trust."


There are quite a few clans that admire Sasha and join Clan Sacred as subsidiaries. But as things stand, there are more clans that are struggling to keep up with Sacred.


In the Heiberg Kingdom alone, there are hundreds of clans, big and small, and thousands of teams.


Even with so many clans and teams, only about 20 to 30 percent of the daily requests get handled. That’s how many troubles and extermination requests there are in this world.


Then, Geist pulled out a letter from his drawer.


"Haise. Aren’t you going to the Graveyard of Ruin yet?"


"I’m not going, or rather... I can’t. Something about the big shots in the Holy Cross Adramelech Theocracy not giving permission to enter the Graveyard of Ruin, causing tension with the Heiberg Kingdom."

"Besides, isn’t it normal for requests like that to be issued only after all preparations are done? How can they ask us to 'get information on the Forbidden Six Labyrinths from the Graveyard of Ruin' when the preparations aren’t even ready? It’s frustrating... I’m still stuck in the capital, worried someone might beat me to it. I shouldn’t have even heard about this information."

The adventurer education system.


The Holy Cross Adramelech Theocracy has an "academy for training adventurers", where young boys and girls who have awakened their abilities are enrolled to learn the ways of adventurers.


They enter at the age of twelve, undergo five years of study and practical experience, and graduate at seventeen.


Graduates are registered as C-rank adventurers.


In the Heiberg Kingdom, adventurers can obtain their license at the age of twelve, starting at F-rank. They typically join veteran adventurer teams, work their way up from the bottom, and gradually increase their rank. It usually takes four to seven years to reach C-rank. Going to school for five years and graduating as a C-rank is pretty standard.


Graduates of the academy are called Elite Adventurers.


In reality, their mastery of abilities, tactics, and combat skills puts them above the average C-rank, and some are even stronger than A-rank adventurers.


"I’ve heard about it. Elite Adventurers look down on those who started from the bottom."
