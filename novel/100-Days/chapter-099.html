S-Rank Adventurer's Guidance 4


 


Haise was troubled.


"Umm..."


Non-lethal bullets—riot-suppressing rubber rounds.


Since he couldn't use live ammunition against the students this time, he opted for these rubber bullets... but they were far less effective than he'd expected. A hit to the limbs wasn't enough to take a student down.


So, it was double the work: using rubber bullets to suppress while relying on hand-to-hand combat to knock them out.


But that wasn't all.


The students were stronger than he'd anticipated.


"Now, what should I—"


"HAISE, MOVE!!"


"Huh?"


The next instant, Hijiri flung a hulking student straight at Haise.


Startled, he barely dodged in time—only for an arrow shot by another student to graze his armband, tearing it off.


"Damn it..."


"GOT IIIIIIT!!"


"Ah..."


A boy caught the armband... and just like that, Haise lost.


"We did it!! We beat an S-Rank adventurer!!"


"Woooh!!"


"VICTORYYYYYYYYY!!"


The students erupted in excitement.


Around twenty had come for Haise—all of them now cheering.


Haise raised both hands and spoke.


"My loss. Good work, everyone."


"""""THANK YOU SO MUCHHHHHHHH!!"""""


Haise smiled, not a trace of bitterness in his expression.


Then, Presea approached with a towel.


"Good work."


"Yeah, thanks."


As he wiped his sweat and took a drink, a few female students stared intently at Haise and Presea.


Finally, one of them spoke up.


"U-Um... by any chance, are you two dating?!"


"Hah?"


"I mean, Presea handed you the towel and drink so naturally..."


"It looked super smooth, right?"


"We're not dating. We just understand each other well enough that we don't need to."


""""Eeeek!!"""


"Hey, don't say weird stuff."


Haise shot Presea a glare before turning his attention to the remaining S-Rank adventurers' battles.


◇◇◇◇◇


Hijiri was wreaking havoc, while Geist fought silently.


A stark contrast—Sasha mused as she deftly handled the attacks of twenty-five students coming at her.


She thought to herself:


(How interesting...)


There were a hundred students in total—twenty teams of five, with five teams assigned to each S-Rank adventurer. If any one of those five teams snatched the armband, they'd clear the challenge.


Most teams charged in with the mindset of "Our team will take the armband!"... but the five teams (twenty-five students) facing Sasha moved as one, coordinating flawlessly.


Sasha's eyes landed on the boy giving orders from the back—Sein.


"Shield team forward, sniper teams spread left and right, vanguard team surround Sasha, rearguard team assist... yeah, like that. Go."


They were relaying commands via someone's ability, maintaining perfect coordination.


The vanguard team encircled Sasha, supported by the rearguard, while snipers positioned themselves in her blind spots. Somewhere among them were a few students lying in wait to target the armband.


Impressive teamwork for an impromptu unit, Sasha noted approvingly.


Then, Miko, wielding a wooden greatsword, stepped forward.


"Sasha, here I come!!"


Miko's body glowed as an enchanter ally bolstered her with physical enhancement magic.


A lower-tier ability under Tycoon's [Sage]. Sasha coated her arms in a faint golden aura and blocked Miko's sword head-on.


"Guh...!"


"Good strength. But—still not enough."


"Ugh...!?"


Sasha deflected the wooden blade with the flat of her sword, then drove a palm strike into Miko's stomach—splintering the greatsword in two.


"Ah...!"


"[Paladin], huh... A fine ability. But—"


"...I’m not cut out for this, am I? Too small, can’t even swing a sword without help..."


"Miko. How much do you actually understand about your ability?"


"Huh?"


"[Paladin] is a composite ability—multiple [Swordsman]-type abilities combined. The most well-known style involves a greatsword and shield... but Miko, you should understand your ability better."


"...Huh?"


With that, Sasha lunged forward.


She cut off the girl issuing commands, then isolated the shield-bearing student. A beast tamer rode a horse-like monster, providing support.


Miko pondered what she should do.


"............[Paladin]."


When Miko first learned of her [Paladin] ability... her parents had told her, "Paladins are so cool! They fight with greatswords and shields, like true knights."


So, she'd wanted to be like that too.


She picked up a greatsword and shield... but couldn't wield both.


So, she settled for just the greatsword... but it was too heavy to swing properly.


"Wait."


She paused—and thought.


She vaguely recalled reading it in an "ability manual" once.


The strongest blade-related ability was [Sword Master]. That was undisputed.


The weakest was [Swordsman]... but [Swordsman] was compatible with all bladed weapons—no, any weapon with an edge.


[Paladin] was specialized for greatswords and shields.


But that wasn't all.


"——...Ah."


It hit her.


She'd been fixated on the cool appearance—greatsword and shield.


But... [Paladin] wasn't just that.


"Gwah!?"


The beast tamer boy was sent flying by Sasha's fighting spirit.


Then, the spear-wielding boy and axe-wielding girl had their weapons knocked away.


Trusting her instincts, Miko—dashed forward.


She snatched up a fallen spear, then leaped onto the horse-like monster the beast tamer had been riding.


Seeing this, Sasha smirked.


"——I can do this!!"


"Correct."


The [Paladin] ability wasn't limited to greatswords and shields.


It had aptitudes for mounted combat—equestrianism and spearmanship.


A spear wasn't as heavy as a greatsword. Even an iron one was manageable for Miko.


The horse monster obeyed her surprisingly well, neighing happily as she gripped the reins.


"YEEEEEAHHH!!"


Miko was certain now—this was her style: spear and steed.


She tightened the reins, urging the horse into a charge—straight at Sasha.
