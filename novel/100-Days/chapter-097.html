S-Rank Adventurer's Guidance 2


 


Unarmed students were assigned to Hijiri, while weapon-wielding students were placed under Haise's instruction.


The unarmed group was mostly male. But the boys who underestimated the petite girl their age were stunned when she caught their full-powered punches with one hand and remarked, "Hey, not bad."


Hijiri wasn’t using her [Metal Master] ability as she sparred with the students.


"You leave too many openings. I get that you rely on power, but if I can stop you, you won’t stand a chance against magical beasts. Increase your combos and move more precisely!!"


"Y-yes!! Th-thanks!!"


"Good response!! Next!!"


She flashed a bright smile at the student, and the boy turned beet red, shrinking into himself.


Her appearance was that of an absurdly beautiful girl. Most boys their age probably didn’t have much experience being smiled at by a girl like her. Surprisingly, Hijiri’s guidance was meticulous—enough to shock even Haise.


Meanwhile, Haise was...


"Cheeeeeriaaaaaaaah!!"


"Whoa."


Dodging the axe-wielding boy’s wild swings, Haise deflected a near-hit by firing a rubber bullet at the side of the axe to alter its trajectory.


This boy had the [Axeman] ability. Relying on it could make anyone somewhat strong.


But only somewhat. Abilities weren’t omnipotent.


After a few minutes of sparring, Haise shot the boy’s fingers with a rubber bullet, forcing him to drop his weapon. Then, he pressed his Desert Eagle against the boy’s forehead.


"Alright, that’s enough. First, stop screaming. I get that you’re hyping yourself up, but it’s just tiring you out. Also, you rely too much on your ability. You’ve got a good build—start by training your body more."


"Y-yes..."


The boy picked up his axe and retreated.


The next opponent was—Miko, gripping a wooden greatsword.


"Haise-san, please go easy on me!!"


"Uh, yeah... That sword, it’s wooden?"


"Yes. It’s a bokken made from Kaka wood."


Kaka wood—a lightweight timber used for firewood and sheds. Cheap and sturdy, it was popular among commoners for building simple storage huts.


Miko’s ability was [Paladin]... On paper, it was a blessed ability.


"Here I go!!"


"Sure."


Haise swapped magazines and racked the slide.


Miko raised her wooden sword and charged straight at him.


The power of [Paladin] improved greatsword handling. And there was one more thing...


"...So you still can’t wield a shield?"


A shield.


Paladins were balanced—greatsword in one hand, shield in the other.


But Miko’s small frame could barely manage the wooden greatsword.


"Haaaaaah!!"


Swinging the greatsword with both hands, she slashed at him.


But even a sharp blade meant nothing without speed. It was clear her physique didn’t match her ability.


What a waste—Haise thought, dodging a few strikes before shooting the sword from her hands.


"Alright, that’s enough."


"Ugh... I didn’t stand a chance."


"............"


"Haise-san?"


"Ah, no..."


Haise hesitated, unsure what to say.


Frankly, she wasn’t cut out to be an adventurer. While everyone else fought with proper weapons, she brought a wooden sword. Not that she could help it—Miko couldn’t wield steel.


Miko nodded. "Ah, I see."


"I get it. My body can’t fully utilize the [Paladin] ability. A [Paladin] who can’t even wield a greatsword or shield is..."


"............"


Haise struggled for words.


Before he could respond, Miko bowed and walked away.


"............"


Feeling guilty, Haise shifted his focus to the next opponent.


◇◇◇◇◇


Geist’s lesson was incredibly straightforward.


"Simply put, having an ability doesn’t make you omnipotent. Even if your ability is [Swordsman], picking up a sword will only make you slightly better than an amateur. Abilities only become usable through continuous training and refinement... According to one researcher, abilities have levels."


A student raised their hand.


Geist had said, "Ask questions anytime, no restrictions." So they did—and he answered every one patiently.


"But levels are considered theoretical..."


"True, some say that. But personally, I believe it. There’s a theory that reaching a certain level awakens further potential."


Geist knew.


For example, Sasha’s battle aura.


Through relentless training with her [Sword Master] ability, Sasha’s aura awakened—first silver, then gold during the Stampede battle. Geist believed mental growth and combat experience triggered it.


"There’s no definitive answer. Believe what you will."


Assistant Miina nodded eagerly.


Then, Geist continued.


"Now then... Let’s hear from an active guild employee about guild operations."


"Y-yes, sir!!"


Caught off guard, Miina began speaking nervously.


◇◇◇◇◇


"—...And that’s how we fought and defeated Shoggoth in the deepest part of the Forbidden Six Labyrinth."


Sasha recounted her battle against Shoggoth Noire Ooze—omitting the demonkin, per confidentiality. Geist had said sharing combat experience was fine, so long as details were vague.


The students listened intently... but there was a slight problem.


"Um, question! How do I join your clan?"


"...Form a team, build a reputation, then apply."


Too many personal, off-topic questions.


At first, she thought they were mocking her. But... no. They were genuinely interested.


Not just her—Presea too. Many boys stared at them with dreamy eyes.


Presea remained expressionless. Sasha sighed quietly.


"...Any questions about me will be addressed later."


Sasha made up her mind.


During the afternoon mock battles, she’d drill these boys properly.
