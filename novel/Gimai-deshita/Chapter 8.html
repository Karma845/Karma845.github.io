Chapter 8: Actually, My "Stepsister" will be Attending the Same High School as me..."

Summer vacation is over, and today is the opening ceremony.

I hurriedly stuffed the homework I finished last night into my backpack and changed into my uniform.

It's been a while since I wore my uniform, thanks to Aunt Miyuki's help in taking it for dry cleaning. There's not a single wrinkle. My shirt, which was already worn out from washing, now seems to have been ironed with a hot iron and looks as clean as new clothes. It feels very comfortable to wear.

It's just past seven o'clock now.

I went downstairs and saw Akira already sitting at the dining table, watching the news on TV and eating breakfast.

"Good morning, Aniki."

"Good morning, Akira. What time did you wake up?"

"Around five o'clock, I was too nervous, so I woke up..."

"I see. It's your first day going to school. Are you all prepared?"

"Mm... yeah, Aniki, you should have breakfast too."

"Oh, right. By the way, where's Aunt Miyuki?"

"Mom said she had a photo shoot early in the morning, so she left first."

"I see. Then we should lock the door properly."

I sat across from Akira.

There was salad, scrambled eggs, and fried sausages on the table. Aunt Miyuki must have prepared them for us before she left.

While the toast was toasting, I boiled water in the electric kettle and poured hot water into a bowl of instant soup and a cup of instant coffee. Soon, the toasted bread turned golden brown and popped out of the toaster.

"Itadakimasu."

I didn't used to say this before meals, but after this past month, it has become a habit.

"Ah... I feel so nervous..."

"You're already nervous even before reaching the school?"

"Because it's my first time transferring schools. I have to introduce myself in front of everyone at the beginning..."

"Ahahaha, that's just a tradition. Well, remember to practice smiling, at least during self-introductions and greetings."

I emphasized the part about "smiling."

Akira is shy by nature, so Aunt Miyuki was worried that she wouldn't be able to smoothly integrate into the new school after transferring.

Akira seemed to be worried too. She has been restless and unable to calm down since the day before yesterday.

I already practiced how to greet people on her first day of transferring yesterday, so it's up to her now.

I told her, "Good luck," but all I got in return was a somewhat unreliable "Mm."

As her older brother, I'm a little worried.

If something happens... No, I hope nothing happens. Let's pray that everything goes well.

◇◇◇

After finishing breakfast, Akira and I closed the windows, locked the door, and left home together.

We chatted about unrelated topics as we headed towards the train station.

It takes about five minutes to walk from home to the Arisuminami Station. After taking the train for about ten minutes, we would arrive at the Jochi Gakuen-mae Station. From there, it's another five-minute walk to Jochi Gakuen.

From the moment we arrived at the platform of Arisuminami Station, Akira suddenly became quiet.

I observed her with confusion and realized that it was because she saw people wearing the same uniform. She became restless and kept stealing glances at those people.

It seems she has entered the "well-behaved" mode.

Perhaps due to nervousness and anxiety, Akira has been clinging to my sleeve since a while ago.

She is no longer the lively younger sister who would casually call me "Aniki" while playing video games. Now she appears pure, dignified, and shy.

I may be too reckless, but seeing her like this, I feel my heart beating faster.

I stepped into the train that arrived and stood next to Akira.

When the train passed one station, Akira's head gently bumped against my chest. And then...

"Hmm... it's okay, just follow the practice, just like in the practice..."

I heard Akira murmuring softly.

She rested her head on my chest like that, relieving her tension.

"Akira, are you okay?"

"Mm, I'm fine. As long as I keep doing this..."

Whenever the train swayed, Akira's head would lightly bump against my chest. It tickled my chest a little.

"Are you really okay?"

"Mm. So, just maintain this for a little while longer... I'm recharging..."

"Am I your charger?"

"Mm... because as long as I'm with Aniki, I'll have full energy..."

What kind of relationship must we appear to others?

Feeling shy, I shifted my gaze to the advertisements inside the train car.

I couldn't help but worry if she could hear my heartbeat.

When we reached the ticket gate at Jochi Gakuen-mae Station, Akira finally let go.

As we got closer to the school, more and more students were heading there.

"Aniki, you're walking too fast..."

"Oh, sorry..."

Akira was wearing loafers instead of her usual sneakers. She and Aunt Miyuki bought them two days ago at the shopping mall.

"These loafers are so hard to walk in..."

"You'll get used to them gradually. It might be a headache at first, but once you get used to it, it'll be fine."

"Like how I stick to you and make you get used to me?"

"Don't bring up that topic once we step out of the house..."

Besides, I haven't gotten used to it yet.

"My ankle is starting to hurt a bit..."

"We're almost there, endure a little longer."

"Mm, I'll do my best..."

"Oh, by the way, why are you wearing loafers? It wouldn't make a difference if you wore sneakers."

"Because loafers look absolutely great with this uniform..."

After she said that, I noticed that there were indeed very few girls around wearing sneakers.

"Well, yeah... you look really nice in them."

"Really?"

"Really."

Even if I look at her again, I still think Akira looks great in this uniform.

Just by changing clothes, she transforms so much. It's surprising, but walking alongside such an adorable sister, I'm also amazed at myself.

Is it the same feeling I have when walking with Kousei and Hinata?

Just as I was thinking that, I saw a familiar blond guy and a girl with a ponytail walking a little distance ahead on the road.

"Oh, I see someone I know."

"Huh?"

"It's the people I mentioned before, the ones who helped you clean your room."

"You mean them?"

"Yes. Shall we go say hello?"

Akira nodded in agreement.

However, she still clung tightly to my back. She must be very nervous.

"Kousei, Hinata, good morning."

I quietly greeted them from behind.

"Hmm? Ry... Ta--"

"Ryuta-gaku... cho--"

As soon as the Ueda siblings turned around and saw me, they both froze in place.

To be precise, they froze upon seeing Akira hiding behind me.

"Ahaha... it feels like it's been a while."

"Oh, uh..."

"Uh... yeah..."

It feels really awkward.

Hinata's expression seems a bit gloomy, and Kousei also acts strangely, displaying an unprecedented awkwardness.

"Oh, right, let me introduce her. Come, Akira."

"I'm Hino Akira..."

Akira lowered her head and bowed.

--Let me add a little explanation.

"Hino" is Akira's surname on her official registration.

As for Aunt Miyuki, after marrying my dad, she moved into his family register. In other words, she is now "Majima Miyuki."

But only Akira remains under her biological father's family register.

I always thought that after remarriage, the child's registration would change along with the parents, but it seems things are not that simple. I found out about this a few days ago but didn't inquire about the details.

It's just that I heard Akira decided to introduce herself with her original surname "Hino" when she heard that her surname might change to "Majima."

Dad and the others didn't hear the reason behind it, they only heard Akira say, "I want to maintain the status quo." I think it must be related to her fondness for her biological father.

I didn't delve into the details of that matter.

Anyway, "Hino Akira"...

Maybe it's because her surname contains the character "姬" that instantly adds a feminine touch to the girl.

"Oh~... So this is Hino-san."

Kousei awkwardly looked at me and Akira in turns.

"This is Ueda Kousei. He looks handsome, but he's actually a really sorry guy--"

"Hey."

"--And this is Hinata. She's Kousei's sister, also a first-year like you."

"Nice to meet you..."

Akira greeted with embarrassment, bowing her head.

"So, what's the relationship between Hino-san and Ryouta-senpai?"

"Oh, right! She's my little sister. She's the child of my dad's remarriage partner, whom I mentioned before."

Upon hearing this, the Ueda siblings couldn't help but exchange puzzled glances.

"Huh? Isn't she your younger brother?"

"Ahahaha... Actually, she's not a brother, but a sister..."

"...What?"

Kousei and Hinata were so astonished that their eyes narrowed into small dots.

◇◇◇

I kept the bath incident a secret and roughly explained the situation to the Ueda siblings in front of the school gate at Yuuki Academy.

"I see... You really are an idiot."

Kousei scolded me like that in the end.

"Oh well, so she's your sister after all!"

Hinata quickly accepted it... thankfully she wasn't dumbfounded.

During the explanation, Akira never spoke up. She just kept looking at us in turns, appearing quite uncomfortable.

"You're pretty unlucky too, huh? Having to live with this idiot."

"Hey, you have no right to say that about me. You useless brother."

Although I lightly complained about Kousei, his impression hit the mark, leaving me feeling frustrated.

Akira, on the other hand, had a complex expression on her face. It seemed like she had something she wanted to say.

"Akira, what's wrong?"

"Aniki, the time..."

"Huh? We still have some time before the ceremony starts."

"Not that, I have to go to the faculty office, so..."

I see. Did she mention going to the faculty office before?

"Ah, in that case, I can take you to the office."

Hinata said with a smile, but Akira seemed a bit unsure.

This might be an opportunity for her to make friends with girls in the same grade.

"Akira, why don't you go with Hinata?"

"Huh..."

"Hinata, can I ask you a favor?"

"Sure... Um, can I call you 'Akira-chan'?"

"Just Akira is fine... Adding 'Chan' feels uncomfortable to me..."

"Great! Nice to meet you, Akira."

"Yeah."

The two beautiful girls took a step towards a friendly first encounter.

But when the two beautiful girls walked together, they became even more eye-catching, and the surrounding students looked at them as if saying something. The boys had lecherous expressions on their faces, while the girls who saw Akira, who could be described as a beautiful boy, excitedly asked, "Who is she?"

I watched as they headed towards the shoe lockers in Kawadou, with Kousei and me following slowly from behind.

"By the way, did you really think that girl was your younger brother and let it go for three weeks?"

"Yeah..."

"I always thought you were a slow guy, but I didn't expect you to be this slow..."

"Um..."

"So how did you realize she was your sister?"

Even though it was Kousei asking, I still couldn't bring myself to mention the bathing incident.

"It... It just happened by chance. That's why I called you, asking how to treat my sister..."

"So that phone call meant that? I thought the heat fried your brain."

"I can't stand you. You're not worth being friends with..."

"But regardless, I plan to leave it to Hinata to handle."

"That's just like you. No wonder I'm afraid to consult with you."

Kousei had a thoroughly fed-up expression, but quickly returned to his usual grumpy face.

"So, your sister... is her name Akira? Is she the same at home?"

"No, surprisingly she's..."

After briefly explaining to Kousei what Akira is like at home, he sighed and said with deep emotion, "I see."

"Well, with Hinata by her side, it should be fine."

"I'm really thankful for her... I'm glad I met you guys this morning."

As we were talking, Kousei, who was walking beside me, stopped before entering Kawadou.

"But your sister is really cute."

"You never praise people, so I didn't expect you to praise Akira now. You wouldn't happen to..."

"It's not like that. I was just thinking, do you have feelings for her?"

"...Don't talk nonsense. Akira is just my sister."

"Well, that's good. But Ryouta, you haven't forgotten about 'Mendel's Law,' have you?"

"I haven't forgotten..."

Kousei and I have been friends since middle school, and he's the only person who knows my secret.

There's no sense of kinship in Mendel's Law... He knows the true meaning behind that statement.

So I understand what Kousei wants to say. He wants to say that if something were to happen between me and Akira, it would hurt our dad and the others.

But he's worrying too much.

I'm different from "that woman."

An individual's nature isn't determined by genetics, so I will never let my family be unhappy.

"...It's my fault. I shouldn't have brought up this topic. Don't make such a scary expression."

"I won't. Are you worried about me? Thank you."

After I finished speaking, Kousei extended his arm and placed it on my shoulder.

"But let's not talk about that now. Did you forget something?"

"Huh? What did I forget?"

Kousei stared at me after hearing my response.

"...It's time for lunch. When we were helping you clean your room, didn't you say you'd treat us next time?"

"Oh yeah, I completely forgot..."

I completely disregarded it, but Kousei remembered, which surprised me.

"I'll be busy with my part-time job for a while, so why don't you treat Hinata? Invite her for a meal next time."

"No, that's not a good idea..."

It would be too awkward to have a meal alone with Hinata.

"She's been waiting for you to contact her throughout the whole summer vacation."

"You're talking about Hinata? Really?"

"Yeah... So don't just focus on your sister all the time. Pay attention to my sister too."

After saying that, Kousei left me behind and walked ahead.

Come to think of it, Kousei isn't usually talkative. It feels like he's in a chatty mood today.

Pay attention to my sister too... Is that so?

I don't have much confidence in taking care of such a clever girl.

But maybe it wouldn't be a bad idea to go out for a meal with Akira and Hinata next time.

◇◇◇

The topic of the transfer student, a beautiful girl, quickly spread.

During lunch break, while Kousei and I were eating our boxed lunches, we overheard some boys in our class talking: "Her last name seems to be Hino."

"Your sister is quite popular."

"Well, because she's my sister."

"Your arrogant face is so annoying... Although I don't really care, shouldn't you do something about it? The guys might have already surrounded her, you know?"

"Sigh, I do care, actually..."

Since the news reached the second grade, perhaps not only the first grade but also the third grade already knew... I heard that some senpai in the third grade are hard to deal with...

"If you care, go check it out."

"I don't want to go alone!"

"You're a girl, it's such a hassle..."

I quickly stuffed the lunch into my mouth and forcibly dragged Kousei, who didn't want to go, up one floor.

After going upstairs, we turned right and looked at the corridor ahead. We could see people coming and going only in front of a certain classroom.

"That seems to be the place."

"And unfortunately, it's Hinata's class..."

It seems that Akira and Hinata are in the same class.

The boys were all eager to catch a glimpse of the transfer student. Kousei and I squeezed through the crowd and arrived in front of the classroom.

When we looked inside the classroom, a circle of girls had formed around a certain spot, while boys sporadically sat around, staring at the seat by the window. We couldn't see the center of the circle from here.

"They must be in there."

"Yeah, it seems like Hinata got dragged into it too."

Our expectations were soon realized. One of the taller girls moved a bit, and through the gap in the crowd, we caught a glimpse of Akira and Hinata's faces.

Akira seemed a bit embarrassed, lowering her head. I guess everyone bombarded her with questions, leaving her unsure how to respond.

Meanwhile, Hinata was helping Akira from the side. She really is a good girl.

"Your sister is popular among girls too?"

"I heard that at her previous school, she was confessed to by girls."

I remember her complaining before that being liked by girls was even more troublesome than being liked by boys.

"But judging by her appearance, boys probably won't be able to get close to her."

"Yeah, anyway, it should be fine. Let's go back to the classroom."

As we turned around, a girl noticed Kousei.

"Ah... Hinata, Kousei-senpai is here! Oh my, he's so handsome!"

At that moment, the human wall parted, and both Hinata and Akira looked in our direction.

Then, in the next second, Akira suddenly rushed out from the crowd.

She walked straight towards me and grabbed my left hand.

"Huh? Akira?"

"Um...!"

Akira looked up at me with teary eyes.

I quickly looked around. The classroom and corridor, which had been filled with noise just a moment ago, suddenly fell silent. Everyone was looking at me standing in front of Akira.

The atmosphere was extremely awkward.

"Hello, everyone. I'm Akira's older brother..."

I forced a smile and scratched my head, but everyone's faces displayed question marks.

◇◇◇

I brought Akira to the deserted area behind the school building, and then I heard a loud sigh.

"Sorry, Aniki. I..."

"Don't worry about it."

Akira seemed to be very concerned about the unnecessary attention I received.

It's true that attracting attention is not pleasant.

But when I thought about how Akira had been enduring such gazes all along, I couldn't blame her.

Right now, Ueda siblings should be in the classroom helping with the aftermath. It should be fine to leave it unattended for a short time.

"But it turned out just as I expected."

"What do you mean?"

"I knew that a beautiful transfer student like you wouldn't go unnoticed."

"Don't tease me..."

Akira made a truly disgusted expression and frequently rubbed her left arm.

"After all, the news spread to our class. I guessed that you must be troubled by it."

"Are you here to save me?"

"Huh? W-well, sort of..."

The way she said it feels a bit strange.

In terms of the result, I did save her, but if I had to put it into words, "concerned" would be more accurate.

However, to put it differently, the situation at that time had become so urgent for Akira that she hoped someone would come to her rescue.

A beautiful girl with a shy personality... It doesn't seem like a very good combination.

If I were to say to her now, "You're a heartthrob on your first day of transfer, that's amazing," it would only sound like I'm mocking her.

So I choose to say this:

"After some time, things should settle down. But when you feel troubled, you can rely on me."

"Aniki..."

"Yeah, yeah, because I'm your big brother."

After saying that, although hesitating a bit, I finally placed my hand on Akira's head.

In the past, when I was sad or upset, Dad would pat my head like this. As he patted me, the uneasiness and anguish would inexplicably fade away. For me, Dad's hand was a magical hand.

Regardless of whether Dad had the ability to perform magic on me, I stroked Akira's head. Though she complained, "It's so embarrassing," she gradually showed a smile.

Speaking of which, her hair is fluffy and nice to touch. It makes me want to keep stroking it.

Even though I feel like it's a bit childish, Akira silently allowed me to stroke her head until my hand withdrew.

"Hehehe, Aniki, thank you..."

"Feeling better?"

"Mhmm!"

At that moment, the preparatory bell rang, and we returned to the school building.

As I watched Akira's figure, I still felt a bit worried, but she should be fine based on her appearance.

However, this state might continue for some time.

Although I don't want her to rely on me in this manner, I'll ask Hinata to take care of Akira a bit more later.

◇◇◇

When I returned to the classroom, Kousei sat in his seat with his usual gloomy face.

"Where did you and your sister run off to?"

"Can you phrase it differently..."

Kousei seemed a bit displeased.

"After you guys left, Hinata had a hard time."

"Wasn't it your fault?"

"I found it troublesome, so I came back to the classroom... And just now, Hinata sent me a LIME message, saying that everyone kept asking about your relationship with your sister, wanting to get to the bottom of it."

"Oh no, I feel sorry for her..."

"She already told everyone that you're siblings."

"I'm truly grateful... I'll thank her later."

"It doesn't matter, leave your sister to Hinata."

I thought he would just push everything onto Hinata as usual, but it doesn't seem to be entirely the case.

I can vaguely sense from Kousei's words that he trusts Hinata.

Although he's a guy who often wears a gloomy face and acts carefree, he actually has a gentle personality and values his relationships. He won't look down on or despise others without reason. That's why our relationship has lasted until now.

"By the way, Kousei. Are you angry or something?"

"Not really..."

"What's wrong?"

"You're annoying."

Even though we have a long-standing friendship, I still can't quite understand this guy.

◇◇◇

"There have been suspicious individuals around the school recently. Girls, in particular, should be careful about their safety and try to travel in groups when going to and from school."

The class meeting, mixed with lengthy lectures, seemed to stretch on indefinitely even after the bell rang, but it finally came to an end, and we were finally released.

Kousei and I left the classroom together.

Our route home was the same until halfway, so today we had planned to walk together with Akira and Hinata.

Akira and Hinata should already be waiting for us in front of the agreed-upon school gate.

The school gate was visible from the Kawadoh.

However, as we approached the gate, we saw a group of people gathered there, and a sense of foreboding arose in our hearts, so we hurried over.

As expected, Akira and Hinata were surrounded by male third-year students. They were probably the troublesome senpai that the rumors were about.

"Tell us your names. Ah, should we exchange contact information?"

"Hey, do you have a boyfriend?"

"Oh, this girl here is so cute!"

Hinata and Akira wore expressions of distress, leaning against the pillar of the gate without saying a word.

I spoke up from behind the third-year students:

"Um..."

"Huh? What's a second-year doing?," one of the seniors asked.

"These two are with me..."

"Oh~... So what?"

The senpai retorted, and I didn't know how to respond. I wanted to go home with the two of them, so I asked, "Can you let them go?"

"I don't understand what you're mumbling about~"

"While we're talking to them, can you not interfere?"

I instantly felt the anger rushing to my head, but in front of Akira and Hinata, I managed to calm myself down.

Just as I was contemplating what to do...

"Hey, third-years. Move aside."

Kousei caught up to me and said in a low voice, glaring angrily at the third-year students.

"Oh... this guy is Ueda from the second-year..."

"Hey, let's go..."

Seeing Kousei, the third-year students trembled in fear and ran away like they were being chased.

"...Seriously, Hinata. Haven't I told you before? If those kinds of guys come to bother you, just drive them away."

"But... but they are senpai, and they're scary..."

"You don't need to be afraid of that kind of trash."

Akira seemed frightened too, her eyes welling up with tears as she came to my side and grabbed the left sleeve of my uniform.

"Aniki..."

"Are you scared?"

"Mhmm..."

"It's all right now."

I placed my hand on Akira's head like earlier at noon.

She seemed to let out a sigh of relief and showed a dependent expression. I felt like she would hug me in the next second, but no matter how bold she was, she didn't seem to have the courage to hug someone in public.

"Okay, let's go home now..."

I said, looking at the Ueda siblings. Kousei frowned, and Hinata tightly pursed her lips.

"What's wrong with you guys?"

"It's nothing..."

Their voices overlapped, perfectly timed to be frightening.

Should I say, as expected? The bond between these siblings might be strong.

Afterwards, we chatted casually as we walked on the way home.

Akira didn't say a word, she just held onto my sleeve and walked.

Kousei still had that sour expression, and even Hinata wasn't as talkative as usual, which worried me.
