In my neighborhood, there’s a very ordinary supermarket.

A variety of vegetables, fresh foods such as meat and fish, many kinds of drinks including milk, and seasonings. A variety of colorful snacks and small sundries. It’s a supermarket with nothing special to mention for people who used to live here.

On this day, we established a new rule to not spend more than 200 yen for a single sweets. That way, we won’t waste too much money.

When I returned from shopping, the sun had already set. I turned on the light and started to cook as soon as possible

[Haha, don’t worry, I’ve been eating lots of vegetables that I started to get tired of it]

Yokubari Hamburger Steak is a hamburger steak that is covered with a lot of vegetables.

In addition to onions, cabbage and eggplants are added to the base mixture, and the sweetness of the vegetables and the fat of the minced meat are absorbed to enhance the steak flavour.

In the past, I used to add all kinds of vegetables that were given to me. But after lots of trial and error, I came up with this mixture.

What I put on it is Japanese mushroom sauce made from various mushrooms such as Shiitake, Shimeji, Maitake, Enoki, and Eringi. Boiled down with soy sauce, mirin, and sugar, and thickened with water-soluble potato starch.

It goes well not only with hamburgers but also with white fish, omelets, and pasta sauces. So I make a lot and store the rest in Tupperware.

And if you don’t want to waste your vegetables scraps, add them to the miso soup and dinner is ready.

[Itadakimasu~! Mmm, It’s hot!!! Onii, water!]
