Interlude – Engagement Annulment

“I am back.”

Evening.

After the sun went down, Arisa returned home.

There was no reply.

But that didn’t mean no one was home.

She walked slowly and a tad bit gravely to the dining room and kitchen.

Her foster mother, Emi Amagi, was in the kitchen washing the dishes.

“I’m back. …Emi-san.”

It’s usually Arisa’s job to cook dinner.

It’s not like there’s a specific rule, but it’s customary for Arisa to make it.

Today, however, she came home late because she had to have dinner with Yuzuru.

That’s why Emi unusually cooked dinner.

Of course, Arisa had informed her about it in advance…

Even so, she felt a little heavy-hearted.

On the other hand, when Emi was approached by Arisa, she continued washing the dishes and replied without turning around.

“Oh, welcome home. …You’re quite late returning home.”

The sarcasm came immediately.

Arisa replied, thinking inwardly that it was troublesome.

“Yes… I’m sorry for the inconvenience.”

“No, I didn’t think it was any trouble at all…You were busy, weren’t you?　With a lot of things to do.”

“…Yes.”

It was an implying tone.

However, Arisa, who has learned that if she takes it seriously or refutes it; it will come back to her several times over, let her words slide.

“Then, I’ll take my leave…”

She wanted to go back to her room and go to sleep, so she tried to leave.

Emi hurled a few words at Arisa.

“Oh, and …please wash your dirty body before you go to bed. Also, don’t put your dirty ‘underwear’… in the same washing machine.”

Emi’s intentions were clear when she limited her words to “underwear” instead of “dirty clothes”.

Emi assumed that Arisa had “slept” with Yuzuru.

“…yes, I understand.”

However, since she was just being sarcastic, Arisa didn’t know if she really meant it or not, and it was not important either.

Therefore, it was useless to correct her.

Besides…

(Well, there’s little point in correcting…)

If her relationship with Yuzuru was a misunderstanding, Arisa would be tempted to deny it…

But it wasn’t that much of a misconception either

Arisa did as she was told and went to the bathroom to take a shower.

“That person …has become a little mature…”

To tell the truth, Emi’s sarcasm was a bit of a letdown for Arisa.

Not so long ago, she would have said something much worse.

Emi’s “verbal knife” was clearly losing its sharpness.

This was probably due to the fact that the relationship between Yuzuru and Arisa was going well.

It’s not as if Yuzuru was putting any special pressure on her…

But his family name was indeed a shield that protects Arisa.

(…I feel sorry that I am always getting saved by him.)

Thinking about it, she received a very expensive gift for Christmas.

If someone asks her if she has been able to repay the gift with something adequate, Arisa cannot affirm with confidence that she did.

Of course, Yuzuru might say that she doesn’t need to return the favor…

(As a person …I don’t want to be corrupted…)

If she continues to rely on him and keep receiving gifts from him, she will definitely be corrupted.　

She didn’t want to be the kind of person whose only value was having a great lover.

After getting out of the shower, Arisa quickly wiped off her body, changed into her nightgown, and dried her hair.

She left the changing room and headed for her room.

Arisa’s room was originally a storage room that was renovated.

The reason for this is not that Arisa was treated very badly, but rather that a “third child” was an irregularity for the Amagi couple.

They are a middle-class family, and they don’t have a lot of money like the Takasegawa and Tachibana families. So they couldn’t afford to build another room for Arisa.

However, even though it was a storage room, the structure of the room, such as insulation, was the same as the other rooms, and the air conditioning was working properly, so Arisa had no particular complaints.

As she walked down the hallway…

“Arisa… you’re back.”

“Yes. A little while ago.”

She ran into her cousin, Haruto Amagi.

Right now, he was back at his parents’ house because his university was on spring break.

(I hope he returns soon…)

Originally, Arisa did not have a good impression of Haruto…

Ever since she found out that he was behind the incident involving her former classmate, her impression of him had plummeted.

‘Hurry up and go back to your college apartment’

She thought to herself regularly

Of course, she doesn’t say it out loud.

“I heard… there was a marathon today, but you returned very late.”

“Yes, I did. Is there a problem with that?”

Arisa replied in a cold voice.

As expected, Haruto seemed to notice that Arisa was in a bad mood and flinched.

“N-no… I’m sorry. That was an inconsiderate thing to ask.”

“…”

Arisa frowned at him.

She realized that he flinched …because he apparently had a strange misunderstanding.

Somehow it made Arisa feel uncomfortable.

“It’s not like anything was going on with Yuzuru-san.”

“I-is that so… That’s good…”

The misunderstanding did not seem to have been cleared up.

Arisa, who was troubled, quickly cut off the conversation and tried to return to her room …

“Arisa.. I’ll help you as much as I can!”

He grabbed her arm while saying that.

“…What are you talking about?”　

“I mean… It’s about that…”

Haruto’s expression twisted a bit as if he was having a hard time saying it…

But he looked straight at Arisa and answered.

“It’s about …the engagement. You don’t have to do what that guy says…”

At that moment, Arisa felt the blood rush to her head.

“Can you please stop saying bad things about Yuzuru-san?”

Her voice came out loudly.

When she saw the stunned Haruto, Arisa huffed.

“…I’m sorry”

She bowed slightly and headed for her room to escape.

Then…

“It’s rare to see you fight.”

A girl was standing in front of her room.

She was Arisa’s cousin and stepsister.

Mei Amagi.

“Ah… Mei-chan… Did I interrupt your study?”

“No, I was just playing with my phone, it’s fine. Ah …please don’t tell mother.”

One hour of gaming a day.

This was the family rule that Emi Amagi had created.

Emi doesn’t like games.

Arisa has never played many games because of this family policy of the Amagi family.

But… even if Emi can manage the game console, she can’t fully understand the games played on cell phones, which is a communication device.

That’s why Mei, who has a good head on her shoulders, used to play games on her cell phone without her mother’s knowledge.

In fact, she would secretly beg her father to let her play even games where a small payment was required.

She had a dexterity that Arisa could not imitate.

“And also, I would like to have a word with you, Arisa-san.”

“…you wanted to talk to me?”

“Yes. Well, it’s about the engagement.”

Arisa’s expression tensed up involuntarily.　

Ignoring her, Mei proceeded with the conversation on her own.

“Actually, father asked me if I could take your place if… the engagement between Arisa-san and Takasegawa-san were to be annulled. Ah… no, of course, he said it in a more considerate way.”

Arisa’s mind went blank.

But somehow, she managed to squeeze out the words.

“Th-that ..um… in what sense?”

“It’s just a hypothetical situation… like, if either Arisa-san or Takasegawa-san, or both, dislike the other and the engagement is in trouble. So this is the backup plan.”

Arisa was relieved to hear Mei’s words and patted her chest.

Apparently, it was not as if the engagement had been called off.

(Ah… speaking of which…)

She remembered that her adoptive father, Naoki, had asked her if she disliked being engaged.

In response to this, …Arisa could not give a clear reply.

She was not sure what his intentions were.

She wanted to answer “No” immediately, but…

She was a little scared thinking that maybe answering “yes” was what Naoki wanted.

That’s why she was reluctant to answer, so she decided to “escape” by not answering.

Naoki said, “You can answer me later”. But she didn’t have a chance to talk to him after that, so it remained unanswered.

“…Is that so? So, what about you, Mei-chan?”

“I haven’t really thought that I want to marry Takasegawa-san. I’ve never even met him in person… And I want to take over my father’s business.”

Since Haruto and Naoki don’t get along – technically Haruto dislikes Naoki unilaterally – Haruto went to a department that has little to do with Naoki’s company.

He had little desire to take over the company, and Naoki does not want to force him to do so.

Mei, on the other hand, seemed to have some intention to take over the company.

But she’s still in the sixth grade, so it was unsure how she’ll turn out.

“I-is that so?”

No matter what Naoki’s intentions were, as long as Mei didn’t want to, Arisa was the only one who could marry Yuzuru.

“Well, but… marrying Takasegawa-san doesn’t mean that I won’t be able to take over the company either”

“…Eh?”

“I’ve never met him in person, but from the pictures I’ve seen… he seems like a very cool guy. And, his personality – well my brother is saying that it is bad, but that is just a minority opinion- seems okay to me.”

Mei then smiled slightly.

“He’s rich, after all. Well, if Arisa-san doesn’t like this engagement… I am not reluctant to marry him. But of course, I have to meet him in person and talk to him first.”

Mei then asked the stunned Arisa.

“With that being said, Arisa-san. How do you really feel about getting married to Takasegawa-san?”

Arisa could not answer Mei’s question immediately.

If Mei was willing to get engaged to Yuzuru, and if Naoki thought Mei was more suitable than Arisa, then…

At least Arisa didn’t have the courage to say no.

“Well, um… What about Mei-chan?”

She could not say yes or no.

Therefore, Arisa chose to “escape” by answering the question with a question.

“I’m the one who’s asking. I mean, I told you earlier what my intentions are…”

Mei made a dumbstruck expression.

She then sighed at Arisa, who kept her mouth shut.

“Well, it’s fine… Marriage is a long way off, and I think it would be strange to give an answer right now. I am also reluctant to decide on a future partner at this age.”

But, isn’t it best if you make your intentions clear?

However, if you feel that you don’t care either way, that’s a different case though.

After saying that, Mei left the place.

Arisa, who was left behind, silently went into her room and closed the door.

And…

“…Yuzuru-san.”

She leaned her back against the door and muttered the name of her beloved as if to ask for help.


AN:

Arisa-chan is quiet when it comes to people who seem stronger than her.

On the other hand, she is strong against those who seem to be weaker than her.

Yuzuru-kun is a stronger opponent than herself in Arisa’s eyes, but because he is kind and has helped her on a daily basis, he is judged as safe.
