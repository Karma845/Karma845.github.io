Afterword

This work is a revised version of a story that was originally posted on Kakuyomu.

I changed the course of the story and I wanted to revise it from beginning to end.

So, how was it? Did you find it interesting? It's interesting, right?

I found it interesting!

Even if it gets axed after the first volume, I'll be satisfied!

...Well, that's a lie. It's definitely a lie. I want to write more (honestly).

As a writer, it might be a problem, but I probably enjoyed this work the most.

I focused solely on bringing out the charm of the story to its fullest potential while writing.

As for the content... I wonder how it turned out?

Personally, I believe it become interesting story, but of course, it may not be enjoyable for all readers. Professional writers said, 'write with the intention of entertaining readers' (I heard that somewhere).

I mostly follow that principle as well, but this time, I wrote and revised the novel in a way that I would also enjoy it.

Yes, that's why I was anxious while writing. I wondered, "Is this really going to be okay?"

When I sent the manuscript to my assigned editor, my heart was pounding.

I thought, "What if they say this content can't be made into a book?"

The moment I sent the manuscript by email, I was like, "Ahh! I really sent it!" and I held my head in despair (this is not a joke, I'm serious). It's been years since I felt so flustered, haha.

I was swayed by various emotions, but the process of turning it into a book was very enjoyable, and I am grateful to the editor who gave me that opportunity.

I am also grateful to Nakamura-sensei for handling the illustrations.

When I saw the character designs, I couldn't help but go, "Woah..."

And now, it's up to the readers whether they can enjoy it.

There will surely be various reactions, and there may even be no feedback.

But that's okay.

If even one person can feel the excitement I experienced, that would be amazing.
