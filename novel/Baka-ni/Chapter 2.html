I sent a message from my smartphone to Sensei, who must still be fighting sleepiness in bed.

【Yuunagi: Good morning, Tenjō-san.

Are you coming over for breakfast?

I’m making fried eggs, so please let me know if you have a preference for how they’re cooked.】

After getting ready for the day, I returned to my room, hearing the alarm from next door for what seemed like the umpteenth time.

◇◆◇◆

As I changed from my pajamas into my uniform, the alarm stopped quickly this time.

“Oh, unusually early today.”

As I stood in the kitchen, a message notification came from my phone.

【Reiyu: Good morning, Yuunagi-kun!

Sorry for the noise so early!

Please make the fried eggs runny!】

I couldn’t help but smile at the lively text early in the morning.

Energized by such exchanges, I started preparing breakfast.

By the time I had two servings of food ready on the table, the doorbell of my room rang.

“Good morning.”

“…….”

I found myself gasping.

There stood Reiyu Tenjō in work mode, her hair neatly done and makeup applied, right in front of my room.

It’s like, well, she’s in an idling state before gearing up for the day, ready to give her best.

A rare expression, somewhere between her work and private life, as she’s in the process of raising her spirits.

“Yuunagi-kun? What’s wrong, you’re spacing out?”

“Seeing Tenjō-san this early in the morning in her precious moments before going to school is a feast for the eyes.”

“? I don’t have much time, so I’m coming in.”

Says Tenjō-san as she quickly enters my room.

Tenjō-san puts down her bag and sits at the table, yet she doesn’t start eating her breakfast.

“It’s so good to be able to have a meal in the morning. What’s really needed in life is leisure, after all.”

She seemed somewhat moved.

Today’s breakfast was a classic menu: toast with homemade strawberry jam, fried eggs and sausages, salad, and soup.

“Come on, Yuunagi-kun, sit down too. We’re going to eat together.”

She says while patting the seat next to her.

“Oh, you can start eating first. I was thinking of making some coffee.”

“Milk is enough. Come on.”

She beckons me hurriedly, so I sit down too.

“Then, itadakimasu!”

Tenjō-san waits for me before she starts eating.

“The fried eggs are perfectly runny. It’s amazing.”

“How would you like it seasoned? Soy sauce, salt and pepper, or ketchup?”

“Soy sauce, of course!”

“Same for me.”

I say, placing the tabletop soy sauce in front of Tenjō-san.

“Mmm, this creamy yolk is great. And the jam Yuunagi-kun made is really the best. Jam toast after such a long time feels nostalgic and delicious.”

I agree. I wonder when was the last time I had it.

“You’re amazing for cooking in the morning! The deli bread I bought would have been enough.”

“There’s no way I’d skimp on the first day after we’ve gone to the trouble of making a Neighbor Agreement. Besides, that deli bread is my lunch for today.”

“Is that enough for you? You should eat more.”

“I usually have lunch at the school store or convenience store, so this is actually an upgrade. You still seem to struggle with mornings, Tenjō-san.”

“No-no, if I’ve promised you, I’ll definitely come to eat!”

“I thought you might not make it in time.”

“You know, like how you sleep in usually but wake up early on trips. You don’t want to miss the hotel’s breakfast buffet, right?”

“Ah, I get that feeling.”

“Right? It was worth getting up this morning. Thank you.”

Tenjō-san enjoyed the ordinary breakfast but finished it in no time.

“I’ll go ahead then. Sorry I can’t help with the cleanup. See you at school.”

“Yes, have a good day.”

“——It’s nice to be seen off at home.”

Tenjō-san says with a smile and heads to the station.

I thought this busy morning scene wasn’t too bad either.

***

“Good morning, everyone!”

Tenjō-sensei, entering the classroom of 2-C, greets everyone with a clear voice.

She looks around the classroom, full of energy from the morning.

“It’s so sunny this morning, feels almost like summer. The UV rays seem intense. You guys better put on sunscreen as soon as possible.”

Saying this, Sensei takes off her jacket, revealing a sleeveless knit underneath.

Her slender shoulders and white arms extend gracefully.

Reiyu Tenjō stands so beautifully, it’s mesmerizing. She would be even more attractive in a swimsuit.

Since Tenjō-sensei became the advisor, I’ve heard that the number of students trying out for the swim team increased just to see Sensei in a swimsuit.

I would like to see Sensei in a swimsuit too if I could.

“Okay, class representatives, please lead the command.”

At the call to stand up, I rise from my seat.

Since entering the classroom, Tenjō-sensei hasn’t looked my way once.

Even as I stand up in front of her, our eyes don’t meet; she always talks while looking over the entire classroom.

I should learn from her thoroughness.

I need to tighten up, having been a bit carried away knowing a bit about Sensei’s private life.

This is school.

To keep our secret and adhere to our Neighbor agreement, we must strictly maintain the line between public and private.

After sitting down, Sensei takes attendance as usual.

“Next, Yuuna──, Nishiki-kun.”

Just when I was impressed, Tenjō-sensei almost slipped up.

That was close! You haven’t called me by my first name long enough to get used to it, have you?

“Yes.”

I respond as if nothing happened.

I can’t react strangely here and arouse suspicion.

Glancing up, I see Tenjō-sensei’s eyes saying, ‘That was close, but still safe.’

After calling all the classmates’ names, she notes, “I see, Kuhouin-san is still not here,” looking towards the window-side desk where the habitual latecomer should be sitting.

The door of class 2-C suddenly opens, and the rumored girl student enters leisurely.

“Today, I’m safe.”

Akira Kuhouin walks up to the teacher’s desk without a greeting, her voice listless.

Last year, she kept her hair short to not interfere with sports, giving her a boyish look. Now, it had grown to her shoulders.

She wore her uniform loosely, with a cardigan tied around her waist.

The top buttons of her shirt were undone, and her skirt seemed unusually short, likely because of her long legs. Her thighs are solidly thick, a remnant of the training from their time in the track and field club.

Her large eyes were half-closed, perhaps from sleepiness, giving her an air of ennui that oddly exuded a decadent allure.

The once athletic girl has become completely lethargic since her retirement due to injury.

“Kuhouin-san, late again. Why were you late again today?”

“My bed wouldn’t let me go.”

It was too brazen to call it an excuse.

Challenging Tenjō-sensei like that took guts. She’s not your average pretty girl.

If you snap at Sensei without cause, you risk being seen as envious of her beauty.

Normally, one would play it safe and not make such a risky move.

But Kuhouin showed no hesitation.

Was it confidence in herself, or is she just being reckless?”

Akira Kuhouin was also considered one of the most beautiful girls in our grade.

Even in her sporty phase last year, she was secretly popular, but it increased significantly after she became more feminine following her retirement.

“Set your alarm to wake up in time for homeroom.”

Tenjō-sensei stops Kuhouin, who was about to head to her seat and reminds her of the obvious.

In front of the blackboard—right in front of me—the two faced each other.

“I’m in a mutual love with my bed, so I want to stay with it forever.”

“Kuhouin-san, even if you love it, there are times you must part. If you keep being late, you might not be able to advance to the third year. You need to change your attitude.”

Tenjō-sensei advised her quietly, clearly worried about the possibility of Kuhouin repeating a year.

“I’ll worry about that when the time comes.”

Kuhouin showed no concern or agitation about her own issues, seemingly indifferent.

“That’s not how it works.”

“Do as you like. Even when I’m an adult, I won’t love school enough to wake up early every day, unlike Sensei.”

Kuhouin probably meant it as sarcasm.

But knowing Reiyu Tenjō’s morning situation, I couldn’t help but burst out a small chuckle.

“Nishiki, did something funny happen?”

Kuhouin narrowed her eyes and stared at me.

I was surprised she even remembered my name. Sensei also looked at me, her face seeming like she wanted to say something.

“Sorry, bad timing. My hay fever is acting up.”

I lied casually.

“That wasn’t a sneeze. Plus, you had a stupid look on your face.”

She giggles softly.

“Hey, Kuhouin. You’re late and now you’re dissing someone’s face first thing in the morning?”

Being picked on just for sitting in the front row is the last thing I need.

If you’re late, at least show some remorse and enter from the back of the classroom.

Why do you have to pass in front of me?

“I only commented on your expression. No comment on the face itself.”

“That makes it even more annoying!”

“Yeah, yeah, Mr. Handsome.”

“That’s too perfunctory.”

“Not at all.”

Our eyes met.

It was as if her eyes were drawing me in.

She suddenly fell silent, stopping our back-and-forth conversation.

Was she actually interested in me and just covering it up with teasing me?

“Wow, you’re blushing. Don’t take a girl’s words too seriously, or you’ll be seen as a delusional guy.”

“Mind your own business, you chronic latecomer.”

“I made it in time for the first period, though.”

‘So it’s not tardiness,’ Kuhouin casually retorted.

Standing next to Sensei at the front of the classroom, her brazenness was almost refreshing.

“In general, not being in the classroom before homeroom is considered tardiness. Look, everyone else made it on time, right?”

I reminded her of a rule we learned in elementary school, urging Kuhouin to look around the classroom.

“Everyone’s so early, that’s amazing…”

Kuhouin said in a listless voice, almost mocking the other classmates.

Watching our exchange, Tenjō-sensei next to me was somehow holding back laughter, her hand covering her mouth.

I could sense her trying to suppress her giggles, almost bending over with laughter.

I could understand if she was angry, but what was so funny?

“Tenjō-sensei?”

Confused by her reaction, I had to ask.

Kuhouin also frowned in puzzlement.

“Ah, sorry-sorry. Don’t worry about it.”

Tenjō-sensei returned to a serious expression and faced Kuhouin again.

“For now, take your seat as the first period is about to start. If your tardiness continues, I’ll have to lecture you in the staff room or call your parents.”

Sensei concluded with that warning and ended the morning homeroom.

Hmm, I still don’t understand why Sensei was holding back laughter.

I’ll ask her when I get home.

***

“I’m home!”

It was past nine at night again.

Working overtime from Monday, that’s dedication.

The intercom of my room rang, and when I opened the door, Tenjō-sensei was there in a good mood.

“Oh, welcome back.”

Her assertive greeting made me reflexively respond in kind.

“Hmm, it’s nice to come back to a lit room.”

“Sensei—no, Tenjō-san. You just said ‘I’m home’…”

“Is it weird? It’s almost like coming home. Ah, I’m tired.”

Tenjō-san took off her shoes and entered my room.

“Your room is next door, though.”

“Don’t worry about the details. Here, I brought you something.”

Just like this morning, Tenjō-san came to my room for dinner.

She pulled out some convenience store sweets from her work bag and handed them to me.

“Aren’t you going to change first? I don’t mind waiting.”

Instead of stopping by her room first, she came directly here still in her suit.

“I didn’t want to keep you waiting.”

“You don’t need to worry about me or bring gifts.”

“It’s just convenience store sweets. Besides, the convenience store on the way home is an oasis for the working adult’s heart.”

“What do you mean?”

“It’s an exclusive space for busy, tired workers to take a break on their way home. A place to reward themselves for a day’s hard work. In my case, I just end up wanting something sweet.”

I see, so shopping on the way home is a bit of a breather.

“And you chose cream puffs and pudding.”

“I have to check out the new products. You can pick whichever you like.”

“Won’t you gain weight if you eat sweets after dinner?”

“I swam a lot today, so no problem! Besides, dessert goes to a different stomach!”

Tenjō-san took off her jacket and confidently flashed a peace sign at me.

It seems she feels no guilt about having sweets at night.

Being the advisor of the swim team, she probably uses her regular swimming as a justification.

Seeing her body, it’s understandable.

“Then please chill it. I’m going to wash my hands.”

After entrusting me with the sweets, Tenjō-san disappeared into the washroom.

It would be tactless to criticize Tenjō-san’s small pleasures.

After putting the sweets in the fridge, I turned on the stove to finish cooking.

Being a teacher is busy and tough, I thought to myself again.

In the morning, they go to school and stand at the podium teaching all day.

In the evening, they move their bodies to coach club activities.

Then back to the staff room for paperwork. By the time work ends, they must be exhausted.

Going out after work would require a lot of energy.

Their daily schedule is packed, and unexpected troubles can arise when dealing with teenagers.

Kuhouin’s habit of being late is a perfect example.

If it’s not corrected, it could significantly change a student’s life.

While some can easily say it’s the student’s responsibility, it’s difficult for a conscientious teacher like Reiyu Tenjō.

I found myself wondering if there was anything else I could do to ease Tenjō-san’s burdens.

◇◆◇◆

“So, what’s for dinner tonight?”

She returned humming a tune, peeking over my shoulder at what I was doing.

“You’re too close.”

When she leaned in to look, her hands naturally rested on my shoulder.

I felt a sudden warmth in my left shoulder where she touched.

“These room hallways are narrow, you know. It’s not my fault.”

With furniture and appliances like shelves and a fridge, the hallway is just wide enough for two people to walk side by side.

It’s inevitable for a reasonably priced one-room apartment in Tokyo to be cramped.

“Can’t you ask me from a distance?”

“I just wanted to see a guy skillfully handling a frying pan up close.”

“Unfortunately, it’s just a simple task of reheating. And please don’t play around near the fire.”

“Okay.”

Tenjō-san’s hands left my shoulder.

Her behavior seemed even more relaxed than in the morning. She really seems to feel at home here.

It looks like I’m the only one who’s tense.

“Are you in high spirits today?”

“Coming home to a warm dinner is something unimaginable for someone living alone. My steps on the way home were lighter than usual.”

“Then it’s worth cooking for you, Tenjō-san.”

“Don’t worry, I’m starving. I’ll devour whatever you make.”

She proudly rubbed her flat stomach with a smug look.

“Does that mean you’ll eat it even if it’s bad?”

“No, the opposite. That means I trust your cooking.”

Her cute gesture made me like her even more.

“I’ll bring it now, so please sit and wait.”

“I can help too.”

“Then please serve the rice. The bowls are in that shelf. Serve as much as you like. I’ll have a normal portion.”

“…Did you wait especially for me? Aren’t you starving, Yuunagi-kun?”

Her cheerful face from before vanished when she realized I hadn’t had dinner yet.

Tenjō-san’s expression turned to one of concern.

“I was just late in cooking. I went shopping at the supermarket after school, then took a nap. Plus, I’ve been tasting it along the way.”

“From now on, you can eat whenever you’re hungry.”

“If I’m hungry, I will.”

“We’re equals here, so you don’t have to be so formal.”

“I also want to see Sensei’s reaction directly. Today’s menu is a new challenge for me.”

I carefully watch the simmered fish being reheated to prevent it from burning.

“Oh~, I like your courage to try something new instead of sticking to your specialties.”

Tenjō-san smiles, showing her white teeth.

I appreciate her lively and positive reactions.

“I would never cook simmered fish just for myself, so I thought I’d give it a try.”

Tonight’s dinner is a Japanese menu: rice, miso soup, simmered fish, rolled omelet, and boiled spinach.

We carry the food to the table and eat dinner together.

“Tenjō-san, were you holding back laughter this morning when I was talking with Kuhouin?”

“Ah, yes. I was trying not to laugh.”

“Was there something funny?”

“No, it’s just that watching Kuhouin-san reminded me of myself as a teenager.”

“Was the young Tenjō-san like that, so edgy?”

“When I was a teenager, everything around me was annoying. I had a period when I was always in a bad mood and rebellious against adults, so I understand the feeling. It felt nostalgic and somewhat embarrassing, you know～”

She squirms as if embarrassed to put it into words.

It’s hard to imagine that from the bright, beautiful, and kind Onee-san image.

“That’s unexpected.”

“Even so, I was seriously troubled and suffering back then. Now I can laugh at my immaturity. It’s like having my high school yearbook opened right in front of me, and I just couldn’t help but laugh.”

What a torturous teenage flashback.

“Well, I became a teacher because I wanted to be an adult who could guide such lost youths in their adolescence.”

“It’s great that you can use your own experiences.”

It’s nice to have an adult nearby who understands the pain and suffering of others.

“At your age, people tend to say harsh things defensively to keep others at a distance. As a homeroom teacher, I’d appreciate it if Yuunagi-kun could help Kuhouin-san as a classmate if she’s in trouble.”

“If there’s any way I can help, I will.”

Tenjō-san smiles contentedly at my response.

“Thank you. Kids like her are particularly easily moved by casual kindness.”

“Tenjō-san, you speak with such conviction.”

“Well, I am also someone who gratefully enjoys my neighbor’s cooking. It’s delicious today as well. Thank you for the meal.”

Tenjō-san ate everything neatly again today.

After finishing the meal, she offered to do the dishes, saying, ‘I’ll take care of the cleanup.’

With her long hair tied back, she dons my apron and stands in the kitchen.

I lie down on the bed for a rest.

Even though I’m the owner of the room, should I really let my teacher wash the dishes?

I tried to refuse Sensei’s offer several times, but she insisted.

Fiddling with my smartphone, I can’t help but watch her from behind.

“I wonder if living together feels like this.”

As I indulge in such sweet daydreams, my phone’s ringtone echoes.

“A phone call? Don’t worry, go ahead and answer it.”

Tenjō-san continues to rinse the dishes without turning around.

My phone vibrates with the call.

The name displayed on the screen is Kaguya Nishiki.

I flip the phone over as if to hide it.

“It’s nothing to worry about, I’m fine.”

“But it’s still ringing. I’ve just finished the dishes, so I’ll keep quiet.”

Tenjō-san brings over the sweets she bought from the fridge.

“I usually don’t answer calls from this person.”

“…If I’m in the way, I can go back to my room.”

“Please, feel free to stay and have your dessert here.”

The smartphone continues to ring.

“Is it a call from your family, perhaps?”

“You’re as sharp as ever.”

“You’re always so open, the only thing I can think of is that it must be a family matter.”

“There should be courtesy even among close friends, right?”

“Sorry. I just couldn’t help but notice.”

Finally, the ringing stops.

“It’s nothing for Tenjō-san to worry about. Let’s have dessert instead.”

While brewing tea after the meal, we share the sweets Tenjō-san bought.

We continue to chat about trivial things while watching TV.

“Let me have a bite of your pudding too.”

“Oh, we’re sharing?”

“I bought it, so it’s fine, right?”

“I thought it was a gift for me.”

“What catches my interest, catches my interest.”

“That’s pushy.”

“It’s normal among girls.”

“I’m a guy, just so you know.”

“I can’t eat it with any excitement if you say something like that.”

Tenjō-san’s cheeks flushed.

“Wanting to eat it even though you’re embarrassed huh.”

I couldn’t help but laugh. In my mind, Reiyu Tenjō has completely become a character who’s always hungry.

But this honesty was becoming increasingly endearing to me.

I was happy that she showed me her natural reactions and vulnerabilities that she doesn’t reveal at school, and I feel a bit proud to be someone who can support her.

Moreover, even when nothing much is happening, just idly spending time with her after meals was fun.

In the end, she happily ate my pudding with a bite.

Perhaps this kind of unremarkable everyday life is what people call happiness.
