Chapter 3: A Relationship Like Waves

“Today, too, the alarm stopped after the first ring.”

It’s been a week since Reiyu Tenjō started having meals at my house.

Normally, her alarm would ring several times, but now it stops after just the first one.

Incredibly, she also sends me a message even before I can get out of bed.

【Reiyu: Good morning. Toast for breakfast today. Looks like we’re almost out of jam. Sad.】

【Yuunagi: Good morning, toast it is then.

I can make as much jam as you want if we have the ingredients.】

【Reiyu: Really? Then, I’ll ask you again.】

Now she even expresses her own wishes, and our life rhythm based on the Neighbor Agreement has completely settled in.

I make breakfast, eat with Tenjō-san, send her off, and then go to school myself with a bit of a time gap.

In the classroom, we basically pretend not to know each other.

I try my best not to talk to her.

But since she’s right there, it’s easy for her to talk to me.

Tenjō-sensei often starts conversations with me as a casual interjection.

“Good morning, everyone! Some of you look sleepy, but did you all have breakfast? You can’t think straight if you don’t eat properly. This morning, I had toast with homemade strawberry jam, and it was absolutely delicious. Oh, Nishiki-kun, what did you have for breakfast?”

Even though she knew it, I watched her enjoy the same menu right beside me.

“I had toast with strawberry jam.”

“Oh, what a coincidence!”

Why does she deliberately say things that could reveal our secret?

“Yes, Reiyu-chan Sensei! You’ve been looking happy recently, did you get a boyfriend?”

The leading girl of the class’s bright and cheerful girls group, Ririka Mayuzumi, sparkled with curiosity.

Reiyu Tenjō is always perceived as bright and energetic, but now that I think about it, she does seem to be in higher spirits than before.

“It’s nothing like that. Now, let’s take attendance.”

She deflected the casual question with a smile and focused on her work.

Today, too, there were no late arrivals or absences.

Even Akira Kuhouin, a habitual latecomer, had been arriving on time for morning homeroom since she was warned.

However, as days passed, her arrival to the classroom became later, and today, she was just on the brink of being late.

Kuhouin was chosen to solve a problem written on the blackboard during the fourth-period math class.

She walked to the front of the classroom——with a listless gait right in front of me.

She glanced at the problem, took the chalk, and smoothly wrote down the solution.

The equations and the answers were the same as the ones I had solved in my notebook, and her handwriting was neat.

“Why are you looking at me?”

While dusting off the chalk dust from her fingers, she noticed my gaze.

“It’s just that Kuhouin is standing in front of me.”

“I’m conscious of your gaze.”

“I’m just earnestly participating in class.”

“Don’t look this way.”

Her eyebrows were raised, giving the eyes a sharp, pointed look.

“Kuhouin, you’re the one with a bad look in your eyes. Is it because of lack of sleep?”

Lack of sleep often makes people irritable.

“I’m just annoyed because I was spoken to by a guy who’s always fawning over the homeroom teacher.”

Her unexpected retort caught me off guard.

“It’s not always.”

I didn’t deny it though.

“You’re too smitten.”

“…Kuhouin, you’re really paying a lot of attention to me…”

“Huh? I’m not particularly watching you.”

“If you’re saying I’m always stretching my nose [fawning over someone], it means you’re always watching me, right?”

When I pointed that out, Kuhouin muttered under her breath, ‘Annoying guy.’

The math teacher gently told us, “If you’re done, please return to your seat.”

Kuhouin glanced at me and then went back to her seat.

By the way, the answer Kuhouin wrote was correct.

Contrary to her delinquent-like behavior, Akira Kuhouin seems to be different from what Tenjō-sensei initially thought.

Curious, I approached Kuhouin’s desk during lunch break.

“Hey Kuhouin, can I talk to you for a second?”

I sat down in front of her desk as she was about to stand up.

“What is it?”

“About earlier, I thought I should apologize. My bad.”

“…It’s nothing. It’s just that Nishiki’s gaze is too lewd.”

She turned her body away, indicating she wasn’t interested in talking with me.

“That’s an exaggeration. If anything, I’m rather supportive of you.”

I listened to her one-sided opinion with a wry smile.

“Support?”

“Kuhouin, you’ve been making it on time in the mornings recently, right? Keep up the good work.”

“…It must be nice to offer support with just words.”

Her sarcastic comeback suggested that maintaining an early waking routine was challenging for her.

“So, you’d be okay with me meddling beyond just verbal support?”

“Being teased is annoying.”

She looked at me with a mix of irritation.

“I’m genuinely concerned about you as a classmate.”

“I sense ulterior motives.”

“As if a guy who’s smitten with the teacher doesn’t have ulterior motives. It’s like standard equipment for a teenage boy.”

“A lust wearing a uniform.”

“I’m not trying to hit on you, though.”

I said with a wry smile.

“There’s more possibility with a classmate rather than a homeroom teacher.”

“I have no idea how to make someone as prickly as you show a softer side.”

Kuhouin seems to be wearing armor around her heart, and I can’t find a way to get through.

“There’s no reason for Nishiki to care about me.”

“You’re so cynical. I just want Kuhouin to advance to the next grade.”

“Why? You having an ulterior motive would be more believable.”

Tenjō-san’s words from the other day come back to me.

『As a homeroom teacher, I’d appreciate it if Yuunagi-kun could help Kuhouin-san as a classmate if she’s in trouble.』

To fulfill that wish, I offer my help.

“It’s hard to believe that someone who thinks it’s okay to repeat a year or drop out is serious about studying. If you’re genuinely struggling with waking up early, I’ll help you.”

If she wants to go to school but can’t, I don’t want to just ignore it.

Whether my feelings got through to her or not, Kuhouin finally faced me.

◇◆◇◆

“Help, like how?”

I had offered to take care of meals for Reiyu Tenjō, who was too busy with housework.

For Akira Kuhouin, who struggles to wake up, what could I do?

“Well… how about I give you a wake-up call?”

“No thanks.”

I was instantly rejected.

“Kuhouin, you wouldn’t be late if someone woke you up every morning.”

“I said I don’t need it.”

“Could it be that you actually live really far away, and that’s why you’re holding back?”

She reluctantly answered with the name of the station where she lived.

“That’s closer than my place…”

I was slightly taken aback. ‘Really, you’re late even though you live that close? Seriously, that’s way too lax.’, I thought.

Apparently, my thoughts were written all over my face, as Kuhouin muttered reluctantly.

“I have low blood pressure, so mornings are extremely difficult for me.”

“So you tried your best to wake up, but it’s always just barely in time.”

“Is that a problem?”

“Rather, I’m surprised you managed to participate in the morning practices when you were in the track and field club.”

“If I wake up really early, my parents are still home before going to work, so they wake me up.”

“Then, all the more reason for me to meddle. Your parents will cry if you repeat a year.”

If my morning call could solve this, it would be a small price to pay.

I’m already up at that time, preparing Sensei’s breakfast, so it wouldn’t be a hassle.

“That’s a problem for sure.”

Kuhouin made a very reluctant face, then muttered as if giving up.

I took that as a ‘yes’.

“Let’s exchange contact information for now.”

“I find it annoying to be contacted persistently.”

“I’ll give you a wake-up call until you get up, so it will definitely be persistent calling.”

“That’s the worst.”

“If you don’t like my calls, make sure you wake up on the first try.”

“…Just so you know, I’m incredibly grumpy in the mornings, so I might say something harsh, but don’t take it personally.”

That’s definitely not the attitude of someone being helped.

“As always, you have such a superior attitude.”

“If you’re going to refuse, now’s the time.”

At this point, I couldn’t help but laugh at her.

Now that she has agreed, I’ll take on the role of morning call duty with determination.

“It’s hard watching you be late and get into trouble with Sensei, so I’ll take the job.”

“…I honestly struggle to wake up with just an alarm, so maybe this is a real help.”

“Good. It’s better to accept help honestly than to be stubborn. That’s what I did before.”

Akira Kuhouin reluctantly exchanged contact information with me.

***

The next morning.

The alarm from next door stopped after one ring, and I received the usual morning message from Tenjō-san.

【Reiyu: Good morning. I’ll go at the same time as yesterday.】

【Yuunagi: Good morning. Understood.】

I’m grateful that Tenjō-san keeps in touch regularly.

I also get up, finish getting ready, and start preparing the meal.

Along the way, the alarm I had set on my smartphone the night before went off.

“Well, now it’s time for the morning call.”

I had asked Kuhouin in advance for the time she needed to leave to be on time, I calculated backward from that and made the phone call.

The phone connected without much wait.

“Good morning, Kuhouin, it’s Nishiki. Time to get up or you’ll be late.”

『…You really called.』

The voice that came from the other end of the phone was spiritless and sounded incredibly sleepy.

The domineering atmosphere she showed in the classroom was completely gone, and her energy seemed at rock bottom.

“Looks like your weakness in the mornings wasn’t a lie.”

『…shut up.』

She sounded like even speaking was a struggle.

“Being annoying is part of a morning call’s job.”

『Go get rid of my sleepiness quickly.』

She seemed barely able to think, just blurting out whatever came to mind.

“Taking a shower might help you wake up.”

『I wish I could be carried from the bed to the bathroom…』

“What kind of noble lifestyle is that?”

『……..』

“Kuhouin?”

『zzZ』

“Hey, don’t go back to sleep! Wake up! You’ll be late again!”

『…maybe humans aren’t meant to wake up early.』

Her statement was grand, but her voice was feeble. It was almost a miracle that a conversation was happening at all.

I tried speaking a bit louder, but it didn’t seem to have any effect.

“Actually, your sleepy voice is pretty cute.”

I kept talking to prevent her from falling back asleep.

『──don’t talk nonsense to me.』

Her response was languid, but there was a sign that she was turning over in bed.

“I’m complimenting you.”

『Do you think any girl would be happy just because you call her cute?』

Her response’s volume suddenly increased. Perhaps the word ‘cute’ triggered something.

“I’m just saying a cute girl who’s making a cute voice is cute.”

『…Nishiki, you’re weird.』

“If I record this call and let other boys listen, it would clearly prove that Kuhouin is cute.”

『Criminal.』

Her words began to carry more emotion.

Seems like the ‘cute’ approach is surprisingly effective.

“I just want to make Kuhouin’s cuteness known.”

『Mind your own business.』

“You’re complaining about a morning call you agreed to.”

I say, holding back a laugh.

『You’re meddling too much.』

“Thanks to that, I get to hear your cute sleepy voice.”

『Pervert.』

“Sure, sure, if you don’t like talking to a pervert, wake up earlier. Or are you pretending to be sleepy on purpose?”

『I’ll kill you.』

It was a completely unconvincing threat.

“Mumbling like that. Try it if you think you can.”

『I’ll scream and burst your eardrums.』

“Announcing it first? That’s so kind. As expected, a cute girl is different.”

『…It’s seriously creepy when you keep saying it.』

It seemed like she was really put off.

“You’re pretty harsh right after waking up.”

『It’s because of your creepy way of waking someone up.』

“I just chose a way of speaking that seemed effective for Kuhouin.”

『As if you’re my lover.』

“Oh, does Kuhouin want to be sweetly awakened by your lover?”

『──Ugh!? That’s just a hypothetical! Nishiki, you’re so annoying!』

Finally, she replied in a clear voice.

I felt like I caught a glimpse of her girlish side, so I couldn’t help but feel amused.

“There’s no need to be embarrassed. It’s a lover’s privilege to be sweet with each other.”

『Shut up. Fine, I’m fully awake now.』

Akira Kuhouin hung up the phone, sounding annoyed.

I prepared breakfast on time, and my neighbor arrived just at the right moment.

◇◆◇◆

“Yuunagi-kun, good morning! So, what’s the menu for today?”

She sat at the table with a big smile.

“I tried something different today, Ogura butter toast.”

“Oh, perfect. The sweet and salty taste is addictive.”

This morning, Sensei too seemed to be enjoying her breakfast with a happy face.

After finishing the meal and seeing off a cheerful Tenjō-san, I cleaned up and left my apartment.

Checking the time on my smartphone and timing my train ride, I sent Kuhouin a message.

【Yuunagi: Have you left home?】

【Akira: Yes, I have. That’s enough already!】

【Yuunagi: Just making sure.】

【Akira: You’re too suspicious of me.】

After a while, I sent another message.

【Yuunagi: Are you on the train?】

【Akira: Huh, what the heck!? Are you a stalker? Why do you know exactly when I’m boarding!?】

【Yuunagi: It’s just a coincidence. Which car are you in?】

【Akira: I don’t know. Probably somewhere in the middle.】

I arrived at the station nearest to the school.

Normally, I would walk to school from there, but this time I waited on the platform bench for the next train.

When the next train arrived and the doors opened, Akira Kuhouin stepped off.

“Good morning, glad you made it on time.”

“Nishiki!? Why are you here?”

“Just making sure.”

“Wait, are you tracking my movements? It’s weird and really creepy to get messages at such precise times so frequently.”

Kuhouin looked genuinely alarmed, her sleepiness completely gone.

“I asked yesterday about how long it takes you from home to the station. As long as that’s correct, I can roughly estimate the times you pass through each point until you get to school.”

“Why go to that extent?”

“I planned a schedule that allows Kuhouin to sleep as much as possible without being late.”

“Nishiki, are you the type who can’t relax unless things go according to schedule? You know, controlling others like that can make you disliked.”

She looked at me as if I were a bit of a creep.

“I’m not that strict. Besides, it wouldn’t have worked if Kuhouin didn’t report correctly.”

“I just can’t stand not being on point with time.”

It was a statement befitting someone who used to be in the track team.

I quickly followed her as she started walking ahead. We exited the station’s ticket gate and headed towards the school.

“Why are you following me? Are you seriously stalking me?”

Kuhouin, walking in front, complained.

“It’s the way to school, so don’t be absurd. I don’t want to be late either.”

“I feel uncomfortable, feels like I’m being followed.”

“Then I’ll go ahead aand pass you .”

I quickened my pace to close the distance.

“I don’t like that either.”

Kuhouin, seemingly in a competitive spirit, also increased her walking speed, and we ended up walking side by side.

“As a former track team member, you hate being overtaken, huh?”

“──It’s not that. It just looks like we’re going to school together.”

“We’re just walking side by side.”

“It bothers me.”

“You’re worrying too much.”

“I feel like we’re being stared at by everyone around.”

I looked around.

Since it was time for school, many Kiyō High School students were walking on the same path.

She’s right, they seemed to be looking in our direction.

“Oh, that’s probably because Kuhouin is cute.”

If a beautiful girl who is not usually seen on their way to school is walking there, it’s natural for people to take a glance or be conscious of her.

“Haa!?”

Kuhouin raised her voice in surprise next to me, causing me to be startled too.

“What are you talking about?”

“I told you the same thing on the phone this morning.”

“…I thought I misheard you because I was half asleep.”

Kuhouin turned her face away with a ‘huff.’

“Akira Kuhouin is a girl who stands out, it’s just a fact.”

“It’s creepy how you say such corny lines so casually.”

She seemed to have gotten goosebumps, rubbing her arms.

“It’s surprising that Kuhouin isn’t used to such comments.”

“I was absorbed in club activities, so love was my secondary concern. Makeup doesn’t make you run faster.”

Kuhouin said it matter-of-factly.

“That straightforwardness of yours, I think it’s good.”

“It’s just being clumsy.”

“It’s proof that you’re concentrating and working hard on one thing. That kind of thing is cool.”

“────Nishiki, you just started talking to me so casually. You didn’t approach me with an ulterior motive, nor were you intimidated…”

Kuhouin’s voice softened a bit.

“I see, you are actually aware that people are intimidated by you…”

I couldn’t help but give a wry smile.

“If you’re treated like a hot potato, you can’t help but notice a few things.”

“With that strong personality and looks, and without being friendly, it’s easy to give off an unapproachable impression.”

I made an objective comment.

To be precise, Akira Kuhouin in Class 2-C is seen as a beautiful girl who is admired by those around her.

They’re intimidated, but she’s not disliked at all. She just always looks sleepy during breaks, so everyone is considerate and doesn’t bother her.

“Talking to such a troublesome girl…Nishiki, you’re an unusual guy.”

Kuhouin finally showed a smile appropriate for her age.

“Oh, that’s great. If Kuhouin approached others with that smile, the walls you feel would be easily broken down.”

Her eyes widened at my advice.

“Do you have sisters, Nishiki?”

“I do have a stepsister.”

“Ahh, that’s why you’re good with girls. You womanizer.”

It didn’t sound like she was complimenting me.

“Then, I feel like I should be more popular.”

My period of popularity, please come to me!

“Nishiki, you seem like you don’t have many friends.”

“That’s something I don’t want to hear from you.”

“What’s wrong with that? Who decided that people with many friends are better?”

Akira Kuhouin said this with a hint of a ‘hmmph’ in her voice.

In a group where many are keen on reading the room, her ability to not be swayed by others is her strength.

Then, we happened to pass by the nearest convenience store to the school.

“So, what about breakfast? If you make a dash for it, there’s still time.”

“Is that also according to schedule?”

“It’s a bonus time because Kuhouin reported honestly.”

“Fine. Wait for me, Nishiki.”

“Uh, I was going to go ahead to school first.”

“Just stay there!”

Kuhouin pointed at me to wait and hurried into the convenience store.

“How much does she hate the idea of me going ahead?”

True to her word, Kuhouin quickly finished her shopping and returned.

“Here, this is for you. Thanks for this morning.”

And then Kuhouin handed me a chocolate bar, separate from her own breakfast.

“No, really, you shouldn’t have.”

“I don’t want to owe you anything. Just take it.”

She put it in my hand and started walking, sucking on a Chupa Chups.

Insisting on returning it would only spoil her mood.

“And by the way, calling me ‘Kuhouin’ is too long. Just ‘Akira’ is fine.”

“Is that okay?”

“I even find my own last name too pompous. I prefer ‘Akira.’ “

“It makes me nervous.”

“Calling a girl by her first name should come naturally to you. It’s pathetic otherwise.”

Kuhouin laughed at me.

“But this is strictly as friends, alright?”

I felt compelled to clarify.

“As a man, sometimes you need to be assertive. It makes it easier for the girl to make excuses too.”

“What if I push too hard and end up being disliked?”

“That’s probably why you don’t have a girlfriend, huh?”

Her words hit me hard.

The girl walking beside me looked at me provocatively.

“I’ll keep this snack for later, Akira.”

I put it in the pocket of my uniform.

While talking like this, we reached the school before we knew it.

◇◆◇◆

When the two of us arrived at the entrance, the class’s lively gal, Ririka Mayuzumi, called out to us in a loud voice.

“Whaaat~ Nikki and Akiaki together? That’s rare!? Might be an unexpected pairing!”

Mayuzumi-san approached us with curiosity while her trademark long twin tails bouncing.

“Nikki, as in me?”

“Yeah. ‘Nishiki’ just sounds like ‘Nikki.'”

I really didn’t get it.

“Ririka. You still haven’t gotten over your habit of randomly giving people weird nicknames.”

Akira visibly distorted her face in annoyance.

“Aww, come on, it’s fun to have nicknames!”

“Annoying.”

“Akiaki, you’re as salty as ever.”

“It’s more like a pepper response than a salt one, right?”

I added my own comment.

Like, spicily sharp.

“Nishiki, shut up.”

Glared intensely, Akira, now in her indoor shoes, quickly walked ahead.

“Definitely more like pepper.”

“Nikki, seriously, that’s so true!”

Mayuzumi-san giggled.

“By the way, Mayuzumi-san, were you close with Akira?”

Last year, Akira, Mayuzumi-san, and I were in the same class too.

I only knew them as classmates and wasn’t really aware of their detailed relationship.

“I dunno? It’s more like Ririka just one-sidedly bugs Akiaki.”

The resilience of a lively person is incredible.

If someone showed such an obviously unwelcoming attitude, normally people would give up trying to talk to them.

In a different sense than Akira, Mayuzumi-san is also a ‘go-my-own-way’ kind of person.

“So, Nikki, why are you with Akiaki?”

Mayuzumi-san’s face was full of interest as if she had caught a scent of gossip.

“We just happened to meet at the station and walked here together.”

In a sense, I wasn’t lying.

And if I freely mentioned her that I was giving Akira morning calls, Akira would definitely be annoyed.

“Hmm~Take this!”

Mayuzumi-san quickly snatched the chocolate bar from my chest pocket.

“Hey, that’s mine!”

“Really Nikki’s?”

“What do you mean?”

“This is Akiaki’s favorite chocolate!”

She looked at me with a knowing sly smile.

“…Knowing Akira’s preferences, I think she and Mayuzumi-san are quite close.”

“Thanks. By the way, Ririka missed breakfast this morning. She really wants something sweet.”

I wonder if this was her way of asking for a bribe.

If Akira found out about this and complained to me, that would be troublesome.

“Go ahead.”

“Yay! Thanks, Nikki!”

Sorry, Akira. It got taken.

After the fourth-period Japanese history class, Ririka Mayuzumi approached Tenjō-sensei.

“Reiyu-chan Sensei, I have a little love advice to ask~”

“What, Mayuzumi-san!?”

Sensei was taken aback by the sudden request.

I tried to leave to buy lunch before the love consultation in front of me started.

“Wait a minute! Nikki, you join in too.”

“Uh, me too? Why?”

“I want a male representative’s opinion too. That’s okay, right, Reiyu-chan Sensei?”

“If Mayuzumi-san doesn’t mind, I’m okay with it.”

I inadvertently made eye contact with Sensei.

“It’s fine. It’s about Ririka’s friend.”

Though it often starts as a ‘friend’s problem,’ it usually turns out to be about the person themselves.

But in Mayuzumi-san’s case, I believe it’s exactly as she says.

“So, there’s this usually unfriendly girl, right? But suddenly she started going to school with a boy. That’s love, isn’t it? It must be love! It’s definitely love!!”

What a clumsy way to ask for an advice!

It’s overly simplistic like elementary school kids declaring two classmates a couple just because they walk together.

Tenjō-sensei also gave a wry smile.

“Mayuzumi-san, that might be a bit too much of a conclusion.”

“Eh, but it has to be. Ririka’s woman’s intuition is super sharp!”

“Did you actually confirm with your friend if she likes someone?”

“Not yet. That girl is actually quite shy.”

“If your friend really likes someone, it might be better for outsiders not to interfere too much.”

“But if it’s serious, I want it to help out.”

“It’s not always the case that liking someone means wanting to date them.”

“Isn’t it harder to be in unrequited love forever? If things are made clear, then you can move on to the next love!”

Mayuzumi-san’s opinion, in its simplicity, is hard to argue against.

“Each person has their own way of thinking about love. You shouldn’t forcefully impose your views, Mayuzumi-san.”

“But it’s definitely more fun to become lovers with someone you like!”

Hmm, they’re at a stalemate.

Neither of them is wrong in what they’re saying.

“What do you think, Nishiki-kun?”

“Are you really asking me now?”

“Nikki, between Reiyu-chan Sensei and Ririka, who do you think is right?”

Both of their gazes were fixed on me.

“Then I’ll vote for Mayuzumi-san. I think it’s better to have the person you like reciprocate your feelings.”

“Yay, Nikki, you’re the best!”

Mayuzumi-san patted my shoulder.

“Nishiki-kun, you traitor.”

Sensei sent a reproachful look my way.

“I’m not in cahoots with Sensei!”

“This isn’t a place to just respect the opinion of your superiors.”

“Since when did Class 2-C become a place where you can’t speak freely?”

“That’s an exaggeration.”

“No-no, Tenjō-sensei’s influence is significant.”

“Really? Am I unknowingly leading everyone? I have to maintain the students’ autonomy.”

The second-year teacher began to worry seriously at my remark.

Maybe I stirred things up too much.

“…Nikki and Reiyu-chan Sensei seem to get along well. You’re in sync.”

Mayuzumi-san observed, changing the topic slightly.

Mayuzumi-san said this, looking back and forth between us with a puzzled expression.

“It’s simply because our seats are close, so we just have more chances to talk.”

I quickly denied any implications.

“Really? Most guys seem tense when they talk to Reiyu-chan Sensei, but Nikki, you’re just normal.”

“Tenjō-sensei is, after all, just our homeroom teacher.”

“Is that so? Reiyu-chan Sensei also seems relaxed when she’s with Nikki.”

Mayuzumi-san seemed unconvinced.

To steer the conversation back, I voiced my own opinion.

“Anyway, I think mutual love is the best too, but like Tenjō-sensei said, it’s not always true that being together guarantees happiness.”

Tenjō-sensei, who had been taken aback by Mayuzumi-san’s remark, was now paying attention to my words.

“That’s too weak～ You won’t know unless you try dating, right?”

That’s a very good point. Mayuzumi-san has nothing but straightforward opinions that are hard to refute.

“Even if you’re lucky enough to start dating, it might end up hurting you.”

“If you stay timid, even a love that could bloom will never bear fruit.”

I was sharply countered.

Someone, please stop this love-slaying gal!

Her truth is so suffocating.

“Ouch, that’s some harsh truth,” even Tenjō-sensei said so with a grim face.

◇◆◇◆

“Mayuzumi-san. There are cases in the world where confessing only leads to more pain.”

If it were easy to be aggressive in love, nobody would have any trouble.

It’s not about not being able to muster courage sometimes. It’s about calmly assessing the situation and choosing to restrain oneself.

“Nikki, did something happen to you in the past? You should heal old love wounds with a new love!”

“Leave me alone!”

Mayuzumi-san, clearly enjoying our exchange, narrowed her eyes like a crescent moon.

“Okay, stop! Continuing this heated debate on love won’t change anything.”

Tenjō-sensei intervened with a referee’s stop.

“What’s important are the feelings of those involved. No matter how much their friends get excited, it’s only a nuisance if it’s just the outsiders. For now, just watch over them quietly.”

“Uh-okay, if Reiyu-chan Sensei says so…”

Mayuzumi-san wilted at the words of the respected Tenjō-sensei.

Perhaps feeling sorry to let her go just like that, Tenjō-sensei offered more words of advice.
