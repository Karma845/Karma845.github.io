～Weapon materials～

The materials used for weapons in this world were roughly divided into two.
The first was minerals, ranging from ordinary ores such as copper, iron, steel, brass, and silver to magic ores such as mithril, light quartz, and orichalcum.
The other was monster part materials such as leather, shells, bones, and fangs produced by monsters.
Ordinary ore weapons were relatively easy to process, and the ones that were inexpensive and made by skilled blacksmiths were sturdy and were the most widely distributed.
Magic ore weapons were difficult to handle and were expensive due to the low production of magic ore itself, but they were very high performance.
Weapons that used parts of monsters were truly diverse, from head to toe.
This was because the quality of materials for the same part of the same monster varied.
However, there were cases in which weapons that used the parts of high-ranking monsters such as dragons had performance that surpassed weapons made of magic ores.
In many cases, they were generally lighter in weight than metal, and some people liked to use weapons made from parts of monsters.
