Chapter 3: Secrets Told at the Fountain, Parties Won't Save Anyone

The legend associated with that girl began 10 years ago.

She was five years old at the time, and caused a [major incident] when she and her butler went hiking in the mountains. As soon as she casually pointed to a mountain in the distance, the slope collapsed with a tremendous noise, scattering mud and soil. Since then, this innocent destroyer has accumulated numerous legends, and before long, the name of Nina Stingray spread throughout the empire.

After all, the misfortune of the three boys who challenged her to a fight just a few moments ago was due to their doubts about the authenticity of the legend.

At first, it was supposed to be just a little prank.

They provoked a famous person who was exempted from the entrance exam, and then they showed everyone around them that they were strong and unafraid of anything, and they took the lead in the race for survival.

With the entrance ceremony scheduled to begin in 20 minutes, they thought there was no possibility of a battle breaking out, and even if it did, they were under the illusion that the three of them would be able to handle it.

It did not take long for them to realize that this was a big mistake.

Nina looked at the three of them as if she were sizing them up, and then muttered with a blank expression on her face.

 

"Do you truly understand what it means to make an enemy of me?"

 

She had beautifully braided platinum-colored hair and azure eyes that shone like works of art. Even her beauty was now nothing more than an element that accentuated the disconcerting nature of the situation.

As if to suppress her tremendous destructive impulse, Nina spoke on cautiously.

 

"Every opponent I've ever fought has had their limbs torn off, without exception."

 

The sun hid behind the clouds, casting an ominous shadow on Nina's face.

Her azure eyes glowed mysteriously as she unceasingly ogled the bodies of the three men. Her fractured smile gave the impression of a definite lack of humanity as a living creature.

The poor victims instantly understood that there was no way they could talk to such a person.

 

"Since it's pure psychokinesis, there doesn't seem to be any particular means of resistance. ...... Ah, it's not that they were in the wrong, you know? It's just that I still can't control my abilities very well. Of course, this time too, I will do my best to prevent you all from being killed. ......"

 

The three of them stopped breathing as they cowered under the words that sounded as if it were someone else's problem.

 

"Now that I think about it, it's strange that I've never been charged with a crime before. I wonder why? Could it be because my father has such a strong influence on the judiciary as well?"

 

Nina turned around and tilted her head like a child who doesn't understand how the world works.

No one could not help but shudder watching the expressions on her face change from one second to the next.

A monster with great power, yet unstable and uncontrollable. Moreover, she has a powerful background that can cover up her crimes.

Everyone present understood that it was a big mistake to get involved with Nina.

Three against one, you say? What makes you so naive to think that you can compete against her?

This monster is no longer in their realm ────.

It is an existence like an inevitable disaster, advancing through the battlefield as if it were taking a walk, producing countless corpses in its path.

It is only her whim that allows them to survive now.

 

"Shall we begin then? At least let me have a little fun, okay?"

 

With a smile that did not match her emotions, Nina held her palm out in front of her.

The three of them were in a state of panic, and in the next moment, they fell on their butts in disgrace. They slumped and backed away, making meaningless pleas.

 

"Hiih~, help me! Please help me!"

 

Nina's innocent killing intent gushed out from her entire body, like a young child trampling on ants. She turned to the three of them who were crawling away on the ground and said as if proclaiming.


"I've memorized your faces, gentlemen. I look forward to seeing you again."

When the auditorium opened, Nina was guided by the instructor to the front row.

Only six students in this year's grade were exempted from the entrance examination and were allowed to sit in this section. Located at the bottom of the bowl-shaped auditorium, this is where all eyes were on her from every possible direction.

 

"Nice to meet you. Are you by any chance Nina Stingray?"

 

Suddenly, a male student sitting in the chair next to her smiled at her.

Nina squinted her azure eyes and observed the good-looking boy. The expression on the boy's face, who introduced himself as Bennett Rohr, already revealed a hint of intimacy.

A high level of social skills unique to people who have been educated from an early age as gifted children. His slender stature and center-parted black hair exuded elegance.

Nina, having no choice but to mobilize all of her social skills, smiled back at him.

 

"I was a little surprised. I never knew there were so many psychics in my generation."

"Even if it's only one in a thousand, it's still not something one can easily perceive, huh?"

"Yes. There were only a few of them living around me after all."

 

Although the percentage of people with psychic abilities among the number of births has been increasing year by year, in Nina's generation it is only about 0.1% of the total.

And most of them are so impotent that they can never be utilized in combat or espionage operations. It is not possible to reach the Heiberg Academy, which is the best school in the country, with just the ability to float or bend a spoon a little bit.

The 278 new students in this auditorium, from all over the empire and from various races, are some of the most extraordinary monsters in the country.

 

"But it's a pleasure to sit side by side with someone as famous as you."

"You think too highly of me."

"Just a few minutes ago, you fought off some students who tried to gang up on you, right? It's becoming a hot topic of conversation all over the place."

"I just repelled some bugs that were flying around in front of me. It happens all the time."

 

Although she tried to lightly intimidate him with her childlike expression, as if she did not know the standards of right and wrong, Bennett only shrugged his shoulders and smiled. As expected, the six people sitting in these special seats must be exceptional among the monsters.

At the scheduled time, the Heiberg School's entrance ceremony began.

After the opening greetings, congratulatory speeches by officials and local dignitaries were delivered at length. It seems that even the most prestigious educational institution is no different from other schools when it comes to mediocre and boring ceremonies like this.

What was rather strange was that the headmaster of the school, Jill Will Weiser, was still nowhere to be seen.

The young instructor who was reading out the greeting instead explained that the headmaster was too busy to attend the ceremony, but what could he possibly prioritize over the entrance ceremony of the school he runs?

Everyone in the Empire knows that he is quite an eccentric person, but not to this extent.

After that, the ceremony proceeded smoothly, yet tediously. The students' drowsiness was awakened when the entire program of the entrance ceremony was over and a female instructor appeared on the stage.

 

"...... Alright, swine. That concludes our celebrations."

 

The beautiful female instructor, who froze the air in the auditorium, began to look around at the appalled students with her reptilian cold eyes behind her silver-rimmed glasses.

After making sure all the students' attention was focused on her, the female instructor opened her mouth.

 

"I'm Isabella, the person in charge of your school year. Pleased to meet you."

 

Without so much as a greeting, the cold-hearted words continued.

 

"First of all, I would like to say to you, you miserable swines who are dreaming of a happy student life here at Heiberg Academy, you'd better request to be expelled from the school right now. Of course, it is all right to make friends and lovers, but you must remember that this is not a mere educational institution."

 

In the 30 years since the end of the Great War, the number of Psychics has increased exponentially, but their percentage of the country's total population is still tiny. Most of them probably have never had anyone of the same generation with psychic abilities around them before. That was why many of the students were so excited to make new friends.

However, the female instructor's words reminded them of the reality of the situation.

 

"As we already explained during the entrance exam, now let's review some of the basic rules of the Heiberg School."

 

Then, the instructor began to give them a brief explanation of the school's rules and regulations.

The explanation, which took about 15 minutes, could be summarized as follows.

 

◇Rule No. 1 : Points are allocated to all students according to their performance on the entrance exam.

◇Rule No. 2 : Points will be increased or decreased according to the results of the <practical exam>, which is held at the end of every other week.

◇Rule No. 3 : Five students with the lowest points on the last day of each month will be expelled from the school.

◇Rule No. 4 : Students who receive zero points will be expelled from the school before the last day of the month.

◇Rule No. 5 : Expelled students will have their memories erased immediately.

 

The female instructor did not bother to explain, but each student had a string of numbers floating in their minds.

It seemed to be a mechanism that enabled them to grasp their respective possession points intuitively by simply thinking about it. This was probably due to the influence of the suggestion the headmaster had given them.

According to the female instructor's explanation, the points possessed by other students will be posted on the information board in front of the classroom building every Monday.

 

"By the way, several outstanding students - the six students who were exempted from the entrance examination - were given a uniform initial score of 1000 points. But on the other hand, the lowest-ranked swine who took part in the <Screening Test> will probably get less than 50 points on average. Do you know what this means?"

 

In other words, this is what Isabella was trying to say.

─The first candidate to be eliminated has already been decided.

Nina looked at the handful of mud-covered students who were forced to stand at the top of the auditorium.

While most of them were overwhelmed with fear and anxiety, there was only one male student who was welcoming the overwhelmingly unfavorable race for survival with a wry smile on his face. His eyes, peeking through a gap in his disheveled black hair, glowed with a vicious gleam.

 

"Come to think of it, I haven't told you about the other rule yet."

 

The female instructor's cold words brought Nina back to her senses.

 

"The sixth rule is ......, you are allowed to have duels between students here. The students involved are free to decide the rules and engage in a match."

 

The female instructor contorted the edges of her mouth in a strange manner.

 

"In the <duel>, each of you will wager the points that have been allocated to you. Do you understand? This system is, in other words, a means of salvation for the lowest-performing students."

 

─A salvation.

Many of the students engraved these words deep in their hearts.

Indeed, this system can be a trump card for those with low grades to survive in the school.

Students whose points are set unfairly low at the time of admission are in danger of being expelled from the school on a monthly basis. The points that can be earned in the bi-weekly examinations are meager, and there is too little hope for students to have only two chances each month to earn points in the first place.

Nevertheless, with the <duel> system in place, the possibility of overcoming these difficulties increases.

 

"The procedure for conducting a <duel> is simple."

 

The female instructor pulled out an empty piece of paper from somewhere.

 

"On a form to be handed out later, write down the [rules] agreed upon by the students, the [date and time] of the duel, the [names and student ID numbers] of all the participants, and the [number of points each is willing to bet on]. Of course, if someone forged his or her name on the form, the duel would be deemed to be invalid."

"Don't we have to submit a form?", one of the boys asked.

"No, that's not needed."

 

The female instructor answered without changing her expression.

 

"As you may already realize, the current Heiberg Academy's system is based on the psychic abilities of the school's headmaster. The same goes for the<duel>. The same goes for dueling. Once the necessary information on the form is filled out, his ability will automatically be triggered."

[I'll explain the rest.]

 

Not a single student was able to immediately recognize the voice that interrupted the female instructor's explanation.

Isabella was certainly the only one standing on the platform.

 

[What are you guys clamoring about? Be quiet.]

 

A voice that sounded like that of a boy in the early transitional phase of his voice, or like that of a woman who still had a childish voice. There were noises mixed in here and there, and there was no intonation at all, so it was somewhat strange to hear a voice coming from a real person.

It was as if the voices were forcefully merged together from the radio.

 

"......Ah!"

 

A female student who was sitting a little behind seemed to have noticed something. Like the other students around her, Nina also looked in the direction she was pointing.

In front of the podium, a tin doll in the shape of a cat stood on two legs.

 

[Have you finally noticed it? This year's freshmen are a bunch of ninnies, huh.]

 

The cat doll was opening and closing its mouth and swaying its body from side to side.

If it weren't for its adorable appearance, this would look like nothing more than something out of a horror movie.

 

[Well, since the "duel" will determine your future fate, it has to be fair. That's why I, Jake-sama, am going to be the witness.]

 

The cat doll, oblivious to the confusion of the students, went on.

 

[No need to be scared, guys. My duty is simply to watch over the <duel>. On the day of the <duel>, by the order of the headmaster, I will attend the duel and confirm that the rules you have set up are being followed. Of course, I won't interfere in any way, no matter how crazy the rules are. It's your own decision after all.]

 

Nina calmly analyzed that Jake was probably in charge of monitoring the students' abilities.

The school values this system so much that they have prepared such an existence that is beyond human knowledge.

Considering the fact that the mechanism of psychic abilities has not been fully understood even 30 years after their emergence, this educational policy that emphasizes actual combat makes perfect sense.

 

"The maximum number of Jakes allocated to you first-year students is four. That means that no more than four dueling groups will be allowed to fight at the same time. The end of the month will be particularly busy, so get your pledges ready as soon as possible."

 

When incomprehensible phenomena ensue so frequently, people are often at a loss for words.

Even though all of the students here have the power to rewrite the laws of physics, it is clearly beyond comprehension to give life to an inorganic object.

 

"That's all for the explanations. After lunch break, class orientation will be held in each classroom. The class assignment list will be posted in front of the classroom building, so please check it yourself."

 

After scanning over the silent auditorium emotionlessly, the female instructor, Isabella, concluded.

 

"Well then, may you have an enjoyable school life."

 

After a long silence, the students finally understood.

In order to survive here for the next three years, they had to let go of any unnecessary doubts.

If they were to be surprised by every single one of those tin monsters, they would not be able to survive. Instead, they should focus on the fact that everyone here has a chance.

 

"You seem to be enjoying yourself, Nina. You seem to be unable to contain your smile."

 

Nina gave Bennett a smile, and he gave her the same kind of expression.

 

"Yes. I will do my best to live up to the name of the Stingray family."

"You have a humble attitude, huh? I must learn from you."

 

She covered up the emotions swirling inside her and shook hands with him amicably. This was a skill Nina had cultivated over the course of her life.

As she gazed at the commotion of the students exiting the auditorium, Nina secretly clenched her fists.

 

Three-year battle is about to begin.

No one will ever know, a one-man war is about to begin.

◇

There was an hour to spare before orientation would begin in each classroom. Nina arrived in front of a fountain that stood quietly away from the classroom building.

The ring-shaped fountain was completely dried up, and moss was growing all over the concrete, indicating that it had been abandoned for some time. Perhaps it was a mistake to build the fountain in such an inconspicuous place in the first place.

She sat down on a bench facing the fountain and let out a small sigh.

 

"...... I guess my life is similar."

 

After making sure that no one was around, Nina took out an object from her bag that was about the size of her pinky finger.

It was a cute little doll in the shape of a rain frog, dressed in a suit lavishly decorated with sequins. It was the only precious thing she had ever owned, given to her by her older sister when she was a child.

Nina let out her emotions for the first time today as she held the rain frog doll in her palm.

 

"......I don't really want to do this~!"

 

Although there was no way she was going to get a response from the expressionless frog, she went on regardless.

 

"The person who came up with this school system is really insane! As a student, one is supposed to be active in studies and sports! Just what the heck is a <duel> even for!?"

 

With this doll, she could speak her true feelings, which she could not let anyone in the world hear. The mask she always wore in public was completely peeled off.

 

"The students are all upper-class elites! I'm exhausted! I should have refused to attend school right away!"

 

She couldn't imagine spending three years in such an abnormal environment. Of course, there were no guarantees that she would still be alive in this school in three years' time.

─No, I have to accomplish it no matter what it takes.

Nina tightly clutched the rain frog that looked up at her with an expression of zero affection.

If by any chance she, the youngest member of the Stingray family, were to be expelled from the school, she had no idea what her father, who was extremely concerned about public appearances, would do to her.

She recalls her father's words as he placed his hand on Nina's shoulder when she was still a child, while he was dragging her around to social gatherings.

 

"Although not as powerful as her two older brothers, this child also has a highly renowned psychic ability......"

"Such a small child has psychokinesis so powerful that it could cause a mountain to collapse......"

"Haha, what an exaggeration. <Queen of Calamity>, you say?......"

"She will graduate from Heiberg Academy at the top of her class?　That's too early to tell......"

 

Nina was able to ignore the adults' impassive stares, clad in an innocent smile.

By that time, Nina had already noticed.

The hand that her father placed on her shoulder was not filled with love. She realized that her father was using her as a tool to increase his political power.

She wondered what would happen to her if she revealed her secret to him.

 

"Anyway, that was a real close call earlier......"

 

The memory of being entangled with three students before the entrance ceremony came back to her vividly.

 

"...... It just so happens to work, but this can't go on forever, can it?"

 

She couldn't seem to suppress her bad habit of going into soliloquy mode whenever she found herself in the mood.

The same feelings as usual - fear, frustration, guilt, and other kinds of rough impurities, kept piling up inside her heart.

Suddenly, she saw a piece of concrete the size of her pinky finger lying in front of her. It was probably debris from the deteriorated fountain.

Nina extended her palm to the piece and concentrated all her mind on it.

 

"...... Move, move, move!"

 

No matter how much Nina wished, the piece would not budge. It neither floated nor shattered, but simply remained in the same state it had been in all along.

 

"Well, I guess it's only natural......"

 

Nina let out a sigh for what might be the second time today.

She is not a psychic, so there is no way she could have moved that piece of debris.

Nina had no idea how real psychics could make objects levitate or explode.

She had never been able to create a psychic phenomenon, even though she had tried to put her power into her palms and concentrate her mind with a suitable expression on her face, just as she had seen the others do.

The reason why such a person was feared as a powerful psychic and was exempted from the entrance examination to enter the Heiberg Academy was only because she kept on accumulating small lies.

 

"...... I wonder, why things ended up like this."

 

It started out as a mere coincidence.

It is said that the psychic ability usually manifests itself at the age of four or five, when one begins to understand things. The underlying mechanism is still unknown, but that is the way it works anyway.

Children born into the Stingray family were subjected to a screening process at that time.

If a child does not show any signs of psychic ability, he or she will be expelled from the family and placed in foster care with a distant relative, even if he or she is the child of the family's own parents. On the other hand, the Stingray family has sometimes forcefully adopted a child from a branch family that has developed psychic abilities.

Nina, the youngest of three siblings, was no exception to this rule.

During this time, the child Nina was under constant surveillance. This was because a child who could not yet control his or her psychic abilities might have a spontaneous outburst of these abilities at any moment.

It was about two weeks after the screening process had begun that something happened.

Accompanied by her butler, Swanson, Nina was climbing a mountain owned by the Stingray family. Apparently, her father was intrigued by a dubious thesis that the vastness of nature could aid in the development of psychic abilities.

That said, the gently sloping paths were enough for a child's feet to climb, and it was pure joy for her to be able to breathe fresh air for the first time in a while.

 

"...... Wow, it's beautiful."

 

When they were about halfway up the mountain, Nina casually pointed to a mountainside in the distance.

The trees there were of a completely different color than the surrounding forest, which caught her interest as a young girl.

However, none of the adults around her recognized it as such.

That was understandable.

The moment Nina pointed her finger, the slope in that direction suddenly began to collapse.

 

"...... Eh? W-What? What's going on?"

 

With a thunderous roar, trees, boulders, and masses of mud and soil of unusual colors began to slide down the slope.

Of course, no one noticed that Nina herself was the most bewildered of everyone. The catastrophic scene made everyone present foresee the birth of a new talent.

After that, incomprehensible phenomena kept occurring around Nina.

A cupboard collapsed even though she did not touch it, a bird flying in the sky suddenly fell, and a water pipe burst, spraying a large amount of water.

Only Nina knew that they were entirely coincidental.

The cupboard was just deteriorating due to aging, the bird just bumped into another bird in mid-air, and the burst of the water pipe was probably the result of the earthquake that had occurred the day before.

Still, the adults never doubted that Nina had a special talent. Her father, who had never been interested in his daughter from the beginning, was completely satisfied after receiving the butler's report.

Of course, there were many times when she wanted to reveal the truth.

But Nina knew what would happen to children who were judged to be fakes, and most of all, she did not want to disappoint the butler who was looking at her expectantly. So she kept pretending that she was someone with a psychic ability.

Fortunately, Nina had a transcendent talent for acting.

She also created a setting where her abilities were too powerful to control, which also helped, and until now, no one has ever uncovered her secret.

 

"......If I confided everything to someone, would it make me feel better?"

 

How many times have she thought that?

It is a dream that will never come true. Now that she's at the Heiberg Academy, there's nowhere she can run away to.

 

◇

 

An information board was installed at the entrance of the classroom building, where a list of class assignments was posted. There were almost no students in the vicinity, probably because she had stayed in front of the fountain until the very last minute before the end of lunch break.

There was only one student who was staring at the information board with a serious expression on his face.

Pale-orange skin, unkempt black hair, and slitted eyes the color of the night. His uniform, worn unceremoniously, was torn in places and stained with mud all over.

The same person seen at the entrance ceremony who took part in the <Screening test>.

 

".........What?"

 

The boy, who seemed to have Eastern blood in him, turned a dubious face toward Nina. She seemed to have been staring at him without realizing it.

 

"...... Ah, excuse me."

"Hm? By any chance, are you......"

 

What he directed at her was a smile. The corners of the boy's mouth were lifted up, and he even showed his white teeth in an exemplary smile.

And yet, there seemed to be no light shining in his eyes. It was as if there was nothing interesting in the world.

To mend the awkward silence, the boy finally opened his mouth.

 

"...... Yup, you're Nina Stingray, right? I never imagined that we would be classmates."

"I'm honored that you know me. By the way, what is your name?"

"Uh, ...... do I have to answer that?"

"Yes?"

 

There would be no way for anyone to refuse to introduce themselves in such a situation. Nina wondered whether she hadn't conveyed the question correctly.

 

"I see you had a little trouble hearing me, so let me repeat the question. May I ask your name?"

"Stingray-san, you came here to find information about your class, I presume?"

"Um, please hear what people said......."

"I heard someone talking about it earlier. You're in [Class 8]."

"......T-Thank you very much."

 

The conversation was somewhat confusing, but she decided to express her gratitude anyway.

After seeing the boy who raised his hand lightly and walked away, she turned her attention to the information board just to be sure. Finally, she found a number written next to her own name, which was, as a matter of fact, written as [Class 6].

─What you're telling me is totally different!

Nina looked around in a panic. The boy who had just lied to her was already heading toward the inner hallway.

Normally, she would have just let it slide, but for some reason, she couldn't seem to bear it this time.

 

 

"Turns out I'm not in [Class 8], though?"

 

The boy looked surprised for a moment, as if he didn't expect her to come after him.

However, he immediately returned to a cold expression and muttered in a very low-pitched tone.

 

"Sorry, sorry. I guess I misunderstood it a little bit."

"You said some random thing to quickly end the conversation, didn't you?"

 

She finally noticed why she couldn't let it slide.

The sense of discomfort emanating from the boy's attitude probably stemmed from hostility.

Nina's two older brothers are members of the <White Knights>. They are hailed as heroes, but it is not surprising that there are those who secretly harbor a grudge against them.

 

"It was really just a misunderstanding."

 

Even so, the boy did not seem to be perturbed.

 

"You see, I heard that this academy is a rather harsh place. I thought it would be good to have a little ordeal on the first day of school to get one's mind right. I'm not necessarily expecting you to be grateful though."

"What kind of reasoning is that?"

"There is a saying, [misfortune is God's guidance]. There is always a reason behind misfortunes. Who knows, maybe your school life from now on will be a pursuit of the significance of being told the wrong class."

"I've never heard such a saying before......"

"Well, of course, I just made it up after all."

 

It had been a long time since she had experienced a situation where she couldn't close her mouth out of shock.

The stress that had accumulated since the entrance ceremony could collapse the levee of reason at any moment.

 

"You really talk a lot, huh...... Despite being unable to read numbers properly."

"There are all kinds of people in the world after all. Well then, see you some other time."

 

The boy left after he was trying to sway her to the end of the conversation.

─Gosh, what's wrong with that guy~!?

Just before her rationality collapsed, Nina was able to take a deep breath to calm herself down.

Don't worry, everything will be fine.

Unless we are in the same class, I will not have anything to do with him in the future.

 

The moment she stepped into the classroom wearing a graceful and calm expression, Nina sensed that the atmosphere in the classroom froze for a moment.

The students, who had not yet taken their seats and were still engaged in idle chit-chat in lieu of self-introductions, turned their eyes to her warily.

Nina was used to being treated like this.

Even if they were to treat her like a monster, it would mean that she didn't have to worry about anyone harming her. And that's good enough for her.

Nina silently made her way up the stairs and sat down on the rightmost seat on the top row.

There was no particular reason why she had chosen this seat. She simply assumed that there would be less trouble with no one sitting around her.

Nina was planning to go about her day quietly, but one of the students, paying no attention to the atmosphere in the classroom, spoke to her.

 

"Nina-chan ...... I presume? Is the seat next to you vacant?"

 

The student's smile radiated an openness reminiscent of the morning sun.

Her deep green eyes sparkled, and her orange short-cut hair gave off an impression of liveliness to her surroundings.

Nina was taken aback, but quickly regained her composure.

 

"Of course it's vacant. Please have a seat."

"Thank you!" Before Nina could finish her sentence, she slid into the seat next to her. "I've always wanted to talk to you, Nina-chan."

 

The girl, who identified herself as Emma Licorice, was apparently worried about whether she would fit in well in class.

With such a bright personality, it seemed like she would be able to make friends quickly, but in reality, Emma seemed to be seriously anxious. Nina thought it was amusing.

As soon as she introduced herself, Emma began to bombard Nina with questions one after another.

About whether or not the rumors that Nina, who developed a psychic ability when she was four years old, caused a mountain near her house to collapse was true or false. About the type of gemstone that fitted into the brooch she wore on her chest. About her life in the Stingray family, one of the most prestigious families in the country.

 

"Eh, Nina-chan, are you living in the dormitory, as well? I thought you had a villa or something like that somewhere nearby."

"Of course not. It is the school's rule that all students must live in the dormitories. I'm strictly abiding by that rule."

"Really!? Then we will have lots of opportunities to play together from now on!"

 

Nina has a habit of putting up an invisible wall between her and others before interacting with them, but she was able to answer Emma's questions in a natural way.

Perhaps it was because she enjoyed seeing Emma's eyes sparkle with anticipation and excitement, and more than anything, it was because she did not feel anything like hidden intentions in her behavior.

─Maybe I could finally lead a normal school life.

Nina secretly crushed the sweet thought that suddenly came to her mind.

Emma is indeed a good girl, and the days she will spend with her may be filled with joy.

However, the place where they met is simply not favorable.

This school is not a place where youthful activities are encouraged. It is a place solely for competition for survival, and if one of them drops out, even the memory of their time together will be erased. In such an environment, there is no way friendship can be nurtured.

Besides, Nina thought.

No matter how kind she may be, if she found out her big secret, she wouldn't treat her the same way as she does now.

 

"...... Oh, there you are! I've been looking for you, you know!?"

 

Unaware of Nina's sentimentality, Emma waved to her friend as he entered the classroom.

The boy tried to ignore her once, but seemed to have decided to relent when Emma didn't give up. He let out a deliberate sigh and came lazily up the stairs.

─That guy, no way.

Nina questioned Emma with an expression as if she had just heard a bad joke.

 

"Emma? Are you by any chance friends with him?"

"Yes, I am."

"Umm, sorry. I'm a little nervous around people......"

"It's my first time meeting him, either. Don't worry, Jin-kun is a nice guy."

 

A nice guy? Him?
