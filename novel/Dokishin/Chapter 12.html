When he started investigating since it was strange, he learned that an irregular unit called 『Cleaners』in the military was targeting me.

In other words, the Karendil Kingdom――or rather, the 『Karendil Kingdom Army』, marked me before they checked my status.
The timing was when they received the report of the troll subjugation.
I guess they were already moving in the direction of getting rid of me at this stage.

[This letter alone is not enough to make a judgment.]

[Yes. Considering the speed of judgment, it seems likely that the decision was made arbitrarily by the military.]

Yes, that’s the problem.
Considering the time, the movements were too fast.
Let’s assume that they knew that I’m a Hero at the time of the troll subjugation report.
The report itself was carried out with almost no time loss as there was a magic tool for communication.
We were attacked on the sixth day, counting the day the troll subjugation report was made as the first day.
Let’s look back on our actions.
On the first day, I ate, drank and slept. This should be the day report of the troll subjugation arrived to them.
On the second day, I spent the day on a date with Marle. Commander Twanning should have prepared a hunter knife at this time. The letter didn’t say when the order was given, but if it wasn’t prepared at this stage at the latest, it wouldn’t be in time.
On the third day, I went to the Knights headquarter, received a status check, and was determined to be a Hero. Me and Marl made preparations for the escape that day.
Early in the morning on the fourth day, we left Crossroads, and it took almost three days to arrive at the ruins.
On the sixth day, around sunset, we were attacked by assassins.

According to the story, it takes one day to get from the Royal Capital Alfen to Crossroads by horse, and about three days on foot.
The assassins must have traveled on horseback, at least to Crossroads, as they would not be able to make it on foot.
Considering that no horses were found after leaving the forest, I wonder if they walked from Crossroads to the ruins.
Since we should have dealt with the enemies along the way, was their marching speed the same as us or a little faster? Well that’s a possibility.
It’s also possible that they came by a different route than us, but it’s unlikely.

The assassin squad should have entered the Crossroads the day we left, or the next day at the latest.
In other words, by the time I checked my status with the knights, the assassin unit had already left for Crossroads.
Considering the time to prepare the hunter’s knife, the decision to assassinate me should be made within a day of the report.
If it weren’t for the fact that I always had the knife engraved with the secret mark during the pursuit, they would need to be very precise in terms of the time schedule.

[That’s way too fast.]

[That’s right. If it’s not the military’s dogmatic decision, it’s moving too quickly. Anyway, I still have to talk about it.]

There was no point in thinking deeply about this area. And just like Marl said, if you don’t talk about it properly, you don’t know when you’ll be scratched in your sleep.
For the time being, we have no choice but to be cautious of the ‘unfortunate accident’ on the way.

However, it seems that the plots were properly organized as we approached the under construction castle walls.

It looks like it’s going to be good bodybuilding exercise for an adventurer who aspires to be a warrior. It’s killing two birds with one stone. I honestly felt sorry.

It’s as if Crossroads had been made bigger and more lively.

It seems that the Adventurer’s Guild was also located in this area.

The inside of the third castle wall was lined with high-end shops and inns.
In addition, there were public buildings such as government offices, churches, and magic schools.

She seemed to be a graduate of a magic school. With her beauty and proportions, she must’ve been very popular.
A magic school was intriguing. But, I don’t think I’d be able to enroll there at my age.

You can say that the inside of the second castle wall was mostly inhabited by Nobles.
All I could see were well-dressed people or guards. I didn’t see many adventurers, probably because they don’t have any business in this area.
And finally, the Royal Castle came into view.

[It’s huge, as expected of the Royal Castle.]

The only castle I could imagine was that one named after a princess in a dreamland.
The castle in front was huge. I could hardly imagine how people would live inside it.
I can’t describe it well, but the king lived there and lived and breathed as the heart of the country.
I could also see some spires. That spire was not just a decoration, it’s made for a purpose, and was actually used.

It’s kind of exciting. It’s like the first time I saw the streets of Crossroads.

As far as I could see from here, the castle seemed to be surrounded by a moat.
There was a drawbridge in front of the gate of the castle wall, and this drawbridge would be raised when defending.
This castle must have experienced many battles. Before it became such a great country, this first wall must have protected people from monsters and wars with other countries.

When I was looking up at the Royal Castle and deep in emotion, Captain Waltz, who seemed to have finished dealing with the guards of the royal castle, called out to me.

[Our escort ends here. Take care, Taichi-dono.]
