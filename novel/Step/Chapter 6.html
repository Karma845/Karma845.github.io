Chapter 6: Actually, There was a Huge Misunderstanding, But Now I Know...

Sometimes, even a massive misunderstanding can go down in history.

For example, in 1492, Columbus discovered the New World.

He initially set out on a western route to India but ended up discovering the New World instead.

However, when he saw the local natives, he not only mistakenly believed he had reached India but also spent his entire life considering the Americas as part of Asia until his death.

The reason why the "West Indies" exist on the Florida Peninsula in the United States, far away from Asia, is because of this.

Or take the year 1989 when the Berlin Wall fell.

East German official Schabowski announced during a press conference, "East German citizens are allowed to legally travel abroad."

When a reporter asked him, "When will this take effect?" he replied, "Immediately!"

But in reality, he hadn't even attended the meeting that decided on this matter and accidentally disclosed information about lifting the ban before it was officially announced, making statements that differed from government policy.

As a result, people who saw the news flocked to the checkpoints.

The "Wall of Shame," which had been built overnight, collapsed instantly due to one man's misunderstanding.

This incident is also known as the "most beautiful misunderstanding in history."

As for the misunderstanding that will remain in my own history... it's the "sibling misunderstanding" that I realized yesterday.

From the moment I first met Akira until yesterday, I mistakenly thought she was my younger brother.

I can't face her now.

If only I had known this fact earlier, more quickly, and correctly!

"That doesn't matter! What's important is Akira, it's Akira! Why didn't you clearly tell me that she's my sister? And also, his name is not Schabonski, it's Schabowski!"

I know I'm just venting my anger. But I can't control the subsequent rage and regret that came rushing in without an outlet.

"Anyway, I always thought Akira was my younger brother, and that's why I treated her that way..."

It's too late for regrets now, but up until today, I've done many foolish things relying on the belief that she was my younger brother.

No, now I'm not important. Akira is important--

"--Akira, you're not my younger brother but my younger sister, right...?"

Under normal circumstances, who in their right mind would ask such a question three weeks later?

When someone is mistaken for their gender, a girl is bound to be deeply hurt.
