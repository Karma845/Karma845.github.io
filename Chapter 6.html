The national newspaper was read by the butler as part of her daily routine. Nina, who was nine years old at the time, made it a habit to look for news about the <White Knights>, to which her two older brothers belonged, first thing in the morning.

She was 13 years old apart from her eldest brother, Victor, and 10 years old from her second brother, Haine, and had no recollection of ever having played together before. Now that they have graduated from the Heiberg School and joined the <White Knights>, the only way to connect with them was through newspaper articles describing their activities.

─Are they really my older brothers?

Such a question always lingered in her little head.

It was hard to believe that Nina, who had no choice but to live her life lying to everyone around her about her psychic abilities, and these people, who continuously fought the enemies of the country as heroes of salvation, had any blood connection at all.

Above all, the ruthless and confident atmosphere they wore was something Nina could never understand.

On a day that seemed to end in a mundane and boring manner, an unexpected change occurred.

As usual, Nina was playing with a frog doll in her room when a voice called out to her from behind.

"Hello Nina, are you playing by yourself as usual?"

Nina felt a shiver as her head was being patted along with a gentle voice.

She didn't have to turn around to know. This voice, this cold hand, this touch─.

"......Haine-niisan."

"It's been a while, Nina. Can you show me your face?"

When she turned around to do as she was told, there she saw a vacant smile that she hadn't seen in two years.

His slightly permed hair has the same platinum color as Nina's. His eyes are a slightly deeper shade of indigo than Nina's, and he cast a penetrating glance at her.

An inhumanly good-looking face, as if designed by a heartless being based on cold calculations.

Although he wore a soft smile, she knew very well that it was nothing more than an elaborate fabrication.

"......Why are you here, brother Haine?"

"I just finished a big job and got a short vacation."

"What big job?"

"Well, I've been fighting some bad guys."

─Surely, it was about that article.

Nina, who had extraordinary insight even back then, saw the truth hidden in the abyss beneath her brother's eyes. Nonetheless, she was only nine years old and didn't know much about how things really worked around the world. She couldn't help but put a lid on the questions that popped into her head and smile as if nothing had happened.

But before she realized it, her lips were moving.

"Hey, why did you kill all those bad people? You should have just arrested them......"

"Haha, Nina sure says some interesting things."

Her brother's next words imprinted an unforgettable horror in the young Nina's heart.

"There's no point in keeping a toy that you've finished playing with forever, is there?"

Like sheep waiting for the hour of judgment, no one said anything.

"...... I'm relieved then."

It was Emma who broke the silence.

She put away the knife in her black coat and gently held out her right hand.

"Righteous indignation, huh...... Well, that's not a bad motive. At least it's better than some feeble reason like atonement for your sins. If you were to say such a thing, I would have abandoned you."

"Emma, would you please stop testing my precious accomplice?"

"We're not in this for fun, you know. So what I did was only natural."

After saying this in a discouraged manner, Emma suddenly whispered.

"...... By the way, don't call me by that name. I've told you many times. I am not Emma Licorice now.""

Nina, who was relieved to be recognized by her new accomplice, couldn't miss what she had just said.

"...... Hmm? What do you mean?"

"See, Nina is confused because you say such confusing things."

"I'm just telling you the truth. Emma and I are completely different personalities. Besides, Emma is not even aware of my existence."

Although she was supposed to be hearing a shocking fact, Nina was strangely convinced.

The normal Emma and the girl in front of her seem so far removed from each other. It was definitely not a level that could be managed by mere acting.

If she is a different persona, then that is probably the truth.

"What's your name, then?"

"...... I'm an intelligence agent. I don't need a name."

"Eeh, that's kind of a pain in the ass. Wouldn't it be too inconvenient not to have a name to call you?"

"Wha-, what the hell ......? "You catch an enemy nation's spy, and it's an inconvenience, you say......?"

"Alright, for convenience's sake, I'll call you Fake Emma."

"Just call me Emma, then! Are you messing with me?"

An accomplice who could draw even an enemy agent into his pace is indeed too dependable.

A chuckle involuntarily spilled out from Nina's light-colored lips.

─A genius fraudster who was raised by a legendary villain who made enemies of the empire.

─A monster who has two older brothers who belong to the <White Knights> and whose name is known throughout the empire.

─And a skilled intelligence agent sent from the Republic, which is in a cold war with the Empire.

If these three dangerous individuals work together, they can turn any miracle into an inevitability.

They can deceive this world filled with lies with even more lies.

"Well, let's wrap these emotional moments up. There is still work to be done."

Jin's dark eyes lit up with a vicious light.

"Let's sort things out for now. First, our common goal is to identify and capture the <Shepherd's Dog>. He's been playing heavy-handed tactics to find the rat that snuck into the school."

"You're the one who led him to do that, are you not?"

The two of them seemed to already have an idea of who the <Shepherd's Dog> was.

And Nina wondered if it was the same person who had popped into her mind.

"Well, either way, we won't survive unless we defeat Karen. Apparently, she's not going to stop using her <Jolly Undertaker> until she sees our dead bodies."

"The current Karen Ashby has every justification for eliminating you two. I'm sure she's not even concerned that someone will suspect her connection to the headmaster after such a bold rampage."

"W-Wait a second! Karen is on a rampage because Satya, her maid, was attacked, right? If so, who attacked Satya?"

"Oh, of course, she made that up."

"That's ......"

You mean that she attacked her own best friend just to create a good reason to attack us?

No, but ...... there was not the slightest hint of cheap lies from the Karen I just met with.

The first thing that came to mind was a possibility that Nina wanted to deny.

Self-brainwashing.

It was the same method that Nina herself had used to defeat Bennett in the past. By believing that it was someone other than herself who attacked Satya, Karen was able to lie with a high degree of accuracy. And it was easy to assume that she has a talent for self-brainwashing from the way she practiced <conditioning> in the dojo.

Nina's arms and legs trembled on their own.

Her throat felt dry, and her vision suddenly blurred.

─I am going to fight with such a monster now?

"...... So, why are you guys smiling? Do you even aware of our current sotuation?"

Fear and elation. Two conflicting emotions, and yet Nina held them together without contradiction.

"Because, you know, it's not every day you get to see such a fun turn of events."

Seeing the edge of Jin's mouth lifted up in front of her, she remembered the golden rule she had been told once.

─A truly elaborate lie is like a kind of art.

"Sorry Emma, I feel the same way as he does."
