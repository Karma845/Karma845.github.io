Chapter 4: Back Alley Skits, Magic Hour Attacks

Casper appeared in front of the bus stop where Nina had agreed to meet him, dressed so stylishly that it was impossible for her to look at him directly.

First of all, the number of accessories he was wearing was outrageous. As if he had never heard of the concept of refrain, silver rings were attached to both of his wrists and to all of its 10 fingers. "That's quite a unique attire......"

"Is it? I'm trying to be rather modest today."

Of course, Casper would not have noticed the sarcasm, and for some reason, he only laughed in a humble way.

Nina was taking a walk around the dormitory when she was approached and asked out on a date at around eight o'clock this morning. The reason there were three hours of free time before the meeting was probably because he was carefully selecting his outfits.

Just like last time, Nina was to follow the instructions that were sent to her from the small transceiver plugged into her ear. However, since Jin was waiting at the knife workshop, the signal could not reach the bus heading for the downtown area.

This also meant that she would have to use her own judgment when talking aboard the bus.

"Sorry, Nina-chan. I know it's not really nice of me to suddenly ask you out on the very same day......"

"It's okay, I was free today anyway."

Actually, Nina wanted to sleep all day to recover from yesterday's fatigue, but this is also an important task, so there was nothing she could do.

She wasn't enthusiastic about the date itself to begin with, but since Jin had prepared so well, she couldn't get off the bus without his approval.

─Right, when I first rode this bus, Jin was next to me......

Nina shook the memory that suddenly came to her mind.

No. I have to concentrate on my current task.

"By the way, Casper-san, why are you carrying a guitar case?"

"Well. I promised you last time that I would write a song about how wonderful you are. I'm still a beginner, but I hope I can convey this passion to you."

"...... If you seriously insist on singing, I'll leave as fast as I can."

─I don't know how serious this guy is.

Jin reminded her to be on her guard, but talking to Casper like this, it seemed unlikely that he is the <shepherd's dog>.

"Come to think of it, Nina-chan, there are members of the <White Knights> in your family, right?"

She didn't expect such a question to come flying at her.

Nina turned the tension switch back on once more.

"...... Yes, my two older brothers belong to it. Did I mention it before?"

"I read in a newspaper article or something, is it true that they don't even tell their families about the specifics of their work?"

"Since it's that kind of a job, it certainly seems that there is a strong obligation to maintain confidentiality. We never know where spies from other countries might be hiding after all."

"It must be very difficult not being able to talk about one's true motives even with one's own family......"

She could not clearly read the emotions from Casper's face as he said this.

At least, there was no trace of acting there. Did he really just bring up the current topic as part of some casual conversation?

The conversation quickly moved in another direction, but Nina couldn't shake the feeling of discomfort until the bus arrived at its destination.

After getting off the bus, they walked for a while through the downtown area, which was lined with street stores. Following the directions she had memorized last night, Nina saw the store she was looking for in a deserted alleyway.

The stylishly written sign reading <Wates & Pete's Cutlery> was of such high quality that it was hard to believe that it had been completed in one night. Gasta, the landscaper whom she had met several times, had a rough appearance, but his workmanship was surprisingly delicate. The unmotivated-looking clerk - a face she had seen when she had built the pit at the school before - was one of Gasta's most trusted subordinates, and it seemed he knew about Nina and Jin's circumstances.

Anyway, the small transceiver signal could reach her now.

Nina focused her attention on the noisy instructions she could hear in her ear.

[Nina, if you can hear my voice, tilt your head to the left. ...... Okay. Let's reconfirm the arrangements.]

Entering the surprisingly spacious store, she listened while acting as if she was gazing intently at the knives.

[Five minutes from now. The three men will enter the store just in time for the counter clerk to be shifted. One of them has a gun, but don't worry.

Mr. Heath, who sold them the gun, has made it so that the bullets won't come out. Their gang is so small that they can't even afford to consume bullets for test firing, so they don't know about it at all.]

Jin must be watching them from behind the hidden door behind the counter.

As for Nina, she has no choice but to trust the information given to her by her accomplice.

[While the two of them intimidate the waiter and the customers, the remaining one is planning to find the finest quality goods in the wooden crate. The rest is up to you, Nina, to successfully incite Casper to do his bidding. As long as we find out his psychic abilities' true nature, today's objective will be accomplished.]

Nina tapped lightly on her ear to convey her understanding.

Casper, who was unaware of the skit that was about to unfold in the store, looked at the knives on display with a childlike gleam in his eyes.

"Hee, I didn't know there were so many different kinds of knives."

They still had a few minutes before the attack, so she decided to continue her conversation.

"Yes. Knives are often associated as something dangerous, but in fact, many of them are very artistic."

"Yeah, I can see why you're into knives, Nina-chan."

"See, what do you think of this one? It has a design of a snake coiled around the handle, and it looks very difficult to use, doesn't it?"

"Certainly, I don't think I could use it for cooking."

"Artistry and practicality don't necessarily match well together, after all. Look at this one with the unusually long blade─"

"Everybody raise your hands!"

The robbers, who had appeared two minutes earlier than scheduled, raised their voices.

The three men were wearing black balaclavas to hide their faces, and the tallest man was clutching a pistol.

As per the script she had thought up beforehand, Nina whimpered.

"...... Casper-san. Looks like we've been caught up in an unfortunate event."

"Looks like it, Nina-chan. What shall we do?"

Contrary to the clerk who surrendered easily, the two of them kept a relaxed expression on their faces.

The leader of the group, who had sensed their unusual reaction, came up to them and said,

"What are you guys whispering about!?"

"You see, it's because we're on a date."

"Can't you see your situation!? We have guns!"

Although she knew there was no danger, this situation still made her tremble with fear. Nina smiled, trying her best to suppress the urge to run away.

"Ahaha, do you think you can handle us with just that thing?"

Laughing in annoyance, Casper lightly tapped the robber on the shoulder.

"Nina-chan, looks like they really mean it."

"D-Don't tell me you guys are heretics-......."

Casper must not know that the pistol has been tampered with. And yet it was clearly outrageous that he could behave in this manner.

[All right, Nina, go incite Casper.]

"...... What's the matter? If your hand shakes so much, you wouldn't be able to aim properly."

"By the way, Casper-san. I may look like this, but I'm a pacifist, and I don't want to slaughter humans unnecessarily, no matter how many robbers there are."

"I see, you're very kind, Nina-chan......"

"And I have my doubts about your ability. No matter how meager the alliance may be, if your psychic abilities are nowhere near as good as Karen-san's, then there's no merit for me."

"...... Huh, wait a minute? Nina-chan, is there some kind of halo shining above you?"

"Umm, are you listening?"

"Haha, I know," Casper laughed lightly, "Shall I crush these annoying people a little bit then?"

Flames of murderous intent lit up the eyes that peeked through the red hair's gaps.

Casper slowly lifted his right hand and thrust his outstretched palm in front of the robber's face.

Five fingers slowly closed as if to crush it. Then the robber suddenly began to scream in agony. The hunched-over man retreated pitifully, his eyes fixed on Casper as if he were looking at something terrifying.

"Y-You, that was......."

"Don't look away, look carefully. And remember how lucky you are to know the cause of your death."

"Hiiih~, you monster......!"

It was not clear if it was a matter of angle or whatever was going on. Perhaps because it was only a moment, the two remaining robbers who rushed to the scene didn't seem to understand either.

─But there is no doubt that there is something with his right hand.

"This is the last warning I'm going to give you now."

Casper stated coldly as he slowly approached the frightened robbers.

"If you don't want to get yourself killed, you've got ten seconds to get the hell out of here. Ten, nine, eight ...... Oh shit, I'm getting tired of counting. Alright, let's change it to three seconds. Three, two......."

The frightened man shouted at his companions, who still didn't seem to realize the gravity of the situation.

"L-Let's get out of here, guys! This guy's nuts......!"

"Come on, hurry up. Don't ever interrupt my date again!"

The robbers ran away as fast as they could, dragging their friends, who were too stunned to stand up, along with them.

─Only a really strong person can repel an opponent with threats alone.

And it is not just a bluff, like Nina's, that is a combination of bad reputation and acting. The robbers clearly were terrified by what they witnessed.

Something so vicious that the robbers were so frightened and yet could instantly understand the danger - what exactly did Casper generate with his psychic ability?

Nina had no clue, even though she had him use his psychic ability right in front of her. All the time and money spent on this farce was wasted.

[I couldn't see the true nature of his ability. It can't be helped, Plan B.]

Nina hastily switched her thoughts.

Determining Casper's psychic ability is the only a way to maintain the alliance with Karen. So, this is not the time to be losing one's composure.

What is truly important for Nina and Jin is to find the answer to the suspicion that Casper may be the <shepherd's dog>. Originally, this plan was a two-step plan.

"What do we do now, Casper-san? Shall we go into another store before the police show up and make things troublesome for us?"

According to Jin, the experience of overcoming an unusual crisis together can help break down psychological barriers. If they can find a moment to loosen Casper's guard, they may be able to detect signs that he is connected with the school's headmaster.

However, Casper was merely scratching his head with an awkward look on his face.

[He is not responding very well. Nina, give another push.]

"...... What's wrong? Let's get out of here."

"Ah, hold on a second."

Casper kept his languid smile on his face, turned his back to Nina, and began to walk toward the back of the store.

His action was so natural that Nina could not immediately perceive that this was an urgent situation.

─Wait a minute, Jin is still at the back of the store.......

She desperately tried to formulate an excuse to stop Casper, but it was unlikely that she would be able to do so in time.

Driven by frustration, her heartbeat grew faster and it became difficult for her to breathe well. She even had the illusion of despair seeping out from the corners of the ceiling like a black mist.

"I've been feeling a presence since a while ago."

Casper, not heeding the clerk who was trying to stop him, reached out his hand to the wall behind the counter.

"Hey, clerk. This is definitely a hidden door, right?"

Casper pushed on the brick wall and a rectangular outline appeared on the wall. A metal shaft was threaded through the shorter side of the rectangle, and the hidden door rotated around it.

Casper smiled like an explorer who had discovered a hidden treasure and entered the darkened office in the back.

What excuse would they use to get out of this predicament?

─Pretend that Jin is hired as a part-timer?

No, that would be too unnatural, especially given that students at the Heiberg Academy receive monthly subsidies.

─Shall I assert that I didn't know anything about it?

No, Casper, who already doubts the relationship between them, will not be deceived.

Nina felt a sense of foreboding as she realized that hell awaited her at the end of every option.

This would be the end of their fraud.

If Casper were the <shepherd's dog>, the two fraudsters would be chased out of the school and left to rot in the dark.

".................. Huh?"

Nina's bad imagination, however, was quickly negated.

Behind the hidden door - the room where Jin was supposed to be hiding - was completely empty. There were only two very simple office desks lined up side by side, and no sign of life.

"That's strange, I definitely felt a presence here."

"...... Casper-san, let's stop here. You're being rude to the clerk."

"Ah! S-S-S-Sorry, Nina-chan! That's not what I intended!"

Casper bowed his head while apologizing excessively even to the clerk. He even broke out in a cold sweat, showing that he was truly sorry, apparently.

[...... Nina, let's call it a day.]

She could hear Jin's voice in her ear, and she almost screamed.

Since it was Jin, he would probably go so far as to prepare even more sneaky measures in the hidden room. Whatever the case, Nina was glad he was safe.

Nina let out a sigh and lowered her tone of voice.

"I think we should end it for today."

"Wait, I've still got ten different date plans in my hand. ......

"That's for another time. Well, see you later."

"Aaah, wait, Nina-chan! I'm sorry~!"

Nina walked away into the crowd, pushing aside her fear of turning her back on an opponent.

Casper screamed pitifully for a while, but did not try to chase after her.

Where in the world is he going?

Turning off the small transceiver, Jin chased after Casper as he walked away.

Casper looked dejected, as if he had lost all his money, and was walking so slowly that people passing by were annoyed.

Was that how much he had devoted himself to this date?

No, it is dangerous to speculate only based on the surface.

The insight to spot a hidden door with a single glance.

The discretion to draw a line between himself and others at the last minute.

There are just too many concerns to judge Casper as an insignificant opponent.

The atmosphere has a distinctly different feeling than that of the main street.

Graffiti all over the walls. Trash lying on the street spread a foul odor. A steel frame protruding from the outline of the building cast countless shadows on the street, as if renovation work was being done on a nearby building, and the sound of single-pipe scaffolding being assembled rang out like an alarm. The melancholy wafting from Casper's back grew thicker, and even the shadows he cast on the ground seemed to be a little darker than the surrounding area.

What on earth could he possibly be up to in such a deserted street?

As a precaution, he raised his vigilance up one more level.

He took a deep breath, stretched his conscious mind to the tips of his toes, and adjusted his concentration to be ready for any situation.

─It was at that very moment that he was tapped on the shoulder from behind.

When he turned around, he saw Casper Crawford standing there with a thin smile on his face. The figure that should have been in front of him just a moment ago has vanished like an illusion or a phantom.

The sound coming from the construction site overlapped with the beating of his heart.

"The Empire has been so rowdy lately that it makes me unnecessarily cautious, huh?"

*Clang, clang, clang, clang.*

Suddenly, the sun was blocked by clouds, making it impossible to read the expression on the face of the person in front of him.

"So? Why did you follow me?"

*Clang, clang, clang, clang.*

First, he had to analyze his opponent's psychic ability. If he were to accept what just happened, Casper's ability would be something like instantaneous movement.

But then, what was the frightened look of those robbers?

Casper's dislike of men is a usual occurrence, but it would be a different story if he had lured them into an alleyway like this by pretending not to notice that he had been followed. It would not be any surprise if a large number of armed men were to pop out of the shadows right now.

"Seriously ...... I could escort her gently, though."

"W-Well, it means that Nina doesn't think so."

─What the heck? Is love really all he cares about?

Jin's usual tactic is to read what's going on behind the scenes and strip the other party's psychology completely bare, but with an opponent this unpredictable, even those basic principles won't work.

Even through the eyes of a fraudster, it is impossible to tell whether Casper really has nothing in mind, or whether he is actually devising a grand scheme behind the scenes, even though he appears to be full of gaps.

"Not at all! I'm actually on fire!"

From now on, Casper would be persistently pestering him.

Whether he truly thought of Jin as a love rival or whether he was actually the <shepherd's dog> and was looking for an excuse to get close to Jin - it would still be a troublesome situation.

If the situation were to get any worse, he would be in a catastrophically bad situation.

So Jin decided to accelerate the board at once.

Discussion.
