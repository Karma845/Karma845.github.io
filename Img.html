Have you ever heard of Carlos Kaiser, the famous fraudster?

He was a soccer player who played in Brazil in the 1980s, and is now known as <The Greatest Fraudster>. He was a liar who made a lot of money by signing contracts with several soccer clubs for more than 20 years by lying and scamming, even though he had almost no ability in soccer.

His methods were very clever. He got contracts by having star players he befriended in bars recommend him, bribed journalists to write articles praising his abilities, and when he was about to be forced to play a match, he had doctors write false medical diagnoses so he could be absent on sick leave. When a powerful owner with ties to gangsters ordered him to participate in a match, he pulled off a maneuver that would have been out of a Con Game movie (check out the movie to see what kind of maneuver he used).

Despite all this, he has never been arrested for fraud. On the contrary, he has been hailed as <the greatest soccer player who never played soccer>, and his life was even made into a movie.

It is a series of artful lies that even the deceivers would like to applaud, and such a miracle of lies has been created.
