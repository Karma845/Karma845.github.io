My eyes caught the flower accessory on her left wrist, something I made.

Queen of the Night.

It’s a beautiful flower that blooms only for one night.

Just before blooming, the bud faces upwards and unfolds its petals while exuding a fragrance.

Contrary to its glamorous appearance, its scent is so intense. Many people seem to dislike it for being too distinctive.

But once you get addicted to that smell, it’s over.

You end up devoting all your energy to caring for the flower, all for the sake of an encounter that blooms only for a single night, just a few hours.

As I looked into Enomoto-san’s serious eyes, such thoughts crossed my mind.

I confessed.

“…when I was in elementary school, I went on a trip to a botanical garden in the next prefecture. You know the one? It’s near Yufu, in a hot spring town.”

“Yes, I know it. They use the warm ground to display tropical plants. Like cacti, giant water lilies…”

Her response came unusually quickly.

Maybe Enomoto-san has been there too?

“I got separated from my sisters there. I had been searching for a long time, and I was exhausted in one of the greenhouses. That’s when I met a pretty girl.”

She was a black-haired girl in a white dress.

I had no idea where she came from. She was lost too.

She was so cute and looked very timid. She was sitting in a corner of the greenhouse, crying all by herself.

Even though I was also having a hard time being separated from my family, I ended up helping her find hers.

She cried the entire time, and it made everything twice as hard for me.

But when she tightly grasped the hem of my clothing and wouldn’t let go… I just couldn’t leave her alone.

I wasn’t wise enough to think that I should tell a staff member, so I just kept searching and eventually found her family… and then we said goodbye.

“She was being selfish, saying she wanted one of the displayed flowers. She was crying so much that I picked it for her. I knew it wasn’t allowed, but we were just kids. She held onto that flower, saying she’d take it home as a memory of today…but by the time we found her family, the flower had wilted.”

…Come to think of it, that was the beginning.

I became interested in flowers and eventually found a way to make them last longer.

“——If I could not only grow flowers but also process them skillfully… maybe, just maybe, I could reach her someday… Ah!”

I said too much. All Enomoto-san wanted to know was why I liked flowers. I don’t need to tell her why I made accessories afterward.

This story is admittedly too naive. Even Himari laughed at me a million times over.

◇ ◇ ◇

“…It’s creepy, right? Now, I wish you’d let it go.”

I shook off Enomoto-san’s hand.

I was a little concerned that I’d been too rough, but this is enough.

I never wanted to share this story with anyone else, and I’ve only ever told Himari.

“I’ll go pay for the tulips now——ugh!?”

I was pulled back again.

This time, my collar was grabbed. The situation felt more life-threatening than before.

“What is it, Enomoto-san? Do you find the story that funny—”

I turned around and involuntarily fell silent.

Enomoto-san had a flushed face as if she was in a fever.

She covered her mouth with the back of her left hand while her eyes were fixed on me.

“It was a hibiscus flower, wasn’t it? The flower the girl wanted to take home…”

“What?”

I was stunned. She had guessed correctly.

Hibiscus.

A large red flower that blooms in tropical regions. It’s the state flower of Hawaii.

In Japan, you can see it in places like Okinawa.

Its flower language signifies ‘delicate beauty’…and ‘new love.’

“How do you know that? Did you hear it from Himari?”

Enomoto-san shook her head.

While avoiding my gaze, she spoke in an almost inaudible voice.

“It was really beautiful. In the greenhouse, a whole wall of vivid red flowers was blooming. You said it resembled the girl’s reddish-black hair, right?”

“…I think I said something like that, yes.”

Then, Enomoto-san’s mouth relaxed a little. She gathered her hair and twirled it around her fingertips.

Her glossy, reddish-black hair swayed.

“She was happy to hear that word, so she wanted to take it home. That girl…she hated this reddish tint in her hair. She’s been mocked for its weird color since she was little. But since then, she has come to think it’s not so bad, um, what I’m trying to say is, uh…”

At that point, she covered her face with both hands.

The Queen of the Night accessory wrapped around my left wrist seemed to be smiling at us.

“Natsume-kun’s flower, it has reached her properly…”

“…….”

I involuntarily looked away.

There’s no way I could look at Enomoto-san’s face in this situation. I finally managed to say just this.

“Thank you for telling me…”

“Mm…”

Enomoto-san chuckled with an “Hehe.”

It was so cute that I thought my heart would burst.

My chest was pounding, and my body shaking.

If I had to describe this complicated feeling… I feel awkward to death, and I really wish Himari would come back soon.

…It seems I’ve run into my first love again after seven years.

♢♢♢

The day before yesterday at the bus stop, Enocchi said,

『Two years ago, when Onee-chan brought this Queen of the Night accessory home, I recognized it at first sight. I didn’t know why, but that boy from back then came to mind… He’s grown a bit though, it was surprising.』

As I listened to the story, I was at a loss for words.

Because I’d heard that story many times from Yuu. About the botanical garden, the girl in the dress—and the vivid red hibiscus.

Could such a thing happen?

It’s like something out of a manga. No, it’s impossible. What kind of odds would lead to something like this?

I thought it must be fate. What else could it be?

(Can I leave Yuu to Enocchi…?)

Rather, I felt like I should.

I had always thought that Yuu could only be left to someone who understood him, but I never thought it would be at this level.

She’s actually closer to Yuu than even a best friend.

So I’ve made up my mind. I’m going to support this love.

With a clenched fist, I swore to the heavens.

As Yuu’s best friend, I’ll make sure it comes to fruition perfectly!

Sorry, big brother(ani-ue)! Your future brother-in-law has found another place to go!

After leaving the flower shop, I went to Tully’s to buy a drink.

“First up is this—”

I got an iced honey milk latte topped with plenty of fluffy cream.

Believe it or not, it’s Yuu’s favorite drink. That guy loves sweets, even though he has such a grumpy face.

It must be fate that Enocchi’s family owns a pastry shop.

A perfect match, huh?

Deliberately, I bought only one. And then I put in two straws.

This is for Yuu and Enocchi to share.

It can’t be helped.

After all, I only have two arms, so I can only buy two drinks.

There’s no way I’m giving up my fruit tea, so it can’t be helped that Yuu and Enocchi have to share one.

It’s logic that even a grade-schooler could understand. Honestly… I wonder if they’ll try to drink together. Both of them are so earnest at heart.

They should feel awkward.

And realize they see each other intensely as the opposite sex.

Hmm. But Yuu is rather dense, he might just handle this situation calmly.

Well, if that’s the case, so be it. I’d be satisfied just seeing Enocchi getting all embarrassed by herself.

Today, we have a surplus of blushing beauty! My eyes are blessed; I won’t run out of material for a week!

(Now, I’m back at the flower shop, but…)

I couldn’t see Yuu and her. Maybe they’re still shopping.

When they’re done, they’ll probably be waiting in front of the store.

I peeked inside the shop. I could see the two of them standing side by side, engrossed in conversation.

…Huh. Unexpected.

I thought for sure they’d be wrapped in awkward silence. Yuu is surprisingly doing well.

But for now, let’s put an end to their alone time, because the cream will melt.

Yuu and the others are waiting for my miraculously cute smile!

“Yuu~! Hurry up and finish paying so we can move on… Oh?”

Feeling the tense atmosphere between the two, I instinctively hid behind a display case.

Enocchi seemed to have captured Yuu in some way. I wonder what Yuu is talking about; looking like he’s caught by the scruff of his neck.

(Ah…)

Listening closely, I heard Enocchi’s words.

“Natsume-kun’s flower, it has reached her properly…”

Heat seemed to rise as Enocchi spoke.

It was almost like her own confession of love.

Her eyes were so desperate that even I was captivated.

Do girls become that adorable when they express feelings they’ve kept hidden for years?

I can’t imagine a guy who wouldn’t be shaken after being confronted with such strong emotions.

And Yuu… was really confused.

His usual stern expression was easily shattered.

His face looked embarrassed and confused… but he definitely didn’t seem to dislike it.

Of course not.

The girl of his first love appeared before him like this, and she had become as cute as Enocchi.

It’s really like something out of a manga.

I realized I was wrong.

Maybe these two didn’t need my help after all.

Because they are connected by a destiny that meant they should meet.

I’m so happy. There’s no way I wouldn’t celebrate when my dear best friend and my precious old acquaintance are promised such a happy future.

But why is it?

(Yuu is going far away…)

When I thought something like that, my feet involuntarily carried me out of the flower shop… Really, I wonder why…
