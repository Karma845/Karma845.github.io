My little sister, who used to shy away from the outdoor activities, now eagerly anticipated beach outings. Not just that, she was also excited to go to school lately.

While it wasn’t exactly “seeing someone with fresh perspective after three days,” having made four friends in just over a month had really brightened Kotomi up.

And it was thanks to Momoi becoming her friend.

If preventing Momoi getting hit on meant Kotomi could have fun, then I was okay with going to the beach even without Takase.

Feeling a bit overwhelmed, I stepped away from the figure shelf and noticed the manga shelf.

“You don’t have that many manga.”

“Yeah, they take up space. I switched to ebooks halfway through. But for manga I really like, I still buy the physical copies for the benefits.”

“Oh, I see… Hey, you’ve got a lighter displayed here.”

I spotted a MioMio metal lighter on the desk. There was also a smartphone stand, a fan, plushies, and an apron draped over the chair. Even the zebra keychain I got her recently was there.

Seeing them displayed in such a special place made me happy to know they were cherished.

“Thanks for the wonderful presents, guys. I’ll make sure to return the favor on your birthdays.”

“I’ll be looking forward to it!”

“Me too!”

Momoi smiled at our response, and then she suddenly remembered something.

“Oh, speaking of presents, I have to give you guys swimsuits and sunglasses!”

With that, she retrieved a storage case from under her closet and popped it open, revealing over ten pairs of sunglasses neatly arranged inside.

“There are so many different kinds.”

The lenses varied in color and shape, but they all exuded a sense of luxury. If I was getting these, I better made sure to prevent any guys from getting close to Momoi.

“It gets boring if they’re all the same. Which one do you want?”

“I can choose anything?”

“Of course. If it’s too hard to decide, I can pick for you.”

“Alright then, I’ll leave it to you. Just pick one that you think suits me.”

“Got it. Any preferences?”

“Something that doesn’t let my eyes show through. And preferably not a weird shape.”

“I don’t remember buying anything weird… would you like a square-shaped one?”

“Which one?”

“This one.”

“Oh, that looks nice!”

It was exactly the black rectangular sunglasses I had in mind. I tried them on and checked my reflection; my eyes were properly concealed. With these, I wouldn’t give away my nervousness even when I saw them in swimsuits.

“Are you sure I can have this?”

“Yep, it’s yours.”

“Thank you very much! I’ll take good care of them!”

“You’re welcome,” Momoi said with a smile, “Now, onto swimsuits. I’ll call you when we’re done. Can you wait in the living room?”

“Roger that.”

I left the two in the bedroom and made my way to the living room. Settling onto the couch, I idly tapped away on my phone until I was called from the other side of the door, “You can come in!”

I opened the door, finding Kotomi in a bikini. It was a brown color, with one shoulder strap hanging off. It must had been designed that way rather than torn.

“H-How do I look? Not weird…?” she asked, shyly averting her gaze. Even without sunglasses, I managed to keep my composure at the sight of my sister in a swimsuit.

“It suits you.”

“Really?”

“Yeah. With that, you’ll blend right in at the beach. Have fun swimming.”

“Yay! I can’t wait to hit the beach! Thank you, Momoi-san!”

“You’re welcome. Let’s have lots of fun!”

“Totally!”

I left the excited pair in the room and waited again in the living room. Shortly after, they both came out.

Now then, our purpose for coming here had been fulfilled, but it felt wrong to just take the gifts and say goodbye. It was only just past four. If Momoi wanted to hang out, I guess we could stick around…

“By the way, how are you two feeling? Are you hungry?”

“We’re kinda hungry.”

“We didn’t really eat much for lunch.”

Momoi looked at us with concern, “Are you feeling unwell?”

“No, it’s just that we’ll be having sushi tonight.”

“It’s a reward for working hard on exams.”

“Ah, I see. In that case, you better work up an appetite.”

Momoi frowned disappointedly. She probably wanted to treat us to a meal. Considering all the things we had received, I shouldn’t let her down.

“If I say I’m hungry, are you gonna make fried rice?”  

“That was the plan, but… are you really hungry?”

Seeing an expectant look in her eyes, I nodded, “I have a separate stomach for your fried rice.”  

“Then I’ll get cooking!”  

With a cheerful bounce in her voice, Momoi headed to the bedroom and returned wearing an apron. It was the one with a dog peeking out of the pocket that Takase had gifted her.

“I’ll whip it up real quick, so just relax and watch some anime.”  

“Then I’m gonna watch Driste,” I said, trying to steer clear of anime I didn’t know and impending stomach churn it brought.  

“Then let’s watch the live scenes! The sound’s way better than at home!”  

“Sounds good to me.”

Kotomi promptly turned on the TV. It seemed Momoi had subscribed to the same streaming service as us, as Kotomi navigated the remote with ease and selected a live episode of Doriste.  

We fast forward through the opening and began watching from the live scene. The dynamic sound from the large speakers filled the room, lifting our spirits effortlessly.

“It feels like we’re at the concert!”  

“The difference in power is insane.”  

“The three of us should go to a concert together someday!”

“I’m totally down! I wanna wave a glow stick!”

“If we buy tons of merch at the booth and then hitting karaoke on the way back, it’ll be so much fun, right?”  

“That sounds like a blast!”  

Both of us were really getting into it. I was interested in concerts too, and if we went, I’d just have to watch the related anime beforehand. I’d definitely tag along if they did decide to go.  

As I was pondering this, Momoi started to cook. A slightly crispy aroma wafted over. Last time, the seasoning didn’t have much flavor, but now I could smell the soy sauce. She said she had been improving day by day, so maybe I could look forward to this.

“Sorry to keep you waiting!”  

When the live episode wrapped up, Momoi brought over the fried rice.  

“Whoa, it looks delicious!” Kotomi said. She wasn’t just being polite, it genuinely did look appetizing.  

The rice was a bit mushy last time, but now it looked fluffy. The ingredients were finely chopped, and the thinly sliced pork tantalized the taste buds.

“Eat it while it’s hot.”  

“Will do.”  

I took a spoonful and tasted it. The flavors of salt, pepper, and soy sauce were perfectly blended with a hint of sesame oil. The rice was stir-fried to perfection, each grain bursting with flavor.

“This is delicious!”

“Really!?”  

“Yeah, it’s so good. If they served this in the cafeteria, I’d eat it every day.”  

“I’m happy you like it so much… hehe.”

Momoi smiled happily, and it was contagious. Her happiness somehow made me happy too.  

“I owe you a big thank you.”  

“Why me?”  

“Because it’s all thanks to you that I can make delicious fried rice now.”

“I didn’t do anything. You’re the one who worked hard.”  

“No, it’s because of you. Your motivation makes all the difference.”

Was she the type who thrive on praise? Even though her improvement was all her own effort,when she said that, I couldn’t bring myself to deny it.

“If you want, I can praise you more. Oh, you’ve mastered yakisoba and neapolitan too, right?”

“Yep! I’ve got those down. I’ll make them for you next time, so come hang out during summer break. Both of you are welcome.”

“Yeah! I’ll definitely come over!”

“But you gotta do your homework first.”

“Then let’s do our homework together. With you here, Haruto-kun, we’ll finish in no time. Then we can have all summer to play!”

“Yay!”

While I preferred Kotomi to do her own homework, it sounded like it could be fun with Momoi around. When I agreed to that plan, the two of them happily exchanged high-fives.
