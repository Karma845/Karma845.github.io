Interlude

“I never imagined I’d actually meet her in person.”

I thought to myself.

I hadn’t considered the possibility of running into the girl who was the reason I ended up going to the bar with Takashi-kun. And of all places, it had to be at my favorite bar.

What a coincidence.

Just like me, she seemed to have her own issues.

Of course, everyone has their own circumstances, reasons I couldn’t possibly know.

“I’d like to have a proper conversation with her sometime, maybe even about Takashi-kun.”

I found myself taking a liking to her, to Yuu-chan.

Admittedly, my first impression wasn’t great.

Who wouldn’t think so?

It’s quite rude to keep your hoodie on during service. But if the establishment is fine with her appearance, then I guess it’s not my place to complaint so much about it?

Instead, I’m dying to know what’s going on in her mind that she continues to wear her hood like that.

“I wonder what Yuu-chan likes…”

What she likes, what she dislikes, and how she became friends with Takashi-kun.

I want to know all sorts of things about her. Because she’s Takashi-kun’s best friend, I’m desperate to know more about her.

“Maybe I can learn more about what kind of person Takashi-kun likes.”

If everyone knew what was in my heart, they’d probably find it creepy or weird. Without really knowing me, most people only know me superficially.

But I don’t care about that. Rather, they’re not even in my sight.

All I need is Takashi-kun.

In the restroom of the bar, I quietly thought so as I touched up my makeup.
