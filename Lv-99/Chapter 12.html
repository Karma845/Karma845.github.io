The buzz of the crowd brought back memories of the events at the festival.

“Kinoshita-san, I…”

“I wish you would play the song you were playing then.”

Yayoi-chan looked at me with innocent eyes as if she was anticipating me to do so, but I was hesitant to touch the piano.

I had not played the piano once since that festival.

“Fukase-kun, what’s wrong?”

I froze, and a concerned voice called out to me.

“No, it’s nothing…”

I was in a rush, remembering my failure, but it was nothing compared to that tense situation.

I just took a little break from the piano. I haven’t played for six months, but that doesn’t mean I can’t play anymore. I’ll just play a few bars quickly and show her.

Yayoi-chan is doing her best to make today’s date go well. She made herself smile so she wouldn’t glare at me. I know it must have been awkward for her to see her father, but she told me what she wanted to do.

I promised you earlier that I would make the date a success, too.

“Okay, I’ll play it.”

I looked at the keyboard from left to right, and finally found the middle “Do” and put my right thumb on it.

The piano felt cold and distant after six months of contact.

I let out a big breath and tried to play.

–Huh?

My fingers wouldn’t move.

–Why?

My mind went completely dark.

–What’s going on?

My hands are shaking.

─ ─ You’re kidding me, right ……?

I can’t play the piano?

 

“Hah”

I heard that sigh.

The sigh of regret that lingered in my mind, traumatized and bound to my body.

I could no longer play the piano.

“Fukase-kun…”

“I’m sorry, I just can’t remember the sheet music.”

I apologized to Yayoi-chan, forcing a smile and trying to eat it up.

“I-is that so?”

Yayoi-chan lowered her eyebrows apologetically, but did not hide her disappointed expression.

I shoved my trembling hands into my pockets so that they wouldn’t be seen.

“I haven’t been playing lately. So I forget how to play so easily.”

Yayoi-chan was looking forward to it, yet I lied and cheated her.

The moment I touched the piano, my fear returned.

What if I fail again? She would laugh at me. She would be disappointed.

Such fear made it impossible for me to play the piano.

“No, it’s fine, it’s fine! It’s my fault for asking you to do something like that out of the blue!”

Yayoi-chan waved her hands dejectedly, seemingly believing my lie.

When they realized I wasn’t going to play the piano, the people around me began to scatter as if they were out of patience.

I manage to catch my breath and try to shake off the unpleasant images in my head.

Why is this happening to me? I can’t believe I froze in front of the piano.

“I’m sorry…”

Yayoi-chan apologized once again. There was no longer any atmosphere of a date.

I was still reeling from the shock of not being able to play the piano, and Yayoi-chan was remembering how seeing her father had brought her back to reality, and we both

seemed to be thinking about different things.

“Shall we go home for today?”

“Yes.”

And so our first date came to an end.

It turned out to be just a date taking place in the morning, with a sweet and sour aftertaste.
