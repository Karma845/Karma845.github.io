It was 4:00 PM.

Just like before, both of them casually browsed through the large shopping center until they felt at ease.

Arriving at the Nozomi Aquarium—where they could encounter about 650 species and approximately 20,000 marine creatures—the two of them finished checking in and chatted while looking at the pamphlet provided.

“Wow, I’m really looking forward to this…” (Haruto)

“Hehe, me too.” (Aya)

For Aya, who had put so much effort into planning today’s date, seeing her partner genuinely excited like this brought her immense joy.

“To be honest, it’s been about ten years since I last came to the aquarium.” (Haruto)

“For me, it’s been exactly ten years!” (Aya)

“Shirayuki-san too!?” (Haruto)

“Yes! That’s why when I got your message saying ‘I like fish’, I was excited. That’s how I decided on the plan.” (Aya)

“That was a good idea. Thank you for planning all this for today.” (Haruto)

“It’s fine, it’s fine!” (Aya)

Choosing an unfamiliar but suitable location was a tough task, but she was receiving such gratitude in return.

Getting to know what Haruto liked, and being able to spend this enjoyable time together.

She felt rewarded.

“…Apparently, this aquarium is open at night too… It says here that the atmosphere is different, but I wonder if it’s that significant.” (Haruto)

“…Haruto-san, the nighttime visitors are definitely all couples.” (Aya)

“Haha, that’s true.” (Haruto)

When researching Nozomi Aquarium, Aya quickly discovered that it was open at night as well.

Considering it was a ‘date’, coming in the evening might have offered a more atmospheric and enjoyable experience.

However, she deliberately left it out of the plan.

One of the reasons was… Haruto’s family situation.

Considering that his sister was waiting for him to return, it wasn’t ideal for the date to last that long.

And the main reason, as she mentioned, was the difference in the crowd.

In a romantic place like an aquarium at night, it was easy to imagine that there would be a lot of couples.

If she were to walk around such a place with someone she cared about… the surroundings might influence them.

She might end up doing something strange while getting carried away by the atmosphere.

She might even end up doing something bold.

To avoid the possibility of doing something that might make her disliked, Aya chose a time when there were many families around.

“Well then, Haruto-san. Which way should we start? If we take the right path, we’ll see the ocean fish first, and if we take the left path, we’ll see the river fish first.” (Aya)

“Can I really decide such an important thing!?” (Haruto)

“I’m honestly fine with either, no need to be considerate.” (Aya)

“Then… I want to see the ocean fish first. I think that’s the main attraction, but I can’t wait any longer!” (Haruto)

“Hehe, then let’s go right!” (Aya)

“Sorry for being selfish, and thanks for indulging me.” (Haruto)

“No, it’s fine. Come on, let’s go, let’s go!” (Aya)

“Yeah!” (Haruto)

For Aya, the order in which they visited didn’t matter.

All she wanted was to be able to walk with Haruto… that was her honest feeling.

To convey that there was no need to apologize or thank her, she firmly pushed Haruto’s back as they entered the right-hand aisle.

And there, they were greeted by small fishes swimming gracefully in tanks taller than their own height.

Some had beautiful patterns, others had distinctive features, and some had cute faces.

Seeing this scene made them instantly realize they were at an aquarium. Drawn in, they stood side by side in front of the large tank.

“Shirayuki-san, just watching this is pretty fun.” (Haruto)

“Same here!” (Aya)

It was fun to see the fish gracefully swim, moving their dorsal and tail fins… and to see Haruto’s side profile as he was absorbed in the fish.

Of course, for the latter, she made sure not to stare so much as not to get caught.

“……” (Haruto)

“……” (Aya)

Though there was a period of silence, it wasn’t awkward at all.

Aya subtly moved closer until their shoulders almost touched and enjoyed the pleasant moment they had together.

“Haruto-san, if you want, it’s okay to take pictures of the fish… As long as you don’t use flash, it’s allowed.” (Aya)

“Ah, no, I’m fine.” (Haruto)

“Really…? Are you holding back because of the atmosphere or something?” (Aya)

“It’s more of a personal thing. When I start taking pictures, I get so focused on getting a good shot that it becomes hard to remember the experience. So, I’ll just take a quick snapshot in my brain.” (Haruto)

“I see.” (Aya)

Haruto’s words reassured her that he was enjoying himself.

Just that alone made her incredibly happy. It filled her from her fingertips to her toes. Her face even started to feel warm.

“On the other hand, Shirayuki-san, aren’t you going to take any pictures…? I don’t think you have the same problem as me.” (Haruto)

“I’m fine as it is. If it were a place I’d only visit once, I wouldn’t say this, but if I ever want to see the fish again, I’ll just rely on Haruto-san to come here with me.” (Aya)

“Haha, I’d be happy to.” (Haruto)

Noticing that he didn’t seem entirely displeased, she quickly looked at the tank to avoid feeling embarrassed.

“…” (Haruto)

“…” (Aya)

And then, once again, another moment of silence.

“…If I had a bit more experience with socializing, maybe I could say something clever in times like these…” (Haruto)

Haruto murmured, lost in thought.

“Hehe, quiet moments like this are important too, so don’t worry about it. Though, I’m curious about what you mean by ‘in times like these’. Did you want to say something earlier?” (Aya)

“Ah, haha. You’re really sharp. It’s just that… yeah. There’s something I haven’t told you yet, Shirayuki-san.” (Haruto)

“Something… you haven’t told me yet?” (Aya)

“Um, well, when I first met you earlier, Shirayuki-san, I couldn’t help but notice how well-dressed you were, so I wanted to tell you that what you’re wearing looks good on you.” (Haruto)

“!!” (Aya)

“…It’s amazing that there are people out there who could say such things so easily.” (Haruto)

“S-Sure there are people like that, but we can go at our own pace. Actually, if you don’t, my inexperienced heart wouldn’t be able to handle it.” (Aya)

“It’s a relief to hear you say that.” (Haruto)

There are certainly those who prefer someone more experienced in romance to take the lead in everything.

However, Aya held a different opinion.

Because they were both inexperienced, she hoped they could build their experiences together. To deepen their relationship step by step, feeling their way through it together.

“Um… Haruto-san, your clothes really suit you too.” (Aya)

“T-Thanks.” (Haruto)

“I… I can’t say things like that unless you say them first, so I think you’re really amazing, Haruto-san.” (Aya)

“Thank you… Personally, I’m happier if you stay just the way you are. I think it’s a guy’s role to say these things.” (Haruto)

“Then, I’ll take you up on that…” (Aya)

It’s less nerve-wracking to respond after being told first. It allows the conversation to flow naturally.

So, knowing that the person she cares about thinks this way, Aya couldn’t be happier.

“And there’s one more thing I have to say… um, it’s beautiful.” (Haruto)

“!!” (Aya)

Haruto said this while glancing at the fish in the tank in front of him.

“Nee! You can’t say something like that and then follow it up with a comment about the fish… It makes me think you’re talking bout me… I’ll misunderstand…” (Aya)

“…Some people need a bit of help to say such things.” (Haruto)

“…Ah.” (Aya)

Aya, who had seriously believed that, felt even more frustrated now.

But as she looked at Haruto’s profile again, she realized she had been mistaken.

It was the same expression he had when they saw each other at the PC specialty shop’s headphone section—a shy, embarrassed expression.

“Hehe… So, from your perspective, Haruto-san, I look beautiful?” (Aya)

“Don’t get too carried away.” (Haruto)

“But I’m happy.” (Aya)

Her expression pleaded for him to say it again, but he ignored her to the point where she knew he had reached his limit.

Still, she could tell he genuinely meant it.

The happiness she felt made her want to dance.

Because she was getting carried away, she had something to say in return.

“Then… I think it looks cool.” (Aya)

Mimicking her words while looking at the fish, Haruto’s shoulders jerked noticeably.

Aya felt his gaze from beside her but didn’t meet it.

Pretending not to notice, she used her hand to cover her reddening ears.

Realizing that none of the exhibited fish could be described as “cool”…

Haruto seemed to have thought the same thing as she did earlier, as Aya caught sight of him scratching his cheek out of the corner of her eye.

“Um, shall we move to the next section soon…?” (Haruto)

“Y-Yeah, sounds good!” (Aya)

“And… thank you for your kind words.” (Haruto)

“I-I don’t know what you’re talking about.” (Aya)

“I was hoping you would answer properly.” (Haruto)

Responding with a “You’re welcome” would be like revealing that she had called Haruto “cool”.

But… there was one thing she wanted to point out.

“Even though you already know what I mean, Haruto-san…” (Aya)

As soon as she said that, Haruto covered his face with one hand to avoid being seen from the side.

“…Hehe.” (Aya)

Seeing her partner less composed than herself brings a sense of relief.

“Haruto-san, when you’re ready, shall we move to the next section?” (Aya)

“That would be great…” (Haruto)

This time, she took the lead.

The best defense is a good offense. That was one of the lessons learned at this moment.

Moving on to the next section, the atmosphere changed drastically from before, to a pitch-black space.

 “A-Amazing…” (Haruto)

“Wow, this looks beautiful…” (Aya)

A sight so beautiful that words couldn’t describe it.

As they gazed around without blinking, Haruto called out to them.

“Shirayuki-san! Can I go take a closer look!? There’s a huge jellyfish over there!” (Haruto)

“Hehe, of course, go ahead.” (Aya)

Seemingly more interested in ‘big’ rather than ‘beautiful’, Haruto eagerly approached the jellyfish with quick steps.

Following him slowly, Aya caught up, Standing next to Haruto again, she took advantage of his fascination with the jellyfish to quietly close the distance until their shoulders touched.

This had already succeeded once before. Perhaps he hadn’t noticed, or maybe he didn’t mind, either way, there were no signs of disdain.

It felt natural.

“Oh, it’s a real jellyfish… It’s really huge…” (Haruto)

(Hehe…)

With sparkling eyes, he leaked his impressions while gazing at the jellyfish swimming around gently.

“Wow…” (Haruto)

Despite his usually mature demeanor, he seemed somewhat childish now.

Captivated by this gap, Aya continued to enjoy the blissful time.

Recalling the compliments of ‘it looks good on you’ and ‘beautiful’, she alternated her gaze between the jellyfish and his face.

It was an unconventional way to enjoy the aquarium, but for Aya, it was the most fulfilling.

“What do you think that jellyfish is thinking as it swims…” (Haruto)

(—Pfft)

Though she could somewhat understand what he meant, she nearly burst out laughing at the unexpected question.

But Aya managed to stifle her laughter.

(Ah, that was close…)

Normally, it wouldn’t be a problem to laugh.

However… Not right now.

For now, she wanted him to focus on what was in front of him.

She didn’t want him to notice her.

(If I’m going to say it, now’s the time… It’s now or never… It’ll be okay, definitely…)

When Aya was preparing the date plan beforehand, she set three goals to achieve during their time together.

She had determined that achieving the second goal would be possible in this section of the aquarium, based on her research of the aquarium beforehand.

As a novice in romance, she knew that if she missed this opportunity, she might never get another chance again.

So, to create unforgettable memories and avoid any regrets, she rallied herself.

Just like how it was during her countless practices, she called out to him.

“…H-Haruto-kun, do you like jellyfish?” (Aya)

“Yeah, I love them. They’re such unique creatures, and I really like their relaxed vibe.” (Haruto)

“I see, I see.” (Aya)

He didn’t seem to notice the change in how she addressed him.

(Hehe, I did it… I finally called him!)

Could a plan even go this smoothly? Aya couldn’t help but smile, feeling so happy that she almost wanted to jump for joy right then and there.

“Do you like jellyfish too, Shirayuki-san?” (Haruto)

“I like them enough to be able to watch them for hours.” (Aya)

“I’m glad to hear that. I was worried it might become boring if you weren’t interested in them.” (Haruto)

“Oh, don’t worry about that. Even if I wasn’t interested, I wouldn’t find it boring with you.” (Aya)

He really is a kind person.

Though it was a bit embarrassing, Aya thought it would be reassuring to let him know.

“Huh? So, does that mean… there’s something else more fun?” (Haruto)

“Yeah. Like observing Haruto-kun’s happy expression.” (Aya)

“W-Well, don’t do that! It’s embarrassing…” (Haruto)

“Hehe, luckily for you, I’m interested in the animals at the aquarium, so there’s no need for that.” (Aya)

“Mou… You’re quite the tease…” (Haruto)

He showed a relieved expression.

(I’m sorry, Haruto-kun. I lied. I couldn’t help but watch you.)

Though she apologized silently in her mind, she couldn’t help but continue to unconsciously glance at his profile.

Because of that, Aya was able to notice.

“Oh! Haruto-kun, Haruto-kun. There’s another big jellyfish up there on the right.” (Aya)

“Where, where, where… Oh! That jellyfish over there has a different pattern.” (Haruto)

“I prefer this jellyfish over here though.” (Aya)

“Hmm… It’s hard to choose for me…” (Haruto)

He pondered with a troubled expression.

There was still no sign that he noticed, but this was also something she had anticipated.

(I’m sorry again… Haruto-kun. I’m really timid… But I’ve wanted to call you this for so long.)

She knows that she should have asked for permission before changing the way she addressed him. She knows that he’s kind and would likely forgive her when asked. 

But she still couldn’t muster up the courage in her novice heart to pull it off.

In Aya’s mind, the moment he notices the change, she could prompt with a ‘Is it okay?’ and that would be her limit.

Still, she managed to achieve one of her goals.

If he notices, then she can ask for permission.

Even if he doesn’t notice, she can continue calling him ‘Haruto-kun’, the name she’s always wanted to use.

(B-But this is nerve-wracking… I hope Haruto-kun can’t hear the sound of my heart pounding…)

She took a step back, trying to create some distance between them.

It was troublesome how she couldn’t shake the feeling of not wanting to be apart from him.

“I know it’s a bit late to ask, but there are various types of jellyfish here, right? Shirayuki-san, are there any jellyfish that caught your interest?” (Haruto)

“Yeah, there are. I’d like to see some small jellyfish too.” (Aya)

“Ahaha… That’s true. I’m sorry for staying here for so long.” (Haruto)

“No, it was fun, so it’s fine.” (Aya)

(…And because of that, I was able to change how I address him.)

For Aya, the big jellyfish that captivated him was her savior.

“It’s a bit dark, so be careful not to trip over any steps, Haruto-kun.” (Aya)

Just like when she secretly bought the same wireless headphones as him and got their first pairing item, she felt like skipping with joy.

With the same feeling as when they synchronized and reached out for the same wireless earphones, Aya quietly took his hand, paid for their tickets, and felt like skipping with joy.

(I’m really happy…)

Thanks to achieving yet another goal, she was able to enjoy the aquarium more than any other visitor.

And she could even imagine herself becoming unable to speak out of nervousness.

(I’d be happy if Haruto-kun felt the same way as me…)

With narrowed eyes, Aya sent her gaze to him, hoping to convey her feelings.

“I-I just want to do it, okay…? To be honest, I’m hoping it might be a chance for me to get closer to imouto-san.” (Aya)

“…Are you really just doing this because you want to, Aya-san? You’re not doing it out of obligation or anything?” (Haruto)

“Rather, I think it’s beneficial for me in the long run, so I hope you can indulge me just this once.” (Aya)

“I-I guess if you put it that way… then, I’ll have no choice but to accept it.” (Haruto)

“Hehe, thank you.” (Aya)

“But, just so you know, expensive sushi is definitely off the table, okay?” (Haruto)

“Got it!” (Aya)

Even though she was the one paying, Aya looked positively delighted. Seeing her smile like that, Haruto couldn’t help but imagine Yuno happily accepting the sushi with a cheerful “Yay”.

Haruto could also envision a future where he would be calmly scolded with “Why did you let her inconvenience herself like that?”

Preparing himself for a good scolding upon returning home, especially with the plushie incident added in, he found himself looking forward to seeing his sister’s rapidly changing expressions.

“Right, before that, we need to choose our dinner too.” (Haruto)

With that, Aya switched her expression and timidly approached the sofa with the tablet in hand.

“Um, maybe… could I sit next to you so we can look at the menu together…?” (Aya)

“Of course. Though it’s kind of weird for the host to say something like that.” (Haruto)

Haruto teased her lightly as he showed Aya the penguin and sea turtle plushies he held in his arms.

“Which one do you prefer, Aya-san, the penguin or the sea turtle?” (Haruto)

“Hehe, then I’ll go with Haruto-kun’s sea turtle!” (Aya)

“Here you go.” (Haruto)

It was evident that she was somewhat nervous. But as he handed the plushie over with both hands, her expression softened considerably, and she settled down next to him.

With the plushies on their lap, they browsed through the menu together.

As they peered into the tablet, there were occasional moments when their shoulders would brush against each other. Haruto tried his best not to be too conscious of it.

On the other hand, Aya was keenly aware of every moment their shoulders touched or their faces came close as they peered into the tablet, trying to make the most of these moments to delay the inevitable separation that would come once they finished their order.

Aya suddenly felt a thought cross her mind.

(I wonder if this is how it feels like to live together…?)

It was the kind of thought that would invite people to tease her if shared with anyone else. But it was precisely because she had this thought that she had decided to reconsider something.

“…” (Aya)

She had changed the way she addressed him for a while now. She had been careful not to let him notice until now.

She couldn’t help but wonder if, by obtaining his permission and acknowledgment for calling him that way, she could feel even more like they were living together.

Once that feeling had taken root, she couldn’t keep it to herself any longer.

“Um, Haruto-kun…” (Aya)

“What’s up?” (Haruto)

They were in the middle of looking at the menu, but she decided to convey her thoughts.

“Um… Haruto-kun, you see, since we entered the jellyfish section at the aquarium, there’s something I’ve been doing… Do you have any idea what it might be?” (Aya)

Anticipating that he might be puzzled, she considered guiding him to the answer by giving hints, and just as she tilted her head to do so…

She was met with something unexpected.

“You mean when you changed how you addressed me from ‘Haruto-san’ to ‘Haruto-kun’?” (Haruto)

“…” (Aya)

That was the correct answer that left her momentarily stunned.

“W-W-Wait a minute! You noticed, Haruto-kun!?” (Aya)

“I was so focused on the jellyfish that I only noticed after being called that a few times.” (Haruto)

“But… you didn’t seem surprised at all?” (Aya)

“I’m called that a lot at work, so I’m used to it. A-And also… I felt happy when you called me that, so I didn’t want to bring it up in case you changed back…” (Haruto)

“!!” (Aya)

She thought he simply hadn’t noticed, but it was completely different.

The words “happy to be called” and the desire not to “change it back”.

And the realization that she had been consciously overusing his name because she thought that “he hadn’t noticed”.

Various emotions surged within, causing heat to flush her face.

Feeling like she might overheat herself, she hugged the plushie so tightly it felt like its neck might break.

“Then, let me share something too. Since that jellyfish section , I’ve been doing something to you, Aya-san. Any guesses?” (Haruto)

“Huh!? W-Wait, something you’ve been doing to me!? L-Like secretly taping something on my clothes?” (Aya)

“Haha, I wouldn’t do something sneaky like that.” (Haruto)

She quickly checked his clothes just in case… but as expected, there was nothing as he mentioned.

“I really have no idea… Are you sure there’s something? Tell me the answer!” (Aya)

“Then, the answer is… I also changed the way I address you.” (Haruto)

“…Hmm?” (Aya)

As soon as she was told, she blinked rapidly, trying to recall how she had been addressed.

“Haruto-kun, you used to call me ‘Aya-san’, right?” (Aya)

“Until before the jellyfish section, I always called you ‘Shirayuki-san’.” (Haruto)

“…………Ah!! Th-That’s right!!” (Aya)

After a long moment of silence, Aya experienced her biggest surprise of the day.

“Eeeh… I-I totally didn’t notice… I-I guess I was just too focused on calling you ‘Haruto-kun’…” (Aya)

“Hahaha, maybe it’s because I sometimes call you ‘Ayaya’ when we play ABEX together, calling you ‘Aya-san’ didn’t feel strange.” (Haruto)

“I’m sure that played a part…” (Aya)

The intention was to inform him about the change in how he was addressed, but somehow, the roles had reversed.

Despite feeling a mix of emotions, a desire to indulge emerged.

“Hey, Haruto-kun. It might be selfish of me, but… I want you to call me that one more time.” (Aya)

“Your name…?” (Haruto)

“Yeah, just because I’m curious, now that you’ve told me…” (Aya)

Now, with this new perspective, the feeling when called will be different.

To confirm this, she made such a request.

“Aya-san.” (Haruto)

“…” (Aya)

Their eyes met as he called her.

Just that alone made her heart race. It was embarrassing.

But hearing it once wasn’t enough; she wanted to hear it again.

“One more time…” (Aya)

“A-Aya-san…” (Haruto)

“…” (Haruto)

“…” (Aya)

“T-That’s all you get for now!” (Haruto)

Their eyes met only until this point.

Closing his mouth tightly, Haruto’s ear turned red again as he shifted his gaze to the tablet.

At that moment, a wave of embarrassment surged through Aya as she witnessed Haruto’s blushing expression, which was a newfound weakness for her.

“…R-Right! Haruto-san, let’s decide on the menu quickly!” (Aya)

“Wait a minute. The way you’re calling me has reverted…” (Haruto)

“I-It’s… on a cooldown, okay?! I’ll… I’ll revert back soon!” (Aya)

“Are you sure?” (Haruto)

“Y-Yeah!” (Aya)

—At this moment, the plushies they were both hugging seemed to be struggling under the pressure.

∮

It was past 9:30 PM.

After finishing the meal delivered to their door, they spent the rest of the time relaxing and enjoying themselves in the living room.

“Thanks for today, Aya-san. I had a really great time.” (Haruto)

“Yeah…” (Aya)

At the entrance of the apartment building, Haruto and Aya exchanged farewells.

“Hey, Haruto-kun… I can walk you home, you know?” (Aya)

“Thank you. But just having that thought means a lot. It’s late for a woman to be out alone.” (Haruto)

“Is it okay if I insist on wanting to walk you home…?” (Aya)

“No, it’s not.” (Haruto)

Haruto, who usually indulges her in anything, responds decisively.

His determination to not give in is clear.

At this point, it seems like no matter what approach she takes, he won’t let her walk him home.

“In exchange, though it may not be much, I’ll be the one to invite you to hang out next time as soon as possible.” (Haruto)

“Really…?” (Aya)

“Absolutely.” (Haruto)

“If you’re lying… I’ll bite your ear, Haruto-kun.” (Aya)

“Haha, I’ll do my best to avoid that.” (Haruto)

“…Then, okay. I’ll endure it…” (Aya)

Though parting ways here is sad, being invited out again makes her happy.

In an effort to express her excitement, Aya nods enthusiastically.

“When I invite you next time, I’ll make sure to plan something even more fun than today, so look forward to it.” (Haruto)

“That’s reassuring to hear. I know you’ll do your best. By the way, you’re always welcome to come over to my place. Just make sure to let me know, okay?” (Aya)

“Got it. And thank you for the sushi.” (Haruto)

“You’re welcome.” (Aya)

“When giving it to her, say hello to imouto-san for me, okay, Haruto-kun?” (Aya)

“I’ll make sure to convey that it’s ‘from Aya-san’.” (Haruto)

“Please do.” (Aya)

And with that, the conversation comes to an end.

From the atmosphere, it’s evident that the next words will be the parting words.

At this moment, Aya desperately tries to think of something, anything, she can do before parting.

“Well then, I guess that’s it… See you later, Aya-san.” (Aya)

“…Haruto-kun.” (Haruto)

“Hmm?” (Aya)

Here, only one thing comes to mind.

“Now, let’s end with a high-five!” (Aya)

“Oh! Then, high five it is.” (Haruto)

As Aya held her hand up with her palm facing out, Haruto extended his hand and overlapped it with hers.

Just this simple gesture brings a sense of warmth to her heart.

But… truth be told, she didn’t want just a high five; she wanted to hold his hand tightly and keep Haruto with her a bit longer.

But, she couldn’t.

This high-five was the most intimate gesture Aya could muster from her timid self.

Dragging this out any further would only worry Haruto’s sister.

So, as their hands separate from the high-five… She put on a smile.

“Haruto-kun, keep up the good work with your job!” (Aya)

“Yeah! Well then, bye-bye, Aya-san.” (Haruto)

“Ahh… I really don’t want to say goodbye…” (Aya)

“Haha, I’ll make sure to contact you once I’m home.” (Haruto)

“Mmm…” (Aya)

Though she unintentionally lets her true feelings slip, she doesn’t want to cause any trouble by acting on them.

She watches Haruto’s back as he gradually moves away.

――He pauses, just as he’s about to turn the corner.

“Ah…” (Aya)

Haruto turned around and waved his hand. As Aya returned the gesture, Haruto finally turned the corner.

He was gone from sight.

Though this was usually the time when she felt lonely…

“Hehe…” (Haruto)

Aya managed a small smile.

It might just be her imagination.

It might just be her imagination, but she felt like he had a reluctant expression, as if he didn’t want to leave.

“N-No! Absolutely not! The only guy I trust enough to invite over is you, Haruto-kun!”
